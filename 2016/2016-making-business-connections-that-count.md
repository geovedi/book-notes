# Making Business Connections That Count
**Authors**: Michal Stawicki | **Year**: 2016

## Summary
"Making Business Connections That Count" is a practical guide that transforms networking from a daunting task into a natural, authentic process. Written by a former hardcore introvert who went from having zero business contacts to building a profitable business and connecting with influencers who have millions of followers, this book proves that anyone can master meaningful professional relationships.

The book's core message is that effective networking isn't about manipulation or secret techniques—it's about authenticity, consistency, and genuine service to others. The author shares his personal journey from an IT employee with no business experience to a successful author who has been featured by major influencers, demonstrating that vulnerability and tenacity are more valuable than any networking "gimmick."

This book is perfect for introverts, aspiring entrepreneurs, freelancers, and anyone who feels overwhelmed by traditional networking advice. Readers will learn how to build genuine relationships that lead to business opportunities without resorting to pushy sales tactics or fake personalities.

## Core Insights

### Authenticity Trumps Technique
The most powerful networking tool is being yourself. The author emphasizes that there's no "secret sauce" to connecting with others—just authenticity and tenacity. When you're genuine, people are naturally drawn to you because authenticity is rare and valuable. Everyone wants to live authentically, but few do, creating high demand for those who dare to be real.

> "There rarely is any 'secret sauce' in any business realm. You need only authenticity and tenacity to be effective in networking. Those are universal human traits and everyone can apply them, including you."

**Quick Take**: Stop trying to impress people and start being yourself. Your authenticity is your greatest networking asset because it's rare and attractive.

### Vulnerability Creates Connection
We're all vulnerable at heart, regardless of success level. When you show vulnerability—sharing your true self, including fears and struggles—you become magnetic to others. Vulnerability isn't weakness; it's courage that everyone admires but few exhibit. The author, a former introvert who couldn't talk to strangers, discovered that sharing his limitations made him more relatable and approachable.

> "Showing vulnerability is a key to making connections. When you demonstrate your vulnerability—when you expose your true self to the world—you become special by default. People admire you. People respect you. People are attracted to you."

**Quick Take**: Share your real story, including struggles and limitations. Your vulnerability makes you human and approachable, cutting through the noise of perfect facades.

### Consistency Over Intensity
Small, consistent actions compound over time to create meaningful relationships. The author built connections with major influencers not through grand gestures but through consistent support: commenting on posts, sharing content, and offering encouragement. Like marketing needing 27 impressions before action, relationships need regular touchpoints to develop.

**Quick Take**: Focus on sustainable relationship-building activities you can maintain long-term, rather than exhausting one-time efforts that lead to burnout.

### Service-First Mindset
Approach networking with a "how can I help?" attitude rather than "what can I get?" When you provide value first—whether through feedback, sharing content, or offering encouragement—you create goodwill that naturally leads to reciprocal opportunities. The author got featured by major influencers because he consistently supported their work before asking for anything.

**Quick Take**: Give value before expecting returns. Your service-oriented approach makes people want to help you succeed.

### Quality Over Quantity
Choose connections based on shared values and approachability, not just follower counts. The author intentionally targets slightly lesser-known influencers who engage with their audience, leading to more meaningful interactions than chasing unreachable superstars. Focus on people who demonstrate authenticity and genuine care for their followers.

**Quick Take**: Build relationships with people who share your values and are accessible, rather than pursuing unreachable "big names" for prestige.

### Everyone Can Network
Your background doesn't determine your networking success. The author started with poor English skills, no business experience, and extreme introversion, yet built a global network. Your perceived disadvantages can actually become advantages when leveraged authentically. Whether you're more qualified or less qualified than the author was, you have everything needed to succeed.

**Quick Take**: Stop comparing yourself to others and start where you are. Your unique story and current abilities are sufficient to begin building meaningful connections.

## Action Items

- **Start small**: Choose one person in your field you admire and begin following their work consistently
- **Provide value**: Comment thoughtfully on their content, share their work, or offer genuine feedback without expecting anything in return
- **Be authentic**: Share your real journey, including struggles and limitations, rather than presenting a perfect facade
- **Stay consistent**: Dedicate small, regular time blocks to relationship-building activities you can maintain long-term
- **Focus on service**: Approach every interaction asking "How can I help?" rather than "What can I get?"
- **Choose wisely**: Target people who demonstrate values you admire and engage genuinely with their audience

## Questions to Consider

- What unique value can you offer others, even with your current skills and experience?
- How might your perceived disadvantages (introversion, limited experience, small network) actually become advantages?
- Who are the slightly-known influencers in your field who would be most approachable and aligned with your values?
- What consistent relationship-building activities can you realistically maintain alongside your current commitments?
- How can you reframe networking from "getting something" to "building friendships"?
- What fears about vulnerability might be holding you back from deeper connections?

## Conclusion

"Making Business Connections That Count" is worth your time if you've ever felt intimidated by traditional networking advice or believed you needed to be someone else to succeed. The author's journey from extreme introvert with zero contacts to successful entrepreneur demonstrates that authentic relationship-building is accessible to everyone, regardless of starting point.

The biggest reason to read this book is its rejection of networking gimmicks in favor of genuine human connection. In a world of fake personas and manipulative tactics, the author's approach of authenticity, vulnerability, and consistent service is both refreshing and effective. His personal story proves that you don't need to transform your personality—you just need to be yourself consistently.

What sets this book apart is its practical, no-nonsense approach grounded in real experience. The author didn't read about networking in theory; he lived it, building his business from nothing through relationships. His methods work because they're based on universal human desires for authentic connection rather than situational tactics that may become outdated.

If you're tired of networking advice that feels inauthentic or if you believe your personality type prevents you from building professional relationships, this book offers both hope and a practical path forward. The principles are simple enough to implement immediately but profound enough to transform your business and career over time.
