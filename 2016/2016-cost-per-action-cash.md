# Cost Per Action Cash
**Authors**: Anthony Ekanem | **Year**: 2016 | **Category**: Business & Finance

## Summary
Cost Per Action Cash is a practical guide to making money through CPA (Cost Per Action) marketing, where you earn commissions by driving users to complete specific actions like email submissions, zip code entries, or form fills. The book provides a comprehensive roadmap from getting accepted into CPA networks to implementing various promotional strategies.

This guide is ideal for beginners looking to enter affiliate marketing, online entrepreneurs seeking additional revenue streams, and digital marketers wanting to diversify their income sources. Readers will gain actionable strategies for selecting profitable offers, creating targeted websites, and driving quality traffic through multiple channels.

## Core Insights

### Understanding CPA Marketing Fundamentals
CPA marketing operates on a simple model: you get paid when someone completes a specific action through your referral link. Unlike traditional affiliate marketing that requires sales, CPA offers compensation for actions as simple as entering an email address or zip code. The system works through two categories - Advertisers (companies paying for promotion) and Publishers (affiliates promoting the offers).

> "It's really amazing that something as simple as entering your email address into a form with one field can result in being paid up to $1.50 in most cases."

**Quick Take**: Focus on email and zip submit offers initially as they have the lowest barrier to conversion, though they require high volume for substantial income.

### Getting Accepted into CPA Networks
Most reputable CPA networks require a professional application process including a phone interview. To increase acceptance rates, create a professional blog with quality content, use a domain-based email address (not free email), and understand your promotional methods beforehand. Select "Banners," "Text Links," and "PPC" as your initial promotional methods during application.

> "Most networks like to work with seasoned veterans within the marketing industry, so it's going to be important that you do not appear to be a newbie when it comes to marketing."

**Quick Take**: Invest time in creating a professional website with at least 10 quality articles before applying to networks - this demonstrates your seriousness and professionalism.

### Selecting Profitable CPA Offers
The book categorizes offers into five main types: Email/Zip Submits ($1.20-$1.50), Financial Offers ($15-$42), Weight Loss Offers ($25-$35), Skin-Care & Anti-Aging ($25-$35), and Insurance Related Offers ($9-$30). Email and zip submits convert highest (40-50% for zip, 30-35% for email) but pay the least, while financial and weight loss offers pay more but convert lower (1-10%).

> "Unlike zip submit offers, there is a bit more value in email submits, as even if the person does not go on to continue the offer, at least their email address was captured."

**Quick Take**: Test multiple offers within each category and focus on those with free trials, as they significantly increase conversion rates compared to full-payment offers.

### Building Niche Targeted Websites
The most profitable approach is creating dedicated websites for specific CPA offer categories. Research buyer-oriented keywords using tools like Google Keyword Planner, register keyword-rich domain names, and create content targeting long-tail keywords with commercial intent. Each site should focus on one specific niche rather than promoting multiple unrelated offers.

> "Your goal is simply to research CPA products, find buyer keywords that relate to your product, register keyword rich domain name, and post articles on your blog that are based off your keyword research."

**Quick Take**: Focus on "buyer keywords" like "buy [product name]" or "[product name] review" rather than informational keywords to attract users ready to take action.

### Traffic Generation Strategies
The book outlines multiple traffic generation methods including article marketing (submitting to EzineArticles.com and other directories), review sites that compare 3-5 products with your CPA offer ranked highest, PPC campaigns on Google AdWords, Microsoft AdCenter, and Yahoo, and creative Craigslist marketing through job and real estate categories.

> "When promoting CPA offers on Craigslist in this fashion. You can perhaps average 10 job applications a day per city if you post your ad at the right time."

**Quick Take**: Use masked redirect pages for direct CPA offer links to prevent commission theft and maintain professional appearance.

## Action Items

1. **Join Multiple CPA Networks**: Apply to at least 10 networks from the recommended list, starting with starred networks like Azoogle, ClickBooth, Hydra Network, Incentaclick, MarketLeverage, MaxBounty, NeverBlue, OfferWeb, and XY7

2. **Create Professional Foundation Websites**: Set up WordPress blogs with at least 10 quality PLR articles, privacy policies, and contact forms before applying to networks

3. **Research Buyer Keywords**: Use Google Keyword Planner and Free Word Tracker to find commercial-intent keywords for your chosen CPA offers

4. **Build Niche-Specific Sites**: Create dedicated websites for each CPA offer category rather than general multi-offer sites

5. **Test Multiple Offers**: Always test 3-5 offers within each category to find the highest-converting option before scaling

6. **Implement Traffic Diversification**: Use at least 3 different traffic generation methods to ensure stable income streams

## Conclusion
Cost Per Action Cash delivers exactly what it promises - a practical, actionable guide to making money through CPA marketing. While the book shows its age with some outdated platform references, the fundamental strategies remain sound. The strength lies in its step-by-step approach from network approval to traffic generation, making it accessible for complete beginners.

The biggest reason to read this book is its focus on sustainable, ethical CPA practices rather than get-rich-quick schemes. However, readers should expect significant upfront work in creating websites and testing offers before seeing substantial returns. For those willing to put in the effort, this provides a solid foundation for building multiple income streams through CPA marketing.

Is it worth your time? Absolutely if you're new to affiliate marketing and want a comprehensive starting point. Just be prepared to adapt some platform-specific advice to current digital marketing landscapes.
