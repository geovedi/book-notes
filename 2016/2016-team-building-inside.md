# Team Building Inside: Teamwork & Coordination
**Authors**: Cristina & Olivier Rebière | **Year**: 2016 | **Category**: Business & Finance

## Summary
This practical guide offers a complete framework for implementing effective team building activities that create and sustain team spirit. Drawing from over a decade of experience organizing hundreds of team building events, the authors provide ready-to-use activities that develop essential teamwork skills while keeping participants engaged and entertained. The book focuses on turning workplace groups into cohesive teams through structured fun and strategic coordination exercises.

Team leaders, HR professionals, facilitators, and managers will benefit from this guide's step-by-step approach to creating memorable team experiences. Readers will learn how to orchestrate activities that reveal hidden talents, improve communication, and build lasting bonds between team members. The book transforms complex team dynamics into manageable, enjoyable exercises that deliver measurable results in workplace collaboration and productivity.

## Core Insights

### The Five Roles of a Team Building Facilitator
Successful team building requires mastering five distinct roles simultaneously. As an **Animator**, you must energize reluctant participants and create enthusiasm. As an **Arbitrator**, you maintain fairness and objective evaluation. As a **Mediator**, you resolve conflicts with diplomacy and tact. As a **Trainer**, you share relevant knowledge effectively. As a **Director**, you improvise when plans go awry while staying connected to participants. Mastering these roles ensures smooth execution and positive outcomes for any team building activity.

> "Team Building deals primarily with interpersonal relationships at work, where you have to meet constraints and objectives."

**Quick Take**: The most effective facilitators anticipate conflicts before they arise and create environments where natural collaboration emerges through structured fun.

### The Pictoskills Framework: Four Stages of Team Development
The authors present a skill development framework based on Bruce Tuckman's model, using visual icons to track team progress. **Forming** focuses on creating team spirit, conceptualization, organization, communication, and trust. **Storming** develops empathy, curiosity, time management, and facilitation skills. **Norming** emphasizes creativity, coordination, sharing, strategy, budgeting, and resource management. **Performing** achieves mastery through project management, tactics, motivation, expedition behavior, and resilience.

> "Unity is strength... And if you take a closer look, Team is everywhere: at school, in your sports club, at work, and even your own Family."

**Quick Take**: Progress through stages systematically—teams must master foundational skills before advancing to higher levels of performance.

### Activity 1: Funny Band - Creative Collaboration Revolution
Funny band transforms teams into musical groups that create original songs, choreography, and instruments from everyday objects. This 60-minute activity develops team spirit through shared creativity, organization through task distribution, coordination through talent discovery, and creativity through artistic expression. Teams perform their creations for everyone, building confidence and revealing hidden abilities. The activity works equally well indoors or outdoors and requires only paper and pens.

> "These 'workshops' are more fun because they involve the active participation of all members of a small team to achieve a specific objective within a specified time frame."

**Quick Take**: Musical creation breaks down workplace hierarchies and allows natural leaders and creative talents to emerge regardless of job titles.

### Activity 2: Funny Transport - Precision Coordination Challenge
Teams transport a glass of water using only a cloth held by each member with one hand. This deceptively simple activity demands perfect synchronization, clear communication, and strategic planning. The winning team balances speed with precision, learning that rushing without coordination leads to failure. The exercise develops organizational skills, strategic thinking, and team spirit through shared physical challenge. It requires minimal equipment and works in various environments.

> "In this activity what is really important is the perfect synchronization of the team members and not time pressure. The teams that favor time over coordination are going to spill water from their glass!"

**Quick Take**: Team success comes from coordinated movement, not individual speed—teaching valuable lessons about workplace collaboration and project execution.

### Activity 3: Special Ski - Unified Movement Mastery
Teams walk together on custom "skis" (wooden bars with ropes) across a set distance, requiring perfect timing and verbal synchronization. This activity develops coordination through physical unity, strategy through planning movements, organization through role assignment, and team spirit through shared challenge. Teams learn that vocal communication and rhythmic coordination outperform frantic, uncoordinated efforts. The activity works best outdoors on flat ground and adapts to various team sizes.

> "The teams which favor a vocal synchronization of the alternative movements of their skis are going to achieve the best time."

**Quick Take**: Success depends on finding a rhythm together, not individual strength—teaching teams that coordinated communication beats raw effort.

### Activity 4: Blazon - Visual Identity Creation
Teams design and create flags representing company values, customer attitudes, or team messages using basic art supplies. This creative activity develops team spirit through shared purpose, creativity through visual expression, organization through task distribution, and communication through visual storytelling. The resulting blazons become lasting symbols of team identity and can be displayed at work to reinforce team bonds.

> "Make sure that the teams can take their blazons back with them. This will please them and will create strong and concrete memories. Some of them will hang their blazon on a wall at the office."

**Quick Take**: Visual representation of shared values creates lasting team identity and reinforces organizational culture through creative collaboration.

## Action Items
- Master the five facilitator roles before leading your first team building activity
- Assess your team's current developmental stage and select appropriate pictoskill-building activities
- Start with Funny Band for creative teams needing to break down communication barriers
- Use Funny Transport for teams struggling with coordination and timing issues
- Implement Special Ski for groups that need practice in strategic planning and execution
- Create Blazon activities when teams need to strengthen shared values and identity
- Always debrief activities to extract workplace applications and learning points
- Take photos and provide tangible mementos to reinforce positive memories
