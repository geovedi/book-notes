# How to Source Products on Amazon FBA
**Authors**: Unknown | **Year**: 2016 | **Category**: Business & Finance

## Summary
This practical guide serves as a comprehensive roadmap for entrepreneurs looking to build a private label business through Amazon's Fulfillment by Amazon (FBA) program. The book breaks down the complex process of product sourcing, from finding local suppliers to navigating international manufacturing relationships, particularly with Chinese suppliers through platforms like Alibaba. With conservative estimates suggesting potential monthly profits of $1,000-$3,000 per product after the initial 3-4 month setup period, this guide aims to demystify the sourcing process and help readers avoid common pitfalls.

This book is perfect for aspiring entrepreneurs who want to escape the 9-to-5 grind and build an online business with relatively passive income potential. Readers will learn how to leverage Amazon's massive infrastructure, source products effectively, understand cultural differences in international business, and build sustainable supplier relationships that can scale into a profitable enterprise.

## Core Insights

### Understanding Amazon FBA: Freedom by Amazon
Amazon FBA revolutionizes e-commerce by handling the most time-consuming aspects of running an online business. When you use FBA, Amazon stores your products in their warehouses, handles packaging and shipping, manages customer service, and deals with returns. This service transforms what could be a full-time job into a manageable business that can be operated from anywhere with an internet connection. The two main costs are the $40 monthly professional seller fee (waived at 40+ units sold) and fulfillment fees based on product size and weight. Despite these costs, the economies of scale Amazon operates on make their services remarkably affordable, allowing sellers to focus on what truly matters: finding and sourcing profitable products.

> "With Amazon's Fulfillment by Amazon (FBA) option (which we like to call Freedom by Amazon) you're allowed to use the resources that Amazon has at their disposal: list a product on their marketplace website, store your products in their warehouse. Promote your products on their webstore through their marketing efforts, ship out your products and package it cheaply to customers. And most of all, they'll deal with angry customers for you."

**Quick Take**: The real money isn't made from putting labels on boxes—it's made from finding, sourcing, and selling the right products. Let Amazon handle the logistics while you focus on product selection and business growth.

### Local Sourcing Strategies: Start Where You Are
Before diving into international manufacturing, the book suggests several local sourcing methods that can help you get started with minimal capital investment. Thrift stores, Walmart, and local China Town districts offer opportunities to find undervalued products that can be resold at a profit on Amazon. The #1 tool for successful local sourcing is your smartphone—use it to instantly research market values on eBay, Craigslist, and Amazon while shopping. This real-time price verification prevents bad purchasing decisions and helps identify profitable opportunities quickly. Technology gives savvy resellers a significant advantage over traditional thrift store shoppers who rely on intuition alone.

> "If there's one tool I can't live without when it comes to reselling items from TS, it's gonna be my Iphone. It helps me to make better and faster decisions. I don't have to think twice if I'm gonna buy a certain item. If I know the market value of that price, I'll buy that item in a heartbeat knowing that I'm gonna make a healthy profit from it."

**Quick Take**: Your smartphone is your most valuable sourcing tool—use it to verify market prices instantly and avoid leaving money on the table through uninformed purchasing decisions.

### Mastering Chinese Business Culture: The Power of Guanxi
Understanding Chinese business culture is crucial for successful international sourcing. The concept of "Guanxi" (relationships/connections) forms the foundation of Chinese business dealings. Unlike Western business culture that prioritizes getting straight to business, Chinese suppliers prefer building personal relationships first. This means sharing meals, discussing family, and learning about each other before diving into business negotiations. Building strong Guanxi leads to better pricing, faster response times, and suppliers who will protect your interests. Chinese culture also values harmony and stability, meaning suppliers may not mention problems unless explicitly asked. Never assume anything—be extremely specific about your requirements, packaging, branding, and quality expectations in writing.

> "Building Guanxi will not only help you have a better trust, it will get you better deals when it comes to negotiations, as well as faster responses to your enquiries. If you are able to build a strong Guanxi with your supplier, they will never try to rip you off and they will always make sure that you get the best they can provide."

**Quick Take**: Invest time in building personal relationships with Chinese suppliers through regular communication and cultural understanding—this investment pays dividends in better pricing, quality, and service.

### Alibaba vs. AliExpress: Choosing the Right Platform
The book clarifies the key differences between two major Chinese sourcing platforms. Alibaba connects you directly with manufacturers willing to do private label production, but requires negotiation and has no built-in payment system. It's ideal for serious businesses looking to scale with custom-branded products. AliExpress operates more like Amazon with buy-it-now pricing and built-in payment processing, but offers limited private label opportunities and slower supplier responses. Most successful sellers start with Alibaba for private label products, using AliExpress only for small test orders or when sourcing unbranded products. The key is understanding that Alibaba suppliers expect serious business inquiries and may not respond to small quantity requests.

> "Alibaba has no built-in payment options. You must negotiate with the suppliers. However, everyone you find on the platform is willing to private-label for you, no question about that. If your inquiry is about 10 – 50 products, probably they don't even answer, because they want serious businesses to get in contact."

**Quick Take**: Use Alibaba for private label manufacturing relationships and scaling your business, reserve AliExpress for small test orders or unbranded product sourcing.

### Communication Mastery: The Written Word Rules
When working with Chinese suppliers, written communication through email is superior to verbal conversations. Use bullet points and numbered lists to clearly specify every detail of your requirements, from product specifications to packaging and branding. Chinese suppliers may copy your designs or logos if you don't explicitly forbid it in writing—cultural differences mean they don't view this as unethical unless specifically prohibited. Always put your logo ownership rights, exclusive distribution agreements, and quality standards in writing. Monitor platforms like Alibaba, eBay, and Amazon to ensure your products aren't being copied, and report any violations immediately to your factory contact.

> "It is always your responsibility to communicate and be very clear on your vision from the beginning. You must always keep your suppliers in touch by being proactive. The main reason is to avoid delivery of faulty products. Your best bet is to put it in writing, and that's also why it is always better to write e-mails, using bullet points and numbers, rather than just mention it."

**Quick Take**: Document everything in writing using clear, numbered lists—assume nothing and specify every detail to prevent miscommunication and protect your brand.

## Action Items

- **Start with Amazon Seller Professional account** - The $40/month fee becomes cost-effective at 40+ units sold and unlocks essential features for advertising and API access

- **Download Amazon seller app** - Use your smartphone to research products and verify market values in real-time while sourcing locally

- **Build supplier relationships** - Invest time in personal communication and cultural understanding with Chinese suppliers to secure better pricing and service

- **Document everything in writing** - Use bullet points and numbered lists in all supplier communications to prevent miscommunication

- **Start with one product** - Focus your initial efforts on finding and launching one successful product before expanding your catalog

- **Monitor for copycats** - Regularly check Alibaba, eBay, and Amazon for unauthorized copies of your products

- **Plan for 3-4 month timeline** - Expect the initial product setup to take several months from research to launch
