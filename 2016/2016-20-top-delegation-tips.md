# 20 Top Delegation Tips
**Authors**: David Salmon | **Year**: 2016

## Summary
This practical guide presents delegation as an essential management skill that enables leaders to multiply their effectiveness while developing their team members. The book frames delegation not as task offloading, but as a strategic approach to empowerment, trust-building, and organizational growth. Through structured frameworks and actionable advice, it transforms delegation from a source of anxiety into a pathway for leadership excellence.

The book is ideal for new managers struggling with letting go of tasks, experienced leaders looking to refine their delegation skills, and business owners seeking to scale their operations. Readers will gain confidence in assigning responsibilities, learn to identify the right tasks and people for delegation, and discover how to create a culture of shared ownership and accountability.

## Core Insights

### The SMART Framework for Effective Delegation
The book introduces the SMART/SMARTER acronym as the foundation for successful delegation: tasks must be **Specific, Measurable, Agreed upon, Realistic, Time-bound, Ethical, and Recorded**. This framework ensures clarity and mutual understanding between delegator and delegatee. When incorporated consistently, these elements create a foundation of trust and accountability that eliminates common delegation pitfalls.

> "Go slow with it so you can go fast – this applies to situations wherein you delegate a new task to someone over the phone, via email or at hurried conversation. If that is the way that you delegate, you may as well expect that results will not go well."

**Quick Take**: Rushed delegation leads to failed outcomes. Invest time upfront in clear communication to prevent misunderstandings and rework later.

### The Seven Levels of Delegation Mastery
The book presents a progressive framework showing how delegation evolves from complete dependence to autonomous operation. Each level represents increasing trust and capability:

1. **"What do I need to do?"** – Complete dependence, no employee initiative
2. **"I think..."** – Employee begins contributing ideas
3. **"I recommend..."** – Employee analyzes and proposes solutions
4. **"I would like..."** – Shared responsibility with authority awareness
5. **"I intend..."** – Clear action plans with final leader approval
6. **"I have done..."** – Initiative taken with post-action reporting
7. **"I've been doing..."** – Full autonomous operation with regular updates

> "Delegation is much like educating – delegation is said to be a two-way process. It means that you learn how to delegate at the same time you are teaching your people how to do as well."

**Quick Take**: Effective delegation grows both the delegator's ability to trust and the delegatee's capacity to deliver results independently.

### The Six-Step Delegation Process
The book outlines a systematic approach to delegation that ensures success while minimizing risks:

1. **Define Assigned Responsibility** – Clearly articulate expected outcomes verbally and in writing
2. **Establish Parameters** – Set specific timelines, budgets, and team boundaries
3. **Meet the Team** – Review assignments, encourage input, and remain flexible on adjustments
4. **Let Them Do the Task** – Resist the urge to micromanage; focus on supervision and monitoring
5. **Check Up** – Provide supportive oversight without interfering with autonomy
6. **Evaluate** – Conduct positive growth experiences and express appreciation for completed work

> "As a manager, you need not to do the task for your employees. You need to let them do it by themselves to measure how they really deserve to get the job."

**Quick Take**: Delegation fails when managers complete tasks themselves rather than developing their team's capabilities.

### Overcoming Delegation Barriers
The book identifies common mindset blocks that prevent effective delegation and provides strategies to overcome them:

- **Perfectionism**: The belief that only you can do it right leads to bottlenecks and team stagnation
- **Fear of failure**: Concern about others' mistakes prevents skill development and team growth
- **Lack of trust**: Starting with small, manageable tasks builds confidence for larger responsibilities
- **Ego attachment**: Effective delegation focuses on organizational success rather than personal indispensability

> "Delegations swings in two ways – when it comes to delegation, you have to be aware there are those who never delegates because they do best at what they do. This means they are indispensable and so delegation does not mean a thing to them. On the other hand, there are also those who delegates but keep on hovering over the task being done."

**Quick Take**: Both extremes—complete control and micromanagement—indicate ego-driven leadership rather than strategic delegation.

## Action Items

- **Start small**: Begin delegating low-risk tasks to build confidence for both you and your team members
- **Document everything**: Use the SMART framework to create clear, written delegation agreements
- **Schedule regular check-ins**: Establish monitoring systems that support without micromanaging
- **Celebrate successes**: Recognize and reward successful delegation outcomes to reinforce positive behaviors
- **Develop delegation habits**: Create systems for identifying opportunities to delegate and matching tasks with team capabilities

## Questions to Consider

- Which tasks are you currently holding that could be delegated to develop team members?
- How can you use the seven levels framework to assess your current delegation approach?
- What fears or beliefs might be preventing you from delegating more effectively?
- How would your organization change if you successfully implemented the six-step delegation process?
- Which team members are ready for the next level of delegation responsibility?

## Conclusion
This book is worth your time if you're a manager who feels overwhelmed by workload, struggles with letting go of control, or wants to develop stronger team capabilities. The biggest reason to read it is its practical, step-by-step approach that transforms delegation from abstract theory into actionable daily practice. While the concepts aren't revolutionary, the book's strength lies in its systematic framework and recognition that delegation is a skill that develops through practice rather than innate talent. The combination of the SMART criteria, seven-level progression model, and six-step process provides a comprehensive toolkit that managers can immediately apply to reduce their workload while building a more capable, autonomous team.
