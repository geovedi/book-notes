# Hiding from the Internet
**Original Title**: Hiding from the Internet · Eliminating Personal Online Information
**Author**: Michael Bazzell | **Year**: 2016

## Summary
This comprehensive guide provides a step-by-step workbook for anyone looking to reclaim their digital privacy and remove personal information from the internet. Written by a former FBI cyber crime investigator, it translates advanced privacy techniques into actionable strategies for everyday use.

The book presents a layered approach to digital privacy, from basic technical hygiene to advanced anonymity methods, designed for everyone from identity theft victims to privacy enthusiasts. It emphasizes that privacy is an active process requiring patience, initiative, and continuous vigilance—anyone can significantly reduce their online footprint with the right methods and persistence.

## Core Protection Framework

### **Level 1: Assessment Phase**
Before you can protect yourself, you need to know what information about you is publicly accessible. Start by conducting a thorough inventory of your digital footprint using advanced search techniques and specialized tools.

**Step-by-Step Discovery**:
1. **Search Engine Assessment**: Use quotation marks for exact name searches across multiple engines (Google, Bing, Yandex, DuckDuckGo)
2. **Username Investigation**: Use KnowEm.com to find all your usernames across 500+ social networks
3. **Location Analysis**: Check Instagram geo-tags and location-based services
4. **Credit Report Review**: Obtain free reports from AnnualCreditReport.com from all three bureaus

> "The process of removing your personal information from the internet is not quick. It may take quite some time and may never be 100% complete."

**Quick Take**: Document everything you find in a spreadsheet before beginning removal efforts.

### **Level 2: Technical Foundation**
Build your privacy protection through proper technical hygiene and security tools. This forms the foundation for all advanced privacy techniques.

**Basic Protection (Essential)**:
- **Antivirus**: Regular system updates and reputable antivirus software
- **Malware Removal**: Malware Bytes and Spybot for system cleaning
- **Browser Security**: Firefox with privacy extensions (Disconnect, AdBlock Plus)
- **System Maintenance**: CCleaner for regular system cleanup

**Intermediate Protection (Recommended)**:
- **Script Control**: NoScript extension to block JavaScript exploits
- **Search Privacy**: DuckDuckGo or StartPage instead of Google
- **VPN Service**: Private Internet Access ($39.95/year for 5 devices)
- **Encrypted Communication**: Signal for messaging, anonymous email services

**Advanced Protection (For High-Risk Individuals)**:
- **Tor Browser**: Anonymous browsing through onion routing
- **Tails OS**: Live operating system that leaves no trace
- **Virtual Machines**: Sandboxed environments for sensitive activities

> "Privacy is no longer a passive process. A lack of participation in the digital world does not make you private."

**Quick Take**: Start with basic protection and gradually add layers as your comfort level increases.

### **Level 3: Financial Privacy**
Your financial information is among the most valuable data available online. Implement these strategies to protect your economic privacy and prevent identity theft.

**Credit Protection Strategies**:
1. **Opt-Out Pre-Screening**: Visit OptOutPrescreen.com to remove yourself from pre-approved credit offers
2. **Fraud Alerts**: Place 90-day renewable alerts at all three credit bureaus
3. **Credit Freeze**: The most effective protection—locks your credit reports completely
4. **Anonymous Payments**: Use prepaid cards and masked credit cards for online purchases

**Documentation Process**:
- Keep copies of all opt-out requests
- Maintain a log of freeze/unfreeze dates
- Monitor credit reports quarterly

> "Bring three things: Initiative, Patience, and Vigilance."

**Quick Take**: A credit freeze is your single most effective tool against financial identity theft.

### **Level 4: Data Broker Elimination**
Remove your information from the hundreds of companies that collect and sell personal data. This requires persistent effort but dramatically reduces your online visibility.

**People Search Engine Removal**:
- **WhitePages, Spokeo, BeenVerified**: Follow their specific opt-out procedures
- **Document Requests**: Keep records of all removal attempts
- **Follow-Up**: Recheck quarterly for reposted information

**Data Broker Strategies**:
- **Public Brokers**: Use DMA Choice and Catalog Choice for marketing lists
- **Non-Public Brokers**: Request files from LexisNexis, Westlaw, and Acxiom
- **Error Correction**: Challenge inaccurate information in your files

**Professional Services**:
- Consider paid removal services for comprehensive coverage
- Balance cost versus time investment
- Focus on the most impactful brokers first

> "Do not become discouraged if you find that you experience a setback."

**Quick Take**: Data broker removal is an ongoing process—schedule quarterly reviews.

### **Level 5: Social Media Cleanup**
Your social media presence often contains the most detailed personal information available online. Systematically clean or delete accounts to protect your privacy.

**Platform-Specific Strategies**:
1. **Facebook**: Understand the difference between deactivation and permanent deletion
2. **Graph Search**: Use advanced search to find hidden content and tagged photos
3. **Twitter/Instagram**: Remove location data and third-party app connections
4. **LinkedIn**: Consider maintaining a professional but minimal presence

**Content Removal Protocol**:
- **Photo Audit**: Remove or privatize all images showing location or personal details
- **Post History**: Delete old posts revealing personal information
- **Tag Management**: Remove tags from others' posts when possible
- **Account Decision**: Choose between complete deletion versus minimal, professional presence

**Alternative Approach**:
- Create professional aliases for business networking
- Maintain separate personal and professional identities
- Use privacy-preserving communication methods

> "Always assume that anything posted to the internet is public. Always choose strong privacy settings, but do not rely on them."

**Quick Take**: It's often better to delete problematic content rather than try to make it private.

## Conclusion

This book is essential reading for anyone serious about reclaiming their digital privacy in an age of pervasive data collection. Michael Bazzell's law enforcement background gives the strategies credibility that's often missing from privacy guides.

The book's greatest strength is its practical, step-by-step approach that makes complex privacy techniques accessible to everyone. Whether you're a victim of identity theft, a public figure seeking discretion, or simply someone who values privacy, this workbook provides the tools and methodology to dramatically reduce your online footprint.
