# Homemade Repellents: The Ultimate Guide to Homemade Repellents and Natural After-Bites Remedies
**Authors**: Andrew Forrest | **Year**: 2016 | **Category**: Science & Nature

## Summary
This practical guide provides safe, organic alternatives to chemical pesticides for managing common household and garden pests. The book offers proven recipes and strategies for creating natural repellents using readily available ingredients like essential oils, herbs, and common household items. It addresses both indoor pests (wasps, ants, roaches, mosquitoes) and outdoor threats, while also providing remedies for insect bites and prevention strategies.

Perfect for health-conscious families, environmentally aware readers, and anyone seeking affordable, non-toxic pest control solutions. You'll gain practical skills to protect your home and family without exposing them to harmful chemicals like DEET.

## Core Insights

### Essential Oil-Based Repellent Framework
Essential oils are the cornerstone of natural pest control, offering potent, non-toxic protection against various insects. The book reveals specific oil combinations that target different pests while leaving your home smelling pleasant.

> "As they do not contain any toxic chemicals they are very safe for use within your home – will not have any side-effects on children and pets if used as per recommendation"

**Quick Take**: Create a master repellent spray using ¼ cup water, ¼ cup vodka, 15 drops tea tree oil, 15 drops peppermint oil, and 7 drops citrus oil for broad-spectrum protection.

### The Cardboard Trap Method for Termites
This ingenious, non-toxic approach exploits termites' natural attraction to cellulose. Dampened cardboard acts as an irresistible lure, concentrating termites in one location for easy removal.

> "Cardboard traps – this is the most non-toxic and easiest way to eliminate termites from your home. The cellulose in the cardboard will attract the termites and they will swarm around the trap."

**Quick Take**: Place damp cardboard in termite-prone areas, monitor for activity, then safely dispose of trapped termites by burning the cardboard.

### Vinegar-Based Solutions for Blood-Feeding Pests
White vinegar emerges as a versatile base for repellents against ticks and mosquitoes, especially when combined with essential oils to mask its strong odor.

> "Ticks abhor vinegar. To keep your pets safe from ticks, make this easy-to-prepare spray. In a spray bottle, add 2 cups of distilled white vinegar, 1 cups of water, and 2 tbsp. of almond or vegetable oil."

**Quick Take**: Mix equal parts water and distilled white vinegar with essential oils for an effective, affordable spray for both humans and pets.

### Prevention-First Approach
The book emphasizes that maintaining a clean, dry, sealed home is more effective than reactive pest control, highlighting how most pest problems stem from environmental factors.

> "In the area of pest control nothing can be farther from the truth than this statement [we'll cross the bridge when we come to it]."

**Quick Take**: Seal entry points, eliminate moisture sources, store food in airtight containers, and maintain regular cleaning to prevent 80% of pest problems before they start.

### Garden-Specific Natural Controls
Outdoor pests require different strategies, including using beneficial insects, physical barriers, and plant-based solutions that protect without harming your garden ecosystem.

> "Natural predators such as lacewing and lady beetles eat up larvae and so you could use them."

**Quick Take**: Introduce beneficial insects, use floating row covers, and apply targeted organic treatments like garlic spray or kaolin clay for garden pest management.

## Quick Action Items

- **Immediate**: Create peppermint oil spray using equal parts water and peppermint oil for wasp and bee protection
- **This Week**: Set up cardboard traps in areas showing termite activity and monitor for results
- **Seasonal**: Plant mint around your home's perimeter to create natural wasp barriers
- **Monthly**: Clean hard-to-reach areas and seal new cracks or crevices in walls and foundations
- **As Needed**: Apply honey or aloe vera gel to insect bites for immediate relief
