# Who: The A Method for Hiring
**Authors**: Geoff Smart, Randy Street | **Year**: 2008
**Category**: Business & Finance

## Summary
The most important decisions businesspeople make aren't about strategy, products, or processes—they're about people. This book reveals a systematic approach to hiring A Players that achieves 90% success rates, transforming hiring from a 50/50 gamble into a predictable advantage. The authors spent 13 years studying over 300 CEOs and interviewing 20+ billionaires to develop a method that helps companies stop losing money to hiring mistakes (which cost 15x an employee's salary) and start building teams that drive extraordinary results.

This book is essential for CEOs, managers, entrepreneurs, and anyone involved in hiring decisions who wants to stop the costly cycle of bad hires and build teams that consistently outperform the competition.

## Core Insights

### The Four Steps of the A Method
The A Method consists of four systematic steps that transform hiring from guesswork into a predictable process:

**Scorecard**: Before you hire, create a blueprint defining exactly what success looks like. This isn't a job description listing activities—it's a clear set of outcomes (3-8 specific results) and competencies (how the person must operate) that define A performance for the role. For example, instead of "make sales calls," define "grow revenue from $25M to $50M by end of year three."

**Source**: Build a pipeline of A Players before you need them. Don't wait until you have an opening to start looking. Systematically source from multiple channels including employee referrals (make sourcing an outcome on every team member's scorecard), personal networks, board connections, and specialized recruiters who understand your culture.

**Select**: Use four structured interviews to gather facts, not opinions:
1. **Screening Interview** (30 minutes): Filter out B/C players quickly
2. **Who Interview** (3 hours): Uncover career patterns and past performance
3. **Focused Interviews** (1-2 hours each): Deep dive into specific outcomes and competencies
4. **Reference Interviews** (30 minutes each): Verify claims with former managers

**Sell**: Secure your chosen A Players by understanding their motivations. Focus on the "5 F's": Fit (show how they'll succeed), Family (address personal concerns), Freedom (autonomy they'll have), Fortune (compensation tied to scorecard performance), and Fun (meaningful work they'll do).

### The Real Cost of Hiring Mistakes
The average hiring mistake costs 15 times an employee's base salary in hard costs and lost productivity. A $100,000 hire gone wrong can cost $1.5 million. Yet most managers have only a 50% hiring success rate. This means most organizations are pouring millions down the drain annually on preventable mistakes.

> "The most important decisions that businesspeople make are not what decisions, but who decisions."
> — Jim Collins, author of Good to Great

### Stop Hiring Generalists, Start Hiring Specialists
One of the biggest hiring traps is pursuing "all-around athletes" who seem impressive but lack deep expertise in what you specifically need. As Nick Chabraja, CEO of General Dynamics, learned: "I made the mistake of putting in place a guy who went on to put more orders in the backlog. Operating margins actually went down." The solution? Hire specialists whose skills exactly match your specific business problem.

> "Each target and company has different needs for the CEO and any management role. I look at our team almost like a football team. If I am hiring for a position, I ask myself, what is this person going to be doing? Are they a quarterback? A center?"
> — Alec Gores, founder of the Gores Group

### Cultural Fit Is Non-Negotiable
One-third of hiring failures stem from cultural mismatch. The best salesperson in the world is wrong if they're openly disrespectful in a culture that values respect. Before hiring, define your culture by asking your leadership team: "What adjectives describe our culture?" Then include cultural competencies on every scorecard.

George Hamilton of the Institute for Sustainable Communities learned this the hard way: "He was an unbelievable pain to work with. Our culture is collaborative, not competitive... In the end, though, it just wasn't workable."

## Action Items

### Immediate Steps
- **Create scorecards for every role**: Define mission, outcomes (3-8 specific results), and competencies for your current team openings
- **Audit your current team**: Use scorecards to evaluate whether you have A Players in each role
- **Build your sourcing pipeline**: Start networking and identifying potential A Players before you need them
- **Train your team**: Teach managers the A Method interviews and hold them accountable for 90% hiring success rates

### System Implementation
- **Make sourcing an outcome**: Add "Source 5 A Players per year" to every manager's scorecard
- **Link compensation to scorecard performance**: Pay top compensation only when A performance is delivered
- **Remove barriers**: Work with HR to eliminate policies that prevent A Method implementation
- **Lead by example**: Use the A Method yourself before expecting others to follow

## Questions to Consider

- What would change in your organization if you achieved 90% hiring success instead of 50%?

- How much are your current hiring mistakes costing in real dollars and missed opportunities?
- What cultural competencies should every person in your organization possess?
- Are you hiring specialists who solve specific problems, or generalists who create new ones?
