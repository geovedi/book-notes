# Power Questions
**Original Title**: Power Questions. Inspiration for Solutions
**Author**: Kerstin Hack | **Year**: 2010 (2nd Edition)

## Summary
This concise microbook presents a powerful framework for problem-solving through the art of asking strategic questions. Rather than providing direct answers, it equips readers with questioning techniques that unlock their own solutions and break free from stuck patterns of thinking. The book is built around the principle that the right question at the right time can transform perspectives and create breakthrough insights.

Perfect for coaches, counselors, leaders, and anyone feeling stuck in personal or professional challenges, this guide delivers a practical 7-step questioning methodology. Readers will learn to let go of ineffective approaches, recognize exceptions to their problems, and discover hidden resources within themselves and their situations. The emphasis is on simplicity, emotional movement, and actionable insights.

## Core Insights

### Let Go to Find Solutions
The foundation of effective problem-solving is letting go of old ways of thinking and unsuccessful solution attempts. The book introduces Einstein's definition of insanity: doing the same thing repeatedly while expecting different results. Letting go isn't just about finding solutions—it's about freeing yourself from mental baggage and living a more relaxed, liberated life.

> "The definition of insanity is doing the same thing over and over and expecting different results."
> _Original: "The definition of insanity is doing the same thing over and over and expecting different results."_
**Quick Take**: When you feel stuck, the first step isn't trying harder—it's releasing your grip on current approaches to make space for new possibilities.

### The Art of Powerful Questions
Good questions are characterized by simplicity, emotional movement, and action-inspiring quality. They stem from genuine interest in others rather than intellectual superiority. The best questions invite both the asker and the answered on a journey of discovery, revealing insights neither could anticipate.

> "Every question possesses a power that does not lie in the answer."
> _Original: "Every question possesses a power that does not lie in the answer."_
**Quick Take**: Focus on asking questions that open doors rather than close them—questions whose answers surprise both you and the person being asked.

### Focus on What You Can Influence
Visualize your concerns as two concentric circles. The inner circle contains questions about your feelings, intentions, and actions—things you can influence. The outer circle contains unanswerable questions about external factors you cannot control. Wisdom lies in focusing your energy on the inner circle and refusing to waste time on the outer one.

> "I do not rack my brains over questions which I cannot answer."
> _Original: "I do not rack my brains over questions which I cannot answer."_
**Quick Take**: Strengthen your life-shaping ability by consciously choosing to engage only with questions you can actually answer and influence.

### The 7-Step Solution Framework
The book presents a systematic approach to problem-solving through strategic questioning:

1. **Recognize the Problem** - Name exactly what troubles you, who's affected, and rate your current situation on a scale of 1-10
2. **Discover Exceptions** - Identify when the problem is less intense and what makes those times different
3. **Define Your Goal** - Clearly envision what a "10" would look like and what would be different
4. **Discover Resources** - Identify your internal capabilities, supportive relationships, and available materials
5. **Get Different Perspectives** - Consult real or imagined advisors to gain fresh viewpoints
6. **Think Outside the Box** - Use paradoxical questions to break free from rigid thinking patterns
7. **Take Constructive Action** - Remember that even the longest journey begins with the first step

> "Only the weak are sent out on paths without perils."
> _Original: "Only the weak are sent out on paths without perils."_
**Quick Take**: Every problem you've successfully solved in the past provides a blueprint for handling current challenges.

## Action Items
- Practice the "miracle question": Imagine your problem is solved overnight—what would be the first clue that something changed?
- For the next 7 days, write down answers to one power question each morning while still "drunken with sleep"
- Use the "name acrostic" exercise: Write your name vertically and list one personal resource for each letter
- When stuck, ask yourself: "What would I advise myself if I were a counselor?"
- Practice the "exception finding" technique: Notice when problems are less intense and identify what makes those moments different

## Questions to Consider
- What problem have you been trying to solve the same way repeatedly without success?
- Which of your current concerns belong in the outer circle of things you cannot influence?
- What resources do you already possess that you haven't fully utilized?
- If you had to make the situation worse intentionally, what would you do—and what does this reveal about potential solutions?
- What would you miss if your problem suddenly disappeared?

## Conclusion
This microbook delivers maximum value in minimum space, offering a practical framework that transforms questioning into a problem-solving superpower. Its greatest strength lies in its simplicity and actionability—readers can immediately apply the questioning techniques to any stuck situation. The 7-step process is elegant enough for professional coaches yet accessible enough for personal use.

What makes this guide exceptional is its focus on empowering others (and yourself) to find your own answers rather than providing generic solutions. The miracle question technique alone is worth the entire read—it consistently produces breakthrough insights by shifting focus from problems to solutions. Whether you're helping others or solving your own challenges, these questioning tools will become an indispensable part of your problem-solving toolkit.

The biggest reason to read this? It teaches you that the quality of your questions determines the quality of your life's answers.
