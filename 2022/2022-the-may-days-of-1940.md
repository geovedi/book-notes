# The May Days of 1940
**Original Title**: De meidagen van 1940
**Authors**: Luuc Kooijmans | **Year**: 2022 | **Category**: History & Biography

## Summary
"The May Days of 1940" is a collective diary that captures the Dutch experience of the German invasion through hundreds of personal testimonies—diaries, letters, and memories from soldiers, housewives, doctors, journalists, students, pastors, farmers, and ministers. This powerful mosaic approach brings readers closer to the reality of war than any traditional historical narrative, presenting the events as they were experienced day by day, from May 1-31, 1940, without the benefit of hindsight.

This book is essential for anyone seeking to understand the human dimension of wartime invasion, perfect for history enthusiasts, students of World War II, and readers interested in personal narratives that reveal how ordinary people respond to extraordinary circumstances. You'll gain intimate access to the range of human emotions—from shock and fear to excitement, resignation, horror, and anger—that characterized the Dutch experience during those fateful days.

## Core Insights

### The Shock of Sudden Invasion
The German invasion on May 10, 1940, shattered the fragile peace that Dutch citizens had maintained despite growing tensions in Europe. Most people had managed to ignore concrete thoughts of war until the early morning when "the frightening sound of bombers, anti-aircraft fire, explosions, and machine gun fire" abruptly awakened the sleeping population. The initial disbelief gradually gave way to the terrifying realization that their country was under attack.

> "Suddenly there was war. In the early morning of May 10, 1940, the unsuspectingly sleeping population of the Netherlands was startled by the frightening sound of bombers, anti-aircraft fire, explosions and machine gun fire."
> _Original: "Plotseling was het oorlog. In de vroege ochtend van 10 mei 1940 werd de argeloos slapende bevolking van Nederland opgeschrikt door het angstaanjagende geluid van bommenwerpers, afweergeschut, explosies en mitrailleurvuur."_
**Quick Take**: Peace can be shattered instantly, and psychological preparation often lags behind geopolitical reality.

### The Reality of Combat for Ordinary Soldiers
The book vividly portrays how young, inexperienced Dutch soldiers—many just teenagers—were thrust into combat. Their testimonies reveal the stark contrast between routine military exercises and the chaos of actual warfare. One sergeant described the exhaustion and fear: "I was completely exhausted. I woke up the next morning in my underwear with one shoe still on, of which I had been unaware all night."

> "We could not understand it. The attackers were not very numerous, however, and some aimed salvos from our artillery made the entire foreground (for the time being) free of Germans."
> _Original: "Wij kunnen dat niet begrijpen. De aanvallers zijn echter niet zeer talrijk en enkele gerichte salvo's van onze artillerie maakt het hele voorterrein (voorlopig) vrij van moffen."_
**Quick Take**: Military training rarely prepares soldiers for the psychological trauma of actual combat.

### Civilian Experience Under Fire
The testimonies capture how civilians suddenly found themselves trapped between opposing forces. In Valkenburg, villagers were caught in the crossfire as they tried to evacuate: "Thus went the sad procession, I estimate 80 to 100 people. It gave a most sorrowful sight. Even though I was involved with my entire family of six people, I still had an eye to oversee what kind of journey this actually was."

> "The houses were deserted. The people evacuated. Some dogs and chickens, not knowing where to look, wandered around."
> _Original: "Verlaten lagen zij daar, de huizen. De mensen geëvacueerd. Enkele honden en kippen, niet wetend waar zij het zoeken moesten, liepen rond."_
**Quick Take**: War transforms familiar spaces into alien landscapes of danger and uncertainty.

### The Emotional Spectrum of Invasion
The collection reveals the complex range of emotions people experienced during the invasion. Beyond fear and shock, there were moments of excitement, defiance, and even dark humor. One witness noted how Dutch soldiers found grim satisfaction in watching enemy aircraft fall: "Every time one was hit, a loud cheer went up. We saw it then, we already thought again that we could easily win the war."

> "I have seen soldiers who came out of a shop, sat on the pavement and alternately ate a large pack of chocolate of at least twenty bars and a pound of butter, after which they celebrated an orgy with juicy ÖL-sardines whose fingers dripped."
> _Original: "Ik heb soldaten gezien die een winkeltje uitkwamen, op de stoep gingen zitten en een groot pak chocola van minstens twintig repen en een pond roomboter, beurtelings in beide happend, achter elkaar opaten, om daarna een orgie te vieren aan smeuige Öl-sardines waarvan de vingers dropen."_
**Quick Take**: Human responses to crisis are rarely uniform—they encompass the full spectrum of emotional possibility.

### The Rapid Collapse of Normalcy
The testimonies illustrate how quickly the structures of daily life disintegrated. Schools closed, businesses shut down, transportation stopped, and communication networks failed. The invasion not only brought physical destruction but also the psychological trauma of having all familiar social anchors disappear within hours.

> "Post connections were gone, train and bus traffic had stopped, every city and village had become a closed whole, a world of fear and worry in itself."
> _Original: "Postverbindingen waren er niet, trein- en autobusverkeer was gestaakt, iedere stad en ieder dorp was een afgesloten geheel geworden, een wereld van angst en zorg op zich zelf."_
**Quick Take**: The infrastructure of civilization is more fragile than we typically acknowledge.

## Key Takeaways

- **Peace is fragile**: The Dutch experience shows how quickly normalcy can collapse, even in a neutral country that hoped to avoid conflict.

- **Human resilience is remarkable**: Despite the shock and terror, people found ways to help each other, maintain hope, and document their experiences.

- **War affects everyone**: From soldiers on the front lines to civilians trying to evacuate, the invasion touched all aspects of society.

- **Personal history matters**: Individual testimonies provide insights that official histories cannot capture—the emotional texture of historical events.

- **The importance of documentation**: The book demonstrates why preserving personal accounts of historical events matters for future generations.

- What can personal testimonies teach us about historical events that official records cannot?

- How does the Dutch experience of May 1940 compare to other civilian experiences of invasion in WWII?

## Conclusion
**The May Days of 1940** is worth your time precisely because it refuses to sanitize or romanticize war. Through its meticulous collection of ordinary voices, it accomplishes what traditional histories often cannot—it makes the past feel immediate, personal, and profoundly human. The biggest reason to read this book is to understand how ordinary people respond when their world shatters, revealing both the fragility of civilization and the remarkable resilience of the human spirit.

Kooijmans' collective diary approach transforms historical documentation into a deeply moving meditation on how we experience crisis. By presenting events without the benefit of hindsight, he forces readers to sit with uncertainty alongside his subjects, making this not just a historical account but a psychological exploration of how ordinary humans navigate extraordinary circumstances. The book's power lies in its ability to make us feel rather than simply know what those five days in May 1940 were like for the Dutch people caught in history's violent crosscurrents.
