# From Fake News to Power
**Original Title**: De las fake news al poder
**Authors**: Anna Clua Infante, Dardo Gómez Ruiz-Díaz | **Year**: 2024

## Summary
"From Fake News to Power" is a comprehensive analysis of the new ultra-right movement that has infiltrated Western democratic systems. The authors argue this isn't your grandfather's fascism—it's a rebranded, modernized version that combines rabid capitalism with social dehumanization, making it particularly dangerous to contemporary democratic societies.

The book serves as both a warning and a defensive manual for citizens who want to understand and resist the ultra-right's advance. It's essential reading for anyone concerned about the erosion of democratic values, the spread of misinformation, and the rise of authoritarian populism in the 21st century. Readers will gain crucial insights into identifying ultra-right tactics, understanding their strategic use of social issues to obscure economic agendas, and learning practical methods to defend democratic institutions.

## Core Insights

### The New Ultra-Right Isn't Old Fascism
Today's ultra-right movement has successfully rebranded itself, distancing from the overt fascism of the past while maintaining its core authoritarian impulses. They're not nostalgic traditionalists but innovators of political discourse who appeal to frustrated youth seeking charismatic leaders to blame for their problems.

> "This 'new' ultra-right is found at the crossroads of rabid capitalism and a dehumanization of social relations that would allow the unrestricted expansion of their theories."
> _Original: "Esta 'nueva' ultraderecha se encuentra, pues, en el cruce de un capitalismo rabioso y una deshumanización de las relaciones sociales que permitiría la expansión sin cortapisas de sus teorías."_
**Quick Take**: The ultra-right has learned that overt fascist imagery fails—instead they present themselves as anti-establishment innovators while pursuing the same authoritarian goals through democratic means.

### Dehumanization as Primary Strategy
The movement's core strategy involves systematically dehumanizing "others"—immigrants, the poor, LGBTQ+ individuals, and anyone different from their ideal citizenry. This process begins by portraying these groups as responsible for society's problems, making them targets for elimination rather than fellow humans with rights.

> "The dissident ceases to be a fellow human to become an element to be expelled or annihilated; they are dehumanized with lies so they cease to be one of your equals, and therefore without your same rights."
> _Original: "El disidente deja de ser un semejante para convertirse en un elemento a expulsar o aniquilar; se le deshumaniza con mentiras para que deje de ser uno de tus iguales, por tanto, sin tus mismos derechos."_
**Quick Take**: Before any group can be persecuted, they must first be stripped of their humanity—this is the first and most crucial step in the ultra-right's playbook.

### Social Issues as Distraction from Economic Reality
The ultra-right masterfully uses emotionally charged cultural issues—national identity, gender roles, family values, and security—to distract from their radical economic agenda: privatizing public services, dismantling social safety nets, and empowering corporate interests over citizens.

> "If the extreme right only spoke about economics, it wouldn't awaken as many passions as when speaking about national identity."
> _Original: "Si la extrema derecha hablara solo de economía, no despertaría tantas pasiones como las que despierta hablando de la identidad nacional."_
**Quick Take**: They avoid detailed economic discussions because their neoliberal proposals (like Milei's in Argentina) would horrify most voters if they understood the true implications.

### Denialism and Conspiracy as Weapons
The COVID-19 pandemic served as a testing ground for deploying denialism and conspiracy theories on a massive scale. From vaccine skepticism to climate change denial, these tactics erode trust in scientific institutions and government authority, creating fertile ground for authoritarian solutions.

> "The last COVID-19 pandemic has acted as a testing ground to deploy in all its intensity two basic theories of the new ultra-right: denialism and conspiracy."
> _Original: "La última pandemia del covid-19 ha actuado como un campo de ensayo para desplegar en toda su intensidad dos teorías básicas de la nueva ultraderecha: el negacionismo y la conspiración."_
**Quick Take**: By manufacturing doubt about established facts, they create a reality where their alternative narratives seem equally valid, undermining the very foundation of rational discourse.

### Media Complicity in Normalization
Many media outlets, whether through commercial interests or misguided notions of neutrality, become unwitting accomplices in normalizing ultra-right discourse. They repeat false statements under "freedom of expression" without context or fact-checking, lending credibility to dangerous ideas.

> "Many media outlets adopt the ultra-right's narrative appearing to show plurality, thus becoming accomplices to the deception. In other cases, they replicate their falsehoods and lies under the pretext of freedom of expression."
> _Original: "Adoptan la narrativa de la extrema derecha aparentando así pluralidad, convirtiéndose así en cómplices del engaño. En otras ocasiones, replican sus falsedades y mentiras bajo el pretexto de la libertad de expresión."_
**Quick Take**: Not all media bias is ideological—sometimes it's commercial. Sensationalist ultra-right statements drive engagement and profit, creating perverse incentives for coverage without context.

## Action Items

- **Verify information before sharing**: Use fact-checking websites like Maldita.es, EFE Verifica, or Verificat to combat the spread of fake news and misinformation.

- **Research political backgrounds**: Investigate the true ideological histories of political candidates who present themselves as "apolitical outsiders" to uncover hidden ultra-right connections.

- **Support independent journalism**: Choose media sources that prioritize truth-telling over ratings and refuse to platform hate speech without proper context and fact-checking.

- **Defend international human rights frameworks**: Support and demand that international organizations hold governments accountable for protecting democratic values and human rights.

- **Challenge economic obfuscation**: When politicians focus only on cultural issues, ask specific questions about their economic plans and how they'll affect public services and social safety nets.

## Questions to Consider

1. How can democratic societies distinguish between legitimate conservative viewpoints and dangerous ultra-right ideology?

2. What role should social media platforms play in preventing the spread of misinformation without becoming censors?

3. How can we maintain commitment to democratic principles while actively resisting those who would use those same principles to destroy democracy?

4. What responsibilities do ordinary citizens have in preventing the normalization of extremist rhetoric in public discourse?

5. How do we balance free speech protections with the need to combat hate speech and deliberate misinformation?

## Conclusion

"From Fake News to Power" is urgently relevant reading for anyone concerned about democratic backsliding in the 21st century. The authors provide both a stark warning and a practical toolkit for recognizing and resisting the ultra-right's advance. The book's greatest strength is its clear-eyed analysis of how this new movement differs from historical fascism while pursuing similar authoritarian goals through democratic means.

This is worth your time if you've been puzzled by the seemingly inexplicable rise of authoritarian populism across democratic nations. The authors' extensive experience in media analysis and human rights advocacy provides invaluable insights into how democratic societies can defend themselves against those who would use democratic processes to dismantle democracy.

The biggest reason to read this book is its preventative value—like a vaccination that works only if administered before infection. Understanding these tactics before they fully take root in your society may be the difference between preserving democracy and losing it to those who promise to save it.
