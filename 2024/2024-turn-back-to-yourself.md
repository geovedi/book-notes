# Turn Back to Yourself
**Original Title**: Повернись к себе
**Author**: Sara Kuburic | **Year**: 2024
**Category**: Self-Help & Personal Development 

## Summary
This profound self-help book explores the universal human experience of "losing oneself" - the painful disconnect between who we are and who we pretend to be. Through personal narrative and psychological expertise, Kuburic guides readers through the journey of reclaiming their authentic self after experiencing the devastating effects of self-loss.

The book begins with the author's own breaking point - a panic attack on a plane that forces her to confront the truth about her unhappy life, unfulfilling marriage, and profound disconnection from herself. As an existential psychologist, she weaves together her personal journey with professional insights, creating a roadmap for others who feel lost, disconnected, or trapped in lives that don't reflect their true selves.

This book is essential for anyone feeling trapped in roles, relationships, or lifestyles that don't align with their authentic self. It offers hope and practical guidance for those experiencing the deep pain of self-loss, whether they're aware of what's happening or just feeling that something fundamental is missing in their lives.

## Core Insights

### The Pain of Losing Yourself
Losing yourself is the inability to be authentic - a profound suffering that manifests when we live disconnected from our true nature, values, and desires. This loss often happens gradually through social conditioning, people-pleasing, and the gradual abandonment of our own needs in favor of others' expectations. The pain of this disconnection eventually surfaces through anxiety, depression, physical symptoms, or a gnawing sense that something is fundamentally wrong with our lives.

> "I am deeply and completely unhappy. I no longer know who I am, and I don't remember when I did know. I feel broken, but I don't remember what broke me."
> _Original: "Я глубина и совершенно несчастна. Я больше не знаю, кто я, и не помню, когда знала об этом. Я чувствую себя сломленной, но не помню, что меня сломило."_

**Quick Take**: Self-loss is the root cause of most suffering - when we can't be ourselves, we experience a profound emptiness that affects every aspect of our lives.

### Understanding Your "Self"
The "self" is not a fixed essence but a dynamic process of becoming - shaped by our choices, actions, and how we use our freedom. Drawing from existential philosophy, Kuburic explains that we create ourselves through our decisions and take responsibility for our lives. Your "self" includes both what you are (your given circumstances) and what you can become (your possibilities and potential).

> "We are condemned to be free" - we cannot escape the responsibility of choosing who we become.
> _Original: "Человек приговорен быть свободным"_

**Quick Take**: You are constantly creating yourself through your choices - there is no pre-written script, only the one you write through your actions.

### The Role of Freedom and Responsibility
True freedom means accepting responsibility for our choices and their consequences. Many people avoid this responsibility by blaming circumstances, other people, or the past for their unhappiness. However, Viktor Frankl's experiences in concentration camps demonstrate that even in the most extreme circumstances, humans retain the freedom to choose their attitude and response.

> "Everything can be taken from a man except one thing: the last of the human freedoms - to choose one's attitude in any given set of circumstances."
> _Original: "У человека можно отнять все, кроме одного: последней свободы человека — выбирать собственное отношение к любым обстоятельствам"_

**Quick Take**: Freedom isn't the absence of limitations - it's how you choose to respond within them.

### Boundaries as Self-Expression
Healthy boundaries are not walls to keep people out - they are the outline of your true self. When you establish clear boundaries, you're teaching others who you are and what you value. Many people struggle with boundaries because they don't know themselves well enough to define what is and isn't acceptable to them.

> "Boundaries are the language of love - let's treat them that way."
> _Original: "Границы — язык любви, давайте так и относиться к ним."_

**Quick Take**: Your boundaries communicate your values and identity more clearly than words ever could.

### The Journey Back to Authenticity
Returning to yourself requires facing uncomfortable truths, grieving what you've lost, and gradually reclaiming the parts of yourself you've abandoned. This process involves isolation, grief, and the courage to disappoint others who prefer the inauthentic version of you. The path includes setting boundaries, listening to your body and emotions, and making choices aligned with your true values.

> "We must become lonely, completely lonely, withdraw completely into the secret 'I'. This is the path of bitterness and suffering. But when the solitude is over, we are no longer lonely, because it turns out that our secret 'I' is spirit, the indivisible Lord."
> _Original: "Мы должны стать одинокими, совершенно одинокими, полностью уйти в сокровенное 'я'. Это путь горечи и страдания. Но когда уединение позади, мы больше не одиноки, потому что оказывается, что наше сокровенное 'я' — это дух, неделимый Господь."_

**Quick Take**: Authentic living requires the courage to be temporarily alone in your truth before others can join you there.

### Clearing Mental and Physical Space
Just as physical clutter drains our energy, mental and emotional clutter prevents us from hearing our authentic voice. The author discovered that minimalism - both physical and mental - creates space for the true self to emerge. This means letting go of thoughts, beliefs, relationships, and possessions that no longer serve who you are becoming.

> "Having less (both literally and figuratively) means freeing yourself from limitations, using will and freedom. The fewer things, the more space for the 'I'."
> _Original: "Иметь меньше (и в прямом, и в переносном смысле) значит освободиться от ограничений, использовать волю и свободу. Чем меньше вещей, тем больше пространства для 'я'."_

**Quick Take**: Create space in your life for your true self by removing everything that weighs you down or doesn't align with your authentic path.

## Action Items

- **Listen to your body's signals** - Physical symptoms, fatigue, and discomfort are often messages from your authentic self trying to get your attention
- **Practice saying "no"** - Start establishing boundaries by declining requests, invitations, or obligations that don't align with your true values
- **Create space for authenticity** - Remove physical clutter, limiting beliefs, toxic relationships, and anything that prevents you from hearing your inner voice
- **Take responsibility for your choices** - Stop blaming circumstances or others for your unhappiness and recognize your power to choose your response
- **Embrace uncomfortable emotions** - Allow yourself to feel grief, anger, and disappointment as natural parts of the journey back to yourself
- **Make small authentic choices daily** - Start with simple decisions like what you truly want to eat, wear, or do with your time
- **Practice self-compassion** - Treat yourself with kindness as you navigate the challenging process of reclaiming your authentic self

## Questions to Consider

- When was the last time you felt truly yourself, and what were you doing?
- What parts of yourself have you suppressed or abandoned to please others?
- If no one else's opinion mattered, what would you change about your life?
- What boundaries do you need to establish to protect your authentic self?
- What are you afraid might happen if you lived completely authentically?
- Which relationships in your life support your true self, and which ones diminish it?
- What would you do if you knew you couldn't fail or disappoint anyone?

## Conclusion
This book is worth your time if you've ever felt that nagging sense that you're living someone else's life or that there's a disconnect between who you are and who you present to the world. Kuburic's raw honesty combined with her professional expertise creates a powerful guide for anyone ready to stop performing and start living authentically.

The biggest reason to read this book is its perfect blend of philosophical depth and practical application. It doesn't just diagnose the problem of self-loss - it provides a comprehensive roadmap for healing and reclaiming your authentic self. The author's personal vulnerability makes the psychological concepts accessible and relatable, while her professional training ensures the advice is grounded in proven therapeutic approaches.

While the journey back to authenticity is never easy, this book provides both the inspiration and the practical tools needed to begin. It reminds us that it's never too late to turn back to yourself and that the pain of self-loss is ultimately a gift - a signal that it's time to start living the life you were meant to live.
