# Petrified
**Authors**: Graham Masterton | **Year**: 2024
**Category**: Horror & Thriller

## Summary
A horrifying supernatural thriller that weaves together multiple stories of tragedy and terror. The novel follows Braydon Harris, a father who kidnaps his five-year-old daughter Sukie only to be involved in a catastrophic car accident that leaves her with devastating burns. Simultaneously, Professor Nathan Underhill and his team successfully recreate a mythical phoenix creature in their Philadelphia laboratory, unleashing an ancient and terrifying force upon the world. As Detective Jenna Pullet investigates what appears to be a supernatural murder involving a petrified gargoyle-like creature, these disparate plotlines begin to converge in a nightmarish tale of parental guilt, scientific hubris, and ancient evil made terrifyingly real.

This gripping horror novel will appeal to fans of supernatural thrillers who enjoy stories that blend personal tragedy with ancient mythology and modern science. Readers who appreciate Graham Masterton's signature blend of graphic horror, psychological tension, and inventive creature features will find this particularly compelling.

## Core Insights

### The Experiment Gone Wrong
Professor Nathan Underhill's team achieves the impossible by successfully recreating a phoenix worm using ancient alchemical texts and modern DNA splicing techniques. However, their triumph quickly turns to terror when the creature metamorphoses into something far more dangerous than anticipated—a winged demon-like being that breaks free from their laboratory and begins a reign of terror over Philadelphia. The novel explores the theme of scientific ambition meeting ancient supernatural forces, asking what happens when humanity succeeds in resurrecting powers that were meant to remain buried in myth and legend.

> "We're dealing with primeval birds and beasts and reptiles, and sometimes a mixture of all three, with powers that we can't even begin to understand. What's more, they've never encountered humans before—unlike wolves and bears and coyotes and alligators—so they have absolutely no fear of us whatsoever."

**Quick Take**: The novel serves as a cautionary tale about the dangers of scientific hubris when dealing with forces beyond human comprehension.

### A Father's Guilt and Desperation
Braydon Harris's story is one of the most emotionally devastating threads in the novel. After losing custody of his daughter Sukie, he makes the fateful decision to kidnap her, leading directly to the horrific car accident that leaves her burned and fighting for her life. His journey through guilt, regret, and desperate attempts to reconnect with his daughter forms the emotional core of the story. The novel explores how parental love can transform into destructive obsession, and how one impulsive decision can ripple outward to create unimaginable suffering.

> "I would rather that she had never been born at all."

**Quick Take**: The story examines the devastating consequences of parental desperation and the weight of guilt that follows tragic mistakes.

### The Supernatural Investigation
Detective Jenna Pullet's investigation into what appears to be a bizarre murder reveals the supernatural elements at play. When a man is killed by a petrified gargoyle-like creature that falls from the sky, Jenna finds herself drawn into a mystery that defies rational explanation. Her interactions with Sister Mary Emmanuelle, who senses the evil presence before the attack, provide crucial supernatural insight into the nature of the threat facing Philadelphia. This storyline blends police procedural elements with horror investigation.

> "It was a living thing, Detective. A creature."

**Quick Take**: The novel effectively combines investigative thriller elements with supernatural horror, creating a compelling mystery that drives the narrative forward.

### Ancient Evil in Modern World
The petrified creatures represent ancient evil unleashed upon contemporary Philadelphia. These beings, described as having "a face like a demon, ugly beyond all description, with bulging eyes and horns" and leathery wings, embody the terror of the unknown invading familiar spaces. The novel explores how modern society is ill-equipped to deal with supernatural threats that defy scientific understanding and rational explanation.

> "To believe in evil is to give it life."

**Quick Take**: The story examines society's vulnerability to ancient forces when modern science and rational thinking prove inadequate against supernatural threats.

## Key Questions to Consider
- How far would you go to reconnect with a child you've lost custody of?
- What responsibilities do scientists have when experimenting with forces they don't fully understand?
- Can modern rational society truly protect itself from ancient supernatural threats?
- How does guilt manifest and consume those who have caused irreversible harm to loved ones?
- What happens when ancient mythological forces are unleashed in modern urban environments?

## Conclusion
**Petrified** is a brutally effective horror novel that succeeds on multiple levels—as a supernatural thriller, a family tragedy, and a cautionary tale about scientific ambition. Masterton masterfully weaves together disparate plotlines into a cohesive and terrifying narrative that keeps readers engaged throughout. The emotional weight of Braydon and Sukie's story provides a human anchor for the more fantastical elements involving the phoenix creature and petrified demons, making the horror more impactful because we care about the characters involved.

The novel is definitely worth your time if you enjoy graphic horror with strong emotional depth and creative creature mythology. Masterton's vivid descriptions of both the supernatural terror and very human suffering create a powerful combination that will linger in your mind long after finishing the book. The biggest reason to read it is the skillful way it balances intimate family tragedy with epic supernatural horror, creating a story that is both personally moving and terrifying on a grand scale.
