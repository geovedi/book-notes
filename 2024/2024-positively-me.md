# Positively Me
**Authors**: Nozibele Mayaba, Sue Nyathi | **Year**: 2024
**Category**: History & Biography

## Summary
"Positively Me" is a powerful memoir that chronicles Nozibele Mayaba's journey from being a role model student and successful young professional to receiving an HIV diagnosis at age 22, despite being a virgin until months before her diagnosis. The book reveals how she transformed this life-altering news into a platform for activism, becoming an internationally recognized HIV advocate, podcaster, and TV host. Her story demonstrates that behind every statistic is a person with dreams, fears, and the capacity to create profound change.

This memoir speaks to anyone facing unexpected life challenges, health diagnoses, or seeking inspiration on turning personal adversity into purpose. Readers will discover how vulnerability can become strength, how speaking your truth can liberate not just yourself but others, and how maintaining your identity in the face of stigma is revolutionary.

## Core Insights

### From Perfect Student to HIV Diagnosis
The book opens with Nozibele as the quintessential success story - a devout Christian who excelled academically, secured scholarships, and landed a prestigious job at Continental Tyre. She was the girl parents pointed to as a role model, having saved herself for marriage and followed all the "right" paths. Her HIV diagnosis at 22 shattered not just her health reality but her entire identity and understanding of how life was supposed to work.

> "Women like me don't get HIV. But then I did."

**Quick Take**: Success and virtue don't immunize you from life's challenges. Your response, not the circumstance, defines your character.

### The Transformative Power of Speaking Out
Initially devastated and suicidal after her diagnosis, Nozibele found freedom through transparency. Sharing her status publicly, first with close friends and family, then increasingly openly, lifted an enormous burden. This act of vulnerability became her superpower, leading to her TV show "#YesIHaveHIV" that helps others disclose their status to loved ones.

> "Speaking out freed me and completely changed my life."

**Quick Take**: The things you're most afraid to say are often the very words that will set you free.

### Education as Liberation
Throughout her childhood in Port Elizabeth townships, Nozibele recognized education as her ticket to a different life. Despite growing up in poverty with a single mother who worked multiple cleaning jobs, she excelled academically and earned full scholarships through the Ubuntu Education Fund. This foundation of educational excellence became crucial when she needed to rebuild her life post-diagnosis.

> "I was trying to be positively me."

**Quick Take**: Educational achievement isn't just about career advancement - it's about developing the resilience and resources to handle whatever life throws your way.

### Redefining Purpose Through Adversity
Rather than allowing HIV to define her as a victim, Nozibele transformed it into her calling. She partnered with major radio stations to create youth-focused podcasts and developed platforms for HIV awareness that reach millions. Her diagnosis became the catalyst for finding deeper purpose than she might have discovered in her corporate career.

> "An HIV-positive diagnosis may no longer be a death sentence, but it still changes everything."

**Quick Take**: Sometimes your greatest challenges become the foundation for your most meaningful contributions to the world.

### The Statistics Have Stories
The book emphasizes how HIV statistics often mask the human reality behind each number. Every person living with HIV has a family, dreams, fears, and a story worth hearing. Nozibele's work centers on humanizing the epidemic and showing that people with HIV can live full, successful lives.

> "Behind every statistic is a person with a name, a family, a story."

**Quick Take**: True advocacy begins when you see individuals behind the numbers and tell their stories with dignity and hope.

## Conclusion
"Positively Me" is essential reading for anyone facing life-altering challenges or seeking inspiration on turning personal adversity into purpose. Nozibele's journey demonstrates that HIV status doesn't define a person - their response does. The book's biggest strength is its raw honesty about the devastating initial impact of diagnosis combined with the hopeful message about finding new purpose.

This isn't just an HIV memoir; it's a masterclass in resilience, vulnerability, and the transformative power of speaking your truth. Nozibele shows that sometimes the worst things that happen to us become the foundation for our most meaningful work. The book is particularly valuable for understanding that success isn't about avoiding challenges - it's about how you respond when they inevitably arrive.

For readers facing their own "positively me" moments - whatever those may be - this book offers both comfort and a roadmap for turning crisis into calling. It's a powerful reminder that your diagnosis, setback, or challenge doesn't have to be your whole story - it can be the beginning of your most important chapter.
