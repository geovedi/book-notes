# 2024-Artificial Intelligence, Big Data, IoT and Blockchain in Healthcare
**Original Title**: Artificial Intelligence, Big Data, IOT and Block Chain in Healthcare: From Concepts to Applications
**Editor**: Yousef Farhaoui | **Year**: 2024 | **Publisher**: Springer Nature

## Summary
This comprehensive edited volume explores how four transformative technologies—Artificial Intelligence, Big Data, Internet of Things, and Blockchain—are reshaping healthcare and other industries. The book moves beyond theoretical concepts to present practical implementations, real-world case studies, and expert perspectives on deploying these technologies to solve pressing challenges in modern healthcare systems.

Healthcare professionals, technology implementers, and policy makers will discover concrete applications of AI in medical diagnosis, IoT for patient monitoring, blockchain for secure health records, and big data analytics for healthcare optimization. The book provides both technical depth and strategic insights for anyone looking to understand or implement these technologies in healthcare settings.

## Core Insights

### AI-Powered Medical Diagnosis and Imaging
Artificial Intelligence is revolutionizing medical diagnosis through machine learning algorithms that can detect diseases earlier and more accurately than traditional methods. The book presents compelling research on melanoma skin cancer detection using Linear Discriminant Analysis and classification algorithms, demonstrating how AI can analyze medical images and patient data to identify patterns that might escape human observation.

> "Through advanced algorithms and data-driven insights, AI holds the promise of revolutionizing industries ranging from healthcare and finance to transportation and entertainment."
**Quick Take**: AI isn't replacing doctors—it's augmenting their capabilities by providing data-driven insights that support more accurate and timely diagnoses.

### Medical Image Compression and Storage Efficiency
The healthcare industry faces a massive data storage challenge, with single medical facilities generating terabytes of medical imaging data annually. The book presents an innovative solution combining Discrete Wavelet Transform (DWT) with Set Partitioning in Hierarchical Trees (SPIHT) algorithms, achieving compression ratios of 15-18x while maintaining diagnostic image quality.

**Performance Results**: The system achieved exceptional image quality metrics—CT scans compressed 18x with 43.44 dB quality, MRI images compressed 16x with 45.42 dB quality, and X-rays compressed 16x with 38.27 dB quality. This breakthrough technology dramatically reduces storage costs and enables faster transmission of medical data for telemedicine applications.

### Internet of Medical Things (IoMT) and Smart Healthcare
IoT technology extends healthcare monitoring beyond hospital walls by embedding sensors in medical devices and wearables that collect real-time patient data. The book explores how these connected devices enable continuous monitoring, automated data collection, and enhanced patient experiences in healthcare delivery.

**Applications include**: Remote patient monitoring for chronic conditions, smart medication adherence systems, connected medical devices that automatically report status and readings, and emergency response systems that detect falls or health crises. This technology is particularly valuable for aging populations and patients in remote locations.

> "The Internet of things (IoT) further extends the reach of interconnected devices, enabling seamless communication and data exchange between physical objects and digital systems. By embedding sensors and connectivity into everyday objects, IoT facilitates automation, optimization, and enhanced user experiences across diverse domains, including smart homes, healthcare monitoring, and industrial automation."
**Quick Take**: IoMT transforms episodic healthcare into continuous health management, enabling proactive interventions rather than reactive treatments.

### Blockchain for Secure Health Data Management
Blockchain technology addresses one of healthcare's biggest challenges: maintaining security and privacy while sharing medical data across different providers and systems. The book presents an enhanced Internet of Medical Things data communication system that combines blockchain with cryptography to ensure secure, immutable medical records.

**Key Benefits**: Patient-controlled health records where individuals decide who accesses their data, secure transmission of medical device data without risking privacy, immutable audit trails for regulatory compliance, and protection against data breaches and ransomware attacks. This technology is particularly crucial as healthcare becomes increasingly digitized and connected.

### Big Data Analytics for Healthcare Optimization
Big Data analytics enables healthcare organizations to extract actionable insights from vast amounts of medical, operational, and patient data. The book demonstrates how machine learning algorithms can detect patterns in patient populations, predict disease outbreaks, optimize hospital operations, and identify healthcare fraud before it causes significant financial damage.

**Applications**: Predictive analytics for hospital bed management and staffing, population health management through analysis of patient demographics and outcomes, personalized treatment recommendations based on similar patient cases, and fraud detection systems that save millions by identifying suspicious insurance claim patterns.
