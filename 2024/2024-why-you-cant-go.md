# Why You Can't Go
**Authors**: Lorraine Cooney, Registered Dietitian | **Year**: 2024
**Category**: Health & Medicine

## Summary
"Why You Can't Go" is a comprehensive guide to understanding and managing constipation, written by registered dietitian Lorraine Cooney. The book breaks down the science behind digestive health, destigmatizes bowel discussions, and provides practical, evidence-based strategies for relief. Cooney combines clinical experience with scientific research to offer a holistic approach that addresses diet, lifestyle, stress, and behavioral factors affecting bowel health.

This book is ideal for anyone struggling with chronic constipation or irritable bowel syndrome with constipation (IBS-C), as well as healthcare professionals seeking deeper understanding. Readers will gain actionable insights, personalized assessment tools, and a step-by-step roadmap to achieve lasting relief and improve their quality of life.

## Core Insights

### Understanding Constipation Beyond the Basics
Constipation affects one in seven people globally, making it incredibly common yet widely misunderstood. The book reveals that constipation isn't just about infrequent bowel movements—it's a complex condition involving stool consistency, difficulty passing stool, and the feeling of incomplete evacuation. Many people suffer in silence due to social stigma, with 65% self-medicating rather than seeking medical help.

> "Constipation is a quiet but very determined troublemaker in one's digestive system. It is, in fact, very common, with most reports indicating that 15 per cent or at least one in seven people are affected."

**Quick Take**: Normal bowel frequency ranges from three times daily to three times weekly—it's about consistency and comfort, not just frequency.

### The Fiber-Focused Approach
Dietary fiber forms the cornerstone of constipation management, but not all fiber is created equal. The book provides detailed guidance on different fiber types (soluble vs. insoluble), specific food recommendations, and gradual introduction strategies to avoid digestive upset. Cooney emphasizes starting slow and building up fiber intake while maintaining adequate hydration.

> "The colon doesn't produce any enzymes, but its claim to fame is that it is teeming with trillions of magical microbes. These play a crucial role in breaking down dietary fibre and other components that enter the colon."

**Quick Take**: Focus on diverse plant-based foods rather than fiber supplements, and increase intake gradually over 2-3 weeks to allow your gut microbiome to adapt.

### The Gut-Brain Connection
The book reveals the profound connection between stress, emotions, and bowel function. The gut-brain axis demonstrates how psychological factors directly impact digestive health through neurotransmitters like serotonin and hormonal influences. Stress activates the "fight or flight" response, which can slow digestion and contribute to constipation.

> "When the balance is off between our stress responses ('fight or flight') and our relaxation response ('rest and digest'), it can throw our digestive system into a bit of a loop, potentially making things move more sluggishly and leading to constipation."

**Quick Take**: Managing stress through relaxation techniques, mindfulness, and adequate sleep is as important as dietary interventions for bowel regularity.

### Proper Toileting Techniques
Surprisingly, many people don't know how to use the toilet correctly for optimal bowel evacuation. The book covers positioning, breathing techniques, and the timing of bathroom visits. The squatting position (using a small stool under your feet) helps align the colon for easier passage, while proper breathing relaxes the pelvic floor muscles.

**Quick Take**: Elevate your feet on a small stool while sitting on the toilet, practice deep belly breathing, and never ignore the urge to go.

### Beyond Diet: Lifestyle Factors
The book emphasizes that successful constipation management requires a multi-faceted approach. Regular physical activity, adequate hydration (2-2.5 liters daily), consistent meal timing, and quality sleep all play crucial roles. The author also discusses the impact of medications, hormonal fluctuations, and travel on bowel regularity.

> "Managing the diagnosis and treatment of chronic constipation resembles piecing together a mosaic, and it's so important to recognise that each person will have their individual approach to assembling the pieces."

**Quick Take**: Create a personalized "bowel-friendly" daily routine that includes movement, hydration, stress management, and consistent meal timing.

## Action Items

- **Track Your Patterns**: Keep a detailed bowel diary for 2-3 weeks, noting frequency, consistency (using the Bristol Stool Chart), diet, stress levels, and symptoms
- **Fiber Graduation**: Increase fiber intake slowly by 3-5 grams every 3 days, focusing on diverse plant sources rather than supplements
- **Hydration Strategy**: Aim for 2-2.5 liters of fluid daily, with most as water, and limit caffeine and alcohol which can be dehydrating
- **Movement Matters**: Incorporate 30 minutes of moderate exercise daily, such as brisk walking, swimming, or cycling
- **Toileting Routine**: Establish a consistent bathroom routine, ideally 15-30 minutes after meals when the gastrocolic reflex is strongest
- **Stress Management**: Practice relaxation techniques like deep breathing, meditation, or yoga for 10-15 minutes daily
- **Sleep Optimization**: Aim for 7-9 hours of quality sleep per night, maintaining consistent sleep and wake times

## Questions to Consider

- What patterns do you notice in your bowel habits related to diet, stress, travel, or hormonal changes?
- Are you consistently responding to your body's signals, or do you frequently delay bathroom visits due to inconvenience or discomfort?
- How does your current fiber intake compare to the recommended 25-30 grams daily?
- What stress management techniques could you incorporate into your daily routine?
- Are your toileting habits and positioning optimal for bowel evacuation?
- How might your sleep quality and physical activity levels be impacting your digestive health?

## Conclusion
"Why You Can't Go" succeeds in demystifying constipation while providing practical, science-backed solutions. Cooney's compassionate approach helps destigmatize a common but rarely discussed condition, making readers feel understood rather than ashamed. The book's strength lies in its comprehensive perspective—addressing not just what we eat, but how we live, stress, sleep, and even use the toilet.

What makes this book worth your time is its balance of scientific rigor and practical wisdom. Cooney doesn't promise quick fixes but provides a realistic roadmap for lasting improvement. The biggest reason to read it? You'll finally understand that you're not alone in your struggle, and that effective relief is possible through the right combination of dietary, lifestyle, and behavioral changes. Whether you've been dealing with constipation for months or years, this guide offers hope and actionable steps toward better digestive health and improved quality of life.
