# Priority 1
**Original Title**: PRIO 1
**Author**: Tom van der Weerd | **Year**: 2024

## Summary
"PRIO 1" is a raw, honest collection of real-life firefighting stories from Tom van der Weerd, a Dutch firefighter who also works as a DJ at Qmusic. The title refers to the highest priority emergency calls that require immediate response with lights and sirens. Through a series of vivid, personal anecdotes, Van der Weerd takes readers behind the scenes of the fire service, revealing the physical, mental, and emotional challenges faced by first responders.

This book offers invaluable insight for anyone curious about emergency services, considering a career in firefighting, or simply wanting to understand the human stories behind those blazing sirens. Readers will gain profound appreciation for the split-second decisions, teamwork, and psychological resilience required in this life-saving profession.

## Core Insights

### Life as a Firefighter: The Dual Reality
Firefighting represents two extreme ends of human experience - moments of intense, life-or-death urgency interspersed with long periods of routine maintenance, training, and waiting. Van der Weerd describes how the "pieper" (pager) can interrupt the most mundane activities, instantly transforming ordinary citizens into emergency responders. The job demands both physical courage and mental fortitude, as firefighters must remain calm when others panic and run toward danger when everyone else flees.

> "We drop everything when the pieper goes. We go inside when others go outside. We leave our loved ones behind when strangers are in trouble."
> _Original: "Wij laten alles uit onze handen vallen als de pieper gaat. Wij gaan naar binnen als anderen naar buiten gaan. Wij laten onze naasten achter als – voor ons – onbekenden in de problemen zitten."_
**Quick Take**: Firefighting requires instant transformation from civilian to lifesaver, always ready to sacrifice personal comfort for others' safety.

### The Priority System: Understanding Emergency Response
The Dutch emergency system uses PRIO 1 and PRIO 2 classifications. PRIO 1 represents life-threatening emergencies requiring lights and sirens - house fires, accidents with injuries, cardiac arrests. PRIO 2 covers urgent but non-immediately life-threatening situations - lift entrapments, storm damage, gas leaks. This system helps allocate resources efficiently while ensuring critical cases receive immediate attention.

> "At PRIO 1, the fire engine drives out with flashing lights and sirens. At that moment, the vehicle is an emergency vehicle with traffic exemptions."
> _Original: "Bij PRIO 1 rijdt de brandweerwagen met zwaailicht en sirene uit. Op dat moment is de auto een voorrangsvoertuig en zijn er vrijstellingen in het verkeer."_
**Quick Take**: Understanding emergency priorities helps the public appreciate why some incidents require full emergency response while others don't.

### The Psychological Toll of Trauma
Van der Weerd powerfully addresses the mental health impact of repeated exposure to traumatic situations. He describes how the fire service provides mandatory support through TCO (Team Collegiale Opvang) after critical incidents, and how writing about his experiences became therapeutic. The book reveals that even experienced firefighters struggle with the emotional weight of their work, from suicide attempts to fatal accidents.

> "This is my first experience with an incident with a fatal outcome. Fortunately, aftercare at the fire service is good, but I notice that I am overcome by deep sadness."
> _Original: "Het is mijn eerste ervaring met een incident met dodelijke afloop. Gelukkig is de nazorg bij de brandweer goed, maar ik merk dat ik word overvallen door een diepe bedroefdheid."_
**Quick Take**: Mental health support is crucial for emergency responders who regularly witness life's most difficult moments.

### Family Legacy and Service Motivation
The author reveals how his father's career as a firefighter inspired his own path, creating a multi-generational family commitment to public service. This legacy provided both inspiration and realistic preparation for the job's challenges. His story shows how many firefighters are drawn to the profession through family connections, community values, and a desire to serve others.

> "I grew up with a father who was in the fire service. When I was seven years old, I found it particularly interesting, especially because I was regularly allowed to visit the fire station."
> _Original: "Ik ben opgegroeid met een vader die bij de brandweer zit. Ik vond het als zevenjarige bijzonder interessant, zeker omdat ik ook regelmatig naar de brandweerkazerne mocht."_
**Quick Take**: Family traditions and early exposure often plant the seeds for future firefighters, creating lasting legacies of service.

### Technical Excellence Through Training
The book emphasizes that firefighting success comes from rigorous training and practice. Van der Weerd describes his progression from youth firefighter to command officer, highlighting the importance of continuous learning and skill development. Technical expertise with equipment, understanding building construction, and mastering rescue techniques all require dedication to ongoing education.

> "After nine months of intensive training, I can now call myself a fire service commander. This moment is like a dream that finally comes true."
> _Original: "Jij kunt jezelf vanaf nu bevelvoerder bij de brandweer noemen. Het voelt onwerkelijk dat ik eindelijk bevelvoerder ben en mijn opleiding heb afgerond."_
**Quick Take**: Professional firefighting requires constant learning and skill refinement to handle life-threatening situations effectively.

## Best Quotes

- "Working for the fire service asks a lot of me and my colleagues. Sometimes a call comes at a really inconvenient time, but even then we go to the station. Although we spend a lot of time practicing new techniques and further training, we sometimes have to overcome our fear to bring an incident to a good end."
  _Original: "Werken bij de brandweer vraagt veel van mij en mijn collega's. Soms komt een melding nét niet goed uit, maar ook dan gaan we naar de kazerne. Hoewel we veel tijd stoppen in het oefenen van nieuwe technieken en bijscholing, moeten we soms over onze angst heen stappen om een incident tot een goed eind te brengen."_

- "Working for the fire service requires not only physical, but also mental effort. Where other people might panic, we have to keep our heads cool."
  _Original: "Werken bij de brandweer vraagt niet alleen fysieke, maar ook mentale inspanning. Waar andere mensen misschien in paniek zouden raken, moeten wij juist ons hoofd koel houden."_

- "Every time the pieper goes, we stand ready and ensure that an incident, big or small, is handled properly. And you know what? I think working for the fire service is the most beautiful profession in the world."
  _Original: "Iedere keer dat de pieper gaat, staan we klaar en zorgen we ervoor dat een incident, groot of klein, goed afgehandeld wordt. En weet je wat? Ik vind werken bij de brandweer het mooiste beroep ter wereld."_

## Key Takeaways

- Firefighting requires both physical courage and emotional resilience to handle life-and-death situations regularly
- Strong teamwork and trust between colleagues are essential for survival and effectiveness in emergency situations
- Mental health support and processing traumatic experiences are crucial aspects of emergency services
- Technical training and continuous skill development are fundamental to professional firefighting
- Family legacies and community connections often inspire career choices in public service

## Conclusion

"Priority 1" is a compelling, authentic glimpse into the world of firefighting that goes beyond Hollywood dramatization to show the real human experience behind the uniform. Van der Weerd's straightforward storytelling style makes complex technical aspects accessible while never losing sight of the emotional core of each incident. The book's greatest strength is its honesty - it doesn't glamorize the profession but presents it with all its fear, frustration, grief, and occasional triumph.

This is absolutely worth your time if you're interested in emergency services, considering public service careers, or simply want to understand the people behind those flashing lights. The biggest reason to read it is the genuine perspective it offers on ordinary people doing extraordinary work, reminding us that behind every emergency response are dedicated humans balancing their own fears with their commitment to helping others.
