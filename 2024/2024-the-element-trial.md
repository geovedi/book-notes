# The Element Trial
**Original Title**: Elementprøven
**Authors**: Julie Midtgaard | **Year**: 2024

## Summary
In 17th century Denmark, two orphan girls—Dawn and Akela—are accused of witchcraft and face execution. When lightning strikes during their trial, they're thrust through mysterious rifts into Luscuro, a magical world at war where elemental magic governs society. There, they discover they're Sixters—people born with elemental powers—but their memories of this world were sealed when they were sent to Earth as infants. Now they must navigate a dangerous new reality while uncovering political conspiracies and finding their true identities.

This coming-of-age fantasy combines witch trial survival with portal fantasy, perfect for readers who enjoy magical school settings, political intrigue, and stories about found family. The book explores themes of identity, belonging, and the moral complexities of power while delivering an immersive world with a unique magic system.

## Fast-Track Story Guide

### The Essential Plot
Dawn and Akela, orphans raised by the wise Dorothea in 17th century Aalborg, face accusations of witchcraft from their rival Erika. During their execution, rifts open during elemental discharges (lightning for Dawn, fire for Akela), transporting them to Luscuro—a world where people called Sixters wield elemental powers tied to earth, water, fire, or air. They discover they're part of this world, sent to Earth as infants for safety during a great war, with their memories magically sealed.

In Notio, one of Luscuro's school towns, they learn about the Element Trial—a dangerous test that determines their future roles in society. Dawn struggles to discover her elemental affinity while Akela, already an Earth Sixter, navigates the complex political landscape involving Queen Sora, who may not be what she seems. As they train and prepare for the trial, they uncover dangerous conspiracies, face magical creatures, and learn that the war that drove them from Luscuro is far from over.

### Key Turning Points
- **The Accusation**: Erika's false witchcraft accusation sets everything in motion
- **The Rifts**: Lightning and fire open portals during execution, transporting them to Luscuro
- **Memory Recovery**: Ritual reveals their true identities as Sixters with sealed memories
- **The Trial**: The Element Trial tests their survival skills and elemental control
- **The Conspiracy**: Discovery that Queen Sora may be using dark magic to control the kingdom
- **The Ambush**: Dawn survives a rattler attack that was secretly orchestrated
- **The Departure**: Akela leaves for the capital Sixten, creating tension between friends

### Main Characters
- **Dawn**: The narrator, a thoughtful teenager who must discover her elemental powers and navigate political intrigue while questioning authority
- **Akela**: Dawn's foster sister, an Earth Sixter who already knows about Luscuro and acts as guide, but whose loyalties become increasingly divided
- **Nix**: A Fire Sixter who arrives with Dawn, possessing natural talent but carrying trauma from her past on Earth
- **Norell**: A Water Sixter student who becomes their friend and guide to Luscuro's customs
- **Cheng**: The Earth Sixter headmistress who suspects corruption in the royal court
- **Queen Sora**: The seemingly benevolent ruler who may actually be a Dark Sixter manipulating the kingdom through illusion magic
- **Dorothea**: The wise woman who raised the girls on Earth, secretly a Sixter herself who prepared them for their return to Luscuro

## Key Takeaways

- **Trust Your Instincts**: Dawn's survival depends on questioning authority and recognizing when something isn't right
- **Found Family Matters**: The bonds between friends can be stronger than blood ties
- **Power Requires Responsibility**: Elemental abilities come with the duty to use them wisely
- **Truth Is Complex**: People and situations aren't always what they seem on the surface
- **Courage Takes Many Forms**: Sometimes the bravest act is admitting uncertainty

## Questions to Consider

- How do our memories shape our identity, and what would happen if they were sealed away?
- What responsibilities come with power, and how do we know when to question authority?
- Can friendship survive political division and conflicting loyalties?
- How do we find our place in a world that seems determined to reject us?
- What does "home" really mean when you belong to multiple worlds?

## Conclusion
*The Element Trial* is worth your time if you enjoy portal fantasies with well-developed magic systems and political intrigue. The book stands out for its unique blend of historical witch trials and magical world-building, creating a compelling origin story that sets up an epic series. The biggest reason to read it is the rich character development—Dawn's journey from uncertain orphan to powerful Sixter feels earned and authentic, while the complex relationships between characters add emotional depth to the magical adventure.

The cliffhanger ending will leave you eager for the sequel, but the book provides enough resolution to satisfy as a standalone story. At its heart, this is a story about finding your place in the world and the courage to question everything you thought you knew about yourself.
