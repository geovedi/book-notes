# Winter Wellbeing
**Authors**: Caroline Artiss, Christine Burke, Elisabeth Carlsson, Cerridwen Greenleaf, Noelle Renée Kovary, Lottie Muir, Alice Peck, Silja Sample | **Year**: 2024
**Category**: Health & Medicine

## Summary
"Winter Wellbeing" is a comprehensive seasonal self-care guide designed to help readers not just survive but thrive during the colder, darker months. This practical playbook offers holistic approaches to winter wellness through mindfulness, immune-boosting practices, self-care rituals, and revitalizing routines that nourish body, mind, and soul.

Perfect for anyone seeking to transform their relationship with winter from one of endurance to one of nourishment and joy. Readers will gain practical tools for staying healthy, maintaining mental wellbeing, and finding beauty and meaning in the winter season.

## Core Insights

### 1. Embrace Winter Rather Than Resist It
Winter offers unique opportunities for mindfulness, appreciation of nature's beauty, and deeper self-reflection. Rather than seeing winter as something to endure, this guide teaches us to savor the season's distinctive pleasures—from the wonder of frost and snow to the magic of visible breath on cold days.

The book introduces mindful walking practices like Kinhin (Zen walking meditation) and chi walking, which help us slow down and appreciate winter's subtle beauty. "Winter may be a time of darker days and cold weather, but when we embrace it there are so many small pleasures and moments of comfort to be found."

> "Walking in the snow is such a thrill, no matter how many times you do it."
**Quick Take**: Shift your mindset from winter-as-burden to winter-as-opportunity for deeper connection with nature and yourself.

### 2. Build Immune Resilience Through Natural Practices
Winter requires special attention to immune health, and this book provides accessible, natural approaches to staying well. The "bee breath" practice (Bhramari pranayama) helps balance serotonin levels and reduce stress, supporting thyroid function and immune response.

The guide includes recipes for immune-boosting teas like rosehip and hibiscus tea (containing 50% more Vitamin C than oranges) and warming winter tea with lemongrass, cinnamon, and rose hips. These remedies work preventatively rather than just treating symptoms.

> "All that begins also ends" and winter walks contain beginnings within the endings.
**Quick Take**: Daily breathing practices and herbal teas can be your first line of defense against seasonal illnesses.

### 3. Create Meaningful Self-Care Rituals
The book emphasizes that winter self-care should be intentional and ritualized rather than random. Starting with clear intentions—whether for personal health, blessing your space, or contributing to community wellbeing—gives practices deeper meaning.

Simple seven-day wellbeing rituals, like lighting candles and repeating affirmations, can transform daily self-care from obligation to sacred practice. The book provides specific breathing techniques for fatigue, joint pain, and other winter-specific ailments.

> "Today I arise on this glorious day, Under this rising sun, hear me say: I will walk in wellness in every way, For body, mind, and soul, I pray."
**Quick Take**: Intention elevates routine self-care from maintenance to meaningful spiritual practice.

### 4. Stay Active Through Winter-Appropriate Movement
Rather than fighting against winter conditions, the book shows how to work with them. Cold exposure and winter walks actually burn 34% more calories than exercise in milder weather, while providing unique mental health benefits.

The guide offers five practical approaches to staying active: morning movement routines, walking or biking to work, hourly "stop, drop, and stretch" breaks, post-lunch walks, and social activities that incorporate movement. The key is reframing exercise as self-love rather than chore.

> "The more we love ourselves, the more we thrive."
**Quick Take**: Winter movement isn't about maintaining summer fitness levels—it's about seasonal-appropriate activity that honors your body's changing needs.

### 5. Establish Supportive Daily Routines
Winter's structure provides an opportunity to implement daily routines that support wellbeing. The book suggests writing out routines and placing them in visible locations as reminders, while being flexible enough to adapt to winter's challenges.

This includes mindful eating practices, energizing morning routines, calming evening rituals, and journaling practices to manage winter-specific mental health challenges. The emphasis is on creating sustainable rhythms rather than rigid regimens.

> "The winter is a great time to put a strong daily routine into practice, as it can help you to cope with the weather and lack of sunlight, sleep better, and promote self-healing."
**Quick Take**: Use winter's natural contemplative energy to build foundational habits that will serve you year-round.

## Action Items

- Try Kinhin (mindful walking) for 10 minutes daily, focusing on breath and foot placement
- Practice "bee breath" (Bhramari pranayama) each morning to support immune health
- Make a batch of rosehip and hibiscus tea for daily immune support
- Create a simple winter wellbeing ritual with candles and affirmations
- Take a 10-minute walk outside daily, regardless of weather
- Implement hourly 2-minute movement breaks during work days
- Start a bedtime journaling practice to calm winter-active minds
- Try cold exposure through brief cold showers or winter walks
- Establish a consistent morning routine that includes movement and intention-setting
- Prepare immune-boosting tea blends to have on hand throughout the season

## Conclusion
"Winter Wellbeing" is an essential guide for anyone seeking to transform their relationship with the cold, dark months from one of endurance to one of flourishing. Rather than offering generic self-help advice, this book provides winter-specific wisdom that honors the season's unique challenges and opportunities.

The biggest reason to read this book is its holistic approach—it doesn't just address physical health or mental wellbeing in isolation, but shows how they intertwine through seasonal awareness. The practical techniques are accessible yet profound, from simple breathing exercises to meaningful ritual practices.

Whether you struggle with seasonal affective disorder, want to avoid winter illnesses, or simply seek to find more joy in the coldest season, this guide offers both immediate tools and deeper wisdom for making winter your most nourishing season of the year.
