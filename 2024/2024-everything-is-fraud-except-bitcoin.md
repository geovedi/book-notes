# Everything is Fraud, Except Bitcoin: How the Money System Enslaves Us and Why Bitcoin Sets Us Free
**Original Title**: Alles Betrug, außer Bitcoin!: Wie das Geldsystem uns versklavt und warum Bitcoin uns befreit
**Author**: Dominik Schwarzer | **Year**: 2024

## Summary
This controversial German book argues that our entire financial system is built on fraud, with inflation being the greatest theft of all. The author presents a comprehensive critique of fiat currency, central banking, and the systematic exploitation of ordinary people through monetary manipulation. Schwarzer examines various levels of fraud in society—from individual scams to corporate deception to state-level monetary manipulation—arguing that Bitcoin represents the only honest alternative that can protect individuals from this pervasive exploitation. The book serves as both an expose of financial corruption and a passionate call to monetary revolution through Bitcoin adoption.

The book is essential reading for anyone skeptical of traditional finance, those interested in Austrian economics, or individuals seeking to understand the philosophical underpinnings of the Bitcoin movement. Readers will gain a critical perspective on inflation, banking, and government control over money, along with practical insights into Bitcoin as a wealth preservation tool.

## Core Insights

### The System of Pervasive Fraud
Schwarzer argues that fraud permeates every level of society, starting with individual scams and extending to corporate and governmental exploitation. He details numerous examples, from the "grandparent trick" phone scams to corporate fraud by companies like Volkswagen and Pfizer, to demonstrate how deception has become normalized. The author suggests that this widespread fraud exists because the entire monetary system is itself fraudulent—fiat currency with its built-in inflation serves as hidden theft that normalizes other forms of deception.

> "When the system you live in is fundamentally based on hidden theft (inflation), why should a rational person refuse an available advantage?"
> _Original: "Wie kann also ein System gerecht und frei von Betrug sein, das auf einer Währung aufbaut, die von Individuen manipuliert wird?"_

**Quick Take**: The book argues that fraud begets fraud—when the monetary system itself is exploitative, it creates a culture where deception becomes normalized and rationalized at every level of society.

### Inflation as Systematic Theft
The core thesis is that inflation represents the greatest fraud of all—a systematic transfer of wealth from the poor and uninformed to the wealthy and connected. Schwarzer explains how central banks and governments manipulate the money supply to silently steal purchasing power from savers while benefiting debtors and political elites. He describes this as "light slavery" where people's time and energy are gradually siphoned away without their awareness.

> "Inflation is like a dark shadow that falls over society. Like a monster that secretly eats the time of anyone who doesn't know how to protect themselves."
> _Original: "Inflation ist wie ein dunkler Schatten, der sich über die Gesellschaft legt. Wie ein Monster, das von allen, die sich nicht zu schützen wissen, unbemerkt Zeit frisst."_

**Quick Take**: Inflation isn't just rising prices—it's a deliberate mechanism for wealth transfer that benefits those closest to money creation at the expense of ordinary savers and workers.

### Banking: The Business of Broken Trust
The book provides a scathing critique of the banking system, documenting how banks repeatedly engage in fraudulent activities while maintaining a facade of trustworthiness. Schwarzer details numerous examples of bank misconduct, including the 2008 financial crisis, money laundering for criminals, and the systematic exploitation of customers through complex financial products. He argues that fractional reserve banking is inherently fragile and fraudulent.

> "I sincerely believe that banking institutions are more dangerous than standing armies."
> _Original: "Ich glaube aufrichtig, dass Bankinstitute gefährlicher sind als stehende Heere [...]." - Thomas Jefferson_

**Quick Take**: Banks present themselves as trustworthy custodians of our money while systematically exploiting their customers through hidden fees, risky behavior with deposits, and government-enabled moral hazard.

### Bitcoin: The Technology of Truth
Schwarzer presents Bitcoin as the antidote to systematic financial fraud—an immutable, decentralized system that replaces trust with verification. He explains how Bitcoin's fixed supply of 21 million units, proof-of-work consensus, and decentralized nature make it immune to the manipulation and inflation that plague fiat currencies. The book argues that Bitcoin represents the first truly honest money in human history.

> "The main problem with conventional currencies is the trust required to make them work. You have to trust that the central bank won't devalue the currency."
> _Original: "Das Hauptproblem bei konventionellen Währungen ist das Vertrauen, das nötig ist, damit sie funktionieren. Man muss der Zentralbank vertrauen, dass sie die Währung nicht entwertet."_ - Satoshi Nakamoto

**Quick Take**: Bitcoin doesn't just offer better money—it represents a fundamental shift from systems requiring trust in human institutions to systems requiring verification through mathematical certainty.

### Money-Time: The Hidden Dimension
One of the book's most innovative concepts is "money-time"—the idea that money represents stored human time and energy. Schwarzer argues that inflation doesn't just steal purchasing power; it steals people's time and future possibilities. Bitcoin, with its fixed supply, protects this stored time from monetary debasement, representing true preservation of human effort.

> "When money equals time, and inflation is actively stealing money-time from savers and transferring it to debtors, you wonder how a society can silently accept that time is being taken from every person who saves money."
> _Original: "Wie kann es eine Gesellschaft stillschweigend akzeptieren, dass jedem Menschen, der Geld spart, Zeit genommen wird?"_

**Quick Take**: Money is stored human time—when inflation erodes the value of money, it's literally stealing portions of people's lives and future possibilities.

## Conclusion
This book is a powerful, if controversial, critique of our financial system that will resonate strongly with Bitcoin advocates and critics of central banking. Schwarzer makes a compelling case that we live in what he calls "light slavery" - a system where our time and wealth are gradually stolen through monetary inflation. While some may find his conclusions extreme, the book provides valuable insights into how monetary systems can be used for social control and wealth extraction.

The biggest reason to read this book is its comprehensive explanation of why Bitcoin matters beyond just being another investment. Schwarzer presents Bitcoin not merely as digital gold but as a fundamental tool for human freedom and protection against systematic exploitation. Whether you agree with his conclusions or not, this book will fundamentally change how you think about money, time, and power in modern society. It's essential reading for anyone seeking to understand the philosophical revolution behind Bitcoin.
