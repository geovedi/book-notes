# The Evil Eye
**Authors**: Mary Wollstonecraft Shelley | **Year**: 2015 (Original: 1827)

## Summary
Set in the rugged mountains of Albania and the coastal regions of Greece, this Gothic romance weaves a tale of supernatural superstition, vengeance, and redemption. The story follows Dmitri, a fearsome Albanian warrior known for his "Evil Eye" - a supernatural ability to harm with a glance - whose tragic past has transformed him from a gentle family man into a hardened bandit. When he becomes entangled in a plot of greed and betrayal involving his sworn brother Katusthius, the lives of innocent families are torn apart, leading to a dramatic confrontation that reveals shocking family connections.

This atmospheric tale will captivate readers who enjoy Gothic romance, historical adventure, and stories about the supernatural power of belief. It offers a compelling exploration of how trauma can transform human nature and how the bonds of family can transcend even the deepest cultural divides.

## Fast-Track Story Guide

### Essential Plot
The story begins with Katusthius Ziani, a disowned Greek merchant's son, persuading his Albanian sworn brother Dmitri to help him seize his rightful inheritance from his half-brother Cyril. Dmitri, once a refined family man turned savage bandit after losing his wife and daughter, agrees to help by kidnapping Cyril's young son Constans. However, Dmitri develops an unexpected attachment to the boy, creating tension with Katusthius. Meanwhile, Cyril and his father-in-law Camaraz (a Mainote chieftain) pursue the kidnappers across Albania. The story culminates in a dramatic confrontation where a shocking revelation emerges: Dmitri discovers that Constans is actually his grandson - the child of his own long-lost daughter Zella, whom Camaraz had once rescued but ultimately failed to save.

### Key Characters

**Dmitri of Korvo** - A fearsome Albanian bandit, once gentle and cultured, now hardened by tragedy and believed to possess supernatural evil powers that can curse with a glance.

**Katusthius Ziani** - A treacherous Greek nobleman, driven by greed and entitlement, willing to commit murder to regain his inheritance from his half-brother.

**Cyril Ziani** - A kind-hearted Greek merchant, devoted husband and father, whose peaceful life is shattered by his brother's betrayal.

**Zella** - Cyril's wife, originally from the warrior Mainote people, who suffers tremendously when her son is kidnapped.

**Camaraz** - Zella's father and chief of the Mainotes, a fierce warrior who helps his son-in-law rescue his grandson.

**Constans** - The innocent young child at the center of the conflict, whose fate determines the story's outcome.

### Emotional Core
The novel explores themes of supernatural belief, the transformative power of loss, and the unexpected ways in which family bonds can cross cultural and enemy lines. What makes this story memorable is Dmitri's journey from hardened villain to redeemed grandfather, showing how love can pierce through even the most hardened hearts created by tragedy.

## Key Moments

### The Evil Eye Strikes
When Dmitri first encounters young Constans, his supernatural presence causes the child to collapse in terror, establishing the supernatural element that drives much of the plot's tension.

### The Kidnapping
Dmitri's dramatic abduction of Constans while pretending to be a dying traveler seeking water showcases the moral complexity of his character.

### The Monastery Sanctuary
The monks who shelter Constans from both Dmitri and Katusthius represent moral goodness amid the surrounding violence and greed.

### The Mountain Confrontation
The final battle where family secrets are revealed and the truth of Constans' parentage comes to light, transforming the entire conflict.

## Questions to Consider

1. How does the supernatural belief in the "Evil Eye" serve as both a literal plot device and a metaphor for how past trauma affects present actions?

2. In what ways does the story explore the tension between cultural loyalty and universal human values like love and redemption?

3. How might Dmitri's character have been different if his daughter had never been taken from him?

4. What does the story suggest about the nature of identity and family bonds that can transcend cultural enmities?

5. How does the setting of the wild Albanian mountains contrast with the more civilized Greek coastal regions to reflect character development?

## Conclusion
This Gothic romance delivers an engaging tale of supernatural intrigue combined with profound emotional depth. While the pacing is deliberate and the language reflects early 19th-century literary style, readers who enjoy atmospheric historical fiction with complex character development will find it worthwhile. The story's greatest strength lies in Dmitri's transformation from feared villain to redeemed grandfather, showing how love can heal even the deepest wounds of betrayal and loss. At its heart, this is a story about how family bonds can overcome even the bitterest enmities and deepest cultural divides, making it a timeless exploration of redemption's transformative power.
