# Bold
**Authors**: Peter H. Diamandis, Steven Kotler | **Year**: 2015

## Summary
"Bold" is a practical playbook for entrepreneurs who want to harness exponential technologies to solve massive global problems while building extraordinary wealth. The book demonstrates how today's biggest challenges represent the biggest business opportunities, and provides frameworks for thinking at scale, leveraging crowd-powered tools, and creating exponential impact.

This guide is perfect for entrepreneurs, innovators, and leaders who want to move beyond incremental thinking and create breakthrough ventures that can genuinely impact billions of people. Readers will learn how to apply exponential thinking, build super-credible projects, and tap into the power of the crowd to accelerate their vision.

## Core Insights

### The Exponential Mindset: Goodbye Linear Thinking
The world is experiencing an asteroid-level disruption through exponential technologies—innovations that double in power at regular intervals. Like the dinosaurs that couldn't adapt to radical environmental changes, linear-thinking companies are being replaced by agile entrepreneurs who understand and leverage exponential growth. The Six Ds of exponentials (Digitalization, Deception, Disruption, Demonetization, Dematerialization, Democratization) provide a framework for understanding how industries transform.

**Key example**: Kodak invented the digital camera in 1975 but failed to recognize its exponential potential. By 2012, they were bankrupt while Instagram—a company leveraging digital photography's democratization—was acquired for $1 billion just 16 months after launch.

> "The world's biggest problems are now the world's biggest business opportunities. This means, for exponential entrepreneurs, finding a significant challenge is a meaningful road to wealth."

**Quick Take**: Either disrupt yourself or be disrupted by someone else. The choice is binary in an exponential world.

### The Five Exponential Technologies
Five key technologies are moving from deception to disruption, creating massive opportunities:

1. **Networks and Sensors**: By 2020, 50+ billion devices will be connected, generating $19 trillion in economic value
2. **Infinite Computing**: Cloud computing makes supercomputing power available to anyone
3. **Artificial Intelligence**: AI is augmenting human intelligence across every industry
4. **Robotics**: Robots are becoming cheaper, smarter, and more capable
5. **Synthetic Biology**: We can now program biology like we program computers

**Quick Take**: These technologies are converging to create unprecedented entrepreneurial possibilities.

### The Psychology of Bold: Super-Credibility
To launch bold projects, you must start above the "line of super-credibility"—where people immediately accept your idea as fact and want to get involved. This requires assembling the right team, building credibility gradually, and leveraging established authorities.

**Key strategy**: When Diamandis launched Planetary Resources (asteroid mining), he spent three years secretly building super-credibility by recruiting top NASA engineers and billionaire investors like Larry Page and Richard Branson before going public.

> "When a new idea is born above this line, you accept it immediately and say, 'Wow, that's fantastic! How can I get involved?'"

**Quick Take**: Don't rush to launch bold ideas. First build the credibility foundation that makes your success inevitable.

### Staging Toward Bold: Break It Down
Break massive visions into executable, bite-sized chunks that build momentum and credibility. Start with smaller successes that demonstrate your ability to execute, then leverage those wins for bigger opportunities.

**Example**: The authors founded the International Space University not by announcing it boldly, but by first organizing a conference to "study" its feasibility, then using that success to launch actual programs.

**Quick Take**: Moonshot thinking requires practical staging. Break the impossible into possible steps.

### The Bold Crowd: Crowdsourcing and Crowdfunding
Today's hyperconnected crowd provides unprecedented access to capital, expertise, and community. Crowdfunding platforms make it possible to raise millions without traditional venture capital, while crowdsourcing gives access to global talent pools.

**Key insight**: The crowd isn't just a source of money—it's a source of innovation, marketing, and community building that can accelerate your venture exponentially.

**Quick Take**: Your crowd is your greatest competitive advantage. Learn to leverage it effectively.
