# Financial Calculation for Treasuries: Banks and Companies
**Original Title**: Cálculo financeiro das tesourarias; Bancos e empresas
**Author**: José Roberto Securato (organizer) | **Year**: 2015 (5th edition) | **Category**: Business & Finance

## Summary
This comprehensive Brazilian finance textbook serves as the definitive "bible" for treasury operations in both banking institutions and corporations. Originally published in 1998 and now in its 5th edition, the book has evolved beyond purely treasury operations to encompass the entire Brazilian business landscape's financial calculations. It bridges the gap between academic theory and practical market application, drawing from the author's 30 years of executive experience in financial markets and 45 years as a professor.

The book addresses Brazil's unique financial environment characterized by high interest rates, persistent inflation, and sophisticated financial products that require deep mathematical understanding. It's designed for graduate students, MBA candidates, trainees, and practicing financial professionals who need precise calculation methods for daily treasury operations.

## Core Insights

### Brazilian Financial Market Fundamentals
The book provides essential understanding of Brazil's sophisticated financial market, which differs significantly from global markets due to its high interest rates, inflation history, and complex financial instruments. The Brazilian market's sophistication demands complete mastery of financial calculations beyond basic mathematics of finance.

> "Desde o lançamento da primeira edição do Cálculo financeiro das tesourarias, em 1998, o conteúdo inerente ao livro superou drasticamente o ambiente das tesourarias bancárias e das empresas, e alcançou por completo o mundo dos negócios no Brasil."
> _Original: "Since the launch of the first edition of Financial Calculation for Treasuries, in 1998, the content inherent to the book drastically surpassed the environment of banking and corporate treasuries, and completely reached the world of business in Brazil."_

**Quick Take**: Brazil's financial market requires specialized knowledge due to its unique characteristics of high rates and inflationary history.

### Interest Rate Calculations and Equivalences
The book covers various types of interest rates crucial for Brazilian financial operations: proportional rates, equivalent rates, nominal versus effective rates, and the Brazilian over rate (taxa over). Understanding these distinctions is fundamental for accurate financial calculations in the Brazilian context.

The text explains how to construct the annual over rate and details the evolution of Brazilian interest rates, including the Selic rate formation and the role of the Central Bank's Monetary Policy Committee (COPOM) in setting benchmark rates.

> "Altas taxas de juros, inflação resistente aliada ao boom da inclusão de operações financeiras em produtos bancários, e produtos ligados a meios de pagamentos e ao consumo varejista são exemplos que exigiram o domínio de conhecimentos sobre cálculo de tesourarias."
> _Original: "High interest rates, resistant inflation allied with the boom of financial operations in banking products, and products linked to payment methods and retail consumption are examples that required mastery of treasury calculation knowledge."_

**Quick Take**: Master different interest rate types to accurately calculate returns and costs in Brazilian financial operations.

### Public Debt and Treasury Securities
A significant portion covers Brazilian public debt operations, including detailed analysis of various government securities (LTN, NTN-F, NTN-B, LFT). The book explains how these instruments work, their rate formation, and practical calculation methods using current market rates.

This section, written by Anderson Caputo Delfino Silva from the National Treasury Secretariat, provides insider knowledge of how government debt functions and serves as a reference for market interest rate formation.

> "O Capítulo 9, escrito por Anderson C. D. Silva, trata da formação das taxas de juros dos títulos públicos federais. É um capítulo importante, em que teoria e prática se interligam, explorando a competência do autor como funcionário da Secretaria do Tesouro Nacional."
> _Original: "Chapter 9, written by Anderson C. D. Silva, deals with the formation of interest rates of federal public securities. It is an important chapter in which theory and practice are interconnected, exploring the author's competence as an employee of the National Treasury Secretariat."_

**Quick Take**: Government securities form the backbone of Brazil's financial market and serve as benchmarks for all other interest rate formation.

### International Operations and Foreign Exchange
The book addresses dollar-denominated operations and international market activities, explaining how international interest rates are formed and how they relate to domestic Brazilian rates. This includes understanding differentials between markets and capturing inherent risks in Brazilian securities.

### Risk Management and Value at Risk (VaR)
Final chapters focus on risk management concepts and Value at Risk (VaR) calculations, essential tools for modern treasury operations. This section provides practical frameworks for measuring and managing financial risks in both banking and corporate environments.

## Conclusion
This book is absolutely essential reading for anyone working with Brazilian finance, from banking professionals to corporate treasury managers. Its value lies in bridging theoretical knowledge with practical, market-tested calculations used daily in Brazilian financial operations. The book's reputation as the "bible" of Brazilian treasuries is well-earned, having sold over 40,000 copies despite its technical complexity.

While highly specialized, the book's practical approach and real-world examples make it accessible to readers with basic financial mathematics knowledge. The 5th edition's updates ensure relevance in Brazil's evolving financial landscape. For finance professionals operating in or with Brazil, mastering these calculations isn't just academic—it's essential for survival and success in one of the world's most sophisticated emerging financial markets.
