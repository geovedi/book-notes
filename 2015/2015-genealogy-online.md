# Genealogy Online
**Elizabeth Powell Crowe** | **Year**: 2015

## Summary
"Genealogy Online" is a comprehensive guide that transforms traditional family history research into a digital adventure. The book shows how internet technologies have revolutionized genealogy, making it possible to trace ancestors from your computer while still valuing offline archival research. It bridges the gap between traditional genealogical methods and modern digital tools, teaching readers to leverage online databases, DNA testing, social networking, and collaborative platforms to discover their heritage.

Perfect for both beginners and experienced researchers, this book provides practical strategies for overcoming genealogical "brick walls" through online resources while maintaining proper documentation and ethical research practices. Readers will learn to find elusive ancestors, connect with distant relatives, and preserve family stories using today's digital tools.

## Core Insights

### The Digital Genealogy Revolution
Online genealogy has fundamentally transformed family history research. The internet now provides access to billions of historical records, connecting researchers worldwide and making previously inaccessible documents available from home. The book emphasizes that while computers haven't replaced traditional archival research, they've dramatically expanded our research capabilities and created new ways to collaborate with other genealogists.

> "More than 20 years ago, my mother asked my brother and me, 'This Internet thing you all are always talking about: Can it help me with my genealogy?' My brother and I were already deep into online discussion groups about writing (me), archaeology (Bob), music, astronomy (both of us), and other topics. Immediately after Mother asked, I knew two things: The answer was yes and I would have to write it all down for her."

**Quick Take**: Online genealogy combines the convenience of digital research with the depth of traditional archival work, creating powerful new research possibilities.

### The Two Golden Rules: Backup and Documentation
The book emphasizes two non-negotiable principles: back up your work regularly and document everything you find. These practices are essential because technology fails, natural disasters happen, and without proper documentation, years of research can become meaningless.

> "Documentation and backup are essential. On these two principles hang all your effort and investment in genealogy. Hurricanes happen. Fire and earthquakes do, too. Software and hard drives fail for mysterious reasons. To have years of work gone with the wind is not a good feeling."

**Quick Take**: Treat your genealogy data like irreplaceable family heirlooms—because that's exactly what it is. Use multiple backup methods including cloud storage and external drives.

### Social Networking as "Cousin Bait"
Modern genealogy thrives on connection. The book teaches how to use social media, blogs, Twitter, and online forums to create "cousin bait" that attracts distant relatives who may hold missing pieces of your family puzzle. Sharing family stories, photos, and research progress online often leads to unexpected breakthroughs through collaborative connections.

**Quick Take**: Every post about your ancestors is potential bait for cousins who might have the missing pieces of your family story.

### DNA Testing: The Frontier of Family Discovery
DNA genealogy has revolutionized how we break through genealogical brick walls. The book explains the three types of DNA tests (mtDNA, YDNA, and autosomal) and how they can confirm family relationships, reveal ethnic origins, and connect with living relatives who share genetic heritage.

**Quick Take**: DNA testing provides scientific evidence to complement traditional paper research, helping to verify family connections and reveal previously unknown relationships.

### Strategic Search: Beyond Basic Google
Effective online genealogy requires sophisticated search strategies. The book teaches advanced search techniques, including using specific databases, understanding search engine algorithms, and leveraging specialized genealogy sites like FamilySearch, Ancestry.com, and Ellis Island Online to find records that basic searches miss.

**Quick Take**: The most valuable genealogical records rarely appear in basic searches—strategic, targeted searching is essential for success.

## Action Items
- Implement a robust backup system using both cloud storage and external drives
- Start documenting sources immediately—don't wait until you're overwhelmed
- Join at least one online genealogy community or mailing list related to your research areas
- Create a simple blog or social media presence to share your family history research
- Learn advanced search techniques for major genealogy databases
- Consider DNA testing from multiple companies to maximize genetic matches

## Conclusion
"Genealogy Online" is essential reading for anyone serious about family history research in the digital age. The book's greatest strength is its practical approach—teaching readers not just what websites to visit, but how to think strategically about combining online and offline research methods. The real-world examples, particularly Bill Ammons' journey from one known grandparent to an enormous family tree, demonstrate the incredible potential of digital genealogy when combined with traditional research methods.

What makes this book invaluable is its emphasis on research fundamentals—documentation, backup, and verification—while embracing cutting-edge tools and technologies. It's not just about finding names online; it's about building a verifiable family history that will stand the test of time. Whether you're just starting with genealogy or looking to break through long-standing research barriers, this book provides both the tools and the mindset needed for successful digital family history research.
