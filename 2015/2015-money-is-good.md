# Money is Good
**Authors**: Rev. Anthony K. Boakye | **Year**: 2015

## Summary
"Money is Good" is a Christian financial guide that challenges the misconception that money is inherently evil. Rev. Boakye argues that God wants His children to prosper and that wealth is a blessing when acquired through righteous means and used for God's glory. The book presents a theological framework for financial prosperity, emphasizing that God is the ultimate source of wealth and that obedience to His principles leads to abundance.

This book is perfect for Christians who struggle with guilt about wanting financial success, pastors who want to understand biblical prosperity, and anyone seeking to align their financial goals with their faith. Readers will gain a solid biblical foundation for pursuing wealth without compromising their spiritual values, practical principles for financial stewardship, and renewed confidence that God desires their prosperity.

## Core Insights

### God Wants You to Prosper
The foundational principle of the book is that poverty is not God's will for believers. God created all resources and wants His children to have abundance. The Bible repeatedly shows God blessing faithful people with wealth - Abraham, Isaac, Jacob, Joseph, Solomon, and others were all prosperous. As stated in 3 John 1:2, "Beloved, I pray that you may prosper in all things and be in health, just as your soul prospers."

> "Poverty is not good; it only leads to shame and disgrace. Money is good and that is why God provided mineral resources such as gold, silver and oil etc."
**Quick Take**: Financial prosperity is your spiritual inheritance as a child of God.

### Work Hard and Excel in Your Calling
Success comes through diligent work in whatever field God has placed you. Every job has the potential to make you rich if done with excellence and commitment. The book emphasizes that laziness leads to poverty, while hard work pays off. As Proverbs 10:4 states, "The hand of the diligent makes rich."

> "Every job is capable of making you rich â€“ all you need to do is to be committed and do it well. People usually fail to expand their jobs and opportunities."
**Quick Take**: Your current job or business is your platform for prosperity if you do it with excellence.

### Understanding the "Love of Money" Misconception
The famous phrase "money is the root of all evil" is often misquoted. The actual scripture says "the LOVE of money is the root of all evil" (1 Timothy 6:10). Money itself is neutral - it's the obsession with wealth that becomes sinful. God understands our need for money and promises to provide it when we seek His kingdom first.

> "Money is a necessity and no one can do without it in this modern world. In most cities now, one needs to pay in order to pass urine."
**Quick Take**: Money is a tool, not an idol. Use it for God's purposes rather than making it your purpose.

### The Power of Giving and Tithing
Giving activates the law of reciprocity in the spiritual realm. When you give generously to God's work and to those in need, you open yourself to receive even more blessings. The book emphasizes tithing (giving 10% of income) as a biblical principle that invites God's provision and protection.

> "Give, and it shall be given unto you, good measure, pressed down, and shaken together, and running over, shall men give into your bosom."
**Quick Take**: Your giving determines your receiving - it's God's established financial principle.

### Timing and Divine Opportunity
Understanding seasons and recognizing divine opportunities is crucial for financial success. Just as Joseph knew the season of plenty and famine, believers must discern God's timing for their financial breakthroughs. Missing divine timing can delay or even derail your prosperity.

> "Your ability to know what to do at every given time is a sure key to open doors of prosperity."
**Quick Take**: Success comes not just from hard work, but from working at the right time.

## Action Items
- Start tithing consistently - give 10% of your income to your local church
- Develop excellence in your current job or business - do it with all your might
- Create a financial plan that includes saving, investing, and giving
- Study biblical examples of prosperity and apply their principles
- Pray specifically for financial wisdom and opportunities
- Eliminate laziness and procrastination from your work habits
- Look for opportunities to bless others with your resources
- Learn to recognize and seize divine opportunities for advancement

## Questions to Consider
- What false beliefs about money have been holding you back financially?
- Are you working with excellence in your current calling?
- How can you better align your financial practices with biblical principles?
- What opportunities might you be missing due to poor timing or lack of awareness?
- Are you giving cheerfully and generously as God commands?
- Do you see money as a tool for God's purposes or an end in itself?

## Conclusion
"Money is Good" offers a refreshing biblical perspective on financial prosperity that challenges the sacred-secular divide in many Christians' thinking. Rev. Boakye successfully argues that wealth accumulation and spiritual devotion are not mutually exclusive when pursued according to God's principles.

The book's greatest strength is its extensive use of Scripture to support each principle, making it difficult to argue with its theological foundation. However, some readers might find the direct correlation between obedience and wealth overly simplistic, as it doesn't fully address the complexity of suffering believers or systemic economic barriers.

Is this worth your time? Absolutely, if you're a Christian struggling with financial guilt or seeking a biblical framework for wealth building. The biggest reason to read it is the paradigm shift it offers - from seeing money as inherently worldly to understanding it as a tool for kingdom impact. While some prosperity gospel teachings can be controversial, this book maintains a balanced approach that emphasizes hard work, wisdom, and stewardship alongside faith.
