# I Am! Be!
**Original Title**: Sois !
**Authors**: Sri Nisargadatta Maharaj | **Year**: 1973-1980 (dialogues compiled)
**Category**: Spirituality & Religion

## Summary
This profound spiritual work presents the raw, direct teachings of Sri Nisargadatta Maharaj, an enlightened master who lived as an ordinary shopkeeper in Bombay until his spiritual awakening at age 35. The book captures sixteen intimate dialogues between Maharaj and spiritual seekers from around the world, followed by the translator's personal account of time spent with the master.

At its core, "Sois !" (Be!) explores the fundamental nature of consciousness and existence. Maharaj teaches that the spontaneous appearance of the sense "I am" is the root of all experience, joy, and suffering. He guides seekers to distinguish between temporary consciousness (the "I am" feeling that comes and goes) and eternal presence (the unchanging awareness that witnesses consciousness).

This book is ideal for spiritual seekers, philosophers, and anyone questioning the nature of reality and self. It offers a radical, uncompromising path to self-realization that bypasses traditional religious dogma and cultural conditioning.

## Core Insights

### The Spontaneous Appearance of "I Am"
The sense of being, the feeling "I am," appears spontaneously without cause. Before birth, there was no experience; with the appearance of "I am," all experience begins. This consciousness is like a single ray of sunlight that instantly illuminates the entire universe of experience.

> "C'est spontanément qu'est apparue cette conscience d'être là, la constatation de ce fait : 'je suis'."
> _Original: "C'est spontanément qu'est apparue cette conscience d'être là, la constatation de ce fait : 'je suis'."_

**Quick Take**: Your being is not something you achieve—it's something you recognize as already present.

### Distinguishing Consciousness from Presence
Maharaj makes a crucial distinction between consciousness (the "I am" feeling) and presence (awareness). Consciousness is relative, comes and goes, and creates duality. Presence is absolute, eternal, and beyond the "I."

> "La conscience va et vient, la présence est toujours là."
> _Original: "La conscience va et vient, la présence est toujours là."_

**Quick Take**: You are not the temporary "I am" consciousness but the eternal presence that witnesses it.

### Transcending the "I Am"
The ultimate spiritual practice is to understand the "I am" and then transcend it. Maharaj teaches that attachment to the "I am" is the root of suffering—the "love of being" that keeps us bound to existence.

> "En fait, vous êtes toujours cet Absolu, Un, complet, total, libre, non lié à quoi que ce soit mais, malheureusement, on ne sait pourquoi, vous êtes aujourd'hui sous le joug du 'je suis'."
> _Original: "En fait, vous êtes toujours cet Absolu, Un, complet, total, libre, non lié à quoi que ce soit mais, malheureusement, on ne sait pourquoi, vous êtes aujourd'hui sous le joug du 'je suis'."_

**Quick Take**: True freedom lies in recognizing yourself as the Absolute, not as the limited "I am" consciousness.

### The Guru's Direct Pointing
Maharaj's teaching style is radically direct. He doesn't use spiritual jargon or complex philosophy but points straight to the truth: "You are the ultimate Reality, the Supreme." This transmission from his Guru awakened him spontaneously.

> "Vous êtes l'ultime Réalité, le Suprême"
> _Original: "Vous êtes l'ultime Réalité, le Suprême"_

**Quick Take**: Enlightenment isn't gradual—it's the sudden recognition of what you already are.

## Best Quotes

- "Spirituality is interested in ourselves. We appeared and the world appeared, suddenly. Why? We don't know, but nevertheless we strive to discover it."
  _Original: "La spiritualité, elle, s'intéresse à nous-même. Nous sommes apparus et le monde est apparu, soudainement. Pourquoi ? Nous ne le savons pas, mais néanmoins nous nous efforçons de le découvrir."_

- "You are nothing other than that Chit Bhaskar, this primordial luminous being, first revelation 'I am'. You don't feel it through the body or mind, suddenly it imposes itself."
  _Original: "Vous n'êtes rien d'autre que cela, Chit Bhaskar, cette êtreté primordiale, lumineuse, première révélation 'je suis'. Vous ne la ressentez ni par le corps, ni par l'esprit, soudainement elle s'impose."_

- "This little consciousness has created the entire universe, but it must be transcended."
  _Original: "C'est cette petite conscience qui a créé tout l'univers, mais il faut la transcender."_

- "The absolute principle is not at all affected by this sense of 'I am,' this being. At this moment, right here, you are not at all separated from it!"
  _Original: "En fait, le principe Absolu n'est nullement affecté par ce sens du 'je suis', cette êtreté. En ce moment, ici même, vous n'en êtes nullement séparé !"_

## Action Items
- Observe the feeling "I am" throughout your day without identifying with it
- Practice being the witness of your thoughts and experiences rather than the experiencer
- Contemplate: Who am I before the "I am" appears?
- Rest in the gaps between thoughts where presence is revealed
- Question all beliefs about who you think you are

## Questions to Consider
- Who were you before the sense "I am" appeared?
- What witnesses the coming and going of consciousness?
- If consciousness creates suffering, what lies beyond it?
- Can you find the boundary between awareness and what is aware?
- What remains when all concepts and identities are let go?

## Conclusion
"Sois !" is a powerful, no-nonsense guide to self-realization that cuts through spiritual confusion with surgical precision. Maharaj's teachings are both simple and profound, accessible yet deeply challenging.

This book is worth your time if you're ready for radical self-inquiry and not afraid of having your entire worldview dismantled. Its greatest value lies in its direct pointing approach—no gradual path, no complex techniques, just the relentless inquiry into what you truly are beyond the "I am."

For those willing to look honestly at their own experience, this book offers not just intellectual understanding but a direct taste of the freedom that lies beyond identification with consciousness. It's a spiritual atomic bomb that can explode the illusion of separate existence.
