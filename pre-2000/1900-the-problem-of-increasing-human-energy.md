# The Problem of Increasing Human Energy
**Authors**: Nikola Tesla | **Year**: 1900

## Summary

Nikola Tesla's groundbreaking essay presents a scientific and philosophical framework for accelerating human progress through the systematic increase of what he defines as "human energy." Using the mechanical formula E = ½MV², Tesla analyzes humanity as a mass moved by forces, identifying three fundamental ways to enhance our collective energy: increasing human population mass, reducing forces that retard progress, and amplifying forces that accelerate advancement. The essay combines visionary scientific predictions with practical technological solutions, many of which were decades ahead of their time. Tesla argues that the greatest problems facing humanity—food scarcity, warfare, and energy limitations—can be solved through scientific innovation and global cooperation.

This masterpiece will benefit scientists, engineers, philosophers, and anyone interested in understanding how technology can serve human progress. Readers will gain profound insights into Tesla's holistic approach to solving global challenges and his vision of a unified, peaceful world powered by clean, abundant energy.

## Core Insights

### Humanity as a Unified Energy System
Tesla presents humanity not as separate individuals but as a single, interconnected entity governed by universal mechanical laws. He argues that all human movement follows rhythmic patterns similar to planetary orbits or electrical oscillations. This understanding leads to his revolutionary insight that human progress can be measured and enhanced using scientific principles.

> "We are all one. Metaphysical proofs are, however, not the only ones which we are able to bring forth in support of this idea. Science, too, recognizes this connectedness of separate individuals, though not quite in the same sense as it admits that the suns, planets, and moons of a constellation are one body"

**Quick Take**: When we recognize humanity as a unified system rather than competing individuals, we can direct our collective energy toward shared progress rather than conflict.

### The Three Fundamental Solutions: Food, Peace, Work
Tesla identifies just three words that solve humanity's greatest challenges: food (to increase mass), peace (to reduce resistance), and work (to increase accelerating force). These solutions correspond to his Christian upbringing but take on profound scientific meaning in his framework.

> "So we find that the three possible solutions of the great problem of increasing human energy are answered by the three words: food, peace, work. These three words sound the key-notes of the Christian religion. Their scientific meaning and purpose now clear to me: food to increase the mass, peace to diminish the retarding force, and work to increase the force accelerating human movement"

**Quick Take**: Humanity's most complex problems reduce to three fundamental areas that can be addressed through systematic scientific and social progress.

### Atmospheric Nitrogen Fixation for Unlimited Food
Tesla's first major solution addresses food scarcity through his invention for oxidizing atmospheric nitrogen using high-frequency electrical discharges. This process, demonstrated with flames 60-70 feet across, would enable unlimited production of fertilizers, making abundant food available worldwide.

> "An abundance of cheap and healthful food, not artificial, but such as we are accustomed to, may thus be obtained. This new and inexhaustible source of food-supply will be of incalculable benefit to mankind, for it will enormously contribute to the increase of the human mass, and thus add immensely to human energy"

**Quick Take**: By harnessing electrical forces to combine atmospheric nitrogen with oxygen, we can create unlimited fertilizer and end food scarcity forever.

### Telautomatics and the End of Warfare
Tesla's second solution proposes using remote-controlled machines (telautomatics) to eliminate human combat. He believes this technology will transform warfare into a purely scientific exercise, eventually making war obsolete through mutual deterrence and the promotion of global understanding via wireless communication.

> "The ideal development of the war principle would ultimately lead to the transformation of the whole energy of war into purely potential, explosive energy, like that of an electrical condenser. In this form the war-energy could be maintained without effort; it would need to be much smaller in amount, while incomparably more effective"

**Quick Take**: Advanced technology can make human warfare obsolete by replacing soldiers with machines and creating global communication networks that foster understanding.

### Wireless Energy Transmission and the World System
Tesla's third solution encompasses his vision for wireless energy transmission worldwide. His World System would use the Earth itself as a conductor, enabling the transmission of power, information, and communication signals to any location without wires.

> "By its means the human voice and likeness will be reproduced everywhere and factories driven thousands of miles from waterfalls furnishing the power; aerial machines will be propelled around the earth without a stop and the sun's energy controlled to create lakes and rivers for motive purposes"

**Quick Take**: By treating the Earth as a giant electrical conductor, we can transmit power and information anywhere on the planet, eliminating energy scarcity and connecting all humanity.

## Best Quotes

- "The scientific man does not aim at an immediate result. He does not expect that his advanced ideas will be readily taken up. His duty is to lay the foundation for those who are to come, and point the way"

- "The year 2100 will see eugenics universally established. In past ages, the law governing the survival of the fittest roughly weeded out the defective, but modern humanity, by its very nature, is preventing this, and the result must be a deterioration of the race"

- "I foresee the development of a new art, or science, namely 'Telautomatics,' which is the art of controlling automatic movements and operations of distant machines without wires"

- "The human race is suffering enormously from war, and the greatest evil of all is organized warfare, which compels millions of the ablest individuals to lives of unproductivity"

- "My belief is that the telegraph, telephone, and similar means of communication will be supplemented by a much simpler, more economical, and more flexible wireless system, requiring no experts to operate"

## Conclusion

"The Problem of Increasing Human Energy" is essential reading because it offers a comprehensive framework for understanding human progress through the lens of energy and physics. Tesla's genius lies not just in his technological innovations but in his ability to weave together science, philosophy, and social vision into a coherent approach to solving humanity's greatest challenges.

The essay is worth your time primarily for its timeless insights into how systematic thinking can address seemingly intractable problems. Tesla's three-part framework—addressing population growth through food security, reducing conflict through peace-promoting technologies, and accelerating progress through energy innovation—remains remarkably relevant today.

What makes this work truly invaluable is Tesla's ability to see technology not as an end in itself but as a means for human elevation. His vision of a world connected by wireless communication, powered by clean energy, and freed from warfare continues to inspire scientists and engineers working to solve global challenges. The essay serves as both a historical document of one genius's vision and a practical guide for addressing contemporary problems through systematic, interdisciplinary thinking.

Tesla's greatest contribution may be his demonstration that the most profound technological advances come from understanding universal principles and applying them to human needs. This approach, combining fundamental science with practical application and humanistic values, offers a powerful model for addressing the complex challenges of our own time.
