# How to Get What You Want
**Authors**: Wallace D. Wattles | **Year**: 1918
**Category**: Self-Help & Personal Development

## Summary

Wallace D. Wattles presents a powerful framework for achieving success based on the principle that success is essentially the same across all fields - it's simply the attainment of whatever you desire. The book reveals that the cause of success lies within you, not in your environment or circumstances. By developing your internal power and applying specific principles, you can achieve anything you set your mind to.

This guide is perfect for ambitious individuals, entrepreneurs, and anyone feeling stuck in their current circumstances who want to understand the universal principles that govern success. Readers will learn how to develop power-consciousness, make constructive use of what they already have, and create unstoppable momentum toward their goals.

## Core Insights

### Success Comes from Within

The fundamental truth is that the cause of success is always internal - it resides within the person who succeeds, not in external circumstances. If success were determined by environment, everyone in similar situations would achieve similar results, but we see vast differences in success levels even among people in identical circumstances. Success is essentially the same whether you seek health, wealth, or position - it's simply attainment without regard to what is attained.

> "It is, therefore, mathematically certain that you can succeed if you will find out the cause of success, and develop it to sufficient strength, and apply it properly to your work."

**Quick Take**: Your success is your personal responsibility. Stop blaming circumstances and start building your internal power to achieve what you want.

### Develop Power-Consciousness

The key difference between successful and unsuccessful people lies in their ability to use their faculties effectively. This ability comes from developing Power-Consciousness - a state where you absolutely know that you can do what you want to do and know how to do it. When you're in full Power-Consciousness, every thought becomes a successful thought and every action becomes a successful action.

Wattles recommends a specific mental exercise: practice thinking quietly about your subconscious mind, hold this consciousness, and repeat with deep feeling: "I CAN succeed! All that is possible to any one is possible to me. I AM successful. I do succeed, for I am full of the Power of Success."

> "Power-Consciousness is what you feel when you know that you can do a thing; and you know HOW to do the thing."

**Quick Take**: Impress your subconscious mind with certainty about your capabilities through consistent affirmations and emotional reinforcement.

### Make Perfect Use of What You Have

To get more, you must make constructive use of what you currently have. The universal law of advancement states that life advances from one plane to another by perfecting function on the lower plane. Whenever an organism contains more life than it can express by functioning perfectly on its own plane, it begins to perform the functions of the next higher plane.

This means doing perfectly all that you can do now, where you are now. When you put enough of yourself into your present work to do it perfectly, your surplus power will extend your work into a larger field. Only by making the most complete use of your present environment will you be placed in a more desirable one.

> "Get into a business which will use your strongest faculties, even if you must commence at the bottom; then develop those faculties to the utmost."

**Quick Take**: Master your current position completely before seeking advancement. Excellence in your present role creates the foundation for future success.

### Put Surplus Life Into Every Relation

Success requires putting more than enough life into every relationship - business, domestic, or social. In business transactions, go beyond merely giving fair value; put genuine interest and life into each interaction. When you can make every customer feel that you're honestly trying to advance their interests as well as your own, your business will grow organically.

This principle applies to all areas of life - be a good spouse, parent, and friend. Failure in personal relationships can prevent your business success from manifesting because it makes you incapable of using your success for the advancement of life. Every relation must be a stepping stone to what you want in the future.

> "Put into every relation, business, domestic or social, more than enough life to fill that relation."

**Quick Take**: Approach every interaction with genuine care and interest in others' welfare. Your success depends on your ability to enrich every relationship.

### Hold a Clear Mental Picture of Your Goal

Get a clear mental picture of what you want and keep it constantly in your consciousness, like a picture on the wall of your room. The more clear and definite your concept, the easier your work will be. Hold this picture in the background of your mind night and day, letting it guide your decisions and actions.

Even if you don't have the resources or environment you want now, you can begin moving toward them. Remember that you move forward only by doing what you can in your present environment. Your current situation is just one department of the success you're building - fix your mind on the ultimate goal and begin assimilating the rest.

> "Know what you want, and keep the picture of it in the background of your mind night and day."

**Quick Take**: Maintain a vivid, constant vision of your ultimate goal while focusing your daily actions on perfect performance in your current role.

## Best Quotes

- "Getting what you want is success; and success is an effect, coming from the application of a cause. Success is essentially the same in all cases; the difference is in the things the successful people want, but not in the success."

- "Power-Consciousness is what you feel when you know that you can do a thing; and you know HOW to do the thing."

- "When you put enough of yourself into your present work to do it perfectly, your surplus power will extend your work into a larger field."

- "Form a clear mental picture of what you want; hold the purpose to get it; do everything perfectly, not in a servile spirit, but because you are a master mind; keep unwavering faith in your ultimate attainment of your goal, and you cannot fail to move forward."

- "It is a law in nature that like causes always produce like effects; therefore, since success is the same in all cases, the cause of success must be the same in all cases."

## Action Items

- Practice the Power-Consciousness affirmation exercise multiple times daily, especially before sleep
- Identify and develop your strongest natural faculties for your chosen field
- Focus on perfect performance in your current role rather than dreaming about future opportunities
- Put genuine interest and care into every business and personal interaction
- Create and maintain a clear, detailed mental picture of your ultimate goal
- Always seek the very best that can be obtained at the present time, never settling for less

## Questions to Consider

- What are my strongest natural faculties, and am I currently using them in my work?
- Where in my current life am I only giving partial effort instead of perfect performance?
- Do I truly believe, at both conscious and subconscious levels, that I can achieve what I want?
- Are my daily interactions adding genuine value to others, or am I just going through the motions?
- What clear mental picture do I hold of my ultimate success?

## Conclusion

This book is absolutely worth your time if you're ready to take complete responsibility for your success. Wattles provides a timeless framework that shifts the focus from external circumstances to internal power development. The biggest reason to read this book is that it offers not just theory, but practical methods for developing the mindset and habits that create success.

While the language is early 20th century, the principles are universal and immediately applicable. The book's power lies in its mathematical certainty about success - if you follow the principles, you cannot fail to advance. It's a masterclass in understanding that success isn't about luck or circumstances, but about systematic internal development and perfect application of universal laws.
