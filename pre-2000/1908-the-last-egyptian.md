# The Last Egyptian
**Authors**: L. Frank Baum | **Year**: 1908
**Category**: Fiction & Literature

## Summary
"The Last Egyptian" tells the story of Kara, a young man of royal Egyptian descent who claims to be the last living heir to the ancient pharaohs. Living in poverty with his grandmother Hatatcha near the Nile, Kara encounters Gerald Winston, an English Egyptologist and amateur archaeologist who becomes fascinated by Kara's claims of royal lineage and hidden treasures. The narrative unfolds as a complex web of romance, revenge, and ancient Egyptian mysteries.

The story explores themes of cultural collision between East and West, the weight of ancestral heritage, and the destructive nature of revenge. As Kara manipulates those around him to reclaim what he believes is his birthright, he becomes entangled in a romantic pursuit of Aneth, an English noblewoman, while simultaneously seeking vengeance against those who wronged his family line.

This historical romance combines elements of adventure, archaeological mystery, and social commentary, making it appealing to readers who enjoy turn-of-the-century exotic fiction with strong character-driven plots and atmospheric Egyptian settings.

## Core Insights

### The Royal Prince's Quest for Restoration
Kara represents the tragic figure of fallen nobility clinging to ancestral glory. As a descendant of the pharaoh Ahtka-Ra, he believes himself entitled to rule modern Egypt, despite centuries having passed since his family held power. His character embodies the tension between ancient traditions and modern realities, showing how heritage can be both a source of pride and a burden that prevents adaptation to contemporary life.

> "It is no comfortable thing to be Kara, the lineal descendant of the great Ahtka-Ra, in the days when Egypt's power is gone, and her children are scorned by the Arab Muslims and buffeted by the English Christians."

**Quick Take**: The story examines how clinging to past glories without practical wisdom leads to self-destruction.

### The Power of Hidden Knowledge
Hatatcha, Kara's grandmother, possesses secret knowledge of ancient Egyptian tombs and treasures that could make them wealthy. However, her refusal to share this information creates tension and demonstrates how wisdom becomes useless when hoarded rather than shared. The novel explores the theme that knowledge without action or generosity serves no practical purpose.

> "Treasures may be kept in a rotten chest."

**Quick Take**: True value lies not in possessing knowledge, but in how it's used to improve one's circumstances and help others.

### The Destructive Nature of Revenge
Kara's elaborate schemes for revenge against those he perceives as wronging his family ultimately consume him. His manipulation of Aneth through blackmail and his pursuit of Winston's downfall demonstrate how revenge becomes a self-perpetuating cycle of destruction that harms the avenger more than the intended targets.

**Quick Take**: Revenge is a poison that destroys the soul from within, making it impossible to find genuine happiness or connection.

### Cultural Collision and Misunderstanding
The novel portrays the complex interactions between English colonials and Egyptians in the early 20th century. Winston's archaeological curiosity, Kara's resentment of foreign presence, and Aneth's naive fascination with Egyptian exoticism all reflect the broader tensions of cultural imperialism and misunderstanding that characterized the era.

**Quick Take**: Cross-cultural relationships require genuine respect and understanding, not romanticized stereotypes or colonial attitudes.

### The Illusion of Magical Protection
Kara's belief in the "Stone of Fortune" talisman from his ancestor represents the psychological reliance on superstition when facing overwhelming obstacles. The loss of this supposed protection becomes a metaphor for how we must ultimately rely on our own capabilities rather than external charms or inherited advantages.

**Quick Take**: Real security comes from personal growth and practical skills, not from magical thinking or inherited objects.

## Takeaways

- **Heritage versus Reality**: Distinguish between pride in ancestral heritage and the practical requirements of living in the present world

- **Wealth Perspective**: True richness comes from wisdom and relationships, not from material possessions or inherited status

- **Cross-Cultural Respect**: Approach other cultures with genuine curiosity rather than romanticized stereotypes or colonial attitudes

- **Letting Go of Revenge**: Recognize how revenge cycles perpetuate suffering and prevent personal growth

- **Practical Wisdom**: Knowledge becomes valuable only when applied thoughtfully and shared appropriately

## Questions to Consider

2. In what ways does the novel critique colonial attitudes while still indulging in some exotic stereotypes of its era?

3. How might the story have been different if Hatatcha had shared her knowledge earlier rather than hoarding it?

4. What does the novel suggest about the relationship between ancient traditions and modern progress?

5. How do the romantic relationships in the story reflect broader cultural tensions between Egypt and England?

## Conclusion

"The Last Egyptian" is worth your time if you enjoy historical fiction with exotic settings and complex character studies. The novel excels in its atmospheric portrayal of Egypt during the colonial era and its exploration of cultural tensions. However, some readers may find the pacing slow and the resolution somewhat abrupt.

The biggest reason to read this book is its fascinating character study of Kara, who embodies the tragedy of someone caught between ancient glory and modern reality. Baum, better known for his Oz books, demonstrates considerable skill in creating a psychologically complex anti-hero whose descent into revenge-driven madness feels both inevitable and tragic.

While the novel reflects some of the racial and cultural attitudes of its time that modern readers may find problematic, it remains a compelling exploration of heritage, identity, and the destructive power of revenge. The archaeological mystery elements and Egyptian setting provide rich atmosphere that makes the journey worthwhile.
