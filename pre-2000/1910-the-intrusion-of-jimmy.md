# The Intrusion of Jimmy
**Original Title**: The Intrusion of Jimmy
**Authors**: P.G. Wodehouse | **Year**: 1910
**Category**: Fiction & Literature

## Summary
"The Intrusion of Jimmy" is a delightful comedic novel that follows the adventures of Jimmy Pitt, a wealthy young American who makes a drunken bet that he can successfully burglarize a house. What begins as a reckless wager evolves into a series of farcical complications when Jimmy inadvertently breaks into the home of Police Captain McEachern. The plot thickens when Jimmy falls in love with McEachern's daughter Molly, setting up a classic Wodehouse conflict between romance, deception, and the inevitable comedic resolution.

This early Wodehouse work showcases his masterful blend of romance, humor, and intricate plotting. Jimmy's attempts to maintain his disguise as a master burglar while navigating his feelings for Molly create hilarious situations that only Wodehouse could orchestrate with such precision and wit.

## The Fast-Track Story Guide

### The Bet That Changes Everything
The story opens at the Strollers' Club where Jimmy Pitt, recently returned from Europe and newly wealthy, drunkenly claims he could successfully burglarize a house. This arrogant boast leads to a bet that sets the entire plot in motion. Jimmy, accompanied by a young Bowery thief named Spike Mullins, sets out to prove his burglarizing prowess.

> "Any man of ordinary intelligence could break into a house."

### The Wrong House, Right Girl
In a twist of fate, Jimmy and Spike break into the home of Police Captain McEachern. Instead of facing arrest, Jimmy's bold claim that he's a master burglar from London intrigues the corrupt police captain. The situation becomes deliciously complicated when Jimmy meets and instantly falls for McEachern's beautiful daughter, Molly.

### Love and Deception
Jimmy finds himself in an increasingly tangled web of lies as he pretends to be a master criminal while genuinely falling for Molly. McEachern, seeing potential profit in associating with a supposedly skilled burglar, facilitates Jimmy's presence in his life, unaware of Jimmy's true identity and growing feelings for his daughter.

### The Resolution
The story culminates in a series of comedic revelations where Jimmy's true identity is revealed. Despite the deception, love triumphs, and Jimmy and Molly end up together. The final scene shows Jimmy saying farewell to Spike Mullins, who is returning to America, revealing that the supposedly valuable necklace they once considered stealing was only worth $100.

## Key Takeaways
- **The danger of drunken boasts**: Jimmy's entire adventure stems from a reckless bet made while intoxicated
- **Honesty in relationships**: The story highlights how deception, even when well-intentioned, complicates genuine connections
- **Appearances vs. reality**: Both Jimmy and McEachern present false fronts that drive the plot's humorous conflicts
- **Wealth doesn't guarantee wisdom**: Jimmy's inheritance enables his adventure but doesn't prevent his foolish decisions

## Questions to Consider
- How does Jimmy's privileged background influence his decision to pursue the burglary bet?
- What does McEachern's willingness to associate with supposed criminals reveal about his character?
- How does Wodehouse use the burglary plot to explore themes of class and social mobility?
- Why do you think Spike Mullins remains loyal to Jimmy throughout their adventures?

## Conclusion
"The Intrusion of Jimmy" is a charming early example of Wodehouse's comedic genius that showcases his signature blend of romance, farce, and social satire. While not as polished as his later Jeeves and Wooster masterpieces, this novel demonstrates Wodehouse's developing talent for creating intricate plots populated by memorable characters.

The book is definitely worth your time if you enjoy:
- Classic British humor with American settings
- Romantic comedies with clever plot twists
- Stories where good-hearted but foolish protagonists navigate tricky situations
- Wodehouse's distinctive witty prose style

The biggest reason to read it is to witness the emergence of one of the 20th century's greatest comic writers perfecting his craft. The novel's premise—rich boy pretends to be burglar to win girl— perfectly encapsulates Wodehouse's ability to find humor in the absurd situations that arise when social conventions are playfully subverted.
