# The Five of Us in Rome
**Original Title**: Wij met ons vijven in Rome
**Authors**: Tine Cool (C.A. Cool), edited by Thomas Cool | **Year**: 1928
**Category**: Art & Creativity 

## Summary
"The Five of Us in Rome" is a charming memoir that recounts the four-year journey (1892-1896) of the Cool family—Dutch artist Thomas Cool, his wife Berber, and their three children—as they leave behind the comforts of Friesland for the artistic bohemia of Rome. Living in the Villa Strohl-Fern, an artists' colony, the family experiences both the triumphs and struggles of an artist pursuing his calling versus commercial success. The narrative captures the golden era of European art at the fin de siècle, when Rome was a magnet for international artists seeking inspiration among ancient ruins and Renaissance masterpieces.

Readers interested in art history, family memoirs, and cultural travel will find themselves transported to late 19th-century Rome, where they'll discover the challenges of artistic dedication, family resilience in the face of hardship, and the transformative power of living abroad. The book offers a unique child's perspective on becoming an artist, making it valuable for anyone interested in creative development and the intersection of family life with artistic ambition.

## Core Insights

### The Artist's Calling vs. Commercial Reality
The central conflict revolves around Thomas Cool's decision to abandon his family's successful marble business to pursue painting. His father's ultimatum—"the business or the art"—leads to a modest annual allowance that enables the family's Roman adventure. This tension between artistic integrity and financial security resonates throughout their journey, as Cool creates monumental canvases of Roman architecture that few can afford or display in their homes.

> "Rembrandt opened our eyes to what deserves to be painted. He taught us his effects of light and shades of brown. I go further, for me it are the phenomena in nature, and the dramatic sentiment with which I see things."
> _Original: "Rembrandt heeft ons door zijn werk de oogen geopend voor 't geen schilderachtig is. Hij leert ons wat zijn licht- en bruineffecten zijn. Ik ga verder, voor mij zijn het phenomena in de natuur en het dramatisch sentiment waarmede ik de dingen zie."_

**Quick Take**: True artistic vision requires complete dedication, even when it means choosing poverty over comfort and creation over commerce.

### The Villa Strohl-Fern: An Artists' Sanctuary
The family's home in Villa Strohl-Fern represents more than just lodging—it's a vibrant international community where artists from various nations live, work, and collaborate. Founded by philanthropist Alfred Wilhelm Strohl-Fern, the villa provides studios with north light and a garden environment that fosters creativity. This community becomes the children's playground and classroom, exposing them to diverse cultures and artistic philosophies.

> "In the Villa, it is now so beautiful and populated by artists of various nations. They come and go, each viewing the world with their own country's character, each a component of the forces that work upon them."
> _Original: "In de Villa, het thans zo schoon is en bevolkt door artiesten van velerlei naties. Zij komen en gaan, ieder met zijn eigen landaard de wereld aanschouwend, ieder Cervella een composante der krachten die op hen inwerkte."_

**Quick Take**: Creative environments that bring together diverse artistic perspectives can transform not only individual artists but entire families.

### Family Resilience Through Artistic Struggle
The memoir reveals how the Cool family turns financial hardship into adventure. When money runs low, they forage for artichokes in the Roman countryside. When illness strikes one of the children, the community rallies around them. The children—Tine (the narrator), her brothers Gerrit and Dientje—grow up with an unusual education that includes sketching in ruins, learning multiple languages, and observing the artistic process firsthand.

**Quick Take**: Challenges faced in pursuit of a meaningful life become transformative experiences that strengthen family bonds and build character.

### The Transformative Power of Rome
Rome itself emerges as a central character—the ancient ruins, Renaissance masterpieces, and vibrant street life provide endless inspiration for both the artist and his family. Thomas Cool spends countless hours in the Pantheon, studying light effects, and wanders the city at night with his friend Wilhelm Kumm, sketching churches and monuments. The city's layers of history and beauty become their classroom and sanctuary.

> "I make never what I see, but that which my eyes transport to higher life."
> _Original: "Ik maak nooit wat ik zie, maar 't geen mijn oogen vervoert tot hooger leven."_

**Quick Take**: Immersion in environments rich with history and beauty can elevate both artistic vision and human consciousness.

## Key Takeaways

- **Follow your artistic calling even when it means sacrificing financial security** – Thomas Cool's dedication to his vision despite commercial challenges
- **Immerse children in creative environments** – The Cool children grew up with art as their natural habitat, leading to lifelong appreciation
- **Find community among fellow creators** – Villa Strohl-Fern provided essential support and inspiration for artistic development
- **Use hardship as creative fuel** – Financial constraints led to innovative solutions and memorable family adventures
- **Value cultural exchange and international perspective** – The diverse artists' community enriched the family's worldview

## Questions to Consider

- What would you sacrifice to pursue your true calling, and how would your family adapt to that choice?
- How does environment shape artistic development, and what modern equivalents exist to the artists' colonies of the past?
- In today's world, where do we find the balance between artistic integrity and commercial viability?
- How can parents create environments that foster creativity while maintaining stability?
- What role should community play in supporting artistic families and individual creators?

## Conclusion
"The Five of Us in Rome" is more than a charming family memoir—it's a timeless exploration of what it means to live an art-centered life. The Cool family's Roman adventure demonstrates that following one's artistic vision, while challenging, can provide immeasurable richness and meaning. Tine Cool's child's-eye view captures both the magic and difficulty of growing up in a household where creation takes precedence over convention.

This book is absolutely worth your time if you're interested in art history, family dynamics, or the transformative power of living abroad. Its biggest strength lies in showing how artistic dedication can shape not just one life but an entire family's worldview. The memoir reminds us that the most meaningful adventures often begin when we choose passion over practicality and create community around shared values. In an age where commercial success often dictates creative choices, the Cool family's story stands as a testament to the enduring value of art as a way of life.
