# 1920: The War with White Poland. Pilsudski's Campaign in Ukraine
**Original Title**: 1920. Война с белополяками. Поход Пилсудского на Украину
**Authors**: Nikolai Kakurin, Vladimir Melikov | **Year**: 1925

## Summary
This comprehensive military analysis examines the Soviet-Polish War of 1919-1920, focusing on Pilsudski's decisive campaign against Soviet Ukraine in 1920. Written by two former Imperial Russian officers who served in both anti-Bolshevik and Red armies, the book provides unique insider perspectives on this pivotal conflict that shaped Eastern European borders for decades. The authors combine detailed operational analysis with candid assessments of military leadership, logistics, and strategic decisions on both sides.

This work is essential reading for military historians, students of early Soviet history, and anyone interested in understanding the complex dynamics that defined post-WWI Eastern Europe. Readers gain unprecedented access to Soviet military thinking, the challenges of managing multi-front warfare, and the brutal realities of fighting across Ukraine's vast terrain.

## Core Insights

### Strategic Context and Prelude to War
The book masterfully explains how the Soviet-Polish conflict emerged from the collapse of German and Austro-Hungarian power in Eastern Europe. As Polish forces under Józef Piłsudski pushed eastward to restore historical borders, Soviet forces moved westward with revolutionary fervor. The authors reveal how both sides initially viewed the Polish front as secondary – Poles focused on capturing Lviv from Ukrainians, while Soviets prioritized defeating White armies under Kolchak and Denikin.

> "The authors considered that the Red Army, conscious of its strength and might, has no need to conceal those mistakes that could have taken place and undoubtedly did take place in the activities of any army, for only he who does nothing makes no mistakes."
> _Original: "Авторы считали, что Красная армия в сознании своей силы и мощи не нуждается в сокрытии тех промахов, которые смогли иметь место и, безусловно, имеют место в деятельности каждой армии, ибо не ошибается только тот, кто ничего не делает."_

**Quick Take**: The conflict demonstrates how revolutionary ideology collided with traditional imperial ambitions in a power vacuum created by World War I's aftermath.

### Pilsudski's Master Plan
The book details Piłsudski's brilliant strategic concept: a powerful, rapid strike from the Novograd-Volynsky region designed to split Red forces and separate the 12th and 14th armies. The plan relied heavily on cavalry, particularly General Rómer's consolidated cavalry division tasked with capturing the critical railway junction at Kazatin. This maneuver warfare approach exploited the vast Ukrainian terrain and Red Army's overextended supply lines.

**Quick Take**: Polish success depended on mobility, surprise, and exploiting internal divisions within Red ranks, particularly the unreliable Galician units.

### Soviet Response and Leadership Challenges
The authors provide candid analysis of Soviet command problems, including coordination issues between Western and Southwestern fronts. They detail how Commander Tukhachevsky attempted to concentrate forces for decisive action while dealing with limited resources and multiple threats. The arrival of Budyonny's Konarmia (1st Cavalry Army) from the Caucasus became the Soviet counterstroke – a force that would demonstrate both the potential and limitations of mobile warfare.

**Quick Take**: Soviet command adapted slowly but showed remarkable resilience in crisis, though internal conflicts between military and political leadership undermined effectiveness.

### The Kiev Operation and Its Aftermath
The book provides detailed analysis of the April 1920 Polish offensive that captured Kiev, examining how simultaneous Galician Army mutinies and Ukrainian partisan activities created perfect conditions for Polish success. The authors describe the chaotic Soviet retreat and the difficult process of reorganizing shattered formations for counteroffensive operations.

**Quick Take**: The Kiev campaign demonstrates how military operations intersect with political instability and national liberation movements in complex borderlands.

### Logistics and the Challenges of Ukrainian Terrain
Perhaps the book's most valuable contribution is its frank assessment of logistical difficulties. The authors detail how inadequate railways, poor roads, and partisan activity crippled Soviet supply operations. They contrast Soviet reliance on local resources with Polish access to French military support and better rail infrastructure.

**Quick Take**: The war proves that logistics often determine victory more than battlefield brilliance, especially across Ukraine's vast distances and limited infrastructure.

### The Battle of Warsaw and Strategic Turning Points
The authors analyze the climactic Battle of Warsaw (the "Miracle on the Vistla"), explaining how Soviet overextension, coordination failures between fronts, and Polish intelligence breakthroughs led to decisive defeat. They examine how this setback fundamentally altered Eastern Europe's strategic balance and forced Soviet leaders to reevaluate revolutionary export strategies.

**Quick Take**: Warsaw demonstrated the limits of military power when political objectives outpace military capabilities and strategic intelligence.

## Key Takeaways
- **Multi-Front Warfare**: Soviet difficulties coordinating between Western and Southwestern fronts created critical vulnerabilities that Polish forces exploited repeatedly
- **Cavalry's Last Role**: This war represented one of history's last major campaigns where traditional cavalry played decisive operational roles
- **National Liberation Complex**: The conflict intertwined conventional warfare with Ukrainian independence movements, creating a volatile mix of military and political objectives
- **Logistics Determines Strategy**: Both sides' operational plans were heavily constrained by railway capacity, supply availability, and terrain challenges
- **Intelligence Value**: Polish success at Warsaw demonstrates how superior intelligence can offset numerical disadvantages

## Questions to Consider
1. How did revolutionary ideology influence Soviet military doctrine and strategic decision-making compared to traditional Polish military thinking?
2. In what ways did the multi-ethnic nature of Ukraine affect military operations for both sides?
3. How might different coordination between Soviet Western and Southwestern fronts have changed the campaign's outcome?
4. What lessons does this conflict offer about conducting military operations in politically complex borderlands?
5. How did the war demonstrate the transition from World War I trench warfare to more mobile operational concepts?

## Conclusion
This work provides unparalleled insights into one of the 20th century's most consequential but understudied conflicts. The authors' unique backgrounds – serving in multiple armies during this turbulent period – give them extraordinary perspective on both military and political dimensions of the Soviet-Polish struggle.

**Is this worth your time?** Absolutely. The book offers rare access to Soviet military self-analysis from the 1920s, before Stalinist censorship hardened historical narratives. Military professionals will appreciate the detailed operational analysis and frank discussions of command problems. History buffs will gain new understanding of how Eastern Europe's borders were determined through blood and iron rather than rational negotiation.

**What's the biggest reason to read it?** The book's value lies in its honesty – the authors freely admit Soviet mistakes while providing sophisticated analysis of Polish military successes. This rare combination of insider access and critical reflection makes it an essential source for understanding not just this war, but the nature of military operations in politically complex environments.
