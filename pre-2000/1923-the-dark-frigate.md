# The Dark Frigate
**Authors**: Charles Boardman Hawes | **Year**: 1923
**Category**: Children & Young Adult

## Summary
"The Dark Frigate" tells the story of Philip Marsham, a young man born to the sea who loses his father in a shipwreck and flees London after accidentally causing chaos in a tavern. His journey takes him from the countryside back to his maritime roots when he joins the crew of the "Rose of Devon," a frigate bound for Newfoundland. The story captures the dangers and adventures of 17th-century seafaring life, including storms, piracy, and the constant threat of betrayal.

This coming-of-age adventure follows Philip's transformation from a grieving orphan to a capable sailor who must navigate the treacherous waters of both the literal seas and human nature. The novel immerses readers in the authentic language and customs of 17th-century maritime England, offering a thrilling glimpse into a world of courage, danger, and honor.

**Perfect for**: Young adults and historical fiction enthusiasts who love authentic maritime adventures, coming-of-age stories, and tales of courage on the high seas. Readers will gain appreciation for 17th-century seafaring life and the enduring values of loyalty and honor.

## Core Insights

### From Orphan to Sailor
Philip Marsham's journey begins when his father Tom Marsham, master of a London ketch, is lost at sea during a storm off North Foreland. The nineteen-year-old Philip, recovering from fever in Moll Stevens' alehouse, accidentally fires a loaded gun, causing chaos and forcing him to flee for his life. This incident launches his transformation from land-dwelling orphan to seasoned mariner.

> "Philip Marsham was bred to the sea as far back as the days when he was cutting his milk teeth, and he never thought he should leave it; but leave it he did, once and again, as I shall tell you."

**Quick Take**: Sometimes our greatest misfortunities become the catalyst for finding our true calling.

The journey tests Philip through storms, pirate attacks, and the constant threat of death. The sea becomes both adversary and teacher, forcing him to develop courage, resourcefulness, and leadership. His experiences reflect the brutal reality of 17th-century maritime life where survival depended on skill, luck, and the ability to adapt quickly.

**Quick Take**: True character is revealed not in calm waters, but in how we navigate the storms that threaten to overwhelm us.

## Key Plot Points
- Philip loses his father at sea and accidentally causes chaos in a tavern, forcing him to flee London
- He eventually finds his way back to maritime life by joining the crew of the "Rose of Devon"
- The journey includes encounters with storms, potential piracy, and various maritime dangers
- Philip grows from a grieving orphan to a capable sailor and leader
- The story concludes with Philip, now Captain Marsham, finding the "Rose of Devon" again and sailing to Barbados

## Conclusion
"The Dark Frigate" is a masterful work of historical fiction that earned the Newbery Medal for its authentic portrayal of 17th-century maritime life. Hawes drew from actual historical documents and maritime records to create a world that feels both historically accurate and emotionally compelling. The novel excels not just in its thrilling adventure elements but in its deep understanding of human nature under pressure.

This book is absolutely worth your time if you appreciate meticulously researched historical fiction, coming-of-age stories, or tales of maritime adventure. The biggest reason to read it is Hawes' exceptional ability to bring 17th-century seafaring to vivid life while exploring timeless themes of courage, honor, and finding one's place in the world. The authentic period language and detailed maritime knowledge may challenge some readers, but those who persist will find themselves richly rewarded by a story that stands as a high point of children's historical fiction.
