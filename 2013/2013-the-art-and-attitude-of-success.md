# The Art and Attitude of Success
**Authors**: Emmanuel Smith | **Year**: 2013 | **Category**: Self-Help & Personal Development

## Summary
"The Art and Attitude of Success" is a Christian-focused self-help book that guides readers through a systematic process of personal transformation and goal achievement. The author, who shares his own experiences of overcoming poverty, homelessness, and hardship, combines practical goal-setting principles with biblical teachings to help readers reclaim their lives and achieve their God-given potential.

The book presents a 10-step journey from liberation and detoxification to vision creation, emphasizing that success requires both divine guidance and personal action. It's designed for readers who feel lost, stuck, or fed up with being "losers" and are ready to take decisive action to change their circumstances.

This book is perfect for those seeking a faith-based approach to personal development, particularly Christians who feel called to achieve more but are held back by past mistakes, negative influences, or lack of direction. Readers will gain a practical framework for self-assessment, goal-setting, and creating a life-changing vision that aligns with their spiritual values.

## Core Insights

### Liberate Yourself: Stop Walking the Wrong Road
The first step to success is recognizing when you're lost and having the courage to stop. You become lost by following the wrong roadmap, pursuing others' destinations instead of your own, or wandering aimlessly without clear direction. The symptoms include dreading Mondays, deteriorating health, relationship problems, and a persistent sense that life isn't going as expected.

> "What this book contains will have no more impact on than you permit it."
**Quick Take**: Success begins with honest self-assessment and the willingness to admit you're heading in the wrong direction.

### Detoxify Yourself: Burn Your Burdens
Conduct a thorough life audit by listing your wrong decisions, bad influences, and quantifiable failures. The crucial step is to literally burn these lists as a symbolic act of self-forgiveness. This ritual releases you from guilt and shame, creating space for a new beginning without the old burdens weighing you down.

> "By that symbolic act, resolve to forgive yourself all those mistakes and errors. Watch them burn before you. They are now gone, reduced to ashes."
**Quick Take**: True transformation requires both acknowledging your past mistakes and consciously releasing their emotional hold on you.

### Invigorate Yourself: Remove the Props
Many people rely on temporary "props" - unprofitable relationships, bad habits, false friends, or unnecessary expenses - that support them but prevent real growth. Like construction props that should be removed once a structure can stand on its own, these crutches must be eliminated for authentic strength and independence to develop.

> "Do you know that you also have the power to speak life into your problem situations?"
**Quick Take**: Real strength comes from removing dependencies, not adding more supports to a weak foundation.

### Get a Vision, Not Just Goals
While goals are personal and short-term, a vision is divinely inspired and far-reaching. A vision is God's program for your life that affects others beyond your immediate circle. Unlike self-serving goals, a genuine vision comes from God, connects to your equipped field, and ultimately serves to bless others while securing your legacy.

**Quick Take**: The most powerful achievements aren't goals you set for yourself, but visions you receive from a higher purpose.

## Action Items

- **Stop and Assess**: Immediately halt any activities, relationships, or situations that are keeping you from your desired destination
- **Conduct a Life Audit**: Make detailed lists of wrong decisions, bad influences, and failures, then burn them as a symbolic act of release
- **Identify Your Props**: Recognize and eliminate temporary supports that prevent you from developing genuine strength and independence
- **Develop Your Skills**: Take inventory of your current abilities and determine what new skills you need to achieve your goals
- **Create a Vision**: Instead of setting goals, seek a God-given vision that extends beyond personal success to benefit others
- **Establish New Routines**: Replace old patterns with daily practices that support your vision and maintain spiritual focus
- **Prepare for Obstacles**: Anticipate barriers to your success and create contingency plans for overcoming them

## Questions to Consider

- What road have you been traveling, and is it leading to your intended destination?
- Which "props" in your life are preventing you from developing authentic strength?
- How would you want to be remembered after you're gone?
- What skills has God already equipped you with for your vision?
- Are you treating symptoms rather than addressing root causes in your life?
- What would you attempt if you knew you couldn't fail?

## Conclusion
"The Art and Attitude of Success" offers a straightforward, faith-based roadmap for personal transformation that stands out for its emphasis on both divine guidance and practical action. The book's greatest strength is its author's authenticity - having experienced extreme hardship himself, his advice comes from genuine experience rather than theoretical knowledge.

The step-by-step approach from liberation to vision creation provides a comprehensive framework that addresses both spiritual and practical aspects of personal development. While the Christian perspective may not resonate with all readers, those comfortable with faith-based approaches will find valuable insights on overcoming past mistakes, breaking negative cycles, and creating a purpose-driven life.

This book is worth your time if you're ready for honest self-assessment and decisive action. It's particularly valuable for those who feel stuck in negative patterns and need both the spiritual framework and practical steps to make lasting changes. The biggest reason to read it is that success requires both vision and systematic action - this book provides both in a concise, accessible format.
