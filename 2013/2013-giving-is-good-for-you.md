# Giving is Good for You
**Authors**: John Nickson | **Year**: 2013

## Summary
This powerful book makes a compelling case that philanthropy is not just beneficial to society but profoundly transformative for the giver. John Nickson, drawing on his extensive experience as a fundraiser and trustee, presents both scientific research and inspiring case studies to demonstrate that generosity is fundamental to human fulfillment and social health. The book serves as both an indictment of modern selfishness and a hopeful call to embrace giving as a path to personal and societal transformation.

This book is ideal for affluent individuals seeking purpose, business leaders who can influence giving cultures, philanthropists looking to deepen their understanding of donor psychology, and anyone interested in the profound connection between generosity and personal fulfillment. Readers will discover the surprising scientific evidence for how giving improves health, happiness, and social connection.

## Core Insights

### The Generosity Paradox: The Poor Give More Than the Rich
Research reveals a striking irony in charitable giving patterns: the poorest 10% of society give proportionally more to charity than the wealthiest 10%. While the UK's 1,000 wealthiest individuals saw their wealth increase by 283.5% since 2000 (from £146 billion to £414 billion), their generosity hasn't kept pace with their means. This generosity gap reflects a broader cultural shift from "we" to "me" mentality, where materialism has replaced community connection.

> "If people are only interested in themselves and only concerned about making as much money as possible for themselves, then that is their loss."
> _Terry Bramall_

**Quick Take**: True generosity isn't measured by absolute amounts but by proportional sacrifice and the willingness to prioritize others' needs.

### The Science of Giving: Health and Happiness Benefits
Research by Richard Wilkinson and Kate Pickett demonstrates that more equal societies have dramatically better outcomes: less violence, higher life expectancy, better mental health, and stronger community bonds. People who prioritize "self-transcendent" values over wealth and status consistently report greater life satisfaction and well-being. The act of giving triggers neurological responses that improve mood and health, creating a positive feedback loop between generosity and personal flourishing.

> "Giving truly makes you happy."
> _A major philanthropist_

**Quick Take**: Giving is not a sacrifice but an investment in your own health and happiness, with scientifically-proven benefits for both mind and body.

### Transformative Giving: Stories That Changed Lives
The book showcases inspiring examples of how giving transforms both giver and receiver. Terry and Liz Bramall, a working-class couple who became philanthropists, fund programs for sex workers in Newcastle. Simon Robertson, former Goldman Sachs chairman, finds deep fulfillment supporting arts and medicine. Duane Jackson, rescued by The Prince's Trust, now employs ex-offenders. These stories demonstrate that meaningful giving is about connecting with causes that reflect your values and experiences.

> "I would not be here but for the kindness of strangers."
> _Michael Moritz on his £75 million donation to Oxford University_

**Quick Take**: The most effective philanthropy combines personal connection with strategic impact, creating meaning for both giver and recipient.

### The Business Case for Generosity
Modern philanthropy is evolving from traditional charity to strategic social investment. The Prince's Trust demonstrates how supporting disadvantaged youth creates both social impact and economic returns - every £1 invested generates £10.26 for society. Companies that embrace giving cultures attract better talent, stronger customer loyalty, and improved financial performance. The new philanthropy recognizes that social problems and business success are interconnected.

> "This is a great investment in both financial and social terms."
> _Duane Jackson, entrepreneur and philanthropist_

**Quick Take**: Strategic generosity isn't just good ethics - it's good business, creating measurable returns for society and donors alike.

### Building a Culture of Giving: The 22 Principles
Nickson outlines practical strategies for transforming giving cultures: be authentic and personal, listen more than you talk, build long-term relationships, maintain ethical standards, and express sincere gratitude. Effective fundraising requires treating donors as partners in a shared mission rather than ATM machines. The goal is creating what Nickson calls a "warm climate" of giving and gratitude that permeates society.

> "Giving means leadership. Giving is doing something positive, about taking a stand and sacrificing something for a greater good."
> _Andy, community organizer_

**Quick Take**: Sustainable giving cultures are built on authentic relationships, shared values, and genuine appreciation rather than transactional appeals.

## Action Items
- **Assess your giving capacity**: Calculate what percentage of your income/wealth you currently give versus what you could give
- **Connect with causes**: Identify organizations that align with your personal values and experiences
- **Research impact**: Investigate how potential donations create measurable change in your community
- **Start strategic**: Begin with focused giving to 2-3 organizations rather than scattered small donations
- **Build relationships**: Engage directly with organizations you support to understand their work deeply
- **Encourage others**: Share your giving experiences and inspire generosity in your personal and professional networks

## Questions to Consider
- What percentage of your income or wealth do you currently give, and how does that compare to your capacity?
- Which personal experiences or values could guide your philanthropic choices?
- How might structured giving enhance your sense of purpose and life satisfaction?
- What social problems in your community could you help address through strategic generosity?
- How could you encourage giving cultures within your workplace or family?
- What legacy do you want to create through your philanthropic impact?

## Conclusion
"Giving is Good for You" is a compelling and timely challenge to the prevailing culture of selfishness and materialism. Nickson combines hard scientific evidence with inspiring real stories to demonstrate that generosity is not a sacrifice but a pathway to greater fulfillment, health, and happiness. The book's strength lies in its practical approach - showing not just why we should give but how we can give effectively and meaningfully.

This book deserves your time because it reframes philanthropy from duty to opportunity, from sacrifice to investment. In an age of growing inequality and social fragmentation, Nickson's message is both radical and simple: the cure for many of our personal and social problems lies in rediscovering the transformative power of giving. Whether you're a potential philanthropist or simply someone seeking greater purpose, this book provides the blueprint for a more generous and fulfilling life.
