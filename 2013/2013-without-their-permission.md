# Without Their Permission
**Author**: Alexis Ohanian | **Year**: 2013

## Summary
"Without Their Permission" is Alexis Ohanian's passionate manifesto about how the open Internet has democratized innovation and entrepreneurship. As co-founder of reddit, Ohanian shares his personal journey from college student to multi-millionaire entrepreneur, while arguing that the Internet enables anyone to create and succeed without traditional gatekeepers. The book combines personal stories, startup wisdom, and a call to protect Internet freedom for future innovators.

This book is perfect for aspiring entrepreneurs, tech enthusiasts, and anyone who wants to understand how the Internet is transforming business and society. Readers will gain practical insights into starting companies, the importance of execution over ideas, and why preserving an open Internet matters for everyone's future.

## Core Insights

### Permissionless Innovation is Changing Everything
The Internet enables "permissionless innovation" - anyone can create and launch ideas without asking for approval from traditional gatekeepers. Ohanian and his co-founder Steve Huffman built reddit with just $12,000 from Y Combinator and sold it to CondÃ© Nast in less time than it took to write their college theses. This democratization means your ideas can win simply because people like them, not because you have connections or credentials.

> "Software developers worldwide are transforming every single industry on the planet thanks to the open Internet, which makes unprecedented 'permissionless innovation' possible."

**Quick Take**: The Internet has removed the barriers that once limited innovation to those with access to capital and connections. Today, anyone with a computer and Internet connection can build something that changes the world.

### Ideas Are Worthless, Execution Is Everything
Ohanian argues that ideas are fundamentally worthless - execution is everything. Most people don't have time to steal your idea, and even if they did, success comes from out-executing competitors. This mindset frees entrepreneurs to focus on building rather than protecting their concepts.

> "Here's the thing about ideas: ideas are worthless. That might sound harsh, but my experience has been that execution is everything."

**Quick Take**: Stop worrying about someone stealing your idea and start focusing on building something people actually want. Success comes from execution, not original concepts.

### Startups Are Designed for Growth
Following Paul Graham's definition, Ohanian explains that a startup is "a company designed to grow fast." Unlike traditional businesses limited by physical constraints, startups can experience logarithmic "hockey-stick growth" thanks to the scalable nature of Internet businesses.

> "The only essential thing is growth. Everything else we associate with startups follows from growth."

**Quick Take**: If your business isn't designed to grow rapidly, it's not really a startup - it's just a small business. Focus on growth from day one.

### The Internet Levels the Playing Field
The open Internet creates what Ohanian calls "a global platform from which ideas can spread" where all links are created equal. This technological leveling means innovators from anywhere can compete with established players, as long as they build something people want.

> "When all links are created equal, your ideas can win simply because people like them. The future of innovation will be made, not managed."

**Quick Take**: The Internet doesn't completely remove society's inequalities, but it does create a technologically level playing field where good ideas can succeed regardless of their origin.

### Use the Internet to Make the World Suck Less
Beyond entrepreneurship, the Internet enables anyone to create positive change through philanthropy, art, activism, and community building. Ohanian shares stories of people like Zach Anner (a comedian with cerebral palsy who built an online community) and Lester Chambers (a musician who crowdfunded his comeback album) to illustrate how the platform empowers creativity and impact.

> "Do your little bit of good where you are; it's those little bits of good put together that overwhelm the world."

**Quick Take**: You don't need to start a business to be entrepreneurial online. Use the platform to create positive change in whatever way matters to you.

### Internet Freedom Must Be Protected
Ohanian warns that the open Internet that enables all this innovation is under constant threat from legislation like SOPA and PIPA. He recounts how Internet users organized the largest online protest in history to defeat these bills, demonstrating the power of collective action to protect digital freedoms.

> "The Internet helps people help themselves. It's no surprise to me that the Internet freedom movement has been so successful here in the United States, given that this core message of entrepreneurship is so baked into the founding of our country."

**Quick Take**: The same Internet that enables innovation also enables the organization needed to protect it. Stay engaged in digital rights issues to ensure the platform remains open for future creators.

## Action Items

- **Start building now**: Don't wait for permission or perfect conditions. Launch your idea and iterate based on feedback.
- **Focus on execution**: Spend less time protecting ideas and more time implementing them better than anyone else.
- **Design for growth**: Build your business with scalability in mind from the beginning.
- **Use the platform for good**: Find ways to use Internet connectivity to create positive change in your community or field.
- **Protect digital freedoms**: Stay informed about Internet policy issues and participate in efforts to keep the web open.
- **Learn continuously**: The Internet provides endless opportunities to acquire new skills - take advantage of free resources to develop your capabilities.

## Questions to Consider

- What idea have you been sitting on because you felt you needed permission or resources to start?
- How could you use Internet platforms to create value or positive change in your community?
- What skills do you need to develop to take full advantage of the opportunities the Internet provides?
- Are you more focused on protecting your ideas or executing them better than competitors?
- How would your life or work change if the Internet became less open and controlled by gatekeepers?

## Conclusion

"Without Their Permission" is both a personal memoir and a call to action that captures the entrepreneurial spirit of the early Internet era. Ohanian's enthusiasm is infectious, and his practical insights about startups, execution, and growth make this valuable reading for anyone interested in technology and entrepreneurship. The book's message about preserving Internet freedom feels even more relevant today, as debates about platform control, content moderation, and digital rights continue to evolve.

This is worth your time if you're looking for inspiration to start something new, practical advice about building Internet businesses, or motivation to engage in protecting digital freedoms. The biggest reason to read it is Ohanian's core message: in an age of permissionless innovation, the only thing holding you back is yourself. Whether you want to build a business, create art, or make the world suck less, the tools are available - you just need to start using them.
