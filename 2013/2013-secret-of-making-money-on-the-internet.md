# Secret of Making Money on the Internet
**Authors**: George Sheema | **Year**: 2013

## Summary
This practical guide demystifies the world of online business and digital entrepreneurship, offering a comprehensive roadmap for anyone looking to generate income through internet-based ventures. The book serves as both an introduction to various online business models and a strategic framework for building sustainable digital enterprises.

Perfect for aspiring entrepreneurs, side-hustlers, and traditional business owners looking to expand into digital markets, this guide delivers actionable insights on everything from company formation and investment strategies to web marketing and payment systems. Readers will gain clarity on the diverse online business landscape and learn practical approaches to minimize risk while maximizing potential returns.

## Core Insights

### The Mindset Shift: Every Idea Has Value
Start with the assumption that for every service or product, there's someone who needs it. Even ideas that seem like "nonsense" can find buyers if implemented correctly and timed well. The book challenges the tendency to focus on why things can't be done, instead encouraging action and experimentation.

> "Start from the assumption that for every service or product (good) there is somebody who needs it, or simply 'wants it'. If we start looking for reasons, even in thoughts, why something cannot be done, we will never do anything."

**Quick Take**: There are no bad ideas, only poor execution or bad timing. Your next "crazy" idea might be exactly what someone somewhere is willing to pay for.

### Business Planning: Your Foundation for Success
A business plan isn't just paperwork—it's your roadmap to avoiding chaos and building systematic operations. Even a simple 20-page plan provides structure, objectives, and clear pathways to follow. The book emphasizes that planning happens automatically in daily life; formalizing it for business ensures consistent progress.

> "The business plan is often seen as a piece of paper and an expensive time delay... This is a major mistake and many start-ups suffer the consequences of the fact that they have no boundaries, objectives, paths and options from the beginning."

**Quick Take**: Invest time in planning upfront to save countless hours fixing preventable mistakes later.

### Investment Diversification: Don't Put All Eggs in One Basket
The key to successful investing is spreading risk across different asset classes. From stocks and commodities to domains and real estate, each investment vehicle offers different risk-return profiles. The book provides detailed comparisons, noting that stocks historically return ~20% annually while commodities can deliver hundreds of percent returns—but with much higher risk.

> "Diversification means the reduction of the overall risk (investment portfolio) by purchasing various kinds (classes) of investment assets from different issuers."

**Quick Take**: Balance your portfolio between safe havens (gold/diamonds at 3-10% returns) and higher-risk options (commodities/domains) based on your risk tolerance.

### Web Marketing: Smart Over Expensive
Effective online marketing doesn't require massive budgets—it requires smart strategies. While many entrepreneurs invest heavily in flashy websites, they neglect visibility. The book recommends viral marketing, opt-in email campaigns, and search engine optimization over expensive banner advertising.

> "Do clever advertising. Create the ways how to enable someone else to visit your website. Every professional marketing company will advise you to pay the PPC (Pay per click) form of advertisement or paid banner space... It is too expensive for efficiency of cost vs. the rate of attendance with a paying client conversion."

**Quick Take**: Focus on organic growth through viral content and targeted email marketing rather than expensive paid advertising campaigns.

### The Three Golden Rules of Wealth
Building wealth follows simple principles that most people fail to execute consistently: (1) It's easier to save money than earn more, (2) Saved money must be invested to generate returns (anything below inflation isn't investing), and (3) If you use something infrequently, rent it rather than buy it.

> "The secret of financial success is to spend only what you have left after putting away the savings and investments. Not to save only what you have left after spending."

**Quick Take**: Pay yourself first by allocating savings before expenses, then live on what remains rather than the reverse.

## Action Items

- **Start Small**: Many internet businesses require minimal or no initial capital. Begin with services, affiliate marketing, or digital content creation.

- **Write Your Business Plan**: Even a simple 20-page outline will provide structure, boundaries, and clear objectives for your venture.

- **Diversify Investments**: Spread capital across different asset classes based on your risk tolerance and timeline goals.

- **Build Web Presence First**: Create a professional website before investing heavily in marketing—your site is your 24/7 sales representative.

- **Learn Digital Marketing**: Focus on viral marketing, email campaigns, and SEO rather than expensive banner advertising.

- **Set Up Payment Systems**: Establish multiple payment gateways (PayPal, electronic wallets) to accommodate global customers.

- **Consider Offshore Options**: Evaluate tax-optimized jurisdictions for company formation if doing business internationally.

- **Focus on Savings First**: Implement the "pay yourself first" principle by allocating savings before spending.

## Questions to Consider

- What unique skills, knowledge, or passions do you have that could be monetized online?

- Are you focusing too much on why ideas won't work instead of testing their market potential?

- Is your investment portfolio properly diversified across different risk levels and asset classes?

- Are you spending on marketing that delivers measurable returns, or just following expensive conventional approaches?

- Do you have a systematic approach to savings and investing, or are you saving only what's left after expenses?

## Conclusion

This book delivers solid, actionable guidance for anyone looking to enter the world of online business or improve their digital marketing and investment strategies. While some content shows its age (particularly around cryptocurrency and social media), the fundamental principles of business planning, diversification, and smart marketing remain timeless.

The strongest value lies in its practical approach to risk management and its emphasis that successful online entrepreneurship doesn't require massive capital—just smart planning, continuous learning, and disciplined execution. For readers willing to look past the outdated examples, this guide provides a solid foundation for digital business success.

Is it worth your time? Yes, if you're new to online business or need a comprehensive overview of digital business models and investment strategies. The biggest reason to read it is its emphasis on actionable principles over theoretical concepts—you'll finish with concrete steps to implement immediately.
