# A Guide to Better Management: How to Get Win Win
**Authors**: Jon Allen | **Year**: 2013 | **Category**: Business & Finance

## Summary
This practical guide applies Neuro-Linguistic Programming (NLP) principles to transform management from command-and-control to collaborative influencing. The book focuses on developing essential skills for influencing and negotiating that create mutually beneficial outcomes in workplace relationships. It presents a comprehensive framework for understanding how successful people achieve their goals and how managers can apply these principles to create more effective, cooperative work environments.

Perfect for managers, team leaders, and business professionals who want to move beyond traditional hierarchical management toward relationship-based leadership. Readers will learn practical techniques for influencing others ethically, building lasting professional relationships, and achieving outcomes where all parties feel successful and satisfied.

## Core Insights

### The Foundation: Understanding Influence as "Flowing Into"
True influence means joining with others' existing direction rather than blocking it. The book explains that influence originally meant "to flow into" - like a stream joining a river and gradually changing its course. This contrasts with the common approach of trying to dam up people's current behaviors and force them into different paths.

> "Influencing means to flow into, it means to join with and then make a difference to."
>
**Quick Take**: Stop trying to force change and start understanding people's existing motivations and directions. Work with their flow rather than against it.

### The Three Dimensions of Outcomes
Most people focus only on immediate results ("I want them to agree with me"), but effective influencing requires considering three dimensions: 1) The specific result you want, 2) How you want the other person to feel after the interaction, and 3) The long-term effect on the relationship.

> "You may think it's everything but in fact if you stop and think a little further you will realise it is only the starting point."
>
**Quick Take**: Always clarify what you want beyond the immediate transaction - relationships matter more than single outcomes.

### Five Levels of Attention
Paying full attention involves more than just hearing words: 1) Listening to their words, 2) Hearing with your inner ear (tone, hesitation, emphasis), 3) Watching with your eyes (body language), 4) Noticing with your gut (intuition), and 5) Bringing your heart (genuine sympathy for their worldview).

> "The very act of offering somebody full attention without being asked to of itself makes them feel that you are welcoming them and that you are on their side."
>
**Quick Take**: Complete attention builds instant rapport and makes people receptive to your influence.

### Understanding Their World Before Influencing
To influence effectively, you must understand how the other person views the world. This means stepping into their shoes and understanding their priorities, constraints, and motivations rather than assuming they share your perspective.

> "If you can fit your message to the way in which they view the world, you will find you have far more agreement than you may have first thought."
>
**Quick Take**: People make decisions based on their values and priorities, not yours. Discover what matters to them first.

### Psycho-Geography: The Power of Physical Positioning
How people position themselves physically affects their psychological stance. Sitting on the same side of a table literally puts people "on the same side" psychologically, while opposite positions create subconscious conflict.

> "If you can literally be facing in the same direction as the other person, you are far more likely to get a 'win win' situation."
>
**Quick Take**: Physical arrangement matters more than we think - same-side positioning creates psychological alignment.

## Best Quotes

- "We need to learn how to get into somebody else's world and make things work from their perspective if we want to be a useful influence."
- "Being told to do something positively de-motivates you and makes you feel that you don't want to do it at all."
- "You have no choice about being an influencer, you are whether you like it or not. The only question is, what sort of an influence are you on people?"
- "It's not about what you say, it's about how you treat other people and how you offer what you have to offer in their terms rather than in your terms."
- "If we can be aware of how influencing works and what makes it work well, then we can use those skills more consciously and more effectively to get to 'win win' for every side of a situation."

## Action Items

- Before any influencing situation, clarify your three-dimensional outcome: immediate result, relationship impact, and long-term effect
- Practice five-level attention in your next conversation: words, inner ear, eyes, gut, and heart
- Research the other person's priorities and constraints before attempting to influence them
- Use their exact language and terminology rather than translating it into your own words
- Intentionally arrange physical positioning to create "same-side" psychological alignment
- Find common ground at different levels - either broader context or smaller details - to put disagreements in perspective
- Approach every interaction with integrity: ensure your influence benefits both sides, not just you

## Questions to Consider

- What are your non-negotiable bottom lines in influencing situations, and are they relationship-focused or outcome-focused?
- How can you better understand your colleagues' and team members' unique worldviews before attempting to influence them?
- In what ways are you currently using "damming" tactics instead of "flowing into" approaches?
- What opportunities exist in your current role to practice win-win influencing rather than command-and-control?

## Conclusion
This book is absolutely worth your time if you're ready to transform your management style from hierarchical to collaborative. Its greatest strength is providing a practical framework for understanding that influencing isn't about winning arguments - it's about creating relationships where both parties thrive. The NLP-based approach may seem esoteric at first, but the techniques are grounded in everyday human psychology and common sense.

The biggest reason to read this guide is that it fundamentally changes how you view influence - not as a tool for getting what you want, but as a way to create mutually beneficial outcomes that strengthen relationships over time. In today's workplace of cross-functional teams and empowered employees, these skills aren't just nice-to-have; they're essential for effective leadership.
