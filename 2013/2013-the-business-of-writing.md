# The Business of Writing
**Authors**: Philip Wylie | **Year**: 2013 | **Category**: Business & Finance

## Summary
"The Business of Writing" is a practical guide that demystifies the publishing industry and provides aspiring non-fiction authors with a roadmap to successful publication. Written by an experienced author with decades of publishing experience, this book focuses specifically on non-fiction writing as the most viable path to making a living from writing. Wylie argues that while fiction writers face brutal competition against established authors like Stephen King and J.K. Rowling, non-fiction authors have significantly better odds of success with the right business approach.

The book offers a step-by-step methodology covering everything from developing a publishing concept and writing compelling proposals to understanding different publishing options and effective marketing strategies. What sets this guide apart is its focus on the business aspects of writing rather than just the craft, emphasizing that successful authors must be entrepreneurs first and writers second.

This guide is ideal for aspiring authors, subject matter experts, coaches, consultants, and professionals who want to establish authority in their field through published non-fiction books. Readers will learn how to avoid costly mistakes, evaluate publishing options, and create a sustainable income from their writing.

## Core Insights

### The Non-Fiction Advantage
Non-fiction offers dramatically higher success rates than fiction for aspiring authors. While most fiction manuscripts remain unpublished, non-fiction authors can secure publishing contracts before writing a single word if they have strong credentials and a marketable concept. The key is understanding that non-fiction serves a specific need - readers are seeking solutions, knowledge, or expertise, making them more willing to pay for books that deliver genuine value.

> "The majority of successfully published writers specialize in non-fiction. This anecdote summarizes the reality of writing - the majority of successfully published writers specialize in non-fiction."

**Quick Take**: Focus on areas where you have genuine expertise and credentials, as readers of non-fiction want authoritative guidance from experts they can trust.

### The Publishing Concept is Everything
Your publishing concept must clearly demonstrate the value you're offering readers and how your book differs from existing titles. A strong concept includes author credibility, market demand, appropriate production quality, and effective marketing strategy. Before investing months in writing, draft a comprehensive publishing proposal that includes a one-page query letter with an attention-grabbing sentence, proposed title, 50-word synopsis, competitive analysis, and your credentials.

> "Ideas alone - without organizational structure and application - are worthless. Successfully published books offer perceived value to the readership and they are marketed effectively to those people who perceive value in your product."

**Quick Take**: Research competing titles thoroughly on Amazon and bibliographic databases, then clearly articulate how your book offers unique benefits that existing books don't provide.

### Self-Publishing vs Traditional Publishing
Modern authors have more options than ever before. Traditional publishers offer prestige but typically pay only 5-10% royalties for print books. E-book publishers pay 40-60% royalties but take a percentage of sales. Self-publishing through platforms like Amazon CreateSpace offers maximum control and higher profit margins but requires entrepreneurial skills. The best approach depends on your goals, expertise, and willingness to handle business operations.

> "Authors need to be market-oriented businesspeople, practitioners of Public Relations and social media enthusiasts. Writers need business skills in addition to their core craft."

**Quick Take**: Start with a professional publishing proposal even if you plan to self-publish, as this strategic document will guide your entire project and improve your chances of success.

### The Economics of Print-on-Demand
Print-on-Demand (POD) technology has revolutionized publishing by eliminating warehousing costs and allowing authors to print books as orders come in. For specialized non-fiction books, print runs of 500+ copies rarely make sense financially. Instead, maintain a small stock of 50 copies for promotional purposes and use POD for customer orders. Your print specification should model successful existing books in your genre.

> "The future of print publishing is Print-on-Demand (POD), especially for non-fiction books. A major benefit of POD is elimination of warehousing costs and inventory management."

**Quick Take**: Research successful books in your niche and use their physical specifications (size, paper quality, binding) as a model for your own book.

### Marketing That Costs Nothing
The most effective book marketing strategies often cost nothing. Securing book reviews in relevant publications, getting excerpts serialized in magazines, and arranging author interviews on radio or TV generate more sales than paid advertising. Your book cover is your most important marketing tool - invest in professional design that appeals to your target readership.

> "A positive book review or serialization in a suitable periodical will help you to sell more books than any paid advertisement would."

**Quick Take**: Create a professional press release and send it to editors of relevant publications, offering them excerpts or review copies in exchange for coverage.

## Action Items
- Develop a comprehensive publishing proposal before writing your manuscript
- Research competing titles on Amazon and identify your unique angle
- Build your author credentials through expertise, experience, and public recognition
- Create a detailed budget and timeline for your publishing project
- Model your book's physical specifications on successful titles in your niche
- Design a professional book cover that appeals to your target readership
- Build relationships with editors, reviewers, and influencers in your field
- Consider Print-on-Demand to minimize upfront investment and inventory risks
- Develop multiple distribution channels rather than relying on a single retailer
- Create a marketing plan focused on editorial coverage rather than paid advertising

## Questions to Consider
- What specific expertise or experience gives you credibility to write on your chosen topic?
- Who is your target readership and how will you reach them effectively?
- What makes your book unique compared to existing titles on the same subject?
- Which publishing option (traditional, e-book, or self-publishing) best aligns with your goals and resources?
- How will you balance writing quality with meeting your production deadlines?
- What is your realistic sales projection and how does it impact your printing decisions?
- Which marketing strategies will you prioritize based on your target audience's media consumption habits?
- How will you leverage your first published book to create additional publishing opportunities?

## Conclusion
"The Business of Writing" is essential reading for anyone serious about making a living from non-fiction writing. Wylie's practical, business-focused approach demystifies an industry that often seems opaque to outsiders. The book's greatest strength lies in its emphasis on treating writing as a business venture rather than just a creative pursuit - a perspective that dramatically increases an author's chances of success.

What makes this guide particularly valuable is its guarantee: if you follow the step-by-step process without taking shortcuts, you will get your non-fiction book published. This confidence comes from Wylie's decades of experience and his focus on non-fiction, where market demand and author expertise create a clear path to publication.

The biggest reason to read this book is that it will save you thousands of dollars and countless hours by helping you avoid the common pitfalls that derail most aspiring authors. Whether you choose traditional publishing, e-publishing, or self-publishing, the strategic framework provided here will help you make informed decisions and create a sustainable career as a non-fiction author.
