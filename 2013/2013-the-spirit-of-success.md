# The Spirit of Success
**Authors**: Norman Drummond | **Year**: 2013

## Summary
"The Spirit of Success" is a transformative guide to connecting your head to your heart in work and life. Norman Drummond, drawing from his experience as a BBC Governor, Church of Scotland Minister, and leadership coach, presents a powerful framework for discovering authentic success beyond material achievements. The book challenges readers to look inward, understand their roots, and build a life based on fundamental values of integrity, responsibility, and humanity.

This book is perfect for professionals feeling unfulfilled despite conventional success, leaders seeking to create values-based organizations, and anyone asking the fundamental questions "Who am I?", "Why am I living this way?", and "What might I yet become?". Readers will learn to overcome the limitations of material success, develop authentic self-awareness, and create lasting impact through values-based leadership.

## Core Insights

### The Three Vital Questions
The foundation of the book rests on three essential questions that everyone must regularly ask themselves: Who are you? Why are you living and working in the way that you are? What might you yet become and do with your life? These questions force us to look beyond the daily grind and examine our authentic selves, our motivations, and our potential.

> "To know himself is the foundation stone of knowledge."
> _General Douglas MacArthur_

**Quick Take**: Self-awareness isn't optional—it's the starting point for all genuine success and fulfillment.

### Understanding Your Roots
Drummond emphasizes the Celtic proverb: "In order to understand where you are going you have to understand where you come from." Our childhood experiences, family background, and early influences shape our current behaviors, beliefs, and patterns. By honestly examining our past, we can understand our fears, limitations, and strengths.

Many successful professionals carry hidden fears of failure rooted in childhood experiences. Nine out of ten executive clients Drummond coaches suffer from anxiety based on fear of failure, despite their external success.

> "When you know and accept who you are, when there are no dark corners of yourself which you avoid, you become a fully centred and grounded human being."

**Quick Take**: Your past isn't something to escape—it's the key to understanding your present and transforming your future.

### The Five Ps Framework
The book's centerpiece is the Five Ps framework for achieving meaningful success:

**People**: Surround yourself with those whose cups are half-full rather than half-empty. Find people who think positively, look for solutions rather than problems, and believe in your vision. Have the courage to part with those who undermine your purpose.

**Partnerships**: Rather than competing with like-minded organizations, seek partnerships that create mutual benefit. As social entrepreneur John Moorhouse says, "There is no limit to what we can achieve, so long as we don't mind who gets the credit."

**Pioneering**: translating pioneering thoughts into pioneering actions requires boldness and determination. The pioneer must build foundations that others can build upon, understanding that you'll never fully understand the pioneering process until you've lived it.

**Persevering**: When striving to make a difference, setbacks come fast and frequent. People will tell you "it's never been done before" or "who are you to attempt this?" Perseverance is the value most needed during pioneering ventures.

**Prayer**: In moments when you feel absolutely stuck, spiritual connection provides strength and comfort. Whether through formal prayer or spiritual reflection, recognizing that you're not alone provides the resilience to continue.

> "The lessons learned through the five Ps of People, Partnerships, Pioneering, Persevering and Prayer are ones which will shape and enable you for the rest of your life."

**Quick Take**: Success isn't a solo journey—it requires the right people, strategic partnerships, pioneering courage, unwavering perseverance, and spiritual grounding.

### Beyond Material Success
Drummond challenges the common definition of success based on material props—luxury cars, generous benefits packages, big pensions. He shares the story of a wealthy client so focused on building his business that he missed his wife's loneliness and unhappiness, eventually losing her to a repairman who actually paid attention to her.

> "After all, the inner strength of the personality doesn't depend on the size of your income, house or car."

**Quick Take**: Real success can't be measured by external achievements—it's measured by the quality of your relationships and your impact on others.

### Values-Based Leadership
The book advocates for leadership that balances head and heart, combining wisdom with compassion and effectiveness with humanity. Great leaders put people before projects, maintain high standards of behavior, and stand out for their humanity and ability to use their heads while not forgetting their hearts.

> "When good people come together, great things can and do happen, for real lasting change happens one on one, one by one and then in partnerships."

**Quick Take**: True leadership isn't about position or power—it's about bringing out the greatness in others while staying true to your values.

## Action Items

- **Ask yourself the three vital questions regularly**: Who am I? Why am I living this way? What might I yet become? Write down your answers and revisit them monthly.

- **Examine your roots**: Spend time understanding your childhood influences and how they shape your current behaviors and beliefs. Make connections between past experiences and present patterns.

- **Surround yourself with positivity**: Identify the people in your life who support your vision and those who undermine it. Make conscious choices about who you spend time with.

- **Seek partnerships instead of competition**: Look for opportunities to collaborate with others who share your values and vision, focusing on mutual benefit rather than individual credit.

- **Develop pioneering courage**: Take one small step toward something you've been afraid to attempt. Remember that pioneering feels unfamiliar but becomes easier with practice.

- **Practice perseverance**: When facing setbacks, remind yourself of your purpose and the people who are counting on you. Perseverance is built one difficult decision at a time.

- **Cultivate spiritual connection**: Whether through prayer, meditation, or time in nature, develop practices that help you feel connected to something larger than yourself.

## Questions to Consider

- What parts of myself am I avoiding or denying, and how does this avoidance limit my success and fulfillment?

- Where did my current definition of success come from, and does it truly reflect my deepest values and desires?

- Who are the people in my life who truly support my growth, and who might be holding me back?

- What pioneering step have I been avoiding, and what would make it possible to take that first action?

- How do I respond to setbacks, and what would it take to develop greater perseverance?

- What does "connecting my head to my heart" mean in my specific work and life context?

- If I were asked those three angel's questions at the end of my life, how would I want to answer them?

## Conclusion
"The Spirit of Success" is a timeless guide that transcends typical business books by addressing the fundamental human need for meaning, connection, and purpose. Drummond's message is both challenging and hopeful—he doesn't promise easy answers, but provides a proven framework for discovering authentic success.

The book's greatest strength lies in its integration of practical business wisdom with deep spiritual insight. Drummond speaks from experience as a leader who has walked the talk, moving successfully between the worlds of business, media, religion, and social entrepreneurship.

This is absolutely worth your time if you're feeling successful but unfulfilled, if you're in a leadership position and want to create meaningful impact, or if you're simply asking the bigger questions about life and purpose. The biggest reason to read this book is that it doesn't just offer more techniques for achieving conventional success—it provides a pathway to redefining success on your own terms, connecting who you are with what you do, and creating a legacy that truly matters.
