# Basic 120 Japanese Kanji N5 Copybook
**Original Title**: Basic 120 Japanese Kanji N5 Copybook/exercise book
**Author**: Iuliia Nelidova | **Year**: 2025

## Summary
This practical workbook provides a structured approach to mastering the 120 essential Japanese kanji characters required for the JLPT N5 proficiency exam. Designed for learners who have already mastered hiragana and katakana alphabets, this copybook emphasizes proper stroke order, consistent character formation, and extensive writing practice to build muscle memory and recognition skills.

Perfect for beginning Japanese language students of all ages who need a hands-on approach to kanji learning. The book's systematic method ensures you develop proper writing habits while building a solid foundation for the N5 exam.

## Core Insights

### The Foundation: Prerequisites Matter
Before diving into kanji, you must master both Japanese alphabets - hiragana (for kun'yomi readings) and katakana (for on'yomi readings). This knowledge is essential because Japanese kanji have two types of readings: Japanese-style (kun'yomi) and Chinese-style (on'yomi), and understanding these reading systems is crucial for vocabulary comprehension.

> "Before using this book, you need to learn the Japanese hiragana and katakana alphabets. Japanese kanji have two types of reading: Japanese (kun'yomi, spelled hiragana) and Chinese (on'yomi, spelled katakana)."

**Quick Take**: Don't skip the alphabets - they're your roadmap to understanding kanji readings.

### The Art of Proper Kanji Formation
The book establishes ten fundamental rules for writing kanji that create the foundation for authentic Japanese handwriting. These rules ensure characters are balanced, stable, and properly proportioned - skills that distinguish casual learners from serious students.

> "Kanji must be stable. Do not allow leaning to the left or right."

**Quick Take**: Master the geometric precision of kanji writing - every character should sit perfectly centered and balanced within its invisible square.

### The Principle of Consistent Dimensions
All kanji are written the same size regardless of their complexity, teaching learners to allocate equal space for simple characters like 一 (one) and complex characters like 語 (language). This principle develops spatial awareness crucial for reading Japanese text naturally.

> "Kanji are written the same size regardless of their complexity."

**Quick Take**: Treat every kanji as equally important - give each character its own square of space, whether simple or complex.

### The Stroke Order Discipline
The book emphasizes that correct stroke order isn't just tradition - it's essential for muscle memory and efficient writing. Following the proper sequence (left to right, top to bottom) creates natural rhythm and flow that becomes automatic with practice.

> "It is very important to observe the correct order of writing traits."

**Quick Take**: Learn stroke order from the start - it's the difference between writing kanji and owning kanji.

### The Spatial Harmony Principle
Left and right components of kanji must match in height and width, creating visual harmony. The book teaches that the right side of left elements should be slightly shorter to create a seamless connection with the right side, demonstrating the subtle artistry in Japanese writing.

> "The left and right parts of the kanji are written the same height and width."

**Quick Take**: Think of kanji as architectural designs where every component must fit together perfectly.
