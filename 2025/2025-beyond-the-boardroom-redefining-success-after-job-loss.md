# Beyond the Boardroom: Redefining Success after Job Loss
**Original Title**: Fodfæste: Om at miste sig selv i arbejdslivet og finde en ny vej
**Author**: Lars Sander Matjeka | **Year**: 2025
**Category**: Business & Finance

## Summary
This raw, honest memoir chronicles a CEO's journey from the pinnacle of corporate success to the depths of unemployment and back again. Lars Sander Matjeka was dismissed from his position as CEO of Aller Media in 2020, forcing him to confront everything he thought he knew about success, identity, and worth. Through intimate journal entries spanning his dismissal, job search, and personal rediscovery, Matjeka exposes the vulnerabilities behind the executive facade and challenges fundamental assumptions about leadership, career, and what it means to live a successful life.

This book is essential reading for anyone who has experienced job loss, faces career uncertainty, or questions whether traditional definitions of success align with their values. Executives, managers, and professionals will find validation in Matjeka's struggle to separate identity from title, while his insights on modern leadership and workplace culture offer valuable guidance for creating more humane, sustainable organizations.

## Core Insights

### Identity Crisis: Moving from Something to Someone
Matjeka discovers that losing his executive title forces a fundamental identity crisis. He had built his entire sense of self around being a "CEO"—something rather than someone. This revelation comes when a reporter asks what it's like to "be nothing" the day after being a CEO, triggering Matjeka's realization that he had been defining himself by his role rather than his essence.

> "As I drove home from the interview, I suddenly realized that I'm in the process of learning how to be someone instead of something. This has been a major issue in my life throughout all my years in therapy."

**Quick Take**: True resilience comes from understanding that your worth as a person exists independently of your professional achievements or job title.

### The Taboo of Honest Dismissal
The author makes the controversial decision to be openly honest about being fired rather than using the standard corporate euphemisms like "leaving by mutual agreement." This transparency breaks an unwritten code in the business world but comes at a social cost, as many people treat dismissals as shameful failures to be hidden.

> "When I was fired, I didn't want to perpetuate a lie. Not only would doing so be in direct conflict with my personal values, but fundamentally I don't believe in the premise that in business we should deceive each other or 'pretend.'"

**Quick Take**: Authentic leadership requires the courage to speak uncomfortable truths, even when it goes against cultural norms.

### The Liberation of Grief
Contrary to his initial resistance, Matjeka discovers that acknowledging and processing his grief over job loss is ultimately liberating. He had been treating his sadness as something to overcome quickly, but learns that giving space to difficult emotions is essential for genuine healing and growth.

> "My ultimate recognition and acceptance of that grief turned out to be truly liberating, because I finally allowed myself to feel it, live with it, and share it."

**Quick Take**: True strength isn't avoiding painful emotions but having the courage to fully experience and process them.

### From Either/Or to Both/And Leadership
Throughout his career, Matjeka has been labeled a "soft" leader because he emphasizes employee wellbeing alongside financial results. He challenges this binary thinking, advocating for a "both/and" approach where organizations can be both profitable and humane, both strategic and caring, both results-driven and people-focused.

> "The movement from either/or to both/and applies not only to breaks and performance, but also to a wide range of areas, so that it becomes both profit and purpose, measurable and perceptible, masculine and feminine traits, brain and heart."

**Quick Take**: Effective leadership requires embracing apparent contradictions rather than choosing between them.

### "Be-stainability": Beyond Sustainable Workplaces
Matjeka introduces the Danish concept of "væredygtighed" (translated as "be-stainability"), which goes beyond traditional sustainability to focus on creating workplaces where people can thrive authentically. This involves building organizations that consider the whole person and reject toxic cultures, even when it comes at a short-term cost.

> "In Denmark, this more recent phenomenon is known as væredygtighed, which means 'be-stainability'—a sustainable way of being and a play on the Danish word for sustainability: bæredygtighed."

**Quick Take**: The most successful organizations create environments where people don't just work but truly belong and thrive.

### The Hidden Cost of Constant Performance
The book exposes the destructive myth that executives must work 70-80 hour weeks to be effective. Matjeka realizes this pace was destroying his health, relationships, and ultimately his leadership effectiveness. He argues that taking breaks—including career pauses—should be valued rather than stigmatized.

> "I continue to be surprised whenever I meet people who almost brag about working 70-80 hours a week. That simply isn't healthy, not for them, not for their family, and certainly not for their company."

**Quick Take**: Sustainable success requires regular recovery periods, not constant performance.

### Letting Go of Outcomes
In his lowest moments, Matjeka learns the transformative power of taking action without being attached to specific results. This shift from doing things to get a job to doing things because they feel right opens new possibilities and reduces his suffering.

> "For me, it means doing things without expecting any results. My only intention for acting is that it feels good and lifts my energy."

**Quick Take**: True freedom comes from acting from authentic intention rather than attachment to specific outcomes.

## Action Items

- **Redefine your identity**: Practice describing yourself without using your job title. Focus on your values, relationships, and essential qualities as a person rather than your professional role.

- **Embrace honest vulnerability**: Share your authentic experiences with career setbacks rather than hiding behind corporate euphemisms. This creates genuine connection and helps normalize career disruptions.

- **Practice the both/and approach**: Look for ways to integrate seemingly contradictory leadership qualities—be both strategic and caring, both results-driven and people-focused, both decisive and collaborative.

- **Prioritize recovery periods**: Schedule regular breaks in your workday and build longer periods for rest and reflection into your career trajectory. View these as essential for sustainable performance.

- **Build diverse relationships**: Cultivate connections with people who value you for who you are, not just for your professional position or influence. Invest in relationships that can sustain you beyond your current role.

- **Take action without attachment**: Practice doing things because they align with your values and bring you energy, rather than always working toward specific outcomes or rewards.

## Questions to Consider

- How much of your identity is tied to your professional role, and what would remain if that role disappeared tomorrow?

- Where in your leadership or life are you choosing between "either/or" when a "both/and" approach might serve you better?

- What workplace cultures or expectations might be damaging your long-term effectiveness and wellbeing, even if they seem to deliver short-term results?

- How could you create more honest, authentic conversations about career challenges and failures in your organization or industry?

- What would it look like to measure success not just by external achievements but by internal fulfillment and impact on others?

## Conclusion
"Beyond the Boardroom" offers a rare glimpse behind the executive curtain, revealing the human vulnerability that exists beneath professional success. Matjeka's journey from dismissed CEO to rediscovered self demonstrates that career setbacks can become opportunities for profound growth and redefinition of what truly matters.

This book is worth your time because it challenges fundamental assumptions about success, leadership, and identity that many professionals never question. Matjeka's raw honesty about his struggles with shame, grief, and self-worth makes the abstract concept of career disruption deeply personal and relatable. His insights about modern leadership, workplace culture, and sustainable success provide a roadmap for creating more humane, effective organizations.

The biggest reason to read this book is its reminder that true success isn't measured by titles or achievements alone, but by the authenticity of our relationships, the integrity of our choices, and our ability to find meaning beyond the boardroom. In an era of increasing burnout and career uncertainty, Matjeka's story offers both hope and practical wisdom for building more sustainable, fulfilling professional lives.
