# Make More Money the Relaxed Way
**Authors**: Simone Janson (ed.), Sebastian Kühn, Holger Jungclaus, Ilja Grzeskowitz, Robert T. Kiyosaki, Dr. Chris Winter | **Year**: 2025
**Category**: Business & Finance

## Summary
This comprehensive guide challenges the traditional high-stress approach to wealth building by presenting a mindful, strategic path to financial freedom. The book emphasizes that anyone can achieve ambitious financial goals with serenity, energy, and calm investment strategies—regardless of their starting point. Rather than promoting hustle culture or risky get-rich-quick schemes, it advocates for conscious decision-making, minimalism, and systematic wealth building through education and strategic planning. The core premise is that financial success comes from clarity, focus, and relaxed determination rather than anxiety and frantic effort.

Ideal for anyone feeling trapped in the income-consumption cycle or seeking to build wealth without sacrificing peace of mind, this book provides practical frameworks for financial planning, investment strategies, and the psychological mindset needed for sustainable wealth creation. Readers will learn how to break free from consumer debt, build strategic reserves, and develop investment approaches that work with their lifestyle rather than against it.

## Core Insights

### The Success Triangle: Career, Finances, and Life Design
The book introduces an integrated knowledge system based on three pillars that together enable a fulfilled and sustainable life. Career with Purpose means pursuing work that aligns with personal values while making meaningful contributions. Finance with Freedom represents conscious money management that creates security while opening possibilities for personal choices. Life Design with Impact focuses on conscious decisions that enhance well-being and positively influence society. Together, these elements create a life that's not just successful but meaningful and self-determined.

> "Success can be planned. Structure it. Visualize it. But if we are honest, personal success often begins where no plan takes effect - but where an impulse arises: an idea, an image, a feeling."

**Quick Take**: True success integrates professional achievement, financial independence, and intentional living rather than treating them as separate domains.

### Minimalism as Financial Freedom
The book presents a powerful counterargument to the consumption-driven approach to wealth building. Rather than focusing on earning more to support an expanding lifestyle, it advocates for reducing dependency on material possessions. The key insight is that every purchase not only costs money but also reduces freedom by creating the need to earn those expenses back. By prioritizing utility over ownership and seeking alternatives like sharing services or digital solutions, individuals can break the cycle of working to sustain unnecessary consumption.

> "It's the cycle of income and consumption that keeps you from earning enough. You will not break this cycle with more and more work, but through a conscious consumption behavior."

**Quick Take**: Financial freedom comes not just from increasing income but from intelligently reducing dependency on material possessions and unconscious spending patterns.

### The 5-10% Wealth Building Rule
A cornerstone of the book's practical approach is the systematic saving strategy of setting aside five to ten percent of earnings immediately and automatically through standing orders to separate accounts. This creates both psychological distance from the money and an "iron reserve" equaling three months of expenses. Only after establishing this emergency fund should actual wealth building begin through strategic investments. This approach removes decision fatigue and makes wealth building automatic rather than willpower-dependent.

> "The rule of thumb is to put five to ten percent of your earnings aside every month - ideally immediately and almost automatically by standing order on a separate account at another bank."

**Quick Take**: Automated wealth building through systematic saving creates financial security without requiring constant discipline or willpower.

### Education as the Ultimate Investment
The book emphasizes that the most reliable investment is in oneself through targeted education and skill development. Unlike market-dependent investments, education consistently increases both market value and earning potential while providing personal satisfaction. The key is pursuing education with clear goals that distinguish you in your field rather than general knowledge. Making these achievements visible through strategic self-promotion further compounds the return on investment.

> "In the long term, education is the best and safest way to improve the quality of life and its earnings."

**Quick Take**: Investing in skills and education provides the most reliable returns while creating opportunities that compound over time.

### Entrepreneurial Mindset for Financial Independence
A critical insight is the shift from employee thinking to entrepreneurial thinking, regardless of your employment status. This means taking responsibility for your financial future, focusing on opportunities rather than limitations, and understanding that "trading time for money" is inherently unreliable in turbulent times. The book advocates developing multiple income streams and owning means of production rather than selling labor, emphasizing that true financial security comes from control rather than employment.

> "I am responsible for my own life and wealth. I think and act like an entrepreneur!"

**Quick Take**: Financial independence requires shifting from employee mindset to entrepreneurial thinking, taking full responsibility for creating multiple income streams.

## Action Items

- Implement automated savings: Set up a standing order to transfer 5-10% of income to a separate account at a different bank immediately upon receiving payment
- Conduct a comprehensive financial audit: List all assets, liabilities, income sources, and expenses to create complete financial clarity
- Identify and eliminate unnecessary commitments: Review subscriptions, memberships, and recurring expenses that don't add genuine value
- Invest in specific, marketable skills: Choose education that directly increases your earning potential rather than general knowledge
- Surround yourself with financially successful people: Evaluate your close relationships and seek mentors who embody your desired financial lifestyle
- Practice utility thinking: Before any purchase, consider whether you're seeking ownership or utility, and explore alternatives like renting or sharing

## Questions to Consider

- What are the "big stones" in your life that deserve priority over minor concerns?
- Which of your current possessions truly add value versus those that create ongoing financial and time obligations?
- How much of your income is trapped in the consumption cycle versus building sustainable wealth?
- What specific skills could you develop that would most significantly increase your market value?
- Are you thinking like an employee (selling time) or an entrepreneur (creating systems and opportunities)?
- What relationships in your life support versus hinder your financial goals, and what changes are needed?

## Conclusion
This book is worth your time if you're seeking a practical, psychology-aware approach to financial freedom that doesn't require sacrificing peace of mind. The biggest reason to read it is its integration of financial strategy with mindfulness and intentional living—showing that wealth building doesn't have to be stressful or all-consuming. Unlike many financial books that focus solely on tactics, this addresses the underlying mindset and lifestyle patterns that determine financial success.

The book's strength lies in its comprehensive approach that covers not just investment strategies but the psychological foundations of wealth building, including minimalism, relationship management, and the importance of education. While some concepts may feel familiar to experienced financial readers, the synthesis of relaxed wealth building with practical systems makes it valuable for anyone wanting to build wealth sustainably without becoming a prisoner to their finances.
