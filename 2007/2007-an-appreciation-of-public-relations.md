# An Appreciation of Public Relations
**Authors**: Ellen Gunning | **Year**: 2007
**Category**: Business & Finance

## Summary
"An Appreciation of Public Relations" is a comprehensive practical guide for both PR students and practicing professionals. Written by an experienced practitioner and educator, this book serves as a complete reference covering everything from foundational ethics and media relations to specialized areas like crisis management and corporate social responsibility. The author emphasizes that PR cannot be studied in isolation—it requires understanding the broader business, political, cultural, and social contexts within which organizations operate.

The book targets PR students (particularly those in HETAC-approved courses), practicing professionals, and anyone studying for the Higher Diploma in Arts in Public Relations. Readers will gain practical skills in news release writing, photography, event management, and strategic planning, all while maintaining strong ethical foundations. The author presents each chapter as standalone material but encourages cross-referencing for deeper learning.

## Core Insights

### Strategic Planning & SMART Objectives
At the heart of effective PR lies systematic planning and clear objective setting. The book emphasizes that every PR program must begin with SMART objectives—Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound. Without knowing what you're trying to achieve, you cannot design an effective program. This structured approach ensures PR efforts are purposeful, quantifiable, and aligned with organizational goals. The author advocates for twelve-month planning cycles with built-in evaluation methods to assess whether target audiences were reached and objectives achieved.

> "At the beginning of any public relations programme, you need to set objectives. If you do not know what you are trying to do, how can you design a programme that will achieve anything?"
**Quick Take**: Treat PR as a strategic management function, not just creative activities. Plan before execution.

### Two-Way Communication & Mutual Understanding
Modern PR is fundamentally about dialogue, not monologue. The book emphasizes that PR practitioners must facilitate genuine two-way communication between organizations and their publics. This goes beyond simply broadcasting messages—it involves listening, understanding stakeholder concerns, and building genuine relationships. The most effective PR creates mutual understanding and establishes beneficial relationships through ongoing communication rather than one-way persuasion campaigns.

> "Public Relations is the conscious organisation of communication. PR is a management function. The task of PR is: to achieve mutual understanding and to establish beneficial relationships, between the organisation and its publics and environment, through two-way communication."
**Quick Take**: Stop treating PR as megaphone communications—start conversations that build lasting relationships.

### Ethics & Integrity as Non-Negotiable
The book positions ethical behavior and truth-telling as the foundation of effective public relations. The author argues that integrity is the PR practitioner's most valuable asset—no client is worth compromising your reputation for. The professional codes of ethics aren't just guidelines but essential requirements for maintaining credibility and public trust. The book reminds us that PR has three ingredients: truth, concern for the public interest, and dialogue. Ethical lapses damage not just individual practitioners but the entire profession's credibility.

> "The only way to compel man to speak good of you, is to do good." - Voltaire
> "You're here for the long haul. The most valuable asset…is our integrity. No client, no matter how much they would spend, would be worth me telling a lie for." - Mary Finan
**Quick Take**: In PR, your personal and professional integrity is your primary currency—protect it at all costs.

### Media Relations & Adaptation to Digital Change
The book provides comprehensive guidance on working effectively with journalists while adapting to rapidly evolving media landscapes. Written during the transition from traditional to digital media, it captures a pivotal moment when blogs were becoming commonplace, media releases shifted to email distribution, and online newspapers emerged. The author emphasizes that PR practitioners must understand both traditional media operations and new digital platforms to effectively manage organizational communication and reputation.

> "Two new Dublin morning freesheets have been introduced, digital television is expanding rapidly and commercial and local stations are making sizeable inroads into national radio listenership figures. Reality television is everywhere... and now spans 'entertainment' and 'hard news'."
**Quick Take**: Master both traditional media relations and emerging digital platforms—effective PR requires understanding the entire media ecosystem.

### Crisis Management & Reputation Protection
The book provides practical frameworks for handling organizational crises and protecting reputations during challenging situations. Through real-world examples like the Dublin Gas explosion and hepatitis C scandal, the author demonstrates how poor crisis communication can severely damage organizational reputation. Effective crisis management requires immediate response, transparent communication, and taking responsibility rather than avoiding difficult conversations.

**Quick Take**: In crisis situations, silence is rarely golden—prompt, honest communication is essential for reputation protection.

## Action Items
- **Implement SMART objectives**: Always begin PR campaigns with specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, and time-bound goals
- **Prioritize ethical behavior**: Make truth and integrity your professional foundation—no exceptions
- **Master two-way communication**: Focus on dialogue and relationship building rather than one-way messaging
- **Stay current with media trends**: Continuously adapt to new digital platforms and changing media landscapes
- **Develop crisis preparedness**: Create communication protocols for handling organizational emergencies
- **Build stakeholder relationships**: Invest time in understanding and connecting with all relevant publics

## Questions to Consider
- How can PR practitioners balance organizational interests with public good while maintaining integrity?
- What does effective two-way communication look like in an age of social media and instant messaging?
- How should PR ethics evolve as digital platforms create new challenges for truth and transparency?
- What role should PR play in organizational strategy beyond media relations and publicity?
- How can practitioners demonstrate the value and ROI of systematic PR planning to management?

## Conclusion
"An Appreciation of Public Relations" is essential reading for anyone serious about professional PR practice. Its greatest strength lies in combining practical skills with strong ethical foundations and strategic thinking. The author successfully bridges the gap between academic theory and real-world application, making complex concepts accessible while maintaining professional rigor.

The book is worth your time because it emphasizes that effective PR is not about clever tricks or publicity stunts—it's about systematic planning, ethical communication, and building lasting relationships. In today's transparency-demanding world, the principles outlined in this book are more relevant than ever. The biggest reason to read it is to understand that PR, when practiced properly, is a strategic management function that builds organizational success through genuine stakeholder relationships rather than superficial publicity.
