# Words Are Spells
**Original Title**: Palavras são feitiços
**Author**: Amanda W. Tintori | **Year**: 2021
**Category**: Spirituality & Religion

## Summary
A profound collection of Portuguese poetry that explores the magical power of words as transformers of consciousness and reality. Through concise, evocative verses, the author weaves together themes of nature connection, personal transformation, emotional resilience, and the inherent wisdom found in both chaos and stillness. The book serves as both meditation and manifesto, inviting readers to reclaim their relationship with the magical aspects of existence that modern rationalism often dismisses.

This poetry collection speaks to seekers, mystics, and anyone feeling disconnected from their inner wisdom. Readers will find solace in verses that validate the power of intuition, the importance of embracing natural cycles, and the transformative potential of words consciously wielded. It's particularly valuable for those navigating personal transformation, seeking deeper connection with nature, or exploring the intersection of spirituality and self-expression.

## Core Insights

### Words as Magical Tools
The central thesis reveals words as spells that can heal or harm, create or destroy. Each thought, spoken word, and written expression carries vibration that ripples through our reality like a drop of ink in water. Written words especially become timeless magical resources that can be absorbed, studied, and applied repeatedly across different contexts.

> "The word is both the poison and the cure, whose seed is planted in thought."
> _Original: "a palavra é o veneno e a cura cuja semente é plantada no pensamento"_

**Quick Take**: Choose your words with the same care you would choose a spell—understanding that every expression carries creative power beyond your immediate intention.

### Embracing Natural Disorder
The poems celebrate chaos as the natural state of the universe, rejecting the illusion of control. Life exists in perpetual cycles, and fighting this movement creates suffering. Even our thoughts and emotions are constantly changing, forming new connections and associations. True peace comes from accepting this fundamental disorder rather than resisting it.

> "The fear of change is unnatural; it goes against reality. Life exists in cycles, in perpetual and synchronized movement."
> _Original: "o medo de mudar é antinatural vai contra a realidade a vida existe em ciclos em movimento perpétuo e sincronizado"_

**Quick Take**: Instead of trying to control life's currents, learn to flow with them while maintaining your inner direction.

### The Wisdom of the Body
The collection challenges mechanistic views of human consciousness, arguing that we are ancestral organisms rather than information-processing machines. Our bodies carry ancient wisdom that rational thought alone cannot access. True knowing comes from embodied experience and our connection to the natural world.

> "I am not a mental machine that processes everything around me; I am an ancestral organism that relates, nourishes or intoxicates itself, that absorbs or expels, attracts or repels."
> _Original: "eu não sou máquina mental que processa tudo que me rodeia sou organismo ancestral que se relaciona se nutre ou se intoxica que absorve ou expurga atrai ou repele"_

**Quick Take**: Trust your body's knowing as much as your mind's analysis; together they provide complete guidance.

### Sacred Transformation Through Pain
The poems explore trauma not as armor but as teacher. While pain teaches important lessons, we must eventually release the teacher to move forward. True strength comes not from carrying our wounds as shields but from integrating their wisdom while choosing optimism and presence.

> "Using pain as a shield is not strength; it's looking at the world through eyes of bitterness. I no longer delude myself into thinking trauma is armor."
> _Original: "usar a dor como escudo não é força é olhar o mundo com olhos de amargura não me iludo mais de achar que trauma é armadura"_

**Quick Take**: Learn your lessons from pain, then release the experience while keeping the wisdom.

## Takeaways

- **Practice conscious speech**: Recognize the creative power in your words and use them intentionally to shape your reality
- **Embrace life's natural cycles**: Accept that change is constant and fighting it creates unnecessary suffering
- **Honor your body's wisdom**: Trust embodied knowing alongside intellectual understanding
- **Transform pain into wisdom**: Allow difficult experiences to teach without becoming permanent shields or identities
- **Connect with nature's rhythms**: Find guidance in the natural world's patterns of growth, decay, and renewal
- **Value the unseen and magical**: Acknowledge that not all meaningful aspects of life can be measured or proven logically

## Questions to Consider

- How have your words created unexpected consequences in your life, both positive and negative?
- Where are you fighting natural cycles instead of flowing with them?
- What wisdom does your body hold that your mind dismisses?
- Which pains have you transformed into armor rather than lessons?
- How would your reality shift if you truly embraced chaos as natural order?

## Conclusion
This collection offers profound medicine for those feeling disconnected from their intuitive wisdom and the magical aspects of existence. The poems work like spells themselves, gradually shifting consciousness and opening readers to deeper ways of knowing. While accessible and emotionally resonant, the verses carry surprising philosophical depth that rewards repeated reading.

The book's greatest strength lies in its ability to validate experiences that modern rationalism often dismisses as irrational or meaningless. Amanda W. Tintori crafts verses that feel both ancient and contemporary, rooted in universal human experience while speaking directly to our current moment of disconnection.

For readers seeking to reclaim their relationship with the mystical, to heal their relationship with words, or to find poetry that speaks to both heart and mind, "Words Are Spells" offers unexpected magic. It reminds us that we are not separate from the world we observe, but active participants in its continuous creation through every thought, word, and action.
