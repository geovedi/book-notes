# Witherward
**Authors**: Hannah Mathewson | **Year**: 2021
**Category**: Fantasy & Science Fiction

## Summary
*Witherward* is a dark urban fantasy novel that follows Ilsa, a young woman in Victorian London who possesses a dangerous secret: she's a Changeling with the ability to shapeshift into animals. Living as a magician's assistant and pickpocket, Ilsa has always believed her powers were unnatural, a curse from the devil as described by the cruel orphanage matron who raised her. When her best friend Martha is brutally murdered by supernatural beings called Oracles who mistake Martha for her, Ilsa's world is shattered.

Rescued by Captain Fowler, a superhuman mercenary from another realm, Ilsa discovers she's not alone—there's an entire magical world coexisting with London. The Witherward is a parallel version of London where magical beings known as Changelings, Oracles, Sorcerers, Whisperers, and other factions live in tense, often violent coexistence. Ilsa learns she's from a powerful Changeling family in the Camden territory, and that her parents died protecting her from political enemies.

As Ilsa navigates this dangerous new world, she must uncover the truth about her family's past, master her shapeshifting abilities, and survive the complex political landscape where different magical factions vie for power. With the help of allies including her cousin Cassia, the enigmatic Fowler, and other Changelings, Ilsa discovers that her powers may be the key to either uniting the fractured magical communities or destroying them completely.

This novel appeals to fans of dark urban fantasy, political intrigue, and stories about found family. Readers who enjoy complex world-building, morally ambiguous characters, and magic systems with deep lore will find plenty to love in Mathewson's debut.

## Core Insights

### The Discovery of True Identity
Ilsa's journey from believing she's "devil's get" to understanding she's part of a powerful magical lineage represents the classic fantasy theme of discovering hidden heritage. Her shapeshifting ability, once a source of shame and fear, becomes her greatest strength in the Witherward. This transformation highlights how our perceived weaknesses can become our greatest assets when viewed in the right context.

> "You ain't of this world," he said. The truth of it chimed through her, rousing equal parts horror and excitement.
> "No," he said. "But then, my lady, neither are you."

**Quick Take**: Sometimes what makes us feel like outsiders in one context can make us belong in another.

### The Complexity of Moral Choices
The novel excels at presenting morally ambiguous situations where right and wrong are rarely clear-cut. Fowler, who saves Ilsa but also kills without hesitation, represents the grey areas of morality in this world. The Changelings are neither purely good nor evil—they're survivors doing what's necessary to protect their own in a hostile environment.

**Quick Take**: Survival often requires making difficult choices that don't fit neatly into conventional morality.

### Found Family vs. Blood Family
Ilsa's relationships with her found family—Martha in the human world, and later Cassia and other Changelings—contrast with her unknown blood family. The novel explores how family can be chosen rather than inherited, and how loyalty can be stronger than blood ties. Her eventual discovery about her true family adds layers to this theme.

**Quick Take**: Family is defined by love and loyalty, not necessarily by blood relations.

### Power and Political Corruption
The Witherward's magical factions are locked in complex political struggles that mirror real-world power dynamics. The Oracles' addiction to substances that enhance their abilities, the territorial disputes between factions, and the manipulation of magical power for political ends all serve as commentary on how power corrupts and how those in control often maintain it through addiction and dependency.

**Quick Take**: Power structures often rely on keeping others dependent and divided.

### The Nature of Reality and Perception
The dual-world structure plays with perception and reality—what seems normal in one world is magical in another, and vice versa. The novel suggests that reality is subjective and that understanding different perspectives is crucial for true wisdom.

**Quick Take**: Reality is often a matter of perspective, and expanding your worldview reveals hidden truths.

## Key Themes
- **Identity and Self-Acceptance**: Learning to embrace who you truly are rather than who others say you should be
- **Survival and Adaptation**: The struggle to survive in hostile environments and the moral compromises it requires
- **Power and Corruption**: How power corrupts individuals and systems, and the price of maintaining control
- **Found Family**: The importance of choosing your own family and the strength of chosen bonds
- **Truth and Deception**: The difficulty of distinguishing truth from lies in a world of political intrigue
- **Prejudice and Fear**: How fear of the unknown leads to prejudice and violence

- What role does addiction play in maintaining power structures within the Witherward?

- In what ways does the novel explore the tension between individual morality and collective survival?

## Conclusion
*Witherward* is a remarkable debut that successfully blends dark urban fantasy with political intrigue and compelling character development. Mathewson's world-building is intricate and original, creating a magical version of London that feels both familiar and terrifyingly alien. The novel's strength lies in its complex characters who inhabit morally grey areas, forced to make difficult choices in a world where survival often means compromising one's ideals.

The book is particularly worth reading for its nuanced exploration of identity, power, and family. Ilsa's journey from a frightened outcast to someone who must decide whether to embrace her destiny is compelling and emotionally resonant. The magical system is well-developed and integral to the plot, with shapeshifting serving as both a literal and metaphorical representation of identity and adaptation.

While the dark themes and violence may not suit all readers, those who enjoy gritty urban fantasy with political depth will find *Witherward* immensely satisfying. It's a promising start to what could become a major fantasy series, and it establishes Mathewson as a voice to watch in the genre.

The biggest reason to read this book is its perfect balance of action, character development, and intricate world-building. It delivers on its promise of magic, intrigue, and emotional depth, making it a worthwhile investment for fantasy fans looking for something fresh and sophisticated.
