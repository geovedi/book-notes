# Tower Of Thorns
**Authors**: Laura Greenwood | **Year**: 2021
**Category**: Fantasy & Science Fiction

## Summary
Rapunzel has grown up knowing her prophecy by heart—that the first time she falls in love, her heart will be broken, and she'll end up locked in a tower. When her controlling Mother enrolls her at Grimm Academy, a school where fairy tale characters go to avoid their fate, Rapunzel finds herself drawn to Gavin, a handsome Lord. Despite their prophecies warning them away from love, the two can't resist their connection, setting in motion events that lead to an even bigger betrayal than Rapunzel ever anticipated.

This young adult fantasy romance is perfect for readers who enjoy fairy tale retellings with a twist, academy settings, and stories about breaking free from predetermined destinies. Fans of strong-willed heroines, sweet romance, and magical school settings will find themselves captivated by this reimagining of Rapunzel.

## Core Insights

### The Weight of False Prophecies
Rapunzel has spent her entire life living under the shadow of a prophecy that may not even be real. Her controlling Mother has used the threat of heartbreak and imprisonment to keep her isolated and obedient, demonstrating how fear can be weaponized to control others. The central conflict emerges when Rapunzel decides to risk everything for the chance at authentic connection.

> "Despite my misgivings, I stay silent. I don't want to run from my prophecy. If it's going to happen, then I want to face it head-on. There's no way it's going to get the better of me."

**Quick Take**: Sometimes the biggest barriers we face are the ones others have placed in our minds, and true freedom comes from questioning what we've been taught to believe.

### Finding Courage in Connection
At Grimm Academy, Rapunzel experiences friendship and romantic love for the first time, discovering that human connection gives her the strength to challenge her Mother's control. Her relationship with Gavin becomes both her greatest vulnerability and her greatest source of courage, showing how love can transform us from fearful compliance into brave resistance.

> "My heart skips a beat at the sight. It's more than twice the size of my chambers in the cottage I share with Mother, and there's bright sunlight streaming in from a large window."

**Quick Take**: The courage to change our circumstances often comes from the relationships that show us what we've been missing.

### The Ultimate Betrayal
The story's major twist reveals that Rapunzel's Mother is not her mother at all, but a healer who stole her as payment when her real parents couldn't pay for services. This revelation reframes the entire story—not just about controlling parenting, but about a more sinister form of exploitation and betrayal.

> "You were a payment. I gave my services as a healer to your parents, and they didn't pay me. By the laws of my kind, I can take what's most precious to them."

**Quick Take**: Sometimes the deepest betrayals come from those we've trusted most completely, revealing that our entire reality may be built on lies.

### Choosing Your Own Destiny
In the climactic confrontation, Rapunzel must decide whether to continue living by the rules set for her or to forge her own path. With the help of the Huntsmen and her own growing strength, she rejects her false prophecy and chooses love over fear, demonstrating that we have the power to rewrite our own stories.

> "I had a prophecy too. This is on both of us," Gavin assures her, showing how they face their challenges together rather than as victims of fate.

**Quick Take**: True freedom comes not from avoiding pain, but from choosing to face challenges on our own terms rather than living by others' rules.

## Conclusion
*Tower of Thorns* offers a fresh take on the Rapunzel fairy tale, transforming it from a passive waiting-for-rescue story into an empowering narrative about choosing your own destiny. While the plot follows some familiar YA romance tropes, the revelation about the Mother's true identity and motivations adds depth to what could have been a simple story about defying prophecy.

This is worth your time if you enjoy academy settings, fairy tale retellings, and stories about finding strength through love and friendship. The biggest reason to read it is for the satisfying moment when Rapunzel realizes she has the power to break free—not just from her tower, but from the psychological chains that have held her captive her entire life.
