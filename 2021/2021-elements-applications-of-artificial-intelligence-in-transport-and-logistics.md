# Elements Applications of Artificial Intelligence in Transport and Logistics
**Authors**: Dmitry Abramov, Alexander Korpukov, Vadim Shmal, Pavel Minakov | **Year**: 2021

## Summary
"Elements Applications of Artificial Intelligence in Transport and Logistics" is an academic exploration of AI's transformative potential in transportation systems. The book traces the historical evolution of artificial intelligence from its origins in the 1950s to modern applications, while examining theoretical frameworks and practical implementations in transport and logistics sectors. Rather than providing a technical manual, it offers a comprehensive foundation for understanding how AI technologies can revolutionize mobility, supply chain management, and transportation infrastructure.

This book is ideal for academics, researchers, students, and transportation professionals seeking to understand the theoretical underpinnings of AI applications in transport. Readers will gain historical context on AI development, insight into current research directions, and a framework for evaluating future AI implementations in transportation systems. The authors successfully bridge computer science theory with practical transportation challenges, making complex concepts accessible without oversimplification.

## Core Insights

### The Evolutionary Foundation of AI in Transport
Artificial intelligence originated in the 1950s when scientists believed machines could exhibit intelligent behavior beyond human brain capabilities. The field evolved through distinct phases: from early symbolic logic systems (1950s-1970s) to neural networks (1970s-1980s) and modern machine learning approaches. Each technological leap expanded possibilities for transportation applications, from basic automation to sophisticated autonomous systems.

> "The term artificial intelligence began to appear in the late 1960s when scientists began to make strides in this area. Some scientists believed that in future, computers would take on tasks that were too complex for the human brain, thus achieving intelligence."
>
**Quick Take**: Understanding AI's evolutionary path helps transportation professionals anticipate future developments and make informed decisions about technology investments.

### Neural Networks and Deep Learning Transformations
Modern artificial neural networks represent a paradigm shift in AI capabilities, enabling systems to explore concepts and relationships without predefined parameters. The rise of computing power, particularly GPU technology in the 2000s, made deep learning practical for complex transportation problems. Convolutional neural networks (CNNs) and other architectures now power everything from traffic prediction to vehicle control systems.

> "With the latest advances in multi-core and GPU technology, training neural networks with multiple GPUs is possible at a fraction of the cost of conventional training. One of the most popular examples is GPU deep learning."
>
**Quick Take**: Transportation organizations should invest in GPU infrastructure and deep learning expertise to remain competitive in AI implementation.

### Autonomous Vehicles: Reality and Challenges
Self-driving technology represents one of the most visible AI applications in transport, but significant challenges remain. The book examines both the promise and limitations of autonomous vehicles, citing real-world incidents including Google's first fatal accident in 2016. Companies like Tesla and Google are developing fully automated vehicles, though cost and scalability concerns persist. Statistics show 94 accidents involving unmanned vehicles in 2017 alone.

> "Google is working on a fully automated vehicle that can operate without human intervention, although this technology is also likely to be more expensive and less scalable than cars with human drivers."
>
**Quick Take**: While autonomous vehicle technology advances rapidly, transportation planners should prepare for gradual adoption rather than immediate replacement of human drivers.

### Railway Intelligence and Optimization
Rail transport applications focus heavily on predictive modeling and capacity optimization using neural networks and simulation techniques. Researchers have developed sophisticated models for predicting passenger traffic, determining railway throughput capacity, and optimizing train schedules. These applications demonstrate how AI can improve efficiency in existing transportation infrastructure without requiring complete technological overhaul.

> "Models for predicting passenger traffic in rail and air transport" and "Determining the available throughput capacity of the railways using simulation for intelligent management of the transportation process"
>
**Quick Take**: Railway operators should prioritize AI implementation for traffic prediction and capacity optimization to maximize existing infrastructure investments.

### Warehouse Automation and Logistics Robotics
AI-powered robots are increasingly suitable for various logistics roles, from healthcare to food production and household products. The book discusses Brain Machine Interface (BMIP) technology that enables robots to communicate and work with humans directly through neural connections. Assembly line robots and warehouse automation systems demonstrate practical AI applications already transforming logistics operations.

> "For many years, robots have been viewed as tools. Over the past decades, new technologies have improved the ability of robots to perform various tasks, such as working on assembly lines and in warehouses."
>
**Quick Take**: Logistics companies should gradually introduce AI-powered automation while preparing workforce for human-robot collaboration models.

### Mathematical Optimization as Transportation Backbone
Linear programming and convex optimization techniques form the mathematical foundation for AI applications in transport logistics. These approaches solve complex problems by minimizing overall costs across transportation networks. The GDL (Gradient Descent Learning) algorithm and other optimization methods enable efficient resource allocation, route planning, and operational decision-making.

> "Many problems can be solved with linear programming, the goal of which is to minimize overall costs."
>
**Quick Take**: Transportation professionals should develop mathematical optimization skills as fundamental requirements for AI implementation in logistics operations.
