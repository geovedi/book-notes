# The Book Every Leader Needs to Read
**Authors**: Colin Iles, Warren Moss, Travis Gale, Thabang Skwambane, Adriaan Groenewald, Deshun Bester, Nyimpini Mabunda, Alistair King, Roze Phillips, Dov Girnun, Jonathan Ayache, Enver Groenewald, Jason Levin, Ian Russell, Lwando Xaso, Adama Sanneh, Richard Mulholland, Veli Ngubane, Dr Alistair Mokoena, Jeanette Marais, Bonang Mohale, Abdullah Verachia, Erik Kruger | **Year**: 2021

## Summary
"The Book Every Leader Needs to Read" is a comprehensive collection of leadership insights from over 20 South African business leaders, entrepreneurs, and thought leaders. Each chapter presents a unique perspective on leadership, drawing from personal experiences, failures, and successes across various industries. The book addresses the critical leadership challenges of our time—from navigating crises and building resilient teams to developing purpose-driven organizations that thrive in uncertainty.

This collection is particularly valuable for emerging leaders, entrepreneurs, and executives seeking practical wisdom rather than theoretical frameworks. The contributors share real-world stories of leadership in action, making the insights immediately applicable to anyone looking to enhance their leadership effectiveness in today's rapidly changing business environment.

## Core Insights

### Purposeful, Curious and Courageous Leadership
Colin Iles presents a powerful framework built on three essential pillars of great leadership. Purpose-driven leaders create missions that win hearts over minds, understanding that emotions drive every decision humans make. They craft compelling narratives that transcend transactional goals, building movements rather than simply managing teams.

> "Do you want to sell sugar water for the rest of your life, or do you want to come with me and change the world?"

This famous question from Steve Jobs to John Sculley exemplifies how visionary leaders elevate ordinary roles into extraordinary callings. The most effective leaders set purposeful targets that emotionally resonate with their teams, offering people the opportunity to do something that feels good in its own right.

Curiosity serves as the engine of leadership growth. Great leaders remain intensely inquisitive about both their industry and their people, continually experimenting with different approaches to bring out the best in their teams. They understand that leadership style cannot remain static—it must adapt based on circumstances, feedback, and results.

**Quick Take**: If you believe you and your team know everything already, you can only ever go backwards.

### Leaders Are Made, Not Born
Warren Moss challenges the myth of innate leadership through the powerful story of Ernest Shackleton's Antarctic expedition. When Shackleton's ship became trapped and eventually sank, his leadership was forged in crisis. He pivoted from the original goal of reaching the South Pole to a new mission: ensuring every crew member survived.

The key insight is that leadership emerges through deliberate actions and conscious choices, especially during challenging circumstances. Shackleton's journey demonstrates how great leaders acknowledge brutal facts while maintaining unwavering commitment to their people's survival and success.

This perspective liberates aspiring leaders from the pressure of having to be "born leaders." Instead, they can focus on developing specific behaviors and mindsets that effective leaders demonstrate consistently.

**Quick Take**: Leadership is not about perfection—it's about how you respond when circumstances change dramatically.

### The Power of Storytelling in Leadership
Dov Girnun reveals that those who tell the best stories make the best leaders. Research shows that while only 5% of people remember statistics from presentations, 63% remember stories. Stories activate the entire brain, creating emotional connections that facts and figures alone cannot achieve.

Great leaders understand that stories are memorable, impactful, and create personal connections. The most successful business leaders are consistently the best storytellers, able to articulate compelling narratives about their business vision and personal journey that resonate with teams, investors, and customers.

Effective storytelling isn't about fabrication—it's about authenticity. The most powerful stories are real ones that demonstrate vulnerability, resilience, and the human side of leadership journeys.

**Quick Take**: When we share statistics, people understand, but don't feel. When we share a story, our whole brain is activated, and real meaning is extracted.

### Courageous Decision-Making in Uncertainty
Jonathan Ayache, CEO of Lift Airlines, demonstrates leadership courage through the counterintuitive decision to launch an airline during the COVID-19 pandemic. His insights reveal that courageous leadership isn't about fearlessness—it's about making calculated risks despite fear.

Effective leaders quantify risks and make decisions based on structured analysis, but they also recognize that not every decision can be measured perfectly. Sometimes, worse than making a mistake is taking too long to make a decision, or not making one at all.

Ayache emphasizes the importance of surrounding yourself with people who complement your risk appetite. If you're naturally risk-averse, having team members who are more comfortable taking calculated risks creates balance and better decision-making.

**Quick Take**: Sometimes, worse than making a mistake is taking too long to make a decision, or not making a decision at all.

### Servant Leadership and Selflessness
Bonang Mohale presents a profound redefinition of leadership as genuine obsession with the development of others. True leadership transcends selfishness and unselfishness to achieve selflessness—doing whatever is required for the betterment of others, even when no one is watching.

Management focuses on getting things done through other people, but leadership elevates this to serving others' growth and development. The most authentic leaders have the confidence to stand alone, the courage to make tough decisions, and the compassion to listen to others' needs.

This perspective transforms leadership from a position of authority to a posture of service, where success is measured by the growth and development of those you lead.

**Quick Take**: We could achieve so much if we did not care about who takes the credit.

## Action Items

- **Lead with Purpose**: Before setting profit or growth targets, identify the deeper purpose your organization serves. Craft a compelling narrative that emotionally resonates with your team and stakeholders.

- **Cultivate Curiosity**: Maintain intense curiosity about your industry, your people, and new approaches. Regularly experiment with different leadership styles and be willing to adapt based on feedback.

- **Develop Your Story**: Create and refine your personal leadership story. Practice sharing it authentically to inspire and connect with others on an emotional level.

- **Practice Courageous Decision-Making**: Learn to quantify risks and make decisions despite uncertainty. Surround yourself with team members who complement your risk appetite.

- **Embrace Servant Leadership**: Shift your focus from personal achievement to the development and success of others. Celebrate your team's wins more than your own.

## Questions to Consider

- What mission beyond profit would inspire your team to give their best effort every day?
- Where have you stopped being curious about your leadership approach, and what assumptions need challenging?
- What personal stories could you share to inspire and connect more authentically with your team?
- What calculated risks have you been avoiding that could propel your organization forward?
- How would your leadership change if you focused entirely on developing others rather than your own advancement?

## Conclusion
"The Book Every Leader Needs to Read" delivers on its promise by providing practical, real-world wisdom from leaders who have navigated extraordinary challenges. The collection's strength lies in its diversity of perspectives and authentic storytelling, making complex leadership concepts accessible and immediately applicable.

This book is worth your time because it avoids theoretical jargon in favor of actionable insights tested in real business environments. The biggest reason to read it is the opportunity to learn from leaders who have successfully guided organizations through crisis, transformation, and growth—providing you with a toolkit of proven strategies for your own leadership journey.

The most valuable takeaway is that great leadership isn't about possessing innate qualities, but about deliberately developing the pillars of purpose, curiosity, courage, and service. Any leader committed to continuous improvement can apply these principles to create more meaningful impact in their organization and community.
