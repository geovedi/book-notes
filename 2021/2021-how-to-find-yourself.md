# How to Find Yourself
**Authors**: Nick Trenton | **Year**: 2021
**Category**: Self-Help & Personal Development

## Summary
This practical guide helps readers navigate the journey of self-discovery through understanding personality theories, identifying personal values, and examining the stories we tell ourselves. The book addresses why so many people feel disconnected from their authentic selves and provides concrete frameworks for reconnecting with who they truly are.

Perfect for anyone feeling lost, inauthentic, or unsure about their life direction, this book offers psychological tools including the Big Five personality traits, values clarification exercises, and self-awareness techniques. Readers will learn to distinguish between their "real self" and the "false self" created by external expectations, family conditioning, and societal pressures.

## Core Insights

### The Real Self vs. The False Self
Many of us live with identities that aren't genuinely our own—masks created by family expectations, cultural conditioning, and media influence. The "false self" develops when we learn to prioritize others' approval over our authentic needs. Your "real self" never disappeared; it's just been buried under layers of external expectations and unconscious conditioning from childhood. Recognizing this distinction is the first step toward authentic living.

> "We might come into the world complete and with a strong sense of what we want, but we can soon lose sight of our own internal compass."
> **Quick Take**: Self-awareness begins with acknowledging that you may have been living someone else's version of your life.

### Finding Your Core Values
Values aren't chosen arbitrarily—they're clarified by examining what you genuinely care about most deeply. When you're confused about life decisions, it's usually because you lack clarity about your true values. The process requires abandoning externally-imposed values and discovering what feels authentically important to you, whether that's family connection, creative expression, financial independence, or service to others.

> "A value is a judgment that makes claims about the priorities we hold in life. They are principles, rules, or beliefs that give meaning to our lives."
> **Quick Take**: Your daily decisions reveal your true values more accurately than your stated intentions.

### The Big Five Personality Framework
The most scientifically validated personality model (OCEAN) helps you understand your natural tendencies: Openness to experience, Conscientiousness, Extroversion, Agreeableness, and Neuroticism. Unlike binary systems, this model recognizes that everyone embodies all these traits to varying degrees. Understanding where you fall on each spectrum helps you leverage your natural strengths and work with your challenges rather than against them.

> "According to this theory, it's how much of each and where we land in the range between the extremes that determine our unique personality."
> **Quick Take**: Personality isn't about being one type or another—it's about understanding your unique combination of traits.

### The Stories We Create About Ourselves
Your identity is largely composed of the narratives you tell about your experiences, achievements, and challenges. By examining these personal stories—especially through exercises like the "seven stories" method—you can identify patterns revealing your authentic values, strengths, and motivations. Your stories hold clues to what truly matters to you and how you naturally operate in the world.

> "The narratives and stories we tell ourselves about who we are constitute a large part of our sense of self."
> **Quick Take**: Analyzing your personal achievements and challenges reveals more about your authentic self than personality tests alone.

### Self-Awareness Through Action Analysis
The most reliable path to self-knowledge comes from examining your actual behaviors, not your intentions or thoughts. Your actions reveal your true priorities, values, and personality more accurately than conscious self-reflection. Questions like "What feels effortless versus exhausting?" or "What would you do with one month left to live?" cut through self-deception and reveal what genuinely motivates you.

> "The best way to gain self-awareness is to ask about your behaviors and actions, not your intentions or thoughts."
> **Quick Take**: Your calendar and checkbook tell you more about your values than your journal entries.

- "When you know yourself, you're happier. Plain and simple. This is because you don't spend effort and time on avoiding or denying who you are. You can relax and be open."

- "Media of all kinds works constantly to send you the message: you are incomplete. You need something else. The ideal customer, reader, app-user is one who feels bad about themselves, and who doesn't really know what they want."

- "Brick by brick, we can build a prison around ourselves with our lifestyle habits and our choices. We are nothing more than the sum of all the tiny actions we take day in and day out."

## Action Items

- **Values Clarification**: List your 25 proudest achievements, narrow them to your top 7, and analyze what values they reveal. Use this insight to guide future decisions.

- **Personality Assessment**: Take the Big Five personality test to understand where you fall on the OCEAN spectrum and leverage your natural strengths.

- **Behavior Analysis**: Track your actual activities for one week to identify what energizes versus drains you—this reveals your authentic priorities.

- **Story Examination**: Write detailed accounts of meaningful life experiences to identify patterns in your motivations, strengths, and values.

- **Daily Alignment Check**: Each evening, ask whether your actions that day reflected your stated values and authentic self.

## Questions to Consider

- What family roles and expectations might be shaping your current identity?
- Which of your daily activities feel authentic versus performative?
- What would you pursue if external approval wasn't a factor?
- How might media and social comparison be influencing your self-concept?
- What stories do you tell yourself about who you are and what you're capable of?

## Conclusion
This book is worth your time if you're feeling disconnected from your authentic self or unclear about your life direction. Trenton provides practical, psychologically-grounded tools for self-discovery rather than superficial personality typing. The strength of this guide lies in its emphasis on values clarification and behavioral analysis over theoretical frameworks.

The biggest reason to read it is the concrete roadmap it provides for transitioning from living as your "false self" to expressing your authentic nature. While some personality frameworks get more attention, this book's combination of accessible psychology and practical exercises makes it valuable for anyone ready to do the real work of self-discovery. The journey to self-knowledge isn't quick, but this guide provides reliable tools for the path.
