# Lamps and Lies
**Authors**: Laura Greenwood | **Year**: 2021
**Category**: Fantasy & Science Fiction

## Summary
Lamps and Lies is a captivating retelling of the Aladdin fairy tale set in the magical world of Grimm Academy. The story follows Alyeesah, a street-smart orphan who discovers a magical ring containing a genie and uses her first wish to transform herself into a Princess so she can attend the prestigious academy and escape life on the streets.

As Alyeesah navigates the challenges of royal life while maintaining her fabricated identity, she becomes entangled in a dangerous prophecy, blackmail schemes, and an unexpected romance with Prince Enver. The story explores themes of identity, truth, and belonging as Alyeesah struggles between the lies that gave her everything she ever wanted and the authenticity her heart desires.

Perfect for fans of fairy tale retellings, young adult romance, and stories about finding your place in the world when you don't quite fit in.

## Core Insights

### The Protagonist's Journey
Alyeesah begins as a resourceful street thief with a conscience - she can't even bring herself to steal from a vulnerable woman, instead helping her and only keeping an apple she finds on the ground. This fundamental goodness stays with her throughout her transformation from commoner to princess, making her question whether the lies sustaining her new life are worth the cost.

> "I have to eat, I have to eat, I have to eat. The more times I think it, the less I feel inclined to go along with my plan."

**Quick Take**: Her internal conflict between survival and morality drives the entire story, making her both relatable and admirable.

### The Wish That Changes Everything
Alyeesah's first wish is cunningly specific - to become "the Princess of a made-up kingdom" with all the trappings needed to maintain the pretense. This careful wording shows her intelligence and forward-thinking, setting up the foundation for her academy experience while also foreshadowing the complications that arise from living a manufactured identity.

> "I wish to be the Princess of a made-up kingdom, and have all the things I need to maintain that pretence, including the right wardrobe, riches, and trappings, none of which will run out, so I can attend Grimm Academy."

**Quick Take**: The story demonstrates how getting what you want through shortcuts often creates more problems than it solves.

### The Genie's Unconventional Role
Unlike traditional genie stories, this genie takes an active interest in Alyeesah's development, spending months training her for royal life. He becomes a mentor and friend rather than just a wish-granting servant, adding emotional depth to their magical relationship and providing guidance that goes beyond mere magical assistance.

**Quick Take**: The story suggests that true magic lies in the relationships we build, not just the wishes we make.

### The Prophecy and Blackmail Thread
The narrative introduces a mysterious prophecy that threatens Alyeesah's carefully constructed world, adding urgency and danger to her academy experience. Combined with blackmail attempts from unknown adversaries, these external forces create tension and drive the plot toward its climactic resolution.

**Quick Take**: Even the most perfect lies eventually crumble under pressure - truth has a way of surfacing when it matters most.

### The Romance and Resolution
Alyeesah's relationship with Prince Enver develops amid her deception, creating genuine emotional stakes. The resolution shows that true love accepts authenticity, and that finding happiness sometimes means letting go of the lies that helped you survive.

> "Because now you really are Princess Alyeesah?"
> "Just Princess Aly. But no. Never better because today I got to become your wife."

**Quick Take**: Real connection requires vulnerability and truth, making the risk of exposure worth taking for genuine relationships.

## Best Quotes
- "Lies always catch up with the ones keeping them, and Alyeesah has several."
- "After everything I've been through, the wishes, the blackmail, and the lies, it's hard to believe I'm here. Or that I'm a Princess for real. But the most wonderful thing about it all, is that I found someone to share my adventures with."
- "I promised myself after everyone found out the truth about me that I'd never tell another lie in my life. And I've stuck to that promise."

## Key Takeaways
- Authentic identity is more valuable than any wish-granted facade
- True friendship and love require honesty, even when it's risky
- The skills that help us survive (resourcefulness, quick thinking) can become our greatest strengths
- Sometimes the greatest adventure is discovering who you really are
- Magical solutions don't always solve fundamental human problems

## Questions to Consider
- What would you wish for if you had three wishes, and what unintended consequences might follow?
- Is it ever acceptable to lie about your identity to achieve your dreams?
- How do our backgrounds shape our opportunities, and what are we willing to do to change our circumstances?
- What defines true royalty - bloodline, character, or something else entirely?

## Conclusion
Lamps and Lies offers a fresh take on the classic Aladdin story, transforming it into a tale of self-discovery and young love set against the backdrop of a magical academy. The book successfully balances fairy tale romance with deeper questions about identity, truth, and belonging.

The biggest reason to read this book is its heartwarming message about authenticity and the power of being true to oneself, even when it's terrifying. Alyeesah's journey from street thief to princess (for real) shows that the most magical transformations happen not through wishes, but through courage, integrity, and allowing others to see who we truly are. The romance subplot adds emotional depth without overshadowing the main character's personal growth, making this a satisfying read for anyone who believes that everyone deserves their happily ever after - especially when they earn it through honesty rather than deception.
