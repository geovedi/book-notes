# How to Learn Project Management Quickly!
**Authors**: Andrei Besedin | **Year**: 2019
**Category**: Business & Finance

## Summary
This practical guide serves as a comprehensive introduction to project management for aspiring professionals. The book breaks down the essential skills, methodologies, and career pathways needed to succeed in project management, emphasizing that real-world experience matters more than certifications alone.

Perfect for beginners or those transitioning into project management roles, this guide offers actionable frameworks for managing teams, understanding methodologies, and developing the people skills that often determine project success more than technical knowledge.

## Core Insights

### 1. Understanding the Project Manager Role
Project managers are multi-faceted leaders who connect projects to business goals while managing team dynamics. Unlike the common definition of management as "getting work done through others," true project management involves proactive decision-making and deep engagement with both people and processes.

> "It is your responsibility to connect the projects to the business world and clients too. You shouldn't forget that the success of the project lies in your hands."

**Quick Take**: Project management is less about delegation and more about active involvement, guidance, and taking responsibility for outcomes.

### 2. Essential Skills Beyond Technical Knowledge
Success requires mastering nine knowledge areas but also developing crucial people skills. Many technically skilled managers fail because they excel with "things" (computers, money) but struggle with human dynamics.

**The Nine Essential Areas:**
- Project Integration Management
- Project Scope Management
- Project Time Management
- Project Cost Management
- Project Quality Management
- Project Human Resources Management
- Project Communications Management
- Project Risk Management
- Project Procurement Management

> "The majority of managers fail massively in that aspect. Why? Most managers today could excellently understand how to use inanimate things - computers and money, to attain their peak performance, but when it comes to allowing people to perform at their optimum, there is little or zero knowledge to that."

**Quick Take**: Technical expertise gets you hired, but people skills determine your success as a project manager.

### 3. Career Development Strategy
There's no single "secret" to project management success. The book emphasizes that experience gained through mistakes and continuous learning matters more than certifications alone. Aspiring PMs should start with smaller projects or internships to build practical knowledge.

> "When a project manager makes mistakes, and he keeps learning from them, such individual will be fit for the job as the days go by."

**Quick Take**: Focus on gaining diverse project experience rather than chasing certifications - real-world application builds competence faster.

### 4. Methodology Mastery
Understanding different project management approaches allows you to select the right framework for each project. The book covers eight major methodologies, from traditional Waterfall to modern Agile approaches.

**Key Methodologies:**
- **Agile**: Flexible, iterative development with continuous collaboration
- **Scrum**: Two-week cycles with daily stand-up meetings
- **Kanban**: Visual task management with three columns (To-Do, Progress, Done)
- **Waterfall**: Sequential, structured approach best for physical products
- **PRINCE2**: Controlled environment with shared authority between project boards and managers

> "If you think knowing Agile as a PM means knowing everything, then you are still in the dark, and the truth is far from you."

**Quick Take**: Methodology selection should match project type, company culture, and team composition rather than following trends.

### 5. Team Management Excellence
Projects fail more often due to people issues than technical problems. Building committed teams requires starting with team development from day one, involving everyone in planning, and understanding the four stages of team development.

**Team Development Stages:**
1. **Forming**: Members look for direction and structure
2. **Storming**: Doubts and questions arise; needs psychological support
3. **Norming**: Conflicts resolve; group identity forms
4. **Performing**: Team works efficiently with minimal supervision

> "The significant causes of project failure are the people who fail to manage projects well."

**Quick Take**: Invest time in understanding team dynamics and individual motivations - this investment pays off in project success.

### 6. Authority and Leadership
Instead of waiting for organizational authority, effective project managers earn it by making proactive decisions and taking responsibility. When you consistently make sound decisions aligned with company goals, authority naturally follows.

> "Many see power as what the organization has bestowed on them, yet, in the real sense, many individuals who aren't keen about authority earn it officially."

**Quick Take**: Demonstrate leadership through action and responsibility rather than waiting for formal authority.

> "A project should be seen as temporal that is geared towards producing a unique outcome in the form of service, result or product."

> "The primary focus is on giving value to customer, eliminating bottlenecks, and improves the process while eliminating waste."

## Action Items

- **Start Small**: Begin with managing smaller projects or teams to build experience
- **Develop People Skills**: Focus on communication, empathy, and team dynamics as much as technical knowledge
- **Learn Multiple Methodologies**: Understand at least 3-4 different project management approaches
- **Build Authority**: Make proactive decisions aligned with organizational goals rather than waiting for permission
- **Master Tools**: Get comfortable with project management software and collaboration platforms
- **Practice Communication**: Learn to give clear instructions and provide helpful guidance, not just tasks

## Questions to Consider

- Are you naturally drawn to organizing groups and achieving collective goals?
- Do you enjoy adapting to new situations and solving problems collaboratively?
- Can you handle pressure and make decisions without emotional attachment to outcomes?
- Are you comfortable with the idea that project management involves continuous learning from mistakes?

## Conclusion
This book delivers practical wisdom for aspiring project managers, emphasizing that success comes from experience, people skills, and adaptive thinking rather than just certifications. It's worth reading if you're considering a project management career, as it provides realistic expectations and actionable guidance for getting started.

The biggest takeaway is that project management is fundamentally about people and relationships, not just processes and tools. Success requires both technical competence and emotional intelligence, making it a challenging but rewarding career path for those who enjoy bringing order to complexity while helping others achieve their best work.
