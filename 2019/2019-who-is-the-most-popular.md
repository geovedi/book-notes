# Who is the Most Popular?
**Original Title**: K for Kara 20 - Hvem er mest populær?
**Authors**: Line Kyed Knudsen | **Year**: 2019

## Summary
When a new girl named Gabriella joins Kara's class, she quickly becomes the center of attention with her fashionable clothes, interesting travel stories, and sophisticated charm. All the girls, including Kara's best friend Rose, are immediately drawn to Gabriella's popularity. However, Kara finds herself feeling excluded and sees a different side of Gabriella—one that can be mean and dismissive. The story explores themes of friendship, peer pressure, and the courage to stay true to oneself when social dynamics shift.

This book is perfect for young readers (ages 8-12) navigating complex friendship dynamics and learning about confidence, inclusion, and what it truly means to be popular.

## Core Insights

### The New Girl Effect
Gabriella arrives with an air of sophistication that immediately captivates the class. Her stories of living in different countries, her fashionable dresses, and her confident demeanor make everyone want to be her friend—except Kara, who sees through the facade.

> "Gabriella apparently already told them about herself, because Karen tells her to sit next to Rose...in Julie's chair."

**Quick Take**: New people can disrupt established friendships, but true character reveals itself over time.

### The Pain of Exclusion
Kara experiences what it feels like to be left out as her friends, particularly Rose, gravitate toward Gabriella. The social dynamics shift quickly, leaving Kara feeling isolated and questioning her own worth.

> "It is as if she is suddenly being excluded by the others."

**Quick Take**: Feeling excluded hurts, but it often reveals who your true friends are and helps you develop resilience.

### Courage to Be Yourself
Despite pressure to conform, Kara maintains her individuality. She doesn't join Gabriella's fashion group, wears what she likes, and engages in activities she enjoys, like playing with the boys and participating enthusiastically in science class.

> "No," she says. "I actually like it."

**Quick Take**: Being true to yourself is more valuable than fitting in with the popular crowd.

### The Power of Kindness
When Gabriella faces embarrassment at her own party—no one wants to try her favorite food, sushi—Kara steps up with compassion and tries the food first, breaking the ice and showing genuine kindness.

> "I'll try it," Kara says and reaches for the plate. "I think it looks interesting."

**Quick Take**: A single act of kindness can transform relationships and show true character.

### Understanding and Forgiveness
The story concludes with Gabriella confessing she acted mean out of fear of being an outsider herself. Both girls learn that everyone struggles with insecurity, and true friendship comes from honesty and understanding.

> "I was so scared of becoming an outsider, so I acted mean to you."

**Quick Take**: Understanding someone's motivations can lead to forgiveness and genuine connection.

## Key Takeaways

- **Stay true to your values**: Don't change who you are to fit in with others
- **Practice inclusion**: Make an effort to include everyone, especially new people
- **Show compassion**: Being kind to someone, even when they've been unkind to you, can change everything
- **Communicate honestly**: Share your feelings and listen to others' perspectives
- **Friendship isn't exclusive**: You can be friends with many different people without taking sides

## Questions to Consider

1. Have you ever felt like an outsider when friendship dynamics changed?
2. What would you do if a new person joined your friend group?
3. Is it better to be popular or to be true to yourself?
4. How can you include someone who feels left out?
5. What does real friendship mean to you?

## Conclusion
"Who is the Most Popular?" is a thoughtful exploration of friendship dynamics that speaks directly to young readers' social experiences. The book skillfully handles topics of exclusion, peer pressure, and self-confidence without being preachy. Kara's journey from feeling isolated to finding her place—and even helping Gabriella feel included—demonstrates that real popularity comes from kindness, authenticity, and the courage to be yourself.

This book is worth your time because it validates the complicated feelings children experience in social situations while providing a positive example of how to navigate them with grace and empathy. The biggest reason to read it? It shows that everyone deserves to feel included, and true friendship means making room for everyone to shine together.
