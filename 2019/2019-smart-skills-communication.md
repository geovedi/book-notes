# Smart Skills: Communications
**Authors**: Patrick Forsyth | **Year**: 2019
**Category**: Business & Finance

## Summary
"Smart Skills: Communications" is a comprehensive guide that transforms communication from an afterthought into a strategic business advantage. Forsyth argues that communication is inherently difficult and requires deliberate thought and preparation to overcome natural barriers like limited attention spans, assumptions, and emotional resistance. The book presents communication as a career-enhancing skill that affects how you're perceived and your ability to achieve results in the workplace.

This practical guide is ideal for business professionals at any career stage who want to enhance their effectiveness across all communication formats. Readers will learn systematic approaches to preparation, face-to-face interactions, writing, electronic communications, presentations, persuasion, and negotiation. The book provides actionable frameworks that can be immediately applied to improve clarity, influence, and professional impact.

## Core Insights

### The Foundation of Communication Success
Communication is inherently difficult, not because people are perverse, but because of inherent psychological and logistical barriers. Success requires accepting responsibility for making yourself understood, recognizing that people have limited attention spans, make assumptions based on past experience, and struggle with change. Effective communicators work to earn attention, ensure accurate understanding, prompt action, and stimulate feedback.

> "Make no mistake: communication can be difficult."
> _Original: "Make no mistake: communication can be difficult."_

**Quick Take**: Never assume communication is simple. Always engage brain before mouth (or keyboard) and recognize that the responsibility for effective communication lies primarily with the communicator.

### The Power of Systematic Preparation
Good preparation follows a six-stage process: listing all possible points, sorting them logically, arranging them in sequence, reviewing for completeness, preparing the actual message, and conducting a final check. Preparation should start with clear SMART objectives (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Realistic, Timed) and separate deciding what to say from how to say it.

> "The ability to express an idea is well nigh as important as the idea itself."
> _Original: "The ability to express an idea is well nigh as important as the idea itself."_

**Quick Take**: Preparation saves time in the long run and prevents communication "derailments." Use the listing-sorting-arranging method to organize thoughts before communicating.

### Mastering Face-to-Face Communication
In-person communication demands attention to appearance, body language, and systematic organization. Success requires looking appropriate for your role, reading non-verbal cues comprehensively, and leading meetings with clear agendas and objectives. Your professional appearance and manner significantly impact how your message is received.

> "It is only shallow people who do not judge by appearances."
> _Original: "It is only shallow people who do not judge by appearances."_

**Quick Take**: People judge by appearances, so ensure your professional look matches your message. Learn to read body language systematically and organize meetings with clear purposes and timeframes.

### Writing That Earns a Reading
Business writing must actively "earn a reading" by focusing on the reader's needs and reducing cognitive cost. Effective writing is understandable, readable, straightforward (KISS principle), and natural. Use short words, sentences, and paragraphs while maintaining flow, avoiding jargon, and making documents brief, clear, and relevant.

> "What is written without effort is in general read without pleasure."
> _Original: "What is written without effort is in general read without pleasure."_

**Quick Take**: Write for your readers, not yourself. Use the "reader's hat" approach and focus on making your message effortless to understand by keeping it simple and structured.

### Navigating Electronic Communication Challenges
Digital communication presents unique dangers where brevity often conflicts with clarity. Email and other electronic methods require professional standards despite their informality. Choose the right medium for your message, prioritize clarity over brevity, and remember that electronic communications create permanent records affecting your professional image.

> "The goal of computer science is to build something that will last until you've finished building it."
> _Original: "The goal of computer science is to build something that will last until you've finished building it."_

**Quick Take**: Don't let speed override clarity in digital communication. Check emails carefully, maintain professional standards, and remember that some messages still deserve personal treatment.

### Delivering Effective Presentations
Presentations represent "the business equivalent of an open goal" – a major opportunity to differentiate yourself. Success requires thorough preparation with clear objectives, a structured three-part format (beginning, middle, end), and visual aids that support rather than lead. The most important visual aid is you – your presence, gestures, and audience engagement.

> "It usually takes me three weeks to prepare a good impromptu speech."
> _Original: "It usually takes me three weeks to prepare a good impromptu speech."_

**Quick Take**: Start with "Why must this presentation be made?" rather than what you'll say. Use speaker notes that guide rather than script, and remember that your presence matters more than your slides.

### The Art of Persuasion
Persuasion is "helping people to decide" – a systematic process of making your case clear, attractive, and credible. Success requires thorough audience research, understanding that decisions involve weighing good points against bad ones, and focusing on benefits rather than features. Always link what you offer to specific needs you've discovered.

> "I persuade, you educate, they manipulate."
> _Original: "I persuade, you educate, they manipulate."_

**Quick Take**: Help people decide by presenting your option as the best solution to their needs. Talk benefits (what features do for them) rather than just features (factual statements about your offering).

### Negotiating for Optimal Results
Negotiation goes beyond persuasion to involve bargaining over specific terms and variables. Success requires aiming high, getting the other party's complete "shopping list" before agreeing to individual elements, and systematically applying negotiation techniques while maintaining professional relationships. Everything is potentially negotiable.

> "If you want a guinea pig, you start by asking for a pony."
> _Original: "If you want a guinea pig, you start by asking for a pony."_

**Quick Take**: Start with your best position (you can only trade down), get their complete needs list before making concessions, and remember that proper preparation is the foundation of successful negotiation.

## Action Items

- Always prepare by setting SMART objectives before any communication
- Use the six-stage preparation process: list, sort, arrange, review, prepare, check
- Practice active listening with full concentration and empathy
- Write for your readers using the "reader's hat" approach
- Match communication medium to message importance and complexity
- Structure presentations with clear beginnings, middles, and endings
- Focus on benefits rather than features when persuading
- Aim high in negotiations and get complete shopping lists before making concessions
- Continuously work on reading and using body language effectively
- Apply systematic preparation to all forms of communication

- How often do I engage brain before mouth or keyboard in daily communications?
- Which communication barriers (limited attention, assumptions, emotional resistance) affect me most?
- Do I prepare systematically for important communications, or do I rely on "winging it"?
- How can I better focus on reader/audience needs rather than my own convenience?
- Which communication format (writing, speaking, electronic, presenting) needs my immediate attention?
- Do I talk benefits or features when trying to influence others?
- How well do I read non-verbal cues in face-to-face interactions?
- Am I choosing the right communication medium for each message?
- What preparation habits could make me more effective across all communication types?

## Conclusion
"Smart Skills: Communications" delivers exactly what its title promises – practical, systematic approaches to mastering business communication. Forsyth's emphasis on preparation as the foundation of success, combined with actionable frameworks for every communication format, makes this an invaluable resource for professionals who want to enhance their impact and career prospects.

The book's strength lies in its recognition that communication is inherently difficult and requires deliberate thought rather than natural talent. By providing systematic methodologies that can be learned and practiced, Forsyth democratizes communication skills and makes them accessible to anyone willing to put in the preparation work.

This is absolutely worth your time if you want to transform communication from an afterthought into a strategic advantage. The biggest reason to read it is that it provides a complete toolkit for professional communication that can be immediately applied to prevent misunderstandings, enhance influence, and achieve better results in the workplace.
