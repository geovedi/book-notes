# Upskill: A 6-Step Solution to Unlock Economic Opportunity
**Authors**: Laurent Probst & Christian Scharff | **Year**: 2019
**Category**: Business & Finance

## Summary
"Upskill" presents a practical 6-step framework to address one of the most critical challenges of our time: the growing skills gap in the digital age. With nearly 47% of jobs at risk of automation and millions of skilled positions going unfilled, the authors argue that traditional approaches to workforce development are failing. This book provides a comprehensive roadmap for governments, corporations, and workers to collaborate in building sustainable upskilling ecosystems that ensure economic opportunity for all.

The book combines compelling data about automation risks with actionable strategies that any organization or region can implement. It addresses the urgent need to transform how we approach lifelong learning in an era where skills become obsolete faster than ever before.

## Core Insights

### Step 1: Analyse & Define Upskilling Initiative
The foundation of successful upskilling begins with strategic analysis and clear definition of objectives. This step involves understanding the specific context, identifying drivers of change, and establishing precise scope, budget, and stakeholder engagement plans. Without proper preparation, upskilling initiatives often fail due to misaligned expectations and inadequate resources.

**Quick Take**: Strategic planning is non-negotiable—organizations must invest significant time in understanding their specific upskilling needs before implementation.

### Step 2: Design Workforce Skills Plan
Once the initiative is defined, organizations must create a comprehensive workforce skills plan that identifies current capabilities, future requirements, and the specific gaps that need bridging. This step requires detailed analysis of industry trends, technological impacts, and organizational transformation needs.

**Quick Take**: Data-driven workforce planning ensures upskilling efforts target the most critical skill gaps and future-proof the organization.

### Step 3: Perform Individual Employee Assessment & Provide Advice
Personalized assessment is crucial for effective upskilling. This step involves evaluating each employee's current skills, learning preferences, and career aspirations. The authors emphasize the importance of providing individualized advice and career guidance to ensure employee buy-in and optimal skill development paths.

**Quick Take**: One-size-fits-all training fails—personalized assessment and guidance dramatically increase upskilling success rates.

### Step 4: Engage Workers & Match Jobs (the new science)
This step focuses on the science of matching workers with appropriate training opportunities and career progression paths. It involves sophisticated job-matching algorithms, psychological assessments, and predictive analytics to ensure the right people receive the right training for the right roles.

**Quick Take**: Modern technology enables precise matching of worker capabilities with training opportunities, maximizing return on upskilling investment.

### Step 5: Select & Provide Training
With clear assessment and matching complete, organizations can select and deliver targeted training programs. The authors emphasize the importance of choosing training methods that align with individual learning preferences and organizational needs, including online learning, apprenticeships, mentorship programs, and hands-on experience.

**Quick Take**: Diverse training delivery methods accommodate different learning styles and increase knowledge retention.

### Step 6: Monitor, Evaluate & Improve Policy
The final step creates a continuous improvement cycle by establishing robust monitoring and evaluation systems. Organizations must track participation, completion rates, skill acquisition, and business impact to refine their upskilling strategies continuously.

**Quick Take**: Continuous measurement and adaptation ensure upskilling initiatives remain relevant and effective over time.

## The Automation Imperative
The book presents stark statistics about automation's impact: approximately 47% of current jobs, particularly in service provision, transportation, logistics, administrative support, and production, face significant automation risk. However, rather than viewing this as purely negative, the authors see it as an unprecedented opportunity to elevate human potential through strategic skill development.

The European Union alone faces a potential gap of 700,000 IT workers by 2025-2030, representing both a challenge and opportunity for those prepared with the right skills.

## Action Items
- **Assess your organization's skills gap**: Conduct a comprehensive analysis of current capabilities versus future needs
- **Develop individualized learning plans**: Create personalized upskilling pathways for each employee
- **Implement continuous learning culture**: Make learning an ongoing organizational priority, not a one-time event
- **Leverage technology for matching**: Use data-driven approaches to connect workers with appropriate training opportunities
- **Establish measurement frameworks**: Track ROI and impact of upskilling initiatives with clear metrics
- **Foster stakeholder collaboration**: Create partnerships between government, business, and educational institutions

## Questions to Consider
1. How will automation specifically impact your industry and organization in the next 5-10 years?
2. Which skills in your workforce are most vulnerable to obsolescence?
3. What systems do you currently have in place to identify and address emerging skill gaps?
4. How can you transform upskilling from a cost center to a strategic investment?
5. What partnerships could accelerate your organization's upskilling efforts?
6. How will you measure the success of your upskilling initiatives beyond completion rates?

## Conclusion
"Upskill" is essential reading for organizational leaders, policymakers, and HR professionals facing the urgent challenges of workforce transformation. The book's greatest strength lies in its practical, step-by-step approach that moves beyond theory to actionable implementation.

The authors make a compelling case that upskilling is not just an HR initiative but a strategic imperative for business survival and economic prosperity. With the convergence of automation, aging workforces, and rapid technological change, organizations that fail to prioritize continuous skill development risk irrelevance.

The 6-step framework provides a comprehensive roadmap that can be adapted to any organizational context, making this book both accessible and immediately applicable. For anyone responsible for workforce strategy or organizational development, this book offers a timely and valuable guide to navigating the complex landscape of future work.
