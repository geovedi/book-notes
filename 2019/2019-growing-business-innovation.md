# Growing Business Innovation
**Authors**: Jonathan Reuvid (Editor) | **Year**: 2019

## Summary
"Growing Business Innovation" is a comprehensive guide for entrepreneurs, innovators, and business leaders seeking to navigate the complex relationship between innovation and intellectual property (IP). The book provides practical frameworks for transforming innovative ideas into valuable, protected assets while maximizing commercial returns. Through contributions from industry experts across legal, academic, and business sectors, it addresses both the strategic challenges and tactical implementations necessary for successful innovation in today's competitive landscape.

This resource is essential for startup founders, SME owners, corporate innovation managers, IP professionals, and anyone involved in product development or business growth who needs to understand how to protect and commercialize their innovative ideas effectively.

## Core Insights

### Innovation Strategy and Leadership
True innovation requires more than just good ideas—it demands deliberate intention and organizational commitment. The book introduces the concept that "intentions have no intelligence" without proper strategic frameworks. Organizations must align their mission, strategic goals, and operating objectives with a clear, communicated innovation strategy. Success comes from balancing short-term commercial requirements with long-term innovative vision while ensuring that every level of the organization understands and commits to the innovation journey.

> "What was deemed impossible a few years ago is not only becoming possible, it's very quickly becoming necessary and expected."
> _Arvind Krishna, Director of IBM Research_

**Quick Take**: Innovation thrives when leadership demonstrates authentic commitment beyond lip service, embedding innovative thinking into every organizational layer and timeline.

### Bridging the Innovation Gap for Large Organizations
The book identifies a significant market gap where "Large Stable Enterprises" (LSEs)—companies too big to be SMEs but too small to be FTSE 500—struggle with true innovation. These organizations often fall prey to short-term thinking, rigid budgetary cycles, and poorly defined success metrics. The solution lies in moving beyond traditional corporate accelerators and open innovation networks toward more collaborative, integrated approaches that connect internal teams with external innovation ecosystems.

> "54% of innovating companies struggle to bridge the gap between innovation strategy and business strategy."
> _PwC, Reinventing Innovation_

**Quick Take**: LSEs need agile innovation frameworks that balance internal capabilities with external partnerships, focusing on collaborative problem-solving rather than prescriptive solution-seeking.

### Intellectual Property as Business Strategy
Patents and other IP protections should be viewed not merely as legal shields but as strategic business tools for market positioning and revenue generation. The book emphasizes that successful IP management requires understanding territorial differences, protection options, and commercial exploitation strategies. Companies must develop comprehensive IP strategies that align with their business objectives and market opportunities, particularly when expanding into international markets like China.

> "In real estate we often hear that the key to a successful investment is: 'Location, Location, Location'. Similarly, profiting from innovation is all about exploiting and commercialising your Intellectual Property Rights."

**Quick Take**: IP strategy should drive business decisions, not follow them—consider protection early, plan globally, and align IP portfolio management with commercial objectives.

### R&D Tax Credits and Financial Incentives
The book demystifies R&D tax credit schemes, revealing that many companies miss significant funding opportunities by not understanding the differences between SME and RDEC schemes. Proper navigation of these programs can provide substantial financial benefits that make innovation self-sustaining. Companies should carefully assess which scheme applies to their projects and ensure claims are optimized to maximize returns.

> "26,255 companies claimed over £2.9 billion in R&D tax credits"
> _HMRC research, 2017_

**Quick Take**: R&D tax relief is not just for large companies or traditional research organizations—many businesses unknowingly qualify for significant financial support they're not claiming.

### University-Industry Collaboration
Strategic partnerships between businesses and academic institutions create powerful innovation ecosystems. The book demonstrates how companies of all sizes can benefit from accessing cutting-edge research, specialized expertise, and emerging talent pools. These collaborations provide pathways to new technologies, improved processes, and market opportunities that would be difficult to develop independently.

> "Industry-academia interactions are naturally two way; companies of all sizes and legal structures can support the Government's research and education agenda by providing more industry and societal challenges to drive relevant and focussed research."

**Quick Take**: University partnerships offer access to innovation resources and expertise that can accelerate development while reducing costs and risks.

## Action Items

- **Audit your innovation strategy**: Assess whether your organization has genuine commitment to innovation beyond rhetoric, and ensure alignment across all levels from mission to operating objectives
- **Develop comprehensive IP protection**: Review your intellectual property portfolio and identify gaps in protection, particularly for emerging markets and international expansion
- **Optimize R&D tax positioning**: Evaluate your eligibility for both SME and RDEC schemes to maximize financial benefits from innovation activities
- **Build strategic partnerships**: Identify potential university or research institution collaborations that can accelerate your innovation pipeline and reduce development risks
- **Implement AI-driven innovation**: Explore how artificial intelligence can enhance your innovation processes, from idea generation to market validation

## Questions to Consider

- How does your organization define innovation success, and are these metrics driving the right behaviors?
- What is the true cost of IP infringement in your industry, and how can you better protect your innovations?
- Are you accessing all available financial incentives for innovation, including R&D tax credits and government grants?
- How could strategic partnerships accelerate your innovation timeline while reducing costs?
- What role can artificial intelligence play in enhancing your innovation capabilities and processes?

## Conclusion

"Growing Business Innovation" delivers exceptional value for anyone serious about turning innovative ideas into commercial success. The book's greatest strength lies in its comprehensive coverage of the entire innovation lifecycle—from initial strategy through IP protection to commercial exploitation—backed by practical advice from industry experts.

This is worth your time if you're looking for a strategic framework that connects innovation directly to business outcomes rather than treating creativity as an abstract concept. The book stands out by providing actionable insights for organizations of all sizes, from startups to large enterprises, with specific guidance on navigating the complex intersection of technology, law, and business strategy.

The biggest reason to read this book is its ability to transform how you think about innovation—from viewing it as a creative exercise to understanding it as a disciplined business process that can be measured, managed, and monetized. Whether you're seeking to protect existing innovations, optimize your R&D investments, or build sustainable competitive advantages, the frameworks and strategies presented here will help you achieve your goals with greater confidence and success.
