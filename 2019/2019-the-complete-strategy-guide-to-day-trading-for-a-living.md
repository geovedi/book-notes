# The Complete Strategy Guide to Day Trading for a Living in 2019
**Authors**: Mark Vogel | **Year**: 2019
**Category**: Business & Finance

## Summary
This comprehensive beginner's guide demystifies day trading as a viable career path, moving beyond the outdated perception of trading as gambling. The book presents strategic approaches to various trading methods—stocks, options, forex, and swing trading—emphasizing that success comes from knowledge, discipline, and proper planning rather than luck. The author frames day trading as a diversified investment strategy that, when executed with the right systems and mindset, can provide substantial financial returns.

Ideal for aspiring traders looking to transition into full-time trading, this guide provides the foundational knowledge needed to develop winning strategies. Readers will learn essential risk management techniques, the importance of capital requirements, and how to set up a professional trading environment. The book's strength lies in its practical approach to turning trading from speculation into a systematic business.

## Core Insights

### Strategic Day Trading: Beyond Gambling
Day trading is fundamentally about strategy, not luck. Unlike the misconceptions from the dot-com boom era, modern day trading relies on systematic approaches and informed decision-making. The internet revolution has transformed trading from floor-based operations to accessible home-based businesses, but success still requires understanding market mechanics and developing proven strategies. The key difference between profitable and losing traders often comes down to having a clear strategic plan and the discipline to execute it consistently.

> "Day trading today is quite different from how it used to be once upon a time. With the evolution of trading options, several misconceptions have been put to rest."

**Quick Take**: Success in day trading comes from treating it as a business, not a hobby. Develop your strategy before risking capital.

### Risk Management: The Foundation of Survival
The most critical aspect of trading is preserving capital. Successful traders risk only 1-2% of their account per trade, understanding that survival in the market depends on living to trade another day. This means with a $50,000 account, maximum loss per trade should be limited to $250-500. This mathematical approach removes emotional decision-making and prevents catastrophic losses that can end a trading career before it begins.

> "Traders who risk less than 1 to 2% of their account per trade will be able to carry out more successful trades."

**Quick Take**: Your first goal isn't making money—it's not losing money. Master risk management before chasing profits.

### The Professional Trader's Toolkit
Modern trading requires more than just a computer and internet connection. Professional day traders need three essential components: a proper trading desk for order execution, reliable news sources for market information, and sophisticated trading software for technical analysis. The software should include automatic pattern recognition, genetic and neural applications for prediction, broker integration for instant execution, and backtesting capabilities to validate strategies. These tools help eliminate emotion and improve decision-making speed.

> "It is extremely important for a day trader to have access to all the important news sources. News sources can provide traders with vital information needed to capitalize upon the opportunities that might arise."

**Quick Take**: Invest in your trading infrastructure before you invest your capital. The right tools are as important as the right strategy.

### Options Trading: Leveraging Opportunities
Options provide unique advantages for traders looking to expand their strategies beyond simple stock trading. Understanding call options (right to buy) and put options (right to sell) opens up new possibilities for both speculation and hedging. American options offer flexibility by allowing execution anytime before expiry, while European options are more restrictive. Short-term options are ideal for day trading, while long-term options (LEAPS) provide extended time for market movements to play out.

> "An options trader can increase profits by combining options and stocks."

**Quick Take**: Options trading isn't inherently risky—it's powerful when understood properly. Start with basic strategies before advancing to complex combinations.

### Trading Psychology: The Emotional Battle
Perhaps more important than any strategy is the psychological aspect of trading. The markets will test your patience, discipline, and emotional control. Successful traders maintain calm during volatility, stick to their plans, and avoid impulsive decisions based on fear or greed. The philosophy of "plan your trades and trade your plans" separates professionals from amateurs. Understanding that losses are part of the business and maintaining realistic profit expectations (50-60%, not 100%) helps traders stay grounded.

> "It will be best to stick with a winning formula compared to chasing profits. Most traders follow a set philosophy that states it is important to plan your trades instead of allowing emotions to guide you."

**Quick Take**: Your biggest opponent in trading isn't the market—it's yourself. Master your psychology before mastering the markets.

## Action Items
- Start paper trading for at least 3 months before risking real capital
- Set up a dedicated trading space with reliable internet and multiple monitors
- Create a written trading plan defining entry/exit rules, risk management, and profit targets
- Limit yourself to 1-2 stocks initially and master their patterns before expanding
- Establish daily loss limits and stick to them religiously
- Build a routine of market analysis before each trading session
- Join trading communities to learn from experienced traders
- Keep a detailed trading journal to track performance and identify patterns

## Questions to Consider
- Do you have the emotional temperament to handle significant financial losses without panicking?
- Can you dedicate 4-5 hours daily to focused market analysis and trading?
- Are you prepared to lose your initial trading capital as part of your education?
- Do you have sufficient savings to support yourself while learning to trade profitably?
- Can you follow strict rules even when emotions are running high?
- Are you willing to continuously learn and adapt your strategies as markets evolve?

## Conclusion
This book provides a solid foundation for aspiring day traders, but it's important to recognize that reading about trading is very different from actually trading. The guide excels at presenting comprehensive strategies across multiple trading types while emphasizing the critical importance of risk management and psychological discipline.

Is it worth your time? Absolutely, if you're serious about pursuing trading as a career. The biggest reason to read this book is that it approaches trading as a business rather than get-rich-quick scheme, providing realistic expectations about the challenges and requirements for success. However, treat this as your first step in a long journey of continuous learning, not a complete roadmap to trading mastery.

The book's greatest strength lies in its systematic approach to building a trading business from the ground up, emphasizing that successful trading is about surviving first and thriving second. For anyone considering day trading as a profession, this guide provides the essential framework needed to begin that journey with eyes wide open to both the opportunities and risks ahead.
