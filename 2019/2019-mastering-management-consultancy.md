# Mastering Management Consultancy
**Authors**: Calvert Markham | **Year**: 2019

## Summary
"Mastering Management Consultancy" is a comprehensive guide that demystifies the consulting profession and provides practical frameworks for delivering value to clients. The book covers the entire consultancy lifecycle from understanding client problems and managing relationships to running successful projects and growing a consulting business.

This essential resource is perfect for aspiring consultants, current practitioners looking to enhance their skills, and business leaders who want to maximize their ROI from consulting engagements. Readers will gain actionable methodologies for problem-solving, client management, and building a sustainable consulting practice.

## Core Insights

### The Nature of Consultancy
Consultancy exists at three distinct levels: technical (specific expertise like tax advice), functional (improving business processes), and strategic (shaping organizational direction). The key insight is that effective consultants must learn to "think at a degree of freedom greater than that of the client" - questioning assumptions that clients take for granted to uncover deeper issues.

> "The consultant's first task is to define the questions that need to be answered to resolve the client's predicament."

**Quick Take**: Always challenge your client's initial problem definition. Often what they present as the issue is merely a symptom of deeper underlying problems.

### The Levels of Intervention Framework
Markham presents a powerful four-level model for determining where to focus consulting efforts:
1. **Purposes**: Questioning the client's ultimate objectives
2. **Problems**: Identifying the real issues blocking success
3. **Solutions**: Developing appropriate approaches to address problems
4. **Implementation**: Executing the solutions effectively

The golden rule is "start at the point of uncertainty" - begin at the highest level where assumptions haven't been validated. This prevents consultants from solving the wrong problems perfectly.

> "If a client cannot provide satisfactory answers to these questions, there may be functional or strategic consultancy work that needs to be done before a project in a technical area can proceed."

**Quick Take**: Before accepting a project at face value, always test whether the fundamental assumptions about purpose and problems are valid.

### Creative Problem-Solving Methodology
The book advocates for a hypothesis-driven approach rather than linear problem-solving. Start with creative generation of possible explanations (hypotheses), then systematically test them through data collection. This mirrors scientific method and prevents consultants from jumping to conclusions prematurely.

Critical to this process is separating creative thinking from evaluation. Most teams mix these modes, which kills creativity. Instead, dedicate distinct phases: first generate ideas without judgment (divergent thinking), then evaluate and select promising ones (convergent thinking).

> "If there is a characteristic that distinguishes excellent consultants from the merely good, it is that of creativity."

**Quick Take**: Use structured brainstorming techniques and always treat your initial ideas as hypotheses to be tested, not conclusions to be defended.

### Client Relationship Management
Successful consulting depends as much on relationship quality as technical expertise. The book emphasizes that consultancy projects are "joint ventures" requiring active client participation, not just passive funding. Key factors include trust building, clear communication, managing expectations, and creating shared ownership of outcomes.

Markham warns against the common pitfall of extending project scope unnecessarily (while genuinely exploring deeper issues when justified). The art is knowing when additional investigation adds value versus when it appears as commercial opportunism.

> "A consultancy's own procedures can also result in high levels of working capital being required."

**Quick Take**: Document all agreements, maintain regular communication, and ensure clients understand their active role in project success.

### The Consultancy Sales Process
The book reveals that effective consulting sales prioritize relationship development over transactional pitching. Most consultancies secure work through existing relationships and referrals, not cold calls. The sales process should follow natural progression: initial contact → relationship building → opportunity identification → proposition development → pitch conversion.

Crucially, clients rarely choose consultants based on lowest price. They prioritize understanding of their problems, quality of proposed consultants, and perceived value over cost differentials.

> "My experience of fee rates is that price is rarely the major determinant in the choice of consultant."

**Quick Take**: Invest in relationships before you need them, and focus your proposals on demonstrating deep understanding rather than competitive pricing.

## Action Items

- **Before accepting any consulting project**: Use the four-level intervention model to identify where uncertainty exists and start at that level
- **When solving client problems**: Always generate multiple hypotheses before data collection, then test systematically
- **In client meetings**: Separate creative brainstorming from evaluation phases to generate better solutions
- **For business development**: Focus on building relationships rather than perfecting pitches - most work comes through existing connections
- **When managing projects**: Document clear terms of reference and maintain regular client communication to prevent misunderstandings

## Questions to Consider

1. Are you solving the right problem, or merely addressing symptoms your client has identified?
2. At what level should you intervene - purposes, problems, solutions, or implementation?
3. How can you separate creative thinking from evaluation in your problem-solving process?
4. Are your client relationships partnerships or transactions?
5. What value does your firm add beyond individual consultant expertise?

## Conclusion
"Mastering Management Consultancy" is worth your time if you're serious about professional consulting. Its greatest strength is providing practical frameworks that can be immediately applied, particularly the levels of intervention model and hypothesis-driven problem-solving approach. The book challenges consultants to think more strategically about their work and deliver greater value by questioning assumptions rather than accepting client definitions at face value.

The biggest reason to read this book is its systematic approach to consulting methodology. While many consulting books focus on inspiration or war stories, Markham provides repeatable processes for the entire consulting lifecycle. This makes it invaluable both for newcomers seeking structure and experienced practitioners looking to professionalize their approach. The focus on creative thinking and questioning assumptions sets it apart from more prescriptive consulting guides.
