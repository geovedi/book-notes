# The Great Boss Mentor
**Authors**: Tim L. Gardner | **Year**: 2019
**Category**: Business & Finance

## Summary
This practical management guide presents 13 essential keys for effective employee management and conflict resolution. Based on extensive research and real-world case studies, including Boeing's successful employee empowerment transformation that generated $140,000 per team from self-initiated projects, the book argues that excellent management goes beyond tactics and strategies to focus on psychological aspects of employee engagement.

The book targets managers, team leaders, and business owners who want to maximize both productivity and employee satisfaction. Readers will gain a comprehensive framework for building trust, implementing effective recognition programs, managing conflicts proactively, and creating an empowered work environment where employees feel valued and motivated to contribute their best efforts.

## Core Insights

### 1. Recognition
Recognition involves appreciating and rewarding outstanding employee efforts through authentic, transparent programs. The key is using measurable parameters and frequent scheduling rather than annual "employee of the month" formats that breed resentment. Recognition should be timely, based on merit, and include both individual and team awards to create healthy competition without alienating high performers.

> "Your job as a manager is to make the most out of recognition programs by rewarding your hardworking employees. As much as the impact of the rewards is a contentious issue, the truth is that many organizations embrace recognition in their employee motivation schemes."

**Quick Take**: Implement weekly awards based on objective metrics like sales performance or social media engagement, making the process transparent and inclusive with runner-up categories.

### 2. Supportive Feedback
Effective supportive feedback builds interpersonal relationships by focusing on improvement rather than criticism. It involves active listening, clear communication, and genuine concern for employee development. The best feedback is timely, specific, and delivered with empathy to create trust and psychological safety.

**Quick Take**: Create structured feedback sessions that balance praise with constructive guidance, ensuring employees feel supported rather than judged.

### 3. Empowerment
True empowerment means transferring both responsibility AND authority to employees, not just delegating tasks. This transforms employees from spectators to active decision-makers. Boeing's success story demonstrates how creating 3,500 empowered teams can dramatically improve productivity and innovation when employees are given the resources and training to succeed.

> "Employee empowerment is about opening boundaries to allow employees to explore beyond their traditionally prescribed roles. This strategy transforms employees from spectators to active participants in decision-making processes."

**Quick Take**: Start by identifying specific decision-making areas where employees can take ownership, then provide the training and resources they need to succeed.

### 4. Consideration
Consideration packages encompass fair compensation, benefits, and working conditions that show employees they are valued. This includes competitive salaries, performance bonuses, health benefits, and work-life balance initiatives. The key is transparency about compensation ranges and clear criteria for advancement.

**Quick Take**: Conduct market research to ensure your compensation packages are competitive, and communicate them clearly during recruitment to set proper expectations.

### 5. Trust
Building mutual trust requires a gradual learning curve where managers demonstrate reliability, consistency, and integrity. Trust develops through open communication, fulfilled promises, and genuine concern for employee wellbeing. The process takes time but creates the foundation for all other management strategies to work effectively.

**Quick Take**: Practice what you preach, maintain consistent policies, and always follow through on commitments to build lasting trust with your team.

### 6. Partnering
Partnering involves creating collaborative relationships within and between teams based on shared goals and mutual benefits. The PwC and E&Y case study demonstrates how strategic partnerships can lead to better outcomes than competition. This approach encourages knowledge sharing and collective problem-solving rather than individual competition.

**Quick Take**: Identify opportunities for cross-functional collaboration on projects that benefit from diverse expertise and perspectives.

### 7. Setting Realistic Expectations and Goals
Goals must be challenging yet achievable to maintain motivation without creating undue stress. Unrealistic expectations lead to burnout and reduced productivity, while goals that are too easy fail to inspire excellence. The key is involving employees in goal-setting to ensure buy-in and alignment with their capabilities.

**Quick Take**: Use the SMART framework (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound) and involve your team in setting both individual and collective goals.

### 8. Proper Communication
Effective communication structures must be proactive rather than reactive, ensuring information flows freely in all directions. This includes regular team meetings, open-door policies, transparent decision-making processes, and multiple channels for feedback and suggestions.

**Quick Take**: Establish regular communication rhythms like daily stand-ups, weekly team meetings, and monthly one-on-ones to maintain consistent information flow.

### 9. Teamwork
Productive teamwork environments require clearly defined roles, shared objectives, and collaborative processes. Successful teams have balanced participation, constructive conflict resolution, and celebrate collective achievements rather than individual glory.

**Quick Take**: Design team projects with clear role definitions, shared success metrics, and regular check-ins to ensure everyone stays aligned and engaged.

### 10. Conflict Management Skills
Effective conflict management requires early detection, professional approach, and unbiased resolution. Managers should watch for warning signs like reduced work quality, absenteeism, and communication breakdowns. The goal is addressing conflicts constructively before they escalate while maintaining respect for all parties.

> "Conflict resolution entails deploying proactive or reactive measures to either reduce the intensity of a conflict or eliminate it completely."

**Quick Take**: Implement an open-door policy and regular check-ins to catch conflicts early, then facilitate solutions that satisfy all parties involved.

### 11. Emotional Intelligence
Emotional intelligence in the workplace involves understanding and managing emotions in yourself and others. This includes self-awareness, empathy, and the ability to navigate interpersonal relationships with sensitivity and skill. High EQ managers build stronger teams and resolve conflicts more effectively.

**Quick Take**: Practice active listening, seek to understand underlying emotions behind behaviors, and respond with empathy rather than judgment.

### 12. Automating Work Processes
Work automation systems streamline routine tasks, reduce human error, and free employees to focus on higher-value activities. Successful implementation requires proper training, change management, and employee involvement in the transition process to ensure adoption and maximize benefits.

**Quick Take**: Identify repetitive, time-consuming tasks that can be automated, then involve employees in selecting and implementing the right technology solutions.

### 13. Growth Opportunities for Employees
Providing growth opportunities means investing in employee development through training, mentorship, career pathing, and new challenges. This shows employees they are valued beyond their current contributions and helps retain top talent while building organizational capability.

**Quick Take**: Create individual development plans for each team member, combining formal training with stretch assignments that build new skills.

## Action Items

- Conduct a self-assessment of your current management methods using the 13 keys as benchmarks
- Implement weekly recognition awards based on objective, measurable criteria
- Create transparent compensation packages and communicate them clearly during recruitment
- Develop a gradual empowerment strategy that transfers both responsibility and authority
- Establish regular communication structures including team meetings and one-on-ones
- Create conflict detection mechanisms and resolution protocols
- Invest in training and development opportunities for all team members
- Build trust through consistent behavior, fulfilled promises, and genuine care

## Questions to Consider

1. If you glance over the 13 keys, do you feel you have been doing enough to prioritize the wellbeing of your employees?
2. What is the most important priority of your organization: productivity, employee wellbeing, or both?
3. How can you balance individual recognition with team achievement in your workplace?
4. What changes in your management style would have the greatest positive impact on your team's performance?
5. Which of the 13 keys presents the biggest challenge for your organization, and what steps can you take to address it?

## Conclusion
"The Great Boss Mentor" is definitely worth your time if you're a manager looking for practical, actionable strategies to improve your leadership effectiveness. The book's greatest strength is its comprehensive framework of 13 specific, implementable keys rather than vague management theories. The real-world case studies, particularly Boeing's transformation, provide concrete evidence that these strategies work in practice.

The biggest reason to read this book is its balanced approach to management that recognizes both productivity goals and employee wellbeing as essential components of success. Unlike many business books that focus solely on financial metrics or employee happiness, this guide provides a holistic approach that creates sustainable high performance through employee engagement and empowerment.

While some readers might wish for more industry-specific examples, the book's principles are universally applicable across different sectors and organization sizes. The self-assessment questions and practical implementation guidance make it easy to apply these concepts immediately in your own workplace.
