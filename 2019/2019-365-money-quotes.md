# 365 Money Quotes
**Original Title**: 365 Money Quotes
**Authors**: Xabier K. Fernao | **Year**: 2019
**Category**: Business & Finance

## Summary
"365 Money Quotes" is a daily motivational collection designed to transform your relationship with wealth through one quote per day. The book presents wisdom from diverse sources—billionaires like Warren Buffett, philosophers like Aristotle, entertainers like Jay-Z, and anonymous proverbs—all focused on developing what the author calls the "millionaire mindset." Rather than promising get-rich-quick schemes, this book serves as a daily foundation for building wealth consciousness, preparing readers to recognize and act on opportunities when they arise.

Perfect for anyone seeking to overcome limiting beliefs about money, establish better financial habits, or maintain daily motivation on their wealth-building journey. Readers will gain practical wisdom, shift their money mindset, and develop the discipline needed for financial success.

## Core Insights

### Money Precedes Mindset
The fundamental premise that your mindset determines your financial reality. Before wealth can manifest in your life, you must first develop the consciousness of a wealthy person. This involves seeing money as a tool, not an end goal, and understanding that financial success begins with your thoughts and beliefs about money.

> "Mindset precedes everything. Mindset precedes more money, more happiness, more success, more health and more wealth."
> _Original: "Mindset precedes everything. Mindset precedes more money, more happiness, more success, more health and more wealth."_
**Quick Take**: Your financial reality is a reflection of your internal money story—change the story first.

### Keep More Than You Make
The timeless principle that wealth accumulation isn't about high income, but about smart money management. True wealth comes from keeping money, making it work for you, and preserving it across generations rather than just earning large sums.

> "It's not how much money you make, but how much money you keep, how hard it works for you, and how many generations you keep it for."
> _Original: "It's not how much money you make, but how much money you keep, how hard it works for you, and how many generations you keep it for."_
**Quick Take**: Financial success equals income minus expenses, multiplied by investment returns and time.

### Money as a Servant, Not Master
The crucial distinction between being controlled by money versus using money as a tool for life goals. When money becomes your master, you become its slave; when money becomes your servant, you gain freedom and control over your life choices.

> "Money is a terrible master but an excellent servant."
> _Original: "Money is a terrible master but an excellent servant."_
**Quick Take**: Use money to achieve your life goals rather than letting money dictate your life choices.

### Hustle as the Great Equalizer
The recurring emphasis that consistent action, hard work, and persistence—what the book calls "hustle"—trump talent and privilege. Many quotes highlight how relentless effort can overcome disadvantages and create opportunities.

> "Talent is cheaper than table salt. What separates the talented individual from the successful one is a lot of hard work."
> _Original: "Talent is cheaper than table salt. What separates the talented individual from the successful one is a lot of hard work."_
**Quick Take**: Sustainable effort beats temporary brilliance every time.

### The Psychology of Saving
The recurring theme that saving money isn't just about accumulating wealth, but about developing discipline, foresight, and character. The habit of saving itself builds virtues that extend beyond financial matters.

> "The habit of saving is itself an education; it fosters every virtue, teaches self-denial and cultivates the sense of order, trains to forethought and so broadens the mind."
> _Original: "The habit of saving is itself an education; it fosters every virtue, teaches self-denial and cultivates the sense of order, trains to forethought and so broadens the mind."_
**Quick Take**: Building the savings habit builds character that serves all areas of life.

## Action Items

- Start with one quote per day rather than rushing through the collection
- Create a journal entry for each quote, relating it to your personal financial situation
- Build an "emergency fund" mindset—save before you spend
- Develop multiple income streams to reduce dependency on one source
- Practice delayed gratification in daily spending decisions
- Surround yourself with people who have healthy money mindsets

## Questions to Consider

- What is your current money story, and how is it serving or limiting you?
- Are you working for money, or is your money working for you?
- What would you do differently if you knew you couldn't fail financially?
- How does your spending reflect your true priorities and values?
- Are you more afraid of losing money or missing opportunities?
- What financial habits are building your future wealth, and which ones are destroying it?

## Conclusion

This book is absolutely worth your time if you're seeking daily motivation for wealth-building and want to fundamentally transform your relationship with money. The power lies not in reading all 365 quotes at once, but in the daily practice of letting one piece of wisdom sink in and influence your financial decisions.

The biggest reason to read this collection is its ability to gradually reshape your money mindset without overwhelming you with complex financial theory. It's accessible, diverse in perspectives, and practical in its approach. However, don't expect detailed investment strategies or step-by-step wealth formulas—this is about the psychological foundation, not the technical mechanics of finance.

Perfect for starting or ending your day with a wealth-building mindset, this book serves as a daily reminder that financial success is less about complex strategies and more about consistent right thinking and disciplined action.
