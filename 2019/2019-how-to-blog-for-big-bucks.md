# How to Blog for Big Bucks
**Authors**: Dale Carnegie | **Year**: 2019

## Summary
This concise guide reveals how to transform blog commenting from a casual activity into a powerful traffic generation and monetization strategy. The book positions thoughtful blog engagement as an underutilized marketing tool that can drive substantial traffic to your website without requiring significant financial investment.

Perfect for bloggers, content marketers, and small business owners looking to increase their online visibility and revenue through strategic community engagement rather than expensive advertising campaigns.

## Core Insights

### The Strategic Value of Blog Commenting
Blog commenting is far more than casual engagement—it's a strategic marketing tool that builds relationships, establishes expertise, and creates valuable backlinks. When done correctly, thoughtful comments on relevant blogs can drive consistent traffic to your site and position you as an authority in your niche.

> "The blog commenting if done well and with a certain level of respect for other bloggers and their contributions can help form a very fruitful relationship between the various parties involved."

**Quick Take**: Focus on adding value rather than self-promotion. Quality comments build credibility and attract interested visitors organically.

### Traffic Generation Mechanics
The core traffic flow works through visibility in comment sections, backlinks to your site, and establishing your presence within blogging communities. By consistently appearing in top commenter lists and contributing valuable insights, you create multiple pathways for potential visitors to discover your content.

> "When the comments are thoughtful and informative, most would appreciate the effort and time taken to post the comment. Also in doing so, the individual is able to create awareness of his or her own presence within the others who may visit the said site and read the comments."

**Quick Take**: Aim for at least 5 quality comments daily across relevant blogs to build momentum and visibility.

### The First-Mover Advantage
Being among the first 3 commenters significantly increases click-through rates to your site. Early comments receive more visibility before the comment section becomes crowded, giving your contribution and link maximum exposure to interested readers.

> "The delight of getting high quality hits from blog commenting is in being the 1st or one of the first 3 to comment, if you're not, you may still get hits however the hits won't be as much as that of those who are the 1st to comment."

**Quick Take**: Use tools like Comment Sniper to receive instant notifications when blogs in your niche publish new content.

### Branding Your Comment Identity
Consistent personal branding across all comments establishes recognition and credibility. This includes using a professional gravatar, maintaining consistent naming conventions, and developing a recognizable commenting style that reflects your expertise.

> "If you'd like to have the name of your blog or your keyword in your title then you are able to have it following your name in any of these formats 'Your Name|Whatever Blog' or 'Your Name – Whatever Blog'."

**Quick Take**: Create a professional gravatar and use consistent name formatting like "John Smith | Tech Blog" across all platforms.

## Action Framework

### 1. Setup Your Commenting System
- Create a professional gravatar at en.gravatar.com
- Establish consistent name formatting (Name | Blog Name)
- Subscribe to blog notifications or use Comment Sniper for early alerts
- Build a targeted RSS feed list of relevant blogs

### 2. Daily Commenting Routine
- Make at least 5 quality comments per day
- Read posts thoroughly before commenting
- Focus on being among the first 3 commenters
- Aim for thoughtful, informative contributions that add value

### 3. Strategic Content Creation
- Make controversial but insightful comments to attract attention
- Share specific expertise and practical examples
- Ask thoughtful questions that encourage discussion
- Reference your own relevant content when appropriate

### 4. Relationship Building
- Engage with other commenters, not just the blog author
- Follow up on responses to your comments
- Build recognition through consistent participation
- Establish yourself as a go-to resource in your niche

## What to Avoid

### ❌ Spam Tactics
- Don't use keyword stuffing in name fields
- Avoid generic comments like "Great post"
- Never post unrelated promotional content
- Skip automated commenting tools

### ❌ Poor Etiquette
- Don't make enemies with offensive or seditious comments
- Avoid being overly critical without constructive input
- Don't ignore blog community guidelines
- Never leave comments without reading the full post

## Questions to Consider

1. Which blogs in your niche have the most engaged communities and could drive your target audience?

2. How can you balance being controversial for attention while maintaining professional credibility?

3. What unique expertise can you offer in comments that others can't easily replicate?

4. How will you track which commenting strategies generate the most qualified traffic?

5. What's your system for scaling from 5 daily comments to 20+ without sacrificing quality?

## Conclusion
"How to Blog for Big Bucks" offers a refreshingly practical approach to traffic generation through strategic community engagement. While the concepts are straightforward, the book's value lies in treating blog commenting as a systematic marketing strategy rather than random activity.

The biggest win for readers is understanding that authentic relationship-building and thoughtful contribution consistently outperform aggressive marketing tactics. This approach creates sustainable traffic growth while establishing you as a genuine authority in your field.

**Bottom Line**: If you're willing to invest 30-60 minutes daily in strategic commenting, this book provides a proven framework for building consistent traffic without paid advertising. The ROI comes not from the comments themselves, but from the relationships and credibility you build through genuine value contribution.
