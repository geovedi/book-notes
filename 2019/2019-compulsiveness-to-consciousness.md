# Compulsiveness to Consciousness
**Authors**: Sadhguru Jaggi Vasudev | **Year**: 2019
**Category**: Spirituality & Religion

## Summary
"Compulsiveness to Consciousness" is a profound exploration of human relationships and how they can either trap us in compulsive patterns or elevate us to conscious living. Sadhguru examines why most relationships bring more pain than joy, despite being essential to human existence. The book reveals that the fundamental issue lies in how we approach relationships - whether we use them to fulfill our needs or to express our joy. Through insightful anecdotes, practical wisdom, and spiritual depth, Sadhguru offers a revolutionary perspective on transforming our relationships from sources of bondage into opportunities for spiritual growth and ultimate liberation.

This book is perfect for anyone struggling with relationship challenges, seeking deeper meaning in their connections, or wanting to understand how to use relationships as a vehicle for personal transformation rather than entanglement.

## Core Insights

### From Compulsiveness to Consciousness
The central theme explores how most human relationships operate on compulsive patterns rather than conscious choice. We constantly bounce between happiness and dejection based on relationship highs and lows, becoming enslaved to our connections. The fear of being alone, not genuine love, often keeps people together. Sadhguru reveals that relationships become transformative when we shift from extracting fulfillment to sharing joy, from meeting needs to expressing our innate completeness.

> "Don't try to manage the other person. Just see how to include the other person."

**Quick Take**: True relationship freedom comes not from controlling others but from expanding your own consciousness to include them naturally.

### The Mechanics of Love
Sadhguru breaks down love as not about someone else, but about your own ability to respond to life. Love happens when you're willing to respond - to people, situations, and existence itself. Most relationships fail because they're transactional - "I'll give if I get" - leading to constant scorekeeping and resentment. Real love is your ability to respond lovingly to everything and everyone, independent of their behavior or response.

> "Love is just a currency for blissfulness."

**Quick Take**: Transform your relationships by becoming loving yourself rather than seeking love from others.

### Body, Mind, and Energy Relationships
The book explores three levels of relationships: body-based (physical intimacy), mind-based (emotional connection), and energy-based (spiritual union). While body-based relationships dominate modern life, they create the deepest entanglements through "runanubandha" - karmic recordings in the body from intimate connections. Multiple partners confuse the body's memory, leading to anxiety and insecurity. Energy-based relationships transcend these limitations and can extend beyond lifetimes.

> "If you go on playing around with too many people, after sometime you become numb."

**Quick Take**: Understanding the different levels of relationship helps you navigate them wisely and avoid unnecessary entanglement.

### Inclusion vs. Exclusivity
The paradox of relationships is that while seeking connection, we often become more exclusive, creating small cliques that exclude us from the rest of life. True freedom comes through inclusion, not exclusivity. The more exclusive your thoughts and emotions, the more excluded you become from the life process itself. Relationships should be opportunities to achieve union that paves the way for greater possibilities, not just compatibility or companionship.

> "The only way you can know freedom is by inclusion. Love is a solvent, not a solution."

**Quick Take**: Use relationships to expand your consciousness and include more of life, not to create exclusive boundaries that limit you.

### Parenting and Friendship
Sadhguru redefines parenting as creating an atmosphere of love, care, and support rather than teaching children what to do. Similarly, true friendship isn't about finding people who support your existing nonsense, but having the courage to tell each other the truth while remaining loving. A true friend helps you see beyond your limitations while staying connected to your potential.

> "When a child enters your house, it is not time to become a teacher, it is time to learn."

**Quick Take**: The best way to guide others (children or friends) is to create a supportive atmosphere and be willing to learn yourself.

## Best Quotes
- "Human relationships are a funny thing. If you look at any relationship that human beings hold with each other, it means a lot to people – most people could not live without it – but at the same time, most relationships bring more pain than joy, more entanglement than freedom, more acrimony than love."
- "The fear of being alone is the major reason why people stay together, not only in marriage but in everything else, but still, because there are moments of emotional fulfillment, people hang on."
- "If you are blissful, then being loving is not a problem, anyway you will be. Only when you are seeking blissfulness through love, then you are very selective about whom to be loving with."
- "A true friend is someone who has the courage to tell you what shit you are, and still be loving and nice to you."
- "Relationships are not about compatibility, companionship or about extracting happiness from each other. Relationships are an opportunity to achieve some kind of union which will pave the way for a greater possibility."

## Action Items
- Shift from seeking fulfillment through relationships to expressing your innate joy
- Practice inclusion rather than exclusivity in your thoughts and emotions
- Become loving yourself rather than trying to get love from others
- Use relationship challenges as opportunities for spiritual growth
- Create supportive atmospheres for others rather than trying to control or teach them

## Questions to Consider
- Are your relationships based on fulfilling needs or expressing joy?
- Do you use love as a currency to get blissfulness, or are you blissful and therefore loving?
- Are your connections making you more exclusive and isolated, or more inclusive and expanded?
- Are you using relationship conflicts as triggers for your own growth and transformation?
- Do you see relationships as bondage or as opportunities for spiritual evolution?

## Conclusion
"Compulsiveness to Consciousness" is a transformative guide that revolutionizes how we view human connections. Sadhguru's wisdom cuts through conventional relationship advice to offer profound spiritual insights that can liberate us from compulsive patterns. The book's greatest strength is its ability to address both practical relationship challenges and their deeper spiritual dimensions.

This is worth your time if you're tired of the emotional rollercoaster of relationships and seek a more conscious, expansive way of connecting. The biggest reason to read it is that it offers not just better relationship strategies, but a completely different way of being in relationship with everyone and everything around you. Sadhguru's perspective can free you from the prison of compulsive relationship patterns and open you to the possibility of using every connection as a doorway to higher consciousness.
