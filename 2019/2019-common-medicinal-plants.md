# 2019 Common Medicinal Plants
**Authors**: Harshita Joshi | **Year**: 2013

## Summary
"Common Medicinal Plants: Uses and Cultivation Practices" is a practical guide that demystifies the world of herbal medicine by focusing on plants that can be easily grown in home gardens or cultivated commercially. The book bridges traditional wisdom with modern cultivation techniques, making medicinal gardening accessible to everyone from casual home gardeners to commercial growers. It emphasizes that medicinal plants offer natural healing without side effects while providing the dual benefits of ornamental beauty and therapeutic value.

This comprehensive resource serves as both an educational introduction to medicinal plants and a hands-on cultivation manual. Readers will learn how to harness nature's pharmacy in their own backyards, understand active plant compounds that provide healing properties, and gain practical knowledge for cultivating, harvesting, and processing these valuable plants. The book particularly highlights how everyday plants like aloe vera, turmeric, and basil have potent medicinal benefits that have been scientifically validated.

## Core Insights

### The Natural Pharmacy in Your Garden
Medicinal plants are essentially living pharmacies that synthesize chemical compounds to survive adverse conditions, and these same compounds provide therapeutic benefits for humans. The book reveals that almost all plants possess some medicinal properties, which is why health practitioners emphasize consuming fruits, vegetables, and herbs. These plants contain active ingredients like alkaloids, flavonoids, glycosides, and volatile oils that work synergistically to heal various disorders without the side effects common in synthetic medicines.

> "In simple words, medicinal plants may be defined as plants that possess medicinal properties and help in curing various ailments of the body either slowly or rapidly."
**Quick Take**: Your garden can become a sustainable source of natural medicine that works gently with your body's systems.

### Medicinal Plants Work Through Active Ingredients
The healing power of medicinal plants comes from biologically active chemicals they synthesize naturally. The book categorizes these compounds: alkaloids (found in periwinkle, poppy, coffee), glycosides (foxglove, digitalis), flavonoids (basil, onion, garlic), saponins (primula, liquorice), volatile oils (lemongrass), and anthocyanins (grapes, blackberry). Each plant species produces specific compounds that target different body systems, from digestive disorders to skin conditions, mental health to respiratory issues.

> "Active ingredients are biologically active chemicals that are synthesized by the plant parts. Some of the examples of active ingredients present in the plants are alkaloids, glycosides, phenols, flavonoids, polysaccharides, saponins, tannins, volatile oils, anthocyanins."
**Quick Take**: Understanding which compounds heal which conditions helps you select the right plants for your specific health needs.

### Home Cultivation is Simple and Rewarding
The book demystifies medicinal plant cultivation by emphasizing that most of these plants can be grown alongside regular vegetables and flowers. Key requirements include well-drained soil rich in organic matter, appropriate spacing (30-60cm for most herbs), and understanding each plant's climate preferences. The author provides specific cultivation details for plants like aloe vera (propagated by suckers, harvested after one year), basil (grown from seeds in rainy season), and turmeric (propagated by rhizomes, harvested after 180-240 days).

> "Medicinal plants if grown in home or kitchen gardens are the cheapest sources to cure one from health disorders."
**Quick Take**: Start with easy-to-grow herbs like mint, basil, and aloe vera to build confidence before expanding to more specialized medicinal plants.

### Natural Pest Management Through Botanicals
A revolutionary concept introduced is using medicinal plants themselves as natural pesticides (botanicals). Plants like neem, garlic, fennel, and pyrethrum produce compounds that repel or kill harmful insects while being safe for the environment. This approach creates a self-sustaining garden ecosystem where medicinal plants protect themselves and other crops. The book details how neem extracts work against nematodes and insects, while garlic sprays deter aphids and other common pests.

> "The positive point to use botanicals for pest control is that these are safer to the environment even if used in large amount in contrast to synthetic chemicals as these are naturally originated and extracted from plants."
**Quick Take**: Plant pest-repelling medicinal species as borders and use their extracts as natural sprays for chemical-free garden protection.

### Commercial Potential and Profitability
Beyond home gardening, the book explores the lucrative commercial cultivation of medicinal plants. With growing demand from herbal and cosmetic industries, plants like ashwagandha, lemon grass, mint, aloe vera, and turmeric offer excellent profit potential. The author notes that medicinal plants require less intensive management than conventional crops and can be grown on marginal lands. Both raw materials and extracted forms command premium prices in the market, with some plants yielding multiple harvests per year.

> "Both the extracted and raw forms of medicinal parts of these plants are purchased by the herbal and cosmetic industries at very higher prices."
**Quick Take**: Medicinal plant cultivation offers sustainable income opportunities while meeting growing global demand for natural health products.
