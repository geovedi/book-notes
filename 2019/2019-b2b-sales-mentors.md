# B2B Sales Mentors
**Authors**: Scott Ingram (Editor) | **Year**: 2019

## Summary
This book is a curated collection of real-world success stories from top 1% B2B sales professionals across various industries. Rather than theoretical frameworks, it delivers practical insights from sellers who have consistently achieved extraordinary results - either as the #1 performer on their teams or in the top 1% of quota attainment at large organizations. The stories serve as both inspiration and actionable guidance for sales professionals looking to elevate their performance.

The book is structured as a "sampler" featuring stories from the extensive Sales Success Stories series, with contributions from enterprise account executives, sales development representatives, and sales leaders at companies like Microsoft, Teradata, Gartner, and various tech startups. Each story concludes with concrete lessons learned that readers can immediately apply to their own sales practice.

Ideal for both seasoned sales veterans and newcomers to B2B sales, this book provides authentic strategies for prospecting, relationship building, deal closing, and career advancement. Readers will gain insights into creating differentiation, maintaining resilience through rejection, building executive relationships, and developing the mindset required for sustained sales excellence.

## Core Insights

### The Champion Mindset: Success Begins Between Your Ears
Kyle Gutzler reveals that the most critical sales asset isn't technique or territory—it's mindset. Drawing inspiration from Russell Wilson's "Why not me?" philosophy, he emphasizes that top performers operate with a reckless belief in their ability to achieve greatness. The four pillars of champion mindset include: unwavering self-belief that transforms thoughts into reality, strategic subtraction of limiting behaviors (like Michelangelo removing stone to reveal David), combining patience with persistence to power through rejection, and maintaining "fire" through goal visualization and surrounding yourself with A-players.

> "You will become what you believe. In the midst of a struggling season in sales, if you believe that you are incapable and that you cannot be a top sales professional, then that's what you will get. However, if you relentlessly fixate your mind on being a winner and being an elite performer, in time, that's what you will eventually become."

**Quick Take**: Your internal narrative determines your external results. Success starts with believing you're capable of it, then backing that belief with relentless action.

### Relationship Architecture: Build Networks Before You Need Them
Trong Nguyen demonstrates that executive relationship building requires strategic patience and a "surround strategy" rather than direct approaches. Using the Merrill-Reid social styles framework (analytical, driving, expressive, amiable), he maps personality types across the entire decision ecosystem, building influence through multiple stakeholders rather than relying on single-point access. This approach took six months of strategic relationship building before meeting his target CIO, but resulted in an immediate connection based on reputation precedents.

> "I completely influenced those who would influence the Global CIO. With my reputation now in the bag, I asked the Chief Technology Officer to introduce me to the Global CIO. When I went to the meeting, she said something to me that I will never forget. She said, 'Trong, after all of the great stories I had heard about you, I just pictured you would be 6'4" and devilishly handsome.'"

**Quick Take**: Build relationships with the influencers of your target decision-makers. Your reputation will precede you, making the eventual connection exponentially more valuable.

### The "Only Practices" Differentiator: Go Beyond Best Practices
Paul DiVincenzo introduces the concept of "only practices"—actions and capabilities that ONLY YOU can provide, creating separation from both internal colleagues and external competitors. When faced with an impossible custom uniform request from a luxury resort that could cover nearly his annual quota, he chose to say "yes" and figure it out rather than declining like every other provider. This led to developing expertise in garment sourcing, design, and manufacturing that became a competitive advantage for years.

> "The reality of sales is that if you stick to the standards of activity, follow up, professionalism, and great manners, you should be pretty successful. However, if you really want to go from average to being consistently at the top, it takes creating something called 'only' practices."

**Quick Take**: Success comes from developing unique capabilities that competitors can't match. Embrace impossible requests as opportunities to create proprietary expertise.

### Mathematical Goal Setting: Reverse Engineer Success
Trey Simonton shares his systematic approach to overachievement by treating sales as a mathematical problem. After hearing Ironman champion Mark Allen speak about changing his approach to finally win, Trey doubled his quota goal to $2.4M, then calculated backward: requiring 80 deals in pipeline at 27% close rate, needing 4 meetings per opportunity, 8 calls per meeting, and 32 emails per call. This created precise daily activity targets that he executed with religious discipline.

> "I took the time to back into what activities it would take daily to reach my higher annual sales goals. Once I knew my numbers, I threw out any thought about my quota and created new habits and routines based on my personal annual sales goal."

**Quick Take**: Set audacious goals, then break them down into measurable daily activities. Execute those activities with unwavering discipline regardless of immediate results.

### Creative Prospecting: Break Through with Personalization
Scott Ingram demonstrates the power of creative, personalized outreach through his story of getting a photo with Steve Madden at a Nordstrom appearance to break through to the shoe company's e-commerce executive. This approach leveraged the company's own brand (5-star ratings theme) and created an immediate conversation starter that cut through inbox noise. The story became part of their ongoing relationship and every subsequent presentation.

> "It's rarely easy to break through and get the attention of a cold prospect. Taking a creative, unique, and personalized approach improves your odds exponentially. Featuring a company's own brand (or executives) in your outreach can set you apart."

**Quick Take**: Standard outreach gets standard results. Creative, personalized approaches that incorporate your prospect's brand or interests dramatically increase response rates.

### SDR Excellence: The 1% Differentiator
Florin Tatulea reveals that the difference between average and elite Sales Development Representatives comes down to minor details executed consistently. His seven differentiation strategies include: using personalized video outreach, sending physical cards/packages, calling outside business hours, deep customization using company research, evening/weekend email timing, value-first LinkedIn connections, and voicemails that direct prospects to emails. These small tweaks, compounded over time, create exponential results.

> "The difference between the average SDR and the top ones are minor. However, minor tweaks over a long period of time lead to exponential results. Always be looking to innovate; these 7 steps that work now may not work in two years."

**Quick Take**: Excellence in sales development comes from consistently executing small differentiators that others won't bother with.

### Discovery as Science: Research Beyond the Obvious
Debe Rapson emphasizes that modern discovery isn't a single meeting step but a comprehensive research process involving 8-12 decision stakeholders. She demonstrates thorough preparation through analyzing annual reports, 10-K filings, executive presentations, technology stacks (builtwith.com), and even social media activity. This deep research enables asking probing questions that uncover the "why" behind purchase decisions and establish trusted advisor status.

> "Today, decisions are made through consensus and unlike the past, discovery is not done solely with one or two key stakeholders, but rather everyone on the decision team, typically between 8 to 12 people."

**Quick Take**: Discovery success is proportional to preparation depth. Research beyond surface-level information to understand strategic initiatives and stakeholder motivations before ever entering the room.

### Service-First Selling: Transform Losses into Opportunities
DeJuan Brown redefines the post-loss conversation from the dismissive "let me know if anything changes" to proactive service continuation. By offering specific value-add resources even after losing deals, he transforms rejections into opportunities. His approach helped turn a lost tax software deal into a $1.8M win when he continued providing comparative analysis services that demonstrated superior value, eventually leading to the prospect replacing their existing solution entirely.

> "Service should be the default setting for Sales Professionals. Consistency in service builds value that can't be uncovered during discovery calls nor articulated in a pitch. Commitment to service even after a 'loss' is a powerful differentiator."

**Quick Take**: Service doesn't end when a deal is lost. Continued value provision can transform rejection into future opportunity and establish differentiation.

### The S.A.L.E.S. Framework: Strategic Customer Partnerships
Phil Terrill shares his S.A.L.E.S. acronym developed through a year-long digital transformation engagement: Scale through leveraging others' expertise, Agility through realistic service level agreements, Leverage of team strengths, Experience through skin-in-the-game execution, and Sacrifice through going beyond status quo. This framework helped guide a global retailer through digital transformation and resulted in a $1.8M deal.

> "The S.A.L.E.S. acronym is a framework that I still consider in my actions, as it provides key objectives or skills that I should constantly be driving towards with customers and partners."

**Quick Take**: Systematic approaches to customer partnerships create consistency in value delivery and relationship development.

### Unstoppable Resilience: The Sales Rollercoaster Mindset
Abel Lomas chronicles a seven-month rollercoaster journey with a dream account that went from near-closed to dead to revived through persistent creativity and relationship building. When traditional methods failed, he broke through with a bold Saturday text message to the CMO that challenged their marketing philosophy. This led to rebuilding the opportunity with a new VP and ultimately closing the deal through face-to-face relationship building.

> "Sales might be a rollercoaster, but it is the REP that needs to be UNSTOPPABLE. Otherwise, the rollercoaster is likely to stop at the wrong point in the buyer journey."

**Quick Take**: Sales resilience requires creativity beyond standard playbook tactics. Sometimes breakthrough moments come from unconventional approaches at unexpected times.

## Conclusion
This book is absolutely worth your time if you're looking for authentic, field-tested strategies rather than theoretical frameworks. The power lies in its diversity—every story comes from practitioners actively executing these methods in real-world scenarios. While the tactics vary across industries and roles, the underlying principles of champion mindset, service-first orientation, creative differentiation, and systematic execution are universally applicable.

The biggest reason to read this book is its emphasis on mindset as the foundation of sales success. Multiple contributors emphasize that technique and territory matter less than belief systems and daily disciplines. This isn't just a collection of sales tips—it's a masterclass in the psychology and operational approach of sustained sales excellence.

For sales professionals feeling stuck or seeking to break through performance plateaus, these stories provide both inspiration and practical pathways. The book's greatest value is demonstrating that extraordinary sales results come from ordinary professionals executing small differentiators with extraordinary consistency and belief.
