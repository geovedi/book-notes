# Relentless Focus
**Authors**: Patrick King | **Year**: 2019
**Category**: Self-Help & Personal Development

## Summary
"Relentless Focus" delivers exactly what its subtitle promises: 27 practical, science-backed tweaks to overcome procrastination, boost productivity, eliminate distractions, and accomplish more in less time. The book is structured around four core phases: breaking inertia to get started, creating and maintaining momentum, sustaining focus, and strategic planning. King combines psychological research, behavioral science, and personal experience to create a comprehensive productivity system that works with human nature rather than against it.

This guide is perfect for busy professionals, students, entrepreneurs, or anyone struggling with procrastination and focus issues. Readers will gain actionable strategies to immediately improve their daily output, eliminate time-wasting activities, and build sustainable productivity habits that compound over time.

#### ðŸŽ¯ Daily Goals Framework
Write your goals the night before with a mix of small wins and challenging tasks. Make lists slightly ambitious to create motivation but not overwhelming to cause paralysis.

> "When you write your list the night before, you'll have a clear and objective view of what you need to accomplish the next day."
**Quick Take**: Plan tomorrow when today's discipline is still high.

Sort tasks into five categories:
1. **Immediate Attention**: Due within hours
2. **In Progress**: Currently working on
3. **Follow-ups**: Responses waiting on others
4. **Upcoming**: Future planning items
5. **Ideas**: Potential projects

> "Instead of a to-do list, you can categorize your tasks. This is helpful because it provides greater context to what you should be focusing on."
**Quick Take**: Context prevents priority confusion.

#### âš–ï¸ The Pareto Principle (80/20 Rule)
80% of results come from 20% of efforts. Identify and focus on high-impact activities while eliminating or delegating low-value tasks. Apply this ruthlessly to time management.

> "The Pareto Principle states that 80% of the results you want out of a task will be produced by 20% of your activities and efforts directed toward it."
**Quick Take**: Find your 20% and multiply it.

#### ðŸ” Focus Diagnosis
Learn to recognize when you're operating at reduced capacity:
- Task switching without completion
- Physically avoiding work tools
- Physical fatigue or sleepiness
- Re-reading content without comprehension

> "Diagnose your focus and learn to recognize when you should take a break. Stop working at 50% capacity and come back when you can go forward at full steam."
**Quick Take**: Quit early rather than work poorly.
