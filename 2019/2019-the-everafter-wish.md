# The Everafter Wish
**Authors**: Rachel Morgan | **Year**: 2019

## Summary
In a world where fae, vampires, and shifters coexist with humans, Elle has spent years enslaved to her cruel stepmother by a magical slave charm. Her only hope for freedom lies in completing an ancient quest that grants humans magic—something the powerful fae king has gone to great lengths to keep secret. When Elle discovers she can obtain magic, she becomes a target of King Belaric, who will stop at nothing to preserve fae dominance by burying the truth that humans are magical equals.

This urban fantasy is perfect for readers who enjoy stories about discovering hidden power, overthrowing oppressive systems, and the transformative journey from victim to hero. The book offers a compelling mix of magic, political intrigue, and romance that will satisfy fans of strong female protagonists and supernatural power dynamics.

## Core Insights

### The Hidden Truth of Human Magic
Centuries ago, humans could obtain magic through a dangerous quest, making them equals to the fae and other magical races. This knowledge has been systematically suppressed by King Belaric, who discovered the truth and killed those who proved it. Elle's successful completion of the quest threatens to unravel the carefully maintained hierarchy that keeps humans subservient.

> "Humans can obtain magic. Hundreds of years ago, it was a normal way of life. Humans can go on a quest, and at the end of it, they walk away with magic. We can't enslave them. They're our equals."

**Quick Take**: True power isn't granted by those in authority—it's inherent and can only be temporarily suppressed.

### The Price of Freedom
Elle's journey reveals that freedom often requires sacrifice. She endures the slave charm, loses her parents, faces death multiple times, and eventually risks imprisonment to break free from her stepmother's control. Her quest demonstrates that the cost of liberation is high, but the cost of remaining enslaved is infinitely greater.

> "I'm not your slave anymore. And I have magic. So don't try anything."

**Quick Take**: True freedom isn't given—it must be claimed through courage, sacrifice, and the willingness to face impossible odds.

### Power Corrupts Absolutely
King Belaric represents how the desire to maintain power can corrupt even those who should protect others. Despite knowing humans are magical equals, he chooses murder and deception over equality, revealing that the greatest threat to justice often comes from those benefiting from injustice.

> "I will do whatever I must to preserve our way of life. To protect the legacy of the fae."

**Quick Take**: Systems of oppression persist not because they're right, but because those in power fear losing their privilege.

### Unexpected Allies and Found Family
The story highlights how allies emerge from unexpected places. Dex, the fae prince who should represent everything Elle opposes, becomes her greatest supporter. Liana, long thought dead, becomes a crucial link to Elle's past and the truth. These relationships demonstrate that solidarity transcends species, status, and background.

> "I know he will. I know he will come for me."

**Quick Take**: True allies are those who stand with you against their own interests when justice is at stake.

## Key Plot Points

- **Elle's Discovery**: After years of enslavement, Elle learns about the ancient quest that grants humans magic and secretly seeks out the Godmother
- **The Quest's Success**: Elle completes the dangerous journey through enchanted forests and trials, emerging with silver human magic
- **King's Betrayal**: When Elle and Dex reveal the truth to King Belaric, he imprisons Elle and threatens her life to silence Dex
- **The Prison Revelation**: Elle discovers Liana, Dex's childhood nanny and her mother's friend, has been imprisoned for years for knowing the same truth
- **Queen's Doubt**: Queen Amra visits Elle in prison, suggesting she may question her husband's actions despite publicly supporting them

## Characters to Remember

- **Elle**: A determined young woman who transforms from enslaved victim to powerful magic-wielder
- **Prince Chevalier "Dex"**: The fae prince who risks everything to help Elle reveal the truth about human magic
- **King Belaric**: The ruthless fae king who maintains power through suppression and violence
- **Liana**: Dex's former nanny, imprisoned for over a decade for knowing humans can obtain magic
- **Queen Amra**: Dex's mother, torn between loyalty to her husband and love for her son

## Questions to Consider

- How does the book explore themes of systemic oppression and the suppression of truth?
- What does Elle's journey from slavery to power reveal about personal transformation?
- How do the different magical races in this world reflect real-world social hierarchies?
- At what point does maintaining tradition become harmful rather than protective?
- How does the relationship between Elle and Dex challenge expectations about power dynamics?

## Conclusion
*The Everafter Wish* delivers a satisfying blend of urban fantasy action, political intrigue, and personal transformation. Rachel Morgan creates a compelling world where magic serves as a metaphor for power, oppression, and the potential for revolution. Elle's journey from enslaved victim to powerful magic-wielder is both inspiring and emotionally resonant.

The book is worth your time if you enjoy stories about overthrowing corrupt systems, discovering hidden strengths, and the power of truth to challenge oppression. While some fantasy tropes may feel familiar, Morgan's execution and the complex relationship between Elle and Dex elevate this above standard urban fantasy fare. The biggest reason to read this is Elle herself—her transformation from frightened victim to confident magic-wielder is compelling and empowering, making this a must-read for fans of strong female protagonists in fantasy settings.
