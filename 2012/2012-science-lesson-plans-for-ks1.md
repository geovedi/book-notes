# Science Lesson Plans for KS1
**Authors**: Pam Larkins | **Year**: 2012

## Summary
This comprehensive resource provides 10 structured science lesson plans designed specifically for Key Stage 1 students (ages 5-7), focusing on the National Curriculum topic of "Sorting and Using Materials." Each lesson follows a consistent pedagogical framework with clear learning objectives, hands-on activities, assessment strategies, and cross-curricular integration opportunities.

Perfect for early years and primary teachers seeking ready-to-implement science investigations that develop both subject knowledge and scientific enquiry skills. The lessons progress systematically from basic material exploration to practical applications, ensuring students build understanding through concrete experiences.

## Conclusion
This practical resource delivers exactly what early years teachers need: ready-to-use, curriculum-aligned science lessons that develop both content knowledge and scientific thinking skills. The strength lies in its consistent structure, hands-on approach, and emphasis on understanding through doing rather than memorizing facts.

The biggest reason to use this book is its comprehensive approach to developing scientific enquiry skills alongside material knowledge. Students don't just learn that wood is hard or metal is shiny - they learn how to observe, test, predict, and conclude like real scientists. These foundational skills transfer across all subject areas and support lifelong learning habits.

For teachers feeling uncertain about teaching science to young children, this resource provides the structure, confidence, and engaging activities needed to create memorable learning experiences. The investment in these ten lessons pays dividends throughout the entire school year as students apply their investigation skills to new topics and questions.
