## Core Frameworks

### The GROW Model - Coaching Session Structure
The GROW model provides a structured approach to coaching sessions, ensuring maximum progression for the coachee through four distinct stages:

**G - Goal Setting**: Establish both session-specific goals and short/long-term objectives. Clear goals provide direction and purpose for the coaching relationship.

**R - Reality Checking**: Explore the current situation through questioning and reflection. Understanding where the coachee stands creates a foundation for meaningful progress.

**O - Options**: Brainstorm alternative strategies and courses of action the coachee can pursue. This stage expands possibilities and encourages creative thinking.

**W - Will/Way Forward**: Conclude with structured solutions involving what will be done, when, by whom, and the motivation behind the progression goal.

> "Coaching assumes the person is well and can solve their own problems; typically has a strong focus on goals."
**Quick Take**: The GROW model transforms coaching conversations from unstructured chats to powerful developmental sessions that drive concrete outcomes.

### Coaching vs. Mentoring - Understanding the Distinction
While often used interchangeably, these roles serve different purposes in organizational development:

**Coaching Characteristics**:
- Short-term focus on specific skills and performance improvement
- Coach may not be a subject matter expert
- Drives goal-setting process
- Typically 3-12 months duration
- Focus on immediate performance gains

**Mentoring Characteristics**:
- Long-term career development focus
- Mentor is acknowledged expert in the field
- Mentee sets more agenda; mentor offers experience-based advice
- Typically 1-2 years duration
- Broader perspective on career and organizational relationships

**Key Insight**: Effective managers often switch between coaching and mentoring roles based on the individual's immediate needs versus long-term development goals.

### The ARROW Model - Alternative Coaching Framework
ARROW provides another structured approach with added emphasis on reflection:

**A - Awareness**: Understanding the current situation and self-awareness
**R - Reflection**: Identifying obstacles and challenges (the added "R" beyond GROW)
**R - Resources**: Identifying available resources and support
**O - Options**: Exploring possible courses of action
**W - Way Forward**: Commitment to specific action steps

**Quick Win**: Use ARROW when dealing with complex situations where self-awareness and obstacle identification are crucial before generating solutions.

## Learning Models for Enhanced Coaching

### Kolb's Learning Cycle - Understanding Learning Styles
Effective coaches recognize different learning preferences:

**Activists**: Concrete experience-seekers who learn by doing
**Reflectors**: Observers who learn through thinking and reviewing
**Theorists**: Abstract thinkers who learn through understanding concepts
**Pragmatists**: Practical doers who learn through testing ideas

**Application Strategy**: Tailor your coaching approach to match the coachee's learning style for maximum effectiveness.

### The Johari Window - Building Self-Awareness
This feedback model helps individuals understand themselves better through four perspectives:

**Open Area**: Known to self and others - leverage these strengths
**Blind Spot**: Unknown to self but known to others - invite feedback here
**Hidden Area**: Known to self but hidden from others - selective disclosure builds trust
**Unknown Area**: Unknown to both - exploration opportunities for growth

**Quick Take**: Effective coaching expands the "open area" through feedback and self-disclosure, building deeper self-awareness.

## Organizational Implementation

### Building a Coaching Culture - Key Success Factors
**Senior Management Buy-In**: Essential for resource allocation and cultural acceptance
**Clear Policy Framework**: Defines roles, responsibilities, and boundaries
**Training for Line Managers**: Equips managers with coaching skills and confidence
**Measurement Systems**: Tracks ROI and demonstrates program value
**Cultural Readiness**: Assess organizational culture using Handy's framework (Power, People, Role, Task, Quality-based cultures)

**Barrier-Breaking Strategies**: Address individual resistance, operational constraints, and organizational barriers through targeted interventions and clear communication of benefits.

### Legal and Ethical Considerations
**Confidentiality Guidelines**: Clear agreements about information privacy
**Power Dynamics**: Awareness of positional authority and its impact
**Cultural Sensitivity**: Adapt approaches for diverse workforce
**Supervision Requirements**: Regular oversight for quality assurance
**Professional Boundaries**: Clear distinction between coaching, counseling, and therapy
