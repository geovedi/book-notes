# The Secret Door to Success
**Authors**: Florence Scovel Shinn | **Year**: 2012

## Summary
"The Secret Door to Success" is a spiritual guide that reveals how thoughts, words, and beliefs create our reality. Florence Scovel Shinn teaches that success comes through understanding and applying universal spiritual laws—particularly the power of spoken words, faith, and right thinking. The book shows how we build invisible walls (like resentment, fear, and doubt) that block our success, and provides practical affirmations and mental techniques to dismantle these barriers.

This book is perfect for spiritual seekers, those interested in New Thought philosophy, or anyone feeling stuck who wants to understand how their mindset affects their results. Readers will learn practical techniques for reprogramming their subconscious mind, using affirmations effectively, and removing mental blocks to success.

## Core Insights

### The Power of Your Spoken Word
Your words are like wands filled with magic and power— they have the ability to create or destroy your reality. Shinn emphasizes that "death and life are in power of the tongue," meaning every word you speak either builds up or tears down your success. When you speak words of lack, limitation, or fear, you reinforce those conditions. When you speak words of abundance, faith, and success, you activate creative forces that manifest those outcomes.

> "Man's word is his wand filled with magic and power!"
**Quick Take**: Start monitoring your self-talk and eliminate words of limitation. Replace "I can't" with "I can" and "I don't have" with "I am receiving."

### Breaking Down Your Walls of Jericho
Everyone builds invisible walls around their success—walls of resentment, envy, fear, discouragement, or past-focused thinking. These walls separate you from your "divinely designed success" and heart's desires. Shinn teaches that just as the Israelites brought down the walls of Jericho through shouting and faith, you can dissolve your own barriers through powerful affirmations and renewed belief.

> "What kind of wall have you built around your Jericho? Often, it is a wall of resentment—resenting someone, or resenting a situation, shuts off your good."
**Quick Take**: Identify your primary mental block to success. Use the affirmation: "The walls of lack and delay now crumble away, and I enter my Promised Land, under grace."

### The Magic of Interest and Enthusiasm
Success comes when you take genuine interest in what you're doing and make it interesting to others. Shinn shares the story of Jim Tully, a former tramp who became a successful author by dramatizing his experiences and finding fascination in his own life story. When you're passionate about your work, others are drawn to your energy and opportunities naturally flow your way.

> "That is the Secret of Success, to make what you are doing interesting to other people. Be interested yourself, and others will find you interesting."
**Quick Take**: Find something genuinely fascinating about your current work or situation. Focus on that aspect and let your enthusiasm become contagious.

### Divine Supply and Right Expectation
You came into this world "financed by God" with everything you need already on your pathway. Success flows when you expect good, trust in divine timing, and maintain faith regardless of appearances. Shinn teaches that lack and limitation are illusions—your true supply is endless and immediately available through faith and the spoken word.

> "Man comes into the world financed by God, with all that he desires or requires already on his pathway."
**Quick Take**: Practice the "magic purse" affirmation: "I have the magic purse of the Spirit; it can never be depleted; as money goes out, immediately money comes in, under grace in perfect ways."

### The System of Success
Success follows a systematic approach: courage, endurance, undivided attention, and loving absorption in your path. Successful people never give up, especially when success seems closest. They maintain their focus, avoid scattering their forces through excessive talking about their plans, and persist through apparent failures.

> "Success is not a secret, it is a System."
**Quick Take**: Choose one goal and give it your undivided attention. Stop discussing your plans extensively and instead focus your energy on consistent action.

## Action Items
- Monitor and eliminate all words of limitation from your vocabulary
- Practice daily affirmations that focus on abundance and success
- Release resentment and envy toward others' success
- Find genuine interest and enthusiasm in your current work
- Maintain faith and positive expectation regardless of appearances
- Focus your energy on one main goal rather than scattering your forces

## Questions to Consider
- What walls have you built around your own "Jericho" of success?
- How do your typical words and conversations reflect your beliefs about success?
- What would you attempt if you knew you were "financed by God"?
- Where in your life are you giving power to appearances instead of faith?
- How can you make your current work more interesting to yourself and others?

## Conclusion
"The Secret Door to Success" is absolutely worth your time if you're open to spiritual principles and ready to take responsibility for your mindset. Florence Scovel Shinn offers timeless wisdom that bridges Biblical teachings with practical metaphysics. While some examples feel dated (the book was written in the 1940s), the core principles are immediately applicable.

The biggest reason to read this book is its emphasis on the power of words and expectations. In an age of information overload, Shinn reminds us that our internal world creates our external reality. The techniques she provides—especially the affirmations and focus on eliminating resentment—are simple enough for anyone to practice but profound enough to transform results. If you feel stuck between your current reality and your desired success, this book provides the keys to unlock your personal "door to success."
