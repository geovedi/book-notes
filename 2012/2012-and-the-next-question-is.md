# And the Next Question Is: Powerful Questions for Sticky Moments
**Authors**: Rachel A. Alexander & Julia M. L. Russell | **Year**: 2012

## Summary
This practical coaching handbook is a comprehensive collection of powerful questions designed for coaches, leaders, and anyone who uses questioning as a tool for development and growth. The book addresses those "sticky moments" when conversations stall and you need the perfect question to get things moving again. Written by two experienced professional coaches, it serves as both a reference guide and a training resource for anyone looking to enhance their coaching practice or leadership communication skills.

The book is organized into 12 focused chapters covering everything from session management and goal setting to confidence building, leadership development, and career transitions. Each chapter contains carefully curated questions drawn from over a decade of professional coaching experience, designed to elicit insight, awareness, and action from clients.

This book is essential for professional coaches seeking to expand their questioning toolkit, managers wanting to adopt a more coaching-oriented leadership style, and anyone who uses questioning to facilitate personal or professional development. It's particularly valuable for new coaches looking for a shortcut to building experience and seasoned coaches who want variety in their approach.

## Core Insights

### The Power of Questions Over Answers
The fundamental premise of the book is that great questions are more valuable than good answers. A well-timed, powerful question can unleash the innate wisdom that already exists within people, helping them find their own solutions rather than receiving external advice. The authors emphasize that masterful coaches often conduct entire sessions using only 6-8 questions, never offering advice but creating space for client discovery.

> "A great question is more valuable than a good answer. We invite you to look through these pages, find the right question for the moment and ask that powerful question which will unleash the innate wisdom in all of us."

**Quick Take**: Shift your focus from having the right answers to asking the right questions—the most transformative conversations happen when you create space for others to discover their own insights.

### Session Management and Foundation Building
The first chapter focuses on establishing the coaching relationship and exploring the client's reality. Key questions help set the foundation for successful coaching by clarifying goals, establishing boundaries, and creating a framework for the coaching journey. These initial questions often determine the success of the entire coaching engagement.

> "What is currently impossible for you to do, that if it were possible, would change everything?"

**Quick Take**: Start every coaching relationship with questions that establish clear boundaries, define success, and explore the client's current reality—this foundation makes everything else possible.

### Goals and Commitment Evaluation
The book provides sophisticated questions to evaluate a client's genuine commitment to their stated goals. These questions go beyond surface-level goal-setting to explore the deeper motivations, potential obstacles, and personal resources available for goal achievement. The authors stress that true commitment reveals itself through the questions clients are willing to explore honestly.

> "If you thought of yourself as the majority stakeholder in your life—how does that change things?"

**Quick Take**: Use questioning to distinguish between goals clients want versus those they're truly committed to—look for alignment between stated objectives and personal values.

### Confidence-Building Through Articulation
Chapter 4 explores how coaches can help clients build confidence by accurately articulating what they observe in clients' communication patterns and behaviors. The questions help clients recognize their own strengths, identify confidence-draining patterns, and develop more self-assurance through increased self-awareness.

> "What would be 100% right now?"

**Quick Take**: Build client confidence by helping them see themselves clearly and recognize their existing strengths—often confidence comes from accurate self-perception rather than external validation.

### Leadership and Communication Development
The leadership and communication sections provide questions that help leaders develop their coaching style and improve their questioning skills. These questions encourage leaders to think about their influence, communication patterns, and the impact they have on others. The authors provide specific questions for different leadership challenges, from team motivation to organizational change.

> "What would be the most powerful question I could ask you right now?"

**Quick Take**: Great leaders develop their teams by asking questions rather than providing answers—cultivate the habit of turning statements into questions to foster team growth.

## Conclusion
"And the Next Question Is" is an indispensable reference for anyone serious about developing their coaching or leadership questioning skills. The book's strength lies in its practical organization—each chapter addresses specific coaching challenges with ready-to-use questions that have been tested in real-world situations. While it's not meant to be read cover-to-cover, it serves as an invaluable toolkit for both new and experienced coaches.

The biggest reason to read this book is its ability to rescue you in those "sticky moments" when your mind goes blank and you need the perfect question to get things moving again. The authors have done the heavy lifting of collecting, categorizing, and field-testing hundreds of questions across every major coaching domain. For new coaches, this book is worth its weight in gold as a shortcut to building experience and confidence. For seasoned practitioners, it offers fresh perspectives and question variations that can breathe new life into established coaching practices.

If you're in a position where you help others grow—whether as a professional coach, manager, therapist, or leader—this book will become one of your most frequently referenced resources. It's the kind of practical guide that pays for itself the first time you use the perfect question in a challenging moment.
