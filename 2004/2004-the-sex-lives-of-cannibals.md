# The Sex Lives of Cannibals
**Author**: J. Maarten Troost | **Year**: 2004

## Summary
When J. Maarten Troost and his girlfriend Sylvia decide to escape their mundane Washington D.C. lives, they impulsively move to Tarawa, a remote atoll in the Equatorial Pacific island nation of Kiribati. What follows is a hilarious and eye-opening two-year adventure in one of the most isolated and challenging places on Earth, where modern amenities disappear and daily survival becomes both absurd and enlightening.

This book is perfect for travelers, expats, and anyone fascinated by what happens when modern Westerners encounter traditional island life. You'll gain insight into cultural adaptation, the absurdities of international development, and finding humor in life's most challenging situations.

## Core Insights

### From Paradise to Reality Shock
Troost arrives expecting a tropical paradise but discovers Tarawa is a harsh, overcrowded slum where basic necessities like water, electricity, and food are luxuries. The island's stunning natural beauty contrasts sharply with its polluted beaches, contaminated water, and infrastructure collapse. He learns that paradise exists mainly in his imagination, and reality is far more interesting.

> "Tarawa wasn't a disaster waiting to happen. It is a disaster."

**Quick Take**: The most rewarding travel experiences often come when we abandon our romantic expectations and embrace reality as it actually exists.

### The Comedy of Survival
Daily life becomes a series of absurd challenges: finding uncontaminated water, avoiding toxic fish, dodging packs of hungry dogs while carrying tuna, and surviving the dreaded "Great Beer Crisis" when the island's beer shipment gets misdirected. Troost discovers that humor is the essential survival tool when faced with overwhelming daily difficulties.

> "I became immune to disease, not physically, but psychologically. Nothing fazed me."

**Quick Take**: When life becomes genuinely difficult, laughter isn't just nice—it's necessary for maintaining your sanity.

### Cultural Collision and Adaptation
The I-Kiribati people live with a fatalistic "akia" (unavailable) attitude toward life's problems, while Troost and other Western expats struggle to maintain their accustomed efficiency and planning. Through two years of cultural immersion, Troost gradually adapts, learning that island time isn't lazy—it's a different, valid way of living.

> "I learned that contrary to what they say, books do not write themselves."

**Quick Take**: True cultural adaptation requires abandoning your own assumptions about how life "should" work and embracing different approaches to living.

### The Paradox of Development Aid
Troost observes the tragicomedy of international aid efforts in Kiribati—well-funded projects that often fail, corrupt officials, and foreign interventions that sometimes make things worse. The millions spent on aid haven't solved basic problems like sanitation, water, or food security, revealing the complexity of "helping" developing nations.

**Quick Take**: Good intentions combined with cultural ignorance often create more problems than they solve, especially in international development.

### Finding Freedom in Limitation
Stripped of modern conveniences, career ambitions, and entertainment options, Troost discovers a strange freedom in simplicity. When your daily concerns are finding fresh water and avoiding parasites, life becomes more immediate and meaningful. He learns to appreciate small joys—sunsets, coconut water, successful fishing expeditions—that we take for granted.

**Quick Take**: Removing options and distractions from your life can reveal what actually matters and bring unexpected contentment.

## Best Quotes
- "It was one or the other, sublime or wretched, never neither. Survival on Tarawa, I decided, would depend on one's reaction to the absurd."
- "Daily consumption of several cans of Victoria Bitter became an integral part of my well-being, possibly because I am of Dutch-Czech stock and thus warmly inclined toward beer, but also because it is immensely fun to quaff beers with your loved one while sitting at reef's edge watching the world's most spectacular sunsets."
- "When I woke up in the morning, my first thought was always, What now? I am quite likely the world's laziest adrenaline-junkie, and so living on Tarawa worked well for me. I didn't have to do anything. Shit just happened on Tarawa."

## Key Takeaways
- Travel to truly remote places will destroy your preconceptions and rebuild your understanding of what's essential
- The ability to laugh at yourself and absurd situations is crucial for mental health in challenging environments
- Cultural differences run deeper than language and customs—they involve completely different approaches to time, resources, and problem-solving
- International development work is far more complex than outsiders realize
- Physical hardship can lead to mental clarity and unexpected contentment

## Questions to Consider
- How would you react if basic necessities like clean water became daily challenges?
- What aspects of modern Western life seem essential but are actually luxuries?
- How do you balance respecting local culture with promoting positive change?
- Can money and foreign expertise actually improve traditional societies?
- What would you discover about yourself if stripped of all modern comforts?

## Conclusion
**Is this worth your time?** Absolutely. This is one of the funniest and most honest travel memoirs you'll ever read. Troost masterfully combines laugh-out-loud humor with genuine insight about cultural adaptation, international development, and finding meaning in unexpected places.

**The biggest reason to read it**: You'll never look at water, electricity, or a trip to the grocery store the same way again. Troost's two years in "paradise gone wrong" will make you appreciate modern life while questioning whether progress always means improvement. His self-deprecating humor and keen observations about both Western and island culture create a story that's both entertaining and profoundly enlightening about what happens when we venture far beyond our comfort zones.
