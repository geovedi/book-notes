# It's Mine
**Authors**: Steven Boykey Sidley | **Year**: 2023 | **Category**: Business & Finance

## Summary
This book explores how cryptocurrency and blockchain technology are fundamentally redefining the concept of ownership in society. Sidley argues that we're witnessing a "Cambrian explosion" of innovation that challenges traditional notions of property, identity, and value through mathematical certainty rather than institutional trust.

The book examines how blockchain's core innovation—the ability to establish provable ownership through cryptography rather than through banks, governments, or other intermediaries—is transforming everything from art and finance to identity and governance. Through 22 chapters, Sidley traces the evolution from early concepts of ownership through the current crypto landscape to future possibilities.

For business leaders, tech professionals, and anyone interested in understanding how blockchain technology will reshape economic and social structures, this book provides a comprehensive yet accessible roadmap to the ownership revolution underway.

## Core Insights

### Mathematical Ownership Replaces Trust-Based Systems
The revolutionary core of blockchain technology is its use of public/private key cryptography and hash functions to create mathematically provable ownership. Unlike traditional ownership that relies on institutions (banks, governments, courts) to validate and enforce property rights, blockchain uses mathematical certainty. As Sidley explains, this allows "authorized parties securely send and receive self-owned cryptotokens to and from each other, without the intervention, surveillance and facilitation of any third party, the whole shebang secured by mathematical secrets."

> "I would prefer maths to be in charge, at least for those things that need perfect predictability. Humans have many fine qualities, but perfect predictability is not one of them."

**Quick Take**: Blockchain replaces institutional trust with mathematical certainty, making ownership truly personal and non-censorable.

### NFTs Are Digital Title Deeds, Not Just Digital Art
While most people associate NFTs with expensive digital art, Sidley reveals they're fundamentally about establishing digital ownership and provenance. NFTs serve as digital title deeds that can represent ownership of anything—art, real estate, concert tickets, professional credentials, or even personal identity. The technology creates permanent, transparent records of who owns what and how ownership has changed over time.

> "Most people associate owning an NFT with owning a piece of expensive and sometimes questionable piece of digital art... But this is not only incorrect, or at least partially misunderstood, because it is also a tip of a very large iceberg."

**Quick Take**: NFTs are the foundation for a new system of digital title registries that could eventually replace many traditional property records.

### The Crypto Ecosystem Is Building Alternative Economic Systems
The book explores how various crypto innovations—DeFi, DAOs, GameFi, and the Metaverse—are creating parallel economic systems that challenge traditional finance and governance. These systems enable peer-to-peer transactions, community-owned organizations, and play-to-earn gaming economies where players actually own their in-game assets and can trade them freely.

> "And then blockchain arrived and cryptocurrencies and NFTs and, unsurprisingly, a fissure opened that quickly turned into an abyss... those upstarts who wanted games to be community-owned, community-led, permissionless and driven by open fungible and non-fungible cryptotoken economies."

**Quick Take**: Crypto is building entirely new economic structures that operate outside traditional financial and corporate systems.

### Self-Sovereign Identity Becomes Possible
Perhaps most profoundly, blockchain technology enables true self-sovereign identity where individuals control their own digital identities without relying on governments or corporations. This could transform everything from travel documents and professional credentials to personal data privacy and digital legacy management.

> "In the future, you will still have pseudonymous identities that still express 'you'; you will have avatars which in part create 'you'; you will have a range of credentials that authenticate 'you'; you will have intelligent assistants that expand 'you'; and you will have a digital legacy to preserve 'you'."

**Quick Take**: Blockchain gives individuals unprecedented control over their digital identities and personal data.

## Conclusion

This book is worth your time if you want to understand one of the most fundamental technological shifts since the internet. Sidley makes a compelling case that blockchain technology isn't just about creating new forms of money—it's about reinventing ownership itself.

The biggest reason to read this book is to grasp how mathematical certainty will eventually replace institutional trust across nearly every aspect of economic life. While the crypto world currently feels chaotic and speculative, the underlying technology addresses fundamental human needs for security, autonomy, and provable ownership.

As the author notes, during the Russia-Ukraine conflict, citizens on both sides turned to cryptocurrency to protect their wealth when traditional banking systems failed. This practical application reveals the profound real-world implications of technology that enables ownership independent of governments or institutions. Whether blockchain ultimately fulfills this revolutionary promise remains to be seen, but understanding its potential is essential for anyone concerned with the future of economic and social organization.
