# Slowly to Heaven
**Original Title**: Traag naar de hemel
**Author**: Evi Hanssen | **Year**: 2023 

## Summary
"Slowly to Heaven" is a deeply moving memoir by Belgian author Evi Hanssen documenting her mother Arlette's final 20-month journey with terminal cancer. Rather than portraying death as purely tragic, this book reveals how the family transformed the dying process into a meaningful, beautiful, and sometimes even joyful experience. Through intimate conversations, shared moments, and Arlette's remarkable wisdom, the author demonstrates that death—when approached with acceptance and love—can be as profound as birth.

This book is perfect for anyone facing the loss of a loved one, healthcare professionals working with terminal patients, or readers seeking to understand how to find meaning and beauty in life's most challenging transitions. Readers will discover practical insights on how to communicate openly about death, support loved ones through their final journey, and transform grief into gratitude.

## Core Insights

### Redefining Death as a Journey Rather Than an Ending
The book challenges society's perception of death as something to be feared and avoided. Arlette approaches her dying process with remarkable acceptance and even excitement, viewing it as "the highest step on the ladder of life." She actively plans her funeral, chooses her burial outfit, and maintains control over her final moments, demonstrating that death can be approached with the same intentionality and preparation as other major life events.

> "Mijn moeder gunde iedereen een gelukkig leven, ik gun iedereen een gelukkige dood. Beide zijn niet vanzelfsprekend. Je moet een beetje geluk hebben en er ten slotte ook bewust voor kiezen."
> _Original: "My mother wished everyone a happy life, I wish everyone a happy death. Both are not self-evident. You need a bit of luck and ultimately also have to consciously choose it."_

**Quick Take**: Death becomes less frightening when we approach it as a natural transition rather than an ending.

### The Power of Open Communication About Death
Arlette and Evi break the taboo surrounding death by speaking openly about it from the moment of diagnosis. They create a podcast called "The Farewell Tour of My Mother" to share their journey publicly. This openness allows them to live more fully in the present, make conscious choices about how to spend their remaining time, and avoid the elephant in the room that often surrounds terminal illness.

> "Door het sterven en lichamelijke lijden te erkennen, werden onze verwondering en dankbaarheid voor het leven steeds groter. Hoezo, de dood is enkel een verdrietig gebeuren?"
> _Original: "By acknowledging dying and physical suffering, our wonder and gratitude for life grew ever greater. How is death only a sad event?"_

**Quick Take**: Talking about death doesn't diminish life—it enhances our appreciation for the time we have.

### Finding Beauty in the Dying Process
Rather than viewing the final months as purely tragic, the family discovers moments of profound beauty and connection. Arlette maintains her sense of humor, dresses in colorful outfits, and continues to find joy in small moments. The author describes her mother's death day as potentially "the most beautiful day of my life," emphasizing that love and presence make even the most difficult moments meaningful.

> "Ik kan me voorstellen dat gisteren wel de mooiste dag uit mijn leven was. Niets had haast, alles was oprecht. Het was een dag die naar de essentie van het leven ging. Liefde, in haar puurste vorm."
> _Original: "I can imagine that yesterday might have been the most beautiful day of my life. Nothing was rushed, everything was sincere. It was a day that went to the essence of life. Love, in its purest form."_

**Quick Take**: Even in dying, there are moments of profound beauty and connection that enrich our lives.

### Creating a Supportive Environment for Peaceful Dying
The family works collaboratively to create the conditions for Arlette's "good death." This includes choosing palliative sedation at home, surrounding her with loved ones, flowers, and music, and following her specific wishes for her final moments. The author emphasizes the importance of providing safety and reassurance rather than fear and anxiety during the dying process.

> "De veiligheid dat ze niet hoeft af te zien in de laatste uren van het leven—ik begrijp nu hoe belangrijk dat is. Want dat wil je niet alleen doorstaan."
> _Original: "The security that she doesn't have to suffer in the final hours of life—I understand now how important that is. Because that's not something you want to endure alone."_

**Quick Take**: A peaceful death requires creating an environment of love, safety, and respect for the dying person's wishes.

### Transforming Grief into Gratitude
The author models how to process grief while simultaneously maintaining gratitude for the relationship and experiences shared. Rather than being overwhelmed by sadness, she focuses on the beauty of their journey together and the wisdom her mother shared. The book demonstrates that grief and gratitude can coexist, creating a more nuanced and healing emotional landscape.

> "Ik was overspoeld door een enorm gevoel van dankbaarheid, ben zo blij dat ik dit nog mee mag maken. Samen zijn we klaar voor een zachte dood."
> _Original: "I was overwhelmed by an enormous feeling of gratitude, so happy that I get to experience this. Together we are ready for a gentle death."_

**Quick Take**: Gratitude for the time we had with loved ones can help balance the pain of their loss.

## Action Items

- Start conversations about death and end-of-life wishes with loved ones before crisis occurs
- Create meaningful rituals and memories during the final months, not just after death
- Approach dying with the same intentionality and preparation as other major life events
- Allow space for both sadness and joy during the dying process
- Document and share stories, photos, and memories with terminally ill loved ones
- Consider how to create a peaceful environment that honors the dying person's wishes
- Practice finding moments of beauty and connection even in difficult circumstances

- What conversations about end-of-life wishes need to happen with your loved ones?
- How can we balance acknowledging the sadness of loss while celebrating the beauty of life lived?
- What role does choice and control play in creating a "good death"?
- How might open communication about death actually enhance our appreciation for life?
- What rituals and practices could help make the dying process more meaningful and peaceful?

## Conclusion
"Slowly to Heaven" is essential reading for anyone seeking to understand how to approach death with grace, love, and intentionality. Evi Hanssen has created a revolutionary guide that transforms our understanding of what it means to die well. Rather than a somber meditation on loss, this book is a celebration of love, wisdom, and the profound beauty that can emerge when we face death with open hearts and minds.

The biggest reason to read this book is its practical wisdom on turning something typically feared into something meaningful. Arlette's approach to dying—maintaining her sense of humor, planning her farewell, choosing her moment—offers a powerful alternative to the medicalized and fearful way modern society often handles death. This book will leave you with a deeper appreciation for life and a more peaceful relationship with its inevitable conclusion.
