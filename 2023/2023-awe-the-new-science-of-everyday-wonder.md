# Awe: The New Science of Everyday Wonder and How It Can Transform Your Life
**Author**: Dacher Keltner | **Year**: 2023

## Summary

Dacher Keltner presents groundbreaking scientific evidence that awe is the key to human flourishing. After twenty years of teaching happiness, he concludes that finding awe—not pursuing pleasure or wealth—is the most reliable path to a meaningful life. Awe is the emotion we experience when encountering vast mysteries that transcend our current understanding of the world. Through studies across twenty-six countries and cutting-edge laboratory research, Keltner reveals that awe quiets our critical inner voice, strengthens our connections to others, boosts creativity, and improves physical health.

This book is for anyone feeling overwhelmed, isolated, or searching for deeper meaning in a fragmented world. Readers will learn to recognize and cultivate everyday awe experiences that are scientifically proven to enhance well-being, reduce stress, and foster a sense of belonging to something larger than themselves.

## Core Insights

### The Eight Wonders of Life
Keltner's global research identified eight universal sources of awe that humans consistently report across cultures. Moral beauty—witnessing others' courage, kindness, and strength—is the most common trigger worldwide. This includes watching someone perform lifesaving CPR, seeing parents care for seriously ill children, or observing strangers interrupt crimes. Collective effervescence occurs during shared experiences like weddings, sports celebrations, political rallies, or dancing with others, creating a "buzzing" sense of unity. Nature ranks third, with people reporting awe at mountains, oceans, storms, and even the awareness that trees seem conscious. Music, visual design, spiritual experiences, the life-death cycle, and sudden epiphanies complete the eight wonders.

> "Awe is the feeling of being in the presence of something vast that transcends your current understanding of the world."

**Quick Take**: You don't need to travel to exotic locations or spend money to find awe—these eight wonders are available in your daily life, from observing a stranger's kindness to noticing the play of light through leaves.

### How Awe Transforms Your Mind and Body
Awe fundamentally alters our sense of self, creating what psychologists call the "small self" phenomenon. When we experience awe, our inner critic—the nagging, status-conscious voice that dominates our thoughts—quietly recedes. This allows us to think more creatively, connect more deeply with others, and recognize patterns we previously missed. Scientific studies show awe decreases inflammation markers in the body, reduces stress hormones, and improves immune function. Unlike fear, which activates the threat response system, awe engages the vagus nerve, triggering relaxation and social connection mechanisms.

> "How does awe transform us? By quieting the nagging, self-critical, overbearing, status-conscious voice of our self, or ego, and empowering us to collaborate, to open our minds to wonders, and to see the deep patterns of life."

**Quick Take**: Just a few minutes of awe daily provides physical and mental health benefits comparable to exercise or meditation, making it one of the most efficient well-being practices available.

### Finding Everyday Awe
Contrary to popular belief, awe isn't limited to once-in-a-lifetime experiences like witnessing the Grand Canyon or having a spiritual awakening. Keltner's daily diary studies reveal people experience awe 2-3 times per week on average—roughly once every couple of days. These everyday moments include watching a friend's generosity toward a homeless person, smelling a flower, observing light patterns on sidewalks, hearing a song that triggers memories of first love, or binge-watching a compelling show with friends. The key is developing awareness to notice these extraordinary moments within ordinary experiences.

> "Everyday awe. Great thinkers, from Walt Whitman to Rachel Carson to Zen master Shunryu Suzuki, remind us to become aware of how much of life can bring us awe."

**Quick Take**: The wonders of life are often nearby—you just need to pause, open your senses, and approach daily experiences with curiosity and wonder.

### Awe as Antidote to Modern Problems
In an age of increasing loneliness, anxiety, and disconnection, awe offers a powerful solution. Awe experiences dissolve the boundaries between self and others, counteracting the isolation epidemic in modern societies. The shared experience of awe during collective events—concerts, religious services, sports events, even funeral rites—creates social bonds that strengthen community resilience. For individuals facing trauma, grief, or major life transitions, awe provides a framework for finding meaning and moving forward. Keltner demonstrates how awe helped him process his brother's death and can help others navigate life's most challenging moments.

> "Feeling part of something much larger than the self is music to our ears. This transformation of the self brought about by awe is a powerful antidote to the isolation and loneliness that is epidemic today."

**Quick Take**: When feeling disconnected or overwhelmed, seek awe experiences that remind you of your place within larger systems—family, community, nature, or humanity itself.

## Action Items

- **Take an "awe walk" once a week**: Follow Keltner's simple instructions for a 15-minute walk where you intentionally notice vastness and mystery in your surroundings—the patterns in leaves, the architecture of buildings, the interactions between people.

- **Practice moral beauty awareness**: Each day, notice one act of courage, kindness, or strength in others, from strangers to family members. Reflect on how their actions benefit others and what motivated them.

- **Create collective effervescence**: Join group activities that involve synchronized movement—dance classes, team sports, choir practice, or even group exercise sessions where moving together creates shared energy.

- **Engage with awe-inducing art**: Listen to music that gives you chills, visit art museums, watch films that expand your understanding, or read poetry that reveals new perspectives on familiar experiences.

- **Connect with nature daily**: Spend a few minutes noticing natural phenomena—the sky, plants, weather patterns, or animals. Approach nature with curiosity rather than just passing through.

## Conclusion

This book is worth your time because it provides scientifically-backed, practical guidance for one of life's most fundamental challenges: finding meaning and connection in an increasingly fragmented world. Keltner's twenty years of research reveal that awe isn't a luxury or rare spiritual experience—it's an essential human emotion accessible to anyone, anywhere, anytime. The biggest reason to read this book is its urgent message: in our age of anxiety, distraction, and disconnection, finding awe is not just pleasant—it's necessary for our physical health, mental well-being, and collective survival. The science is clear: brief daily moments of awe can transform your life more effectively than expensive therapies, complex self-improvement regimens, or the pursuit of conventional success metrics. This book gives you the roadmap to access this powerful, freely available resource for human flourishing.
