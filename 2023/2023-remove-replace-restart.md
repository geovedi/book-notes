# Remove, Replace, Restart
**Authors**: Christian Greiser | **Year**: 2023
**Category**: Business & Finance

## Summary
This practical career maintenance manual presents a powerful framework for professional transformation using a car maintenance analogy. Christian Greiser, former Boston Consulting Group Senior Partner turned executive coach, offers a proven six-step process for when your "engine for success" starts to stutter. The book is designed for leaders, managers, and entrepreneurs at professional turning points who feel stuck or that their career momentum has stalled.

The core insight is that career problems, like car troubles, require systematic diagnosis and maintenance rather than quick fixes. Through real case studies from his coaching practice and personal career journey, Greiser demonstrates how to navigate career transitions, overcome blind spots, and regain professional momentum. The book is particularly valuable for those who have achieved formal success but feel misaligned or overwhelmed by their current roles.

## Core Insights

### üö® The 6 Rs Framework: Your Career Maintenance System
This is the central framework for diagnosing and fixing career issues:

**Reduce Speed**: Recognize and respond to warning signs before major breakdowns occur. Most career crashes are preceded by subtle signals that we often ignore in our drive to keep moving forward.

**Resolve**: Untangle cause and effect. Many professionals misdiagnose their career problems, treating symptoms rather than root causes. This requires honest self-assessment and sometimes external feedback to identify blind spots.

**Remove**: Discard patterns of behavior that no longer benefit you. This includes letting go of success formulas that worked in the past but are now limiting your growth, like micromanagement when you should be delegating.

**Replace**: Adopt new ways of doing things. This isn't just about changing habits but developing entirely new approaches to leadership, time management, and professional relationships.

**Rigorously Test**: Experiment with new identities and approaches before fully committing. This "try before you buy" approach reduces risk and builds confidence for bigger changes.

**Restart**: Regain momentum after a break. Like breaking in a new car, new career patterns require adjustment and patience before they feel natural.

### üéØ The Success Paradox: Achievement Can Create Blind Spots
High performers often develop specific patterns that create their success, then become trapped by those same patterns when circumstances change. The book reveals how the very behaviors that drove early career success (like hands-on involvement or technical expertise) can become liabilities at senior levels.

## Action Items

- **Monthly Engine Check**: Set aside monthly reflection time to ask: "What warning signs am I ignoring in my career right now?"
- **Behavior Audit**: Identify one pattern of behavior that served you well in the past but may be limiting you now
- **Experiment Calendar**: Schedule one small career experiment each month to test new approaches or skills
- **Delegate Tracker**: Keep a log of delegation opportunities and your response patterns to build awareness
- **Success Portfolio**: Document recent achievements and analyze which behaviors actually enabled them

## Questions to Consider

- What warning lights are currently flashing in your career that you're choosing to ignore?
- Which of your past success formulas might no longer be serving you in your current role?
- If you could remove one professional behavior that's holding you back, what would it be?
- What new identity or role are you curious about but hesitant to try?
- How comfortable are you with being "between worlds" during career transitions?

## Conclusion
This is absolutely worth your time if you're at any kind of professional crossroads or feeling that your career momentum has stalled. The car maintenance analogy provides an accessible framework for understanding complex career dynamics, and the real-world case studies make the concepts immediately applicable.

The biggest strength of the book is its recognition that career challenges are rarely about knowledge gaps‚Äîthey're about behavioral patterns, identity attachments, and emotional blind spots. Greiser's engineering background combined with his coaching experience creates a unique blend of systematic thinking and emotional intelligence.

What makes this book particularly valuable is its permission to be imperfect during career transitions. Unlike many career guides that push for constant forward motion, Greiser acknowledges the value of pauses, experiments, and even temporary periods of reduced success as necessary parts of sustainable career growth. If you're feeling stuck or that your current approach isn't working anymore, this book provides both the diagnostic tools and the practical repair manual you need.
