# Judgment
## A Literary Rebellion
- The aftermath of the Wager affair and the competing narratives that emerged from the survivors.
- The official court-martial failed to address the full extent of the mutiny, leaving room for different accounts to surface.
- Reverend Walter published a book called "A Voyage Round the World," which became the dominant account of the expedition.
- The book was largely ghostwritten by Benjamin Robins under the direction of Admiral Anson himself.
- Walter's book presented a skewed version of events, portraying Anson as a heroic and capable commander, downplaying the mutiny, and emphasizing British imperial interests.
- Sponsored by Anson, the book became a bestseller, influencing prominent thinkers like Rousseau and Montesquieu and carried by renowned explorers such as James Cook and Darwin.
- The empire's chosen narrative, reinforcing the image of British superiority and presenting a mythic tale of triumph.
- The fate of John Duck, the free Black seaman who was abandoned and sold into slavery, highlighting the silenced narratives and dark realities that empires choose not to acknowledge.
