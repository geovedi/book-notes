# Decoded Money
**Authors**: Brijesh Parikh & Planetwealth | **Year**: 2023

## Summary
"Decoded Money" is a practical personal finance guide that breaks down wealth creation into five fundamental principles. Written by a SEBI-registered investment advisor with over two decades of experience, this book serves as a comprehensive roadmap for individuals looking to achieve financial freedom through systematic money management. The author, Brijesh Parikh, draws from his experience helping hundreds of families manage their finances and his mission to help 100,000 families live financially worry-free lives.

This book is perfect for beginners who feel overwhelmed by financial jargon and those who've been investing without a clear strategy. Readers will learn how to harness the power of compounding, create effective budgets, set meaningful financial goals, manage debt wisely, and understand real returns after taxes and inflation.

## Core Insights

### Principle 1: Understanding Power of Compounding
The power of compounding is the eighth wonder of the world. Those who understand it earn it, and those who don't understand it pay it. Compounding works by earning interest on your interest, creating exponential growth over time. The earlier you start, the more powerful compounding becomes.

Starting at age 28 with Rs. 1,000 monthly investment until retirement at 58 would accumulate approximately Rs. 21 lakh at 10% annual returns. Starting 10 years later at age 38 with even 50% higher monthly investment (Rs. 1,500) would only reach Rs. 11 lakh - nearly 50% less despite investing more money.

> "Power of compounding is 8th Wonder of the world. Those who understand it earn it and those who do not understand pays it." - Albert Einstein

**Quick Take**: Start investing early, even with small amounts. Time is more valuable than the amount you invest when harnessing compounding power.

### Principle 2: Managing Cash Flow Through Budgeting
Money behaves like water - if you don't provide direction, it finds its own path, which may not align with your life priorities. Budgeting gives you control over where your money goes and ensures it serves your goals.

Create a budget by listing all income sources, tracking regular and variable expenses, and calculating your surplus. The key formula is Income - Savings = Expenses, not Income - Expenses = Savings. Pay yourself first by automating savings before you have a chance to spend the money.

> "Money and water share same nature. If you do not provide direction to them, they will find their own direction, which may not be the direction as per your life priority."

**Quick Take**: Automate your savings and treat them as non-negotiable expenses. A budget isn't about restriction - it's about intentional spending.

### Principle 3: Financial Vision and Goals Articulation
Starting your financial journey without clear goals is like driving without a destination - you'll end up somewhere, but probably not where you want to be. Most people avoid financial goal-setting because they find future planning daunting, but this prevents them from maximizing their wealth creation potential.

Working with a professional financial planner can help you articulate your financial vision much better. One client increased his monthly investment from Rs. 40,000 to Rs. 70,000 (an 80% increase) after completing financial goal articulation and budgeting exercises.

> "IF you start your journey without deciding your destination, where will you reach? Probably nowhere, or at least not where you want to reach."

**Quick Take**: Take time to define what financial freedom means to you and create specific, measurable goals. Your investment capacity might be much higher than you think.

### Principle 4: Effective Debt Management
There are three pillars of wealth creation: cash flow management, generating positive real returns, and effective debt management. Understanding the difference between good debt and bad debt is crucial for financial success.

Good debt generates assets that appreciate (home loans, education loans, business loans), while bad debt funds depreciating assets (car loans, credit card debt). Before taking any loan, ensure the cost of borrowing is less than the returns you can generate from the asset, and verify that you can maintain your lifestyle and investments after paying EMIs.

> "There are 3 pillars of wealth creation: first one is effective Cashflow management to create required investible surplus, second one is generating positive real returns (Return-Taxes-Inflation) and third one is managing debt effectively."

**Quick Take**: Use debt strategically as a wealth-building tool, not for lifestyle inflation. Pay off high-interest bad debt aggressively while strategically managing good debt.

### Principle 5: Understanding Real Returns
Your investment returns face two enemies: taxes and inflation. Real returns are what matters - your portfolio returns after accounting for both taxes and your personal lifestyle inflation, not government-reported inflation.

Real Returns = Portfolio Returns - Taxes - Lifestyle Inflation

Aim for at least 0% real returns if you're extremely conservative, or positive real returns (1-5%) based on your risk profile. Negative real returns mean you're actually losing purchasing power over time, even if your nominal returns look good.

> "While managing your investments, your risk optimized real returns should be positive. Which means your returns from your investment portfolio should be positive after providing for Taxes and Inflation."

**Quick Take**: Focus on after-tax, after-inflation returns rather than nominal returns. This ensures your money maintains and grows its purchasing power over time.

## Action Items
- Start investing immediately, even with small amounts, to maximize compounding benefits
- Create a detailed monthly budget using the Income-Savings=Expense formula
- Automate your savings through systematic investment plans (SIPs)
- Write down specific financial goals with timelines and required amounts
- Differentiate between good debt (appreciating assets) and bad debt (depreciating assets)
- Calculate your real returns after taxes and personal inflation rate
- Review your portfolio annually to ensure positive real returns
- Consider working with a fee-only financial advisor for goal-based planning

## Questions to Consider
- How much am I actually losing by delaying my investment start date?
- What is my current real return rate after accounting for taxes and my lifestyle inflation?
- Am I treating savings as a priority or as what's left over after spending?
- What does financial freedom specifically mean to me and my family?
- Is my current debt structure helping or hindering my wealth creation journey?

## Conclusion
"Decoded Money" is worth your time if you're looking for a straightforward, practical guide to personal finance that cuts through complexity. The book's strength lies in its simple, actionable framework that anyone can implement regardless of their financial knowledge level.

The biggest reason to read this book is its focus on real returns rather than nominal gains - a distinction that many financial guides miss. By understanding and implementing these five principles, you'll be better equipped than 90% of retail investors who continue to make common financial mistakes.

While the examples and currency are India-specific, the principles are universal and applicable to anyone seeking financial literacy. The author's mission-driven approach and extensive experience add credibility to the advice, making this a solid foundation for your financial education journey.
