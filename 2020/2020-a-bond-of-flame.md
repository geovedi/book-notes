# A Bond of Flame
**Authors**: Richard Fierce | **Year**: 2020

## Summary
Eldwin, a failed student from the Citadel dragon rider school, is on the run after escaping his former master, Master Pevus. While working as a caravan guard in the Necra Desert, he discovers a young red dragon being transported illegally and forms a powerful bond with her, naming her Sion. Together they escape captivity and embark on a journey of survival and discovery.

The story follows Eldwin and Sion as they navigate the complex world of dragon riders, griffons, and political intrigue between the kingdoms of Osnen and Midia. Eldwin is manipulated by Altin, a seemingly helpful healer who secretly serves the Necromancer, into believing that the dragon riders are evil oppressors who attack innocent civilians. The truth is slowly revealed as Eldwin encounters his former classmate Maren and discovers that the Citadel riders are actually fighting against the Necromancer's forces.

This fantasy adventure is perfect for readers who enjoy dragon bonding stories, coming-of-age narratives, and complex political conflicts where nothing is as it seems. Fans of Eragon and dragon rider fiction will find themselves captivated by the developing relationship between Eldwin and Sion.

## Core Insights

### The Power of the Dragon-Rider Bond
The emotional heart of the story lies in the telepathic connection between Eldwin and Sion. Their bond grows from initial mistrust to deep mutual understanding, allowing them to communicate thoughts and emotions across distances. This connection proves stronger than external manipulation attempts and serves as their greatest asset in battles and challenges.

> "I realized something was blocking our connection. I could feel it in the way, some sort of invisible web tangled over the bond."

**Quick Take**: True partnerships transcend external manipulation - trust and communication create unbreakable bonds.

### Nothing Is Ever As It Seems
The book masterfully explores themes of deception and betrayal. Altin presents himself as a helpful healer fighting against dragon rider oppression, while actually serving the Necromancer. The burned cities that Eldwin witnesses were actually destroyed by the Necromancer's forces, not the Citadel riders as he's led to believe.

> "We've been deceived," I told her.

**Quick Take**: Always question who benefits from the information you receive - truth is often hidden beneath layers of manipulation.

### Finding Your Own Path
Despite being told he failed at the Citadel, Eldwin discovers his own way of connecting with and training Sion. His unconventional methods prove effective, showing that formal training isn't the only path to mastery. His mangled hand, once seen as a weakness, doesn't prevent him from becoming a capable dragon rider.

> "I was forced to take any job that kept me moving. I didn't know if Master Pevus had dragoons searching for me, but I wasn't going to take any chances."

**Quick Take**: Your unique circumstances and perceived weaknesses can become your greatest strengths when embraced rather than hidden.

## Action Items

- **Look beyond surface appearances**: When encountering new information or people, dig deeper to understand hidden motivations and true agendas.
- **Trust your instincts**: Eldwin's bond with Sion serves as his internal compass - develop your own forms of intuition about situations and people.
- **Embrace unconventional solutions**: Eldwin succeeds despite not having formal training - sometimes your unique approach is more valuable than following established methods.
- **Question authority**: The Citadel riders aren't what they seem - maintain healthy skepticism about institutional narratives.

## Questions to Consider

- How does the bond between Eldwin and Sion evolve throughout the story, and what does it reveal about trust and communication?
- In what ways does Altin manipulate Eldwin, and what early warning signs might readers notice?
- What does the story suggest about the nature of power and manipulation in political conflicts?
- How does Eldwin's status as an outsider both help and hinder his journey?
- What role does deception play in the larger conflict between the kingdoms?

## Conclusion

"A Bond of Flame" is a worthy second installment in the Dragon Riders of Osnen series that successfully expands the world while deepening the character relationships. The book's strength lies in its exploration of manipulation versus truth, and the developing bond between Eldwin and Sion provides genuine emotional weight to the political machinations.

While the middle section involving Eldwin's deception by Altin feels somewhat prolonged, the battle sequences and revelations about the true nature of the conflict provide satisfying payoff. The cliffhanger ending leaves readers eager for the next installment, particularly with the mysterious shadow beast and Esmond's fate unresolved.

For fantasy fans who enjoy character-driven stories with dragons, political intrigue, and themes of finding one's own path, this book delivers exactly what they're looking for. The relationship between Eldwin and Sion alone makes it worth your time, offering a fresh take on the classic dragon-rider bond trope.
