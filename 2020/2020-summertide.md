# Summertide
**Authors**: Charlotte E. English | **Year**: 2020
**Category**: Fantasy & Science Fiction

## Summary
"Summertide" is a whimsical fantasy novel that begins when the ancient Kottow Tree, home to a community of misfits and magical beings, suddenly awakens and begins to walk. The Tree carries its inhabitants away from their familiar town and back toward a lost magical forest they'd long forgotten. As the journey unfolds, the residents discover their true identities as fey folk, dryads, and other magical creatures who had been living as ordinary humans for generations.

The story follows their return to the enchanted realm where they must restore the balance between seasons, reconcile Summer and Winter courts, and find their place in a world they never knew they belonged to. It's a heartwarming tale about belonging, the power of home, and the magic that lies dormant within us all.

Perfect for readers who love cozy fantasy, found family themes, and gentle magical realism with touches of British folklore charm.

## Fast-Track Story Guide

### The Great Awakening
The story begins with a mysterious shaking in the great Kottow Tree, where a diverse community of "odd folk" have made their homes. Mother Gantry, the practical matriarch who prefers stability over change, and Diggory Stokey, a humble carpenter, are among the first to witness the impossible: the Tree is pulling up its roots and preparing to walk away from the town it has stood beside for centuries.

> "The Tree's on the move," said Diggory stubbornly.

### Journey to the Lost Forest
As the Tree carries its inhabitants across the countryside, the residents begin experiencing strange transformations. Memories of their true magical selves return, and the mist that clouded their minds lifts. Maut, once just an eccentric squirrel-loving woman, remembers she is actually Mauvian, a fey being. Others discover they are dryads, wood-spirits, and other magical creatures who had been living as humans for so long they'd forgotten their true nature.

### The Seasonal Crisis
Upon reaching the lost forest, they discover a magical imbalance: the Summer Court and Winter Court have been disrupted by the absence of their people for a century. Ysabelon, the Moon-Queen, and her brother Gowerlon face a crisis - someone must take on the mantle of Winter to restore the natural order, but none want to leave the warmth and joy of Summer.

### The Transformation of Berengaria
Berengaria Dow, a mortal woman from Kottow who was accidentally brought along on the journey, surprises everyone by volunteering to become the new Queen of Winter. Unlike others who fear the cold and darkness, Berengaria sees beauty and possibility in the ice and snow, envisioning a Winter Court filled with skating, warm feasts, and the shimmering beauty of frozen landscapes.

### Home and Belonging
The story culminates in a joyous celebration where all the characters embrace their true identities while preserving the bonds they formed as humans. Even Diggory Stokey, who remains mortal, finds his place among the magical folk, realizing that home isn't about where you come from but who you love and where you're accepted.

## Key Takeaways

- **Embrace Change**: Even the most stable foundations (like a centuries-old tree) may need to move to fulfill their true purpose

- **Found Family**: Community isn't limited by blood or species - it's built on mutual care, acceptance, and shared experience

- **Hidden Magic**: Everyone has dormant potential within them; sometimes it just takes the right circumstances to awaken it

- **Balance is Essential**: Both light and dark, summer and winter, have their place and beauty in the natural order

- **Home is Where You're Accepted**: True belonging comes from being valued for who you are, not fitting into predefined roles

## Questions to Consider

- If you discovered you had a hidden magical nature, what aspects of your current life would you want to preserve?

- How do you balance the comfort of stability with the necessity of change and growth?

- What makes a place feel like "home" - the location itself, or the people and connections you build there?

- Have you ever felt like you were playing a role that didn't quite fit your true self?

## Conclusion
"Summertide" is worth your time if you're seeking gentle, uplifting fantasy that warms the heart without sacrificing depth. Charlotte E. English crafts a cozy magical world that feels both familiar and wondrously new. The book's greatest strength lies in its perfect balance of whimsy and emotional resonance - it's a story about finding your place in the world that never becomes preachy or overly sentimental.

Read this when you need a reminder that magic exists in the everyday, that chosen families are just as real as blood ties, and that sometimes the greatest adventures begin when something seemingly terrible (like your house walking away) turns out to be exactly what you needed. It's the literary equivalent of a warm cup of tea on a rainy day - comforting, magical, and deeply satisfying.
