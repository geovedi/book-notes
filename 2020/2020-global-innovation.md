# Global Innovation
**Editor**: Jonathan Reuvid | **Year**: 2020
**Category**: Business & Finance

## Summary
"Global Innovation" is a comprehensive guide for British exporters navigating the post-Brexit landscape while leveraging innovation as a competitive advantage. The book addresses the critical challenges facing UK businesses: climate change, digitization, and disrupted global trade relationships. With the UK's merchandise export ranking slipping from 5th to 10th globally over two decades and the trade deficit widening from $43 billion to $192 billion, the book presents innovation as the key to revitalizing British export performance.

This essential resource is designed for business leaders, exporters, entrepreneurs, and policy makers who need practical strategies for international expansion, innovation management, and market adaptation. Readers will gain actionable frameworks for university-industry partnerships, brand protection, funding acquisition, and export market entry.

## Core Insights

### Innovation as a Competitive Necessity
The book argues that innovation is no longer optional but critical for survival in today's rapidly changing global marketplace. Companies that can adapt and respond with new solutions to climate change and digitization challenges will succeed, while those trading into international markets will be more resilient than domestic-only businesses. The UK's historical decline in manufacturing competitiveness, combined with post-Brexit uncertainties, makes innovation-driven export strategies essential for economic recovery.

> "What is a certainty is that innovation will be critical in finding sustainable solutions to the issues we all face. Those companies that can adapt and respond with new solutions to the everchanging world around us will be those more likely to succeed."

**Quick Take**: Innovation must be central to business strategy, not an afterthought, especially for companies seeking international growth.

### Industry-Academia Collaboration Framework
The book presents ten distinct models for university-industry partnerships, emphasizing that successful collaborations require strong personal relationships, shared vision, clear communication, and organizational support. These partnerships range from collaborative research projects and contract research to Knowledge Transfer Partnerships (KTPs) and access to state-of-the-art equipment. The UK's goal to increase R&D expenditure to 2.4% of GDP by 2027 makes these collaborations increasingly vital.

> "Good partnerships and collaborations can be long-lasting and rewarding; many are characterised by committed individuals and champions on both sides."

**Quick Take**: Strategic university partnerships provide access to cutting-edge research, talent pipelines, and funding opportunities that accelerate innovation.

### Brand Strategy for Global Markets
The book provides a comprehensive framework for building and protecting global brands, emphasizing that trademark protection must evolve with business expansion. Companies should invest 10% of revenue in IP protection and maintain an additional 10-15% enforcement budget. The strategy covers brand creation, clearance, expansion, registration, management, and enforcement across multiple jurisdictions.

> "In summary, as you scale up, a strong, well-protected brand is crucial to your business's success. There is a clear and direct causal link between repeat sales and increased profit margins to the strength of your brand."

**Quick Take**: Brand protection is an investment, not an expense, requiring proactive management across all target markets.

Innovate UK serves as the national innovation agency, supporting technology-enabled businesses and R&D partnerships. > "In general there are four types of public funding incentives available in the UK: Grants, Soft loans, Tax incentives, and Awards."

**Quick Take**: Strategic grant funding provides non-repayable capital while serving as a quality stamp that attracts additional investment.

### Export Market Expansion Strategy
The book identifies 15 priority markets for UK exporters and provides frameworks for market entry, including patent protection, Incoterms understanding, export credit, and supply chain integration. It emphasizes the need to diversify beyond traditional markets and target high-growth import markets where UK presence is currently weak.

> "Instead of focusing on those markets only where the UK has been most successful, exporters now need to broaden the list of priority targets to include high-growth import markets where they have less or minimal traction."

**Quick Take**: Export success requires strategic market diversification combined with innovative product offerings that go beyond traditional quality and price advantages.

- Invest in R&D to reach the UK target of 2.4% of GDP expenditure on research and development
- Implement Knowledge Transfer Partnerships to bridge academic research and commercial applications

## Conclusion
"Global Innovation" is an essential read for UK business leaders navigating the post-Brexit economic landscape. Its strength lies in providing both strategic vision and practical tactical advice for innovation-driven export success. The book successfully bridges the gap between academic research and commercial application, offering actionable frameworks that businesses can implement immediately.

The biggest reason to read this book is its comprehensive approach to combining innovation strategy with export market development. Rather than treating these as separate disciplines, the book shows how they must be integrated for sustainable international growth. For UK businesses facing declining export performance and increasing global competition, the strategies outlined in this book provide a roadmap for revitalization through innovation and strategic market expansion.

This is particularly valuable for SMEs and scaling businesses that need practical guidance on accessing funding, protecting intellectual property, and entering new markets. The book's focus on collaboration, partnership, and strategic alignment with government priorities makes it a timely and relevant resource for building resilient, innovative businesses capable of thriving in the global marketplace.
