# Lesson Design and Planning from Scratch: Technology Integration
**Original Title**: Дизайн урока и планирование с нуля / Lesson Design and Planning from scratch Technology Integration
**Author**: Марина Юрьевна Грабарь (Marina Yuryevna Grabar) | **Year**: 2020
**Category**: Education & Teaching

## Summary
This practical guide serves as a comprehensive resource for modern educators seeking to master 21st-century teaching standards through effective lesson design and technology integration. Written by a former Coursera mentor from Arizona State University's "Lesson Design and Assessment" course, the book combines theoretical foundations with hands-on practice labs and real-world examples.

The book addresses the fundamental challenges teachers face in creating engaging lessons while incorporating meaningful technology integration. It provides a structured approach to curriculum design that emphasizes backward planning, clear instructional objectives, and the SAMR model for technology enhancement. This bilingual resource (Russian/English) is specifically designed to help educators transition from traditional teaching methods to modern, technology-enhanced pedagogy.

Teachers will gain practical frameworks for designing lessons that build critical thinking skills, incorporate STEM principles, and foster student engagement through meaningful activities and assessments.

## Core Insights

### 1. Writing Instructional Objectives
Clear instructional objectives form the foundation of effective lesson design. The book emphasizes starting with specific, measurable learning outcomes that guide both teaching and assessment. Objectives should be written from the student's perspective and focus on observable behaviors rather than internal processes.

The approach encourages teachers to think about what students will be able to DO after the lesson, not just what they will know. This shift in perspective helps create more engaging and authentic learning experiences that demonstrate real understanding and application.

**Quick Take**: Write objectives that answer "What will students be able to do by the end of this lesson?"

### 2. Technology Integration Through SAMR Model
The SAMR framework (Substitution, Augmentation, Modification, Redefinition) provides a structured approach to technology integration. Rather than simply replacing traditional tools with digital ones, the model encourages teachers to transform learning experiences through meaningful technology use.

The book guides educators through each level: from basic substitution (tech as a direct replacement) to redefinition (creating entirely new learning tasks previously impossible without technology). This progression helps teachers move beyond superficial tech integration to truly transformative teaching practices.

> "Curriculum and Technology must be aligned. Add technology."

**Quick Take**: Use SAMR to move from tech replacement to learning transformation.

### 3. The Four Lesson Improvement Principles
The book presents four powerful principles for enhancing lesson effectiveness:

1. **Think Outside (No Box Required)**: Break free from traditional constraints and explore innovative teaching approaches that challenge conventional classroom structures.

2. **Start at the Test (And Work Your Way Back)**: Use backward design by beginning with your assessment goals and planning instruction that leads students to success.

3. **Keep the Bigger Picture in Mind**: Connect individual lessons to broader curriculum goals and real-world applications to give learning context and purpose.

4. **Stay Flexible**: Remain adaptable to student needs and unexpected learning opportunities while maintaining clear objectives.

**Quick Take**: Design lessons with the end in mind but remain responsive to student needs.

### 4. Effective Warm-Up Activities
Warm-up activities serve as crucial engagement tools that prepare students for learning while creating a positive classroom environment. The book provides extensive examples of warm-ups that can be technology-enhanced or technology-free, ensuring accessibility for all classroom situations.

Effective warm-ups should be relevant to the lesson content, appropriately challenging, and designed to activate prior knowledge. The book offers practical examples for different subjects and age groups, with special attention to STEM integration and cross-curricular connections.

**Quick Take**: Design warm-ups that activate prior knowledge while building anticipation for learning.

### 5. STEM Integration and Project-Based Learning
The book emphasizes STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, Mathematics) integration as a natural way to blend multiple disciplines while developing critical 21st-century skills. Project-based learning approaches encourage students to tackle authentic problems while applying knowledge from various fields.

Examples include social issues addressed through art projects, engineering challenges that incorporate mathematical concepts, and technology-enhanced research projects. This approach helps students see connections between subjects and apply their learning in meaningful contexts.

**Quick Take**: Use projects that naturally blend STEM disciplines to solve authentic problems.

## Action Items

- **Write Clear Objectives**: Start each lesson plan by writing 2-3 specific, measurable learning objectives from the student's perspective

- **Apply SAMR Framework**: Evaluate your current technology use and identify opportunities to move from substitution to redefinition

- **Implement Backward Design**: Begin lesson planning by defining assessment criteria, then design activities that lead to success

- **Create Tech-Enhanced Warm-ups**: Develop a repertoire of 5-10 engaging warm-up activities that incorporate appropriate technology

- **Plan STEM Integration**: Identify natural connections between subjects and design at least one cross-curricular project per semester

## Questions to Consider

- How can you transform your current lessons from technology substitution to learning redefinition using the SAMR model?

- What authentic problems or projects could naturally integrate multiple STEM disciplines in your curriculum?

- How might backward design change your approach to lesson planning and assessment?

- Which traditional teaching constraints could you challenge to create more innovative learning experiences?

- How can you ensure technology enhances rather than distracts from your core learning objectives?
