# Smart Skills: Persuasion
**Authors**: Patrick Forsyth | **Year**: 2011 | **Category**: Business & Finance

## Summary
"Smart Skills: Persuasion" is a comprehensive guide to mastering the art of influencing others in business and professional contexts. The book presents persuasion not as manipulation, but as a collaborative process of "helping people make decisions" - a two-way dialogue that respects the other person's needs and perspectives while achieving your objectives.

The book breaks down persuasion into a systematic process covering preparation, initial approach, building a compelling case, handling resistance, and securing agreement. It emphasizes that persuasion is a specialist form of communication that requires specific techniques and mindset, distinct from basic communication skills.

This guide is essential for business professionals, managers, salespeople, and anyone who needs to influence others to achieve goals. Readers will gain practical frameworks, psychological insights, and actionable techniques to become more persuasive in meetings, presentations, negotiations, and everyday professional interactions.

## Core Insights

### The Foundation: Persuasion as Helping People Decide
The book's central premise redefines persuasion as "helping people to make a decision" rather than something you "do to people." This fundamental shift transforms persuasion from a one-way monologue into a two-way dialogue that respects the other person's decision-making process.

People make decisions by moving through four psychological stages: first considering their personal needs ("I'm the one who matters"), then evaluating the merits and implications of your proposal, assessing how it will work in practice, and finally determining what specific action they must take. Understanding this sequence is crucial for structuring persuasive communication effectively.

> "Persuasion is best viewed from this perspective. As has been said, it is not something that you do to people – it is the mirror image of the decision making process – something that is inherently two-way."

**Quick Take**: Focus on the other person's decision-making journey, not just your desire to convince them.

### Preparation: The Magic Formula
Preparation comes close to being the "magic formula" for persuasion success. The book outlines a four-stage preparation process: setting SMART objectives (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Realistic, Timed), checking facts, planning the meeting structure, and gathering supporting materials.

The right mindset is equally crucial. Effective persuaders adopt an approach centered on empathy - seeing things from others' perspectives - while maintaining appropriate projection of confidence and authority. This balance prevents appearing either too aggressive or too passive.

> "The person who runs rings around others as it were is probably not inherently persuasive; more likely they understand how this sort of communication works – and they 'do their homework'."

**Quick Take**: Always do your homework - preparation is the closest thing to a magic formula for persuasive success.

### The Power of First Impressions
The opening moments of any persuasive interaction critically influence success. Taking initiative, appearing well-organized, and suggesting a clear agenda establishes your credibility and sets the tone for the entire conversation.

Two interrelated factors are especially important: projection (your overall approach and authority) and empathy (the ability to see and be seen seeing things from others' perspectives). The right balance prevents appearing dictatorial while maintaining appropriate influence.

> "Well begun is half done."

**Quick Take**: Take charge of the interaction from the start - project confidence while showing genuine understanding of the other person's perspective.

### Building the Compelling Case
Persuasive communication must be understandable, attractive, and convincing. Language plays a crucial role - avoid an introspective focus on "I" statements, eliminate uncertainty with phrases like "I think" or "maybe," and ensure your message flows with clear structure.

The most persuasive approach focuses on benefits rather than features. As the book notes, people don't buy precision drills (features) but the ability to make precision holes (benefits), and only because of deeper needs like repairing a car or putting up shelves.

> "Many people know the phrase about selling the sizzle and not the sausages, and the head of a cosmetics company, Elizabeth Arden, is reputed to have said, rather less kindly, 'I don't sell cosmetics, I sell hope.'"

**Quick Take**: Sell benefits and outcomes, not features and processes. Always translate what you offer into what the other person gains.

### Handling Resistance Effectively
Objections are not signs of failure but indications of interest. The book encourages welcoming objections as opportunities to demonstrate competence and build credibility. When handled well, objection resolution becomes a final stepping-stone toward agreement.

Too many objections may signal problems with your approach - perhaps you've failed to understand the person's situation accurately, are making suggestions too soon, or coming across as delivering a standard presentation rather than a tailored solution.

> "Obstacles are made to be overcome."

**Quick Take**: View objections as buying signals - handle them confidently to build credibility and move toward agreement.

### The Art of Closing
Many persuasive interactions fail not because of weak arguments but because the persuader never actually asks for agreement. The book emphasizes the necessity of actively closing - seeking commitment at every appropriate stage, not just at the end.

Watch for "buying signals" - comments about how things will work after agreement, questions about implementation details, or other signs that the person is mentally moving to a decision. These signals indicate the right moment to seek commitment.

> "You must therefore resolve to close – to actually ask for agreement. You have to be thick-skinned about any rejection; even the most persuasive people do not have a 100% strike rate."

**Quick Take**: Always ask for agreement - fear of rejection guarantees missed opportunities, while asking creates possibilities for success.

## Action Items
- **Before any persuasive interaction**: Set SMART objectives and research the other person's needs and situation
- **During conversations**: Focus on "you" language rather than "I" language, emphasize benefits over features
- **When handling objections**: Welcome them as signs of interest, address them confidently, and use them to build credibility
- **Always**: Listen actively - the book reminds us we have two ears and one mouth for a reason
- **To close successfully**: Watch for buying signals and ask for agreement at appropriate moments throughout the conversation

## Questions to Consider
- Are you approaching persuasion as helping others decide, or as something you do to them?
- How well do you prepare before important conversations or presentations?
- Do you focus more on features or benefits when making your case?
- How comfortable are you with handling objections and seeking agreement?
- Are you listening actively enough to truly understand others' perspectives and needs?

## Conclusion
"Smart Skills: Persuasion" delivers exactly what it promises - a practical, systematic approach to becoming more influential in professional contexts. The book's strength lies in its emphasis on preparation, psychological understanding, and actionable techniques rather than vague theories or manipulative tactics.

The biggest reason to read this book is its framework for preparation and systematic approach to persuasion. Unlike many communication books that focus solely on what to say, Forsyth provides a complete roadmap for the entire persuasive process, from initial planning through closing and beyond. The emphasis on viewing persuasion as helping others make decisions rather than overcoming resistance creates a more ethical and effective approach.

For busy professionals who need to influence others to achieve results, this book provides a comprehensive toolkit that can be applied immediately in meetings, presentations, and everyday business interactions. The time invested in reading and applying these principles will pay dividends throughout any professional career.
