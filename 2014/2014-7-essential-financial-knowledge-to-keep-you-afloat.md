# 7 Essential Financial Knowledge To Keep You Afloat
**Authors**: Dr Ben Watts | **Year**: 2014

## Summary
This practical financial guide presents seven fundamental principles for achieving and maintaining financial stability in an unpredictable world. Dr. Ben Watts distills decades of financial wisdom into actionable strategies designed to prevent financial ruin and build lasting wealth. The book addresses common financial pitfalls that plague many individuals, offering straightforward solutions that anyone can implement regardless of their current financial situation.

Perfect for anyone feeling overwhelmed by financial decisions or struggling with debt management, this book provides a clear roadmap to financial literacy. Readers will learn how to build emergency funds, set achievable financial goals, use credit wisely, plan for retirement early, create long-term wealth strategies, distinguish needs from wants, and avoid dangerous shortcuts to wealth. The approach is deliberately simple and accessible, making complex financial concepts understandable for beginners while reinforcing foundational principles for those already on their financial journey.

## Core Insights

### 1. Build Liquid Savings First
Liquid savings are funds you can access immediately for emergencies, unlike illiquid assets like properties or stocks that require time to convert to cash. The author emphasizes keeping at least 20% of monthly income as readily accessible cash savings. This creates a foundation of financial security and peace of mind, ensuring you're prepared for unexpected expenses without resorting to high-interest debt.

> "There is a feeling of self-assurance to have money kept somewhere for instant use."

**Quick Win**: Open a separate high-yield savings account today and set up automatic transfers of 10-20% of your income. Start small if needed - even 5% builds the habit.

### 2. Set Realizable Financial Goals
Dreaming without planning is "building a house in the wind." Clear, actionable goals are essential for wealth management. Whether you want to buy a house or car, you need concrete steps and timelines to achieve these objectives. Every dollar spent without purpose can derail your financial future.

> "It is silly to dream without making efforts on how to turn the dreams to realities."

**Quick Win**: Write down your top 3 financial goals and create a specific monthly savings target for each. Use SMART goals (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound).

### 3. Use Credit Strategically and Responsibly
Credit should be a tool, not a crutch. Only take on credit when you're confident you can repay the full amount each month. Timely credit repayment builds your financial reputation and opens doors to future lending opportunities. The book stresses that good credit management contributes to overall mental and financial health.

> "Credits are meant to serve a purpose and once fulfilled should be repaid on time."

**Quick Win**: Review all your current credit commitments and create a payoff plan. Set up automatic minimum payments to avoid late fees, and aim to pay more than the minimum whenever possible.

### 4. Start Retirement Planning Immediately
The power of compound interest makes early retirement planning crucial. Using his father as an example, Watts shows how someone who started planning in their early 40s could still retire comfortably in their early 50s through strategic planning and investment diversification. The key is recognizing the difference between active working years and retirement lifestyle needs.

> "If you fail to plan, you plan to fail."

**Quick Win**: Research your employer's retirement plan options and start contributing at least enough to get any matching funds. If self-employed, open a Roth IRA or SEP IRA this month.

### 5. Develop Long-Term Wealth Management Strategy
Wealth creation is a marathon, not a sprint. Focus on future rewards rather than immediate gratification. Long-term strategies might include property investments, stock market participation, and high-interest savings accounts. The patient approach consistently outperforms get-rich-quick schemes that often lead to financial ruin.

> "Your main goal should be the reward you will receive in the future and not in the present."

**Quick Win**: Automate investments in a diversified portfolio of low-cost index funds. Even $50-100 per month grows significantly over time through compound interest.

### 6. Distinguish Needs from Wants
Crowd mentality and keeping up with trends are major wealth destroyers. The world's wealthiest people typically don't follow bandwagon trends but make deliberate, value-based spending decisions. Your financial strength differs from others, so comparing yourself to friends or neighbors is a recipe for disaster.

> "Your wants can ruin you financially and put you in debt if you do not watch it."

**Quick Win**: Implement a 24-hour rule for non-essential purchases. Before buying something you want but don't need, wait 24 hours. Most impulse desires fade, revealing unnecessary spending.

### 7. Avoid Shortcuts to Wealth
Quick wealth schemes often involve illegal activities with severe consequences including fines, jail time, and permanent record damage. True wealth creation requires patience and consistent effort, much like extracting gold from deep within the earth - it takes time, energy, and refinement.

> "Success doesn't come on a platter of gold; you have to work for it."

**Quick Win**: Focus on increasing your income through skill development and career advancement rather than seeking investment shortcuts. Invest in education or certifications that boost your earning potential.

## Action Items
- Build an emergency fund covering 3-6 months of expenses
- Set up automatic savings and investment transfers
- Create specific financial goals with monthly action steps
- Review and optimize all credit card usage
- Research and enroll in retirement planning options
- Implement a waiting period for non-essential purchases
- Focus on skill development to increase earning potential

## Questions to Consider
- What percentage of my income is currently going toward liquid savings versus illiquid investments?
- Which of my current financial goals lack specific action plans and timelines?
- How am I currently using credit, and what changes need to be made?
- At what age do I want to retire, and what specific steps am I taking today to reach that goal?
- What recent purchases were wants rather than needs, and what patterns do I notice?
- Where am I currently seeking shortcuts in my wealth-building journey?

## Conclusion
This book is absolutely worth your time if you're feeling financially uncertain or just starting your wealth-building journey. While the advice may seem basic to experienced investors, these fundamental principles are exactly what most people get wrong, leading to financial stress and debt. The biggest reason to read this book is its simplicity and directness - Dr. Watts doesn't overwhelm readers with complex financial jargon but provides clear, actionable steps that anyone can implement immediately.

The book's strength lies in its focus on behavior change rather than just financial knowledge. Each chapter addresses a common financial mistake and provides practical solutions. For the price of a few cups of coffee, this guide could save you thousands in financial mistakes and set you on the path to genuine financial security. It's particularly valuable for young adults or anyone who has recently experienced financial setbacks and wants to rebuild their foundation properly.
