# Instant Goals
**How to Set Goals and Achieve Them Instantly!**
**Authors**: Instant Series Publication | **Year**: 2014

## Summary
"Instant Goals" is a practical, no-nonsense guide to setting and achieving realistic goals that actually work in your daily life. Unlike typical motivational books filled with inspirational stories of billionaires, this book provides a step-by-step framework for turning your desires into achievable objectives without overwhelming yourself.

The book emphasizes that the biggest mistake people make is setting unrealistic goals beyond their personal realities. Through practical exercises and real-world examples, it teaches readers how to prioritize effectively, categorize goals by timeframes, and create actionable plans that lead to steady progress. The approach is designed for anyone tired of hearing "you need goals" without knowing exactly how to set them properly.

This book is perfect for procrastinators, overwhelmed planners, and anyone who has failed at goal setting before. You'll walk away with a concrete system for identifying what truly matters to you and creating realistic pathways to achieve those goals without burning out.

## Core Insights

### Set Realistic Goals Based on Your Reality
The foundational principle of the book is that most goal-setting failures come from setting unrealistic objectives. Instead of "reaching for the stars," start with changing small things in your life that align with your current financial and time resources. The book uses the example of a waitress earning $10/hour wanting a $100,000 BMW—a goal that would lead to debt and stress rather than success.

> "Stop reaching for the stars, and start with changing little things in your life."

**Quick Take**: Goals must align with your current resources (time and money) to be achievable. Before setting any goal, honestly assess whether it fits within your monthly budget and available time.

### Categorize Goals by Timeframe and Complexity
The book introduces a systematic approach to categorizing goals into short-term (days to 6 months) and long-term (1+ years) objectives. For long-term goals, further categorize them by urgency and cost—1 year, 2 years, 5+ years. This prevents overwhelm and allows you to focus on what needs attention now versus later.

> "Regroup the goals into specific time frames: what you can achieve within the next few days, weeks, and 6 months."

**Quick Take**: Use a tiered system: handle immediate needs first (like education for a 5-year-old), then medium-term goals (wedding in 2 years), and finally long-term objectives (retirement savings over 10+ years).

### Keep Your Goals Private Until Achieved
Contrary to popular advice about "accountability partners," the book suggests keeping your goals to yourself until you achieve them. This protects you from others' insecurities and criticism that could derail your motivation. People often project their own fears and limitations onto others' ambitions.

> "Don't talk about your plans, or what you are currently trying to accomplish to anyone. Keep it to yourself until you actually reach that goal."

**Quick Take**: Share your successes, not your aspirations. Let your results speak for themselves rather than inviting premature feedback that could discourage you.

### Use Journaling to Track Progress and Stay Motivated
Maintain a detailed journal where you check off completed goals, document challenges, and note lessons learned. This creates a visual record of progress and helps you discover patterns in your behavior that can lead to better strategies. The act of writing and checking items provides psychological momentum.

> "Write down a checklist and check off all the goals that you are achieving progressively."

**Quick Take**: Your journal is both a tracking system and a motivation tool. Seeing your progress visually documented makes abstract goals feel concrete and achievable.

### Break Down Complex Goals into Daily Actions
The book provides extensive examples of how to break large goals into manageable daily steps. Whether learning a language or planning a wedding, the key is identifying the smallest possible actions and scheduling them specifically. This transforms overwhelming objectives into doable tasks.

> "Plan your days the nights before by prioritizing the shortest goal(s) in terms of time (or money). Make sure that in your plan you have given the goal the appropriate time span."

**Quick Take**: Every big goal should have a daily action component. If you can't identify what to do today toward your goal, your goal isn't properly broken down yet.

## Action Items

- **Create a priorities notebook**: Write down everything you need to do in the next 6 months and everything you want by year-end, then organize by cost and time requirements.
- **Limit concurrent goals**: Never set more than 5 goals at once during the same day or week—spread them throughout the month to maximize effectiveness.
- **Plan daily**: Write your goal tasks the night before in a different color ink and post them where you'll see them constantly.
- **Weekly assessment**: Set aside time each week to evaluate what went well, what went wrong, and how to improve your approach.
- **Start with least complex goals**: Rank goals from least to most complex and tackle the simpler ones first to build momentum and free up resources.

## Questions to Consider

1. What does "success" specifically mean to you, beyond society's definition?
2. Which of your current goals are actually someone else's expectations rather than your own desires?
3. What small, realistic change could you make this week that would build momentum toward bigger goals?
4. How much time and money do you honestly have available for goal pursuit after necessities?
5. What past "failures" were actually learning experiences that prepared you for future success?

## Conclusion

"Instant Goals" is worth your time if you're tired of motivational fluff and want a practical, step-by-step system for achieving what matters to you. The book's strength lies in its realistic approach—no promises of overnight success or billionaire mindsets, just solid strategies for turning desires into achievable objectives.

The biggest reason to read this book is its emphasis on personal reality over aspiration. By teaching you to align goals with your actual resources and circumstances, it provides a sustainable approach to personal growth that doesn't lead to burnout or disappointment. The detailed examples, especially the wedding planning breakdown, show exactly how to implement these strategies in real life.

While some may find the advice basic, its simplicity is also its strength. This isn't about complex productivity systems or psychological breakthroughs—it's about practical, doable steps that anyone can implement starting today. If you've ever felt overwhelmed by goal setting or failed to follow through on New Year's resolutions, this book offers the reset you need to start achieving goals you can actually accomplish.
