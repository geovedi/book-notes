# How to Read Literature Like a Professor
**Original Title**: Искусство чтения. Как понимать книги. Живое и занимательное руководство по чтению вообще и между строк в частности
**Author**: Thomas C. Foster | **Year**: 2014 (Revised Edition) 

## Summary
This accessible and engaging guide reveals the secret language that literature professors use to analyze texts. Foster demonstrates that literary analysis isn't about finding "the right answer" but about learning to recognize patterns, symbols, and connections that exist beneath the surface of every story. The book teaches readers how to move from passive consumption to active engagement with literature, transforming the reading experience from simple entertainment to intellectual discovery.

Perfect for book club members, students, and curious readers who want to deepen their understanding of literature but feel intimidated by academic analysis. You'll learn to spot the hidden meanings and structural patterns that great authors use to communicate their ideas.

## Core Insights

### The Language of Reading: Literature Has Its Own Grammar
Foster argues that literature operates like a language with its own grammar, codes, and conventions. Just as we learn to speak and understand a foreign language, we can learn to "read" literature more deeply by recognizing its patterns and structures.

"Профессор – несколько более опытный читатель и за годы работы с текстами овладел своего рода «языком чтения», который еще только предстоит изучить его студентам."
> "A professor is a more experienced reader who, over years of working with texts, has mastered a kind of 'language of reading' that his students have yet to learn."
> _Original: "Профессор – несколько более опытный читатель и за годы работы с текстами овладел своего рода «языком чтения», который еще только предстоит изучить его студентам."_
**Quick Take**: Start asking "what could this mean?" instead of just "what does this mean?" - the shift opens up multiple layers of interpretation.

### Everything Is a Symbol (But Not Allegory)
The book teaches that objects, actions, and settings in literature rarely appear by accident. However, unlike allegory (where X always means Y), true symbols have multiple possible meanings that depend on context and reader interpretation.

"Символ означает ровно то, что вы в нем видите. По крайней мере, лично для вас."
> "A symbol means exactly what you see in it. At least, personally for you."
> _Original: "Символ означает ровно то, что вы в нем видите. По крайней мере, лично для вас."_
**Quick Take**: Trust your instincts about symbolic meaning, but test them against the text's context and your knowledge of literary traditions.

### There's Really Only One Story
Foster emphasizes the concept of intertextuality - all literature responds to, references, and builds upon other literature. Recognizing these connections enriches understanding and reveals the universal themes that unite seemingly disparate works.

The three most frequently referenced sources in Western literature are:
- Shakespeare (the universal storyteller)
- The Bible (foundational for Western cultural literacy)
- Greek/Roman mythology (the original archetypal stories)

**Quick Take**: When you encounter a familiar situation or character type, ask yourself what other stories it reminds you of - the answer often reveals deeper meaning.

### Geography Matters: Place as Character
The physical setting of a story is never accidental. Geography shapes characters, drives plot, and reinforces themes. Rivers represent journeys and transformation, mountains symbolize challenges or spiritual quests, and deserts suggest testing or purification.

"География как персонаж и тема"
> "Geography as character and theme"
> _Original: "География как персонаж и тема"_
**Quick Take**: Pay attention to where stories happen - the location often tells you as much about the characters' internal journey as their actions do.

### Politics Is Always Present (Even When Hidden)
Every literary work reflects the political and social context of its creation, but great literature addresses universal human concerns rather than narrow political agendas. Foster distinguishes between timeless works that speak to universal human experience and propaganda that quickly becomes dated.

**Quick Take**: Look for the human story beneath the political message - great literature uses specific situations to explore universal truths.

### Symbols Require Multiple Tools for Interpretation
Understanding symbols requires bringing your full life experience, literary knowledge, and analytical skills to bear. Foster suggests approaching symbols with questions rather than assumptions, and being open to multiple valid interpretations.

"Что еще сказано о пещерах у Форстера? Что мы знаем о пещерах в принципе? Что можно сказать вот об этой конкретной пещере?"
> "What else does Forster say about caves? What do we know about caves in general? What can be said about this particular cave?"
> _Original: "Что еще сказано о пещерах у Форстера? Что мы знаем о пещерах в принципе? Что можно сказать вот об этой конкретной пещере?"_
**Quick Take**: Approach symbols like a detective - gather evidence from the text, from your knowledge of the symbol in other works, and from your own life experience.

## Action Items
- **Keep a symbol journal**: Track recurring images, objects, or actions across different books you read
- **Ask connection questions**: When reading, constantly ask "What does this remind me of?" and "Where have I seen this before?"
- **Practice context reading**: Before starting any book, learn when and where it was written
- **Develop your symbol toolkit**: Build mental associations for common symbols (water=rebirth, fire=destruction/purification, journeys=growth)
- **Read like a writer**: Pay attention to how authors construct meaning through choice, arrangement, and emphasis

## Questions to Consider
- What personal experiences do you bring to every reading, and how do they shape your interpretation?
- How might understanding the "language of literature" change your approach to favorite books?
- What symbols or patterns have you noticed in recent reading that you might have missed before?
- How does knowing that all literature is connected change your relationship with reading?

## Conclusion
This guide democratizes literary analysis, making complex concepts accessible without dumbing them down. Foster's conversational style and practical approach transform potentially intimidating ideas into tools that any reader can use. The biggest reason to read this book is that it makes reading more fun - you'll start seeing connections and meanings that enhance your appreciation of literature across all genres.

The book works because it respects both the complexity of great literature and the intelligence of ordinary readers. Foster doesn't pretend there's a secret password that unlocks all meaning; instead, he provides a framework for asking better questions and making more informed interpretations. You won't become a literature professor overnight, but you will become a more confident, engaged, and thoughtful reader.

The lasting value lies in Foster's central insight: reading isn't about finding the "right" answer but about entering into a conversation with the text, its author, and the entire literary tradition. Once you learn this language, every book becomes richer and more rewarding.
