# An Irreverent Curiosity
**Author**: David Farley | **Year**: 2009
**Category**: History & Biography

## Summary
"An Irreverent Curiosity" is a hilarious and bizarre travel memoir about American writer David Farley's quest to solve one of the Vatican's strangest mysteries: the 1983 theft of the Holy Foreskin of Jesus Christ from the medieval Italian village of Calcata. What begins as curiosity about a truly peculiar religious relic becomes a three-year obsession that immerses Farley in the eccentric world of Calcata, a cliffside village populated by hippies, artists, and locals who guard their secrets fiercely.

This book is perfect for readers who love travel writing with a mystery twist, religious history, and stories about culture clashes. You'll gain insight into the strange world of Catholic relics, the beauty of off-the-beaten-path Italy, and the universal human desire to believe in something extraordinary—even when it's completely absurd.

## Core Insights

### The Holy Foreskin: Christianity's Strangest Relic
The Holy Foreskin was considered the only piece of Jesus' body that could have remained on Earth after his ascension, making it one of Christianity's most coveted relics. According to tradition, it was discovered in Calcata in 1557 when a vision revealed its location in a cave beneath the village church. For over 400 years, this "red chickpea"-sized relic was Calcata's pride, drawing pilgrims and bringing prosperity to the isolated hill town until its mysterious disappearance in 1983.

> "It was the carne vera sacra, 'real holy flesh,' as the people of Calcata admiringly referred to it. It was the foreskin of Jesus Christ, the only piece of the Redeemer's body that he could have conceivably left on earth after his ascension into heaven."

**Quick Take**: The book reveals how faith can transform the bizarre into the sacred, and how communities build their identities around the most unlikely objects.

### Calcata: Italy's Hippie Ghost Town
Calcata is a medieval village carved from tufo rock, perched atop 450-foot cliffs and accessible only through a narrow stone gateway. Abandoned by its original residents in the 1960s after being declared structurally unsafe, it was subsequently discovered by hippies, artists, and bohemians who turned it into a unique artistic community. Farley's immersion in this strange world becomes as much a story about cultural discovery as it is about religious relics.

> "Three cats sprawled out on the chunky river-rock of the cobbled square and lanes of Calcata, absorbing the sun's rays. Two- and three-story buildings with faded and chipped paint made up the L-shaped square."

**Quick Take**: Sometimes the most fascinating destinations are those where old traditions collide with new-age counter-culture in unexpected harmony.

### The Mystery and Its Investigation
When the local priest announces in 1983 that the relic has vanished from its shoebox storage in his closet, it kicks off one of the strangest theft cases in history. Farley's investigation takes him from Vatican archives to interviews with eccentric locals, revealing theories ranging from Vatican conspiracy to petty theft. The more he digs, the more he realizes this mystery says more about human nature than about the actual relic.

> "Who would steal it? And what would they want with it? The mystery of just what the Holy Foreskin was doing in the priest's house—in a shoe box at the back of his wardrobe, no less—and why and how it disappeared, kicked off the most cryptic case of relic theft in centuries."

**Quick Take**: The journey of investigation often reveals more about the investigator and the society than about the mystery itself.

### Faith, Skepticism, and Cultural Discovery
Throughout his quest, Farley navigates the tension between his secular skepticism and his genuine fascination with the power of belief. He discovers that whether the relic was real or not matters less than what it represented to the people of Calcata—a connection to the divine, a source of pride, and the foundation of their community's identity.

> "I came back from that first trip to Europe infected with a new curiosity about the world. I had, in the words of the Church, an 'irreverent curiosity.' I got off gawking at relics—the more bizarre, the better."

**Quick Take**: The most meaningful discoveries often come from embracing the contradictions between what we believe and what fascinates us.

## Takeaways

- **Embrace cultural curiosity**: The best travel experiences come from investigating local traditions and beliefs, even when they seem bizarre
- **Question assumptions**: Both faith and skepticism have value, and the truth often lies in the tension between them
- **Listen to local stories**: Every community has fascinating tales that reveal deeper truths about human nature
- **Stay open to the unexpected**: Sometimes the most rewarding journeys begin with the strangest premises

## Questions to Consider

- What makes certain objects sacred, and how do communities build meaning around them?
- How does tourism and outsider curiosity affect traditional cultures and their practices?
- What's the line between respectful investigation and exploitation of local beliefs?
- Why are humans drawn to mysteries and relics, especially when they involve the divine?

## Conclusion

"An Irreverent Curiosity" is absolutely worth your time if you love travel writing that goes beyond the typical tourist experience. Farley's quest is hilarious, fascinating, and ultimately profound—not because he solves the mystery of the missing relic (which he doesn't), but because he captures something essential about human nature, faith, and the strange beauty of cultural traditions that survive against all odds.

The biggest reason to read this book is its perfect blend of humor, history, and genuine human connection. Farley treats his subjects with respect while maintaining enough distance to keep the story entertaining. The book reminds us that the world is full of bizarre and wonderful things, and the courage to investigate them—even when others think you're crazy—is what makes life interesting. Whether you're interested in religious history, Italian culture, or just enjoy a good mystery with plenty of laughs, this book delivers on all fronts.
