# Macrowikinomics: Rebooting Business and the World
**Authors**: Don Tapscott & Anthony D. Williams | **Year**: 2010 | **Category**: Business & Finance

## Summary
Macrowikinomics expands on the authors' previous work "Wikinomics" to demonstrate how mass collaboration is transforming not just business, but every sector of society. The book argues that we're at a critical turning point in history where traditional industrial-age institutions are failing and a new model based on networked intelligence, openness, and collaboration is emerging. Through compelling case studies ranging from disaster relief in Haiti to open-source automotive design, the authors show how the principles of mass collaboration can solve complex global problems and create more prosperous, just, and sustainable societies.

The book is essential reading for business leaders, policymakers, entrepreneurs, and anyone interested in understanding how digital collaboration is reshaping our world. Readers will gain practical insights into harnessing collective intelligence, building open organizations, and participating in the fundamental restructuring of economic and social systems.

## Core Insights

### The Networked Intelligence Revolution
The digital age represents a transformation as profound as the printing press revolution. Just as Gutenberg's invention democratized knowledge and sparked the Renaissance, today's networked technologies are democratizing innovation and problem-solving on a global scale. The authors argue that we're moving from hierarchical, closed systems to open, collaborative networks where anyone can participate in creating value.

> "The Web makes everyone a publisher. Printing enabled the distribution of knowledge. The Web provides a platform for networking human minds. Printing allowed people to know. The Web enables people to collaborate and to learn collectively."

**Quick Take**: The internet isn't just changing how we communicate—it's fundamentally restructuring how society organizes itself to solve problems and create value.

### The Five Principles of the New Economy
The authors identify five core principles that define successful organizations in the age of networked intelligence: collaboration, openness, sharing, integrity, and interdependence. These principles aren't just business strategies; they're foundational values for creating a prosperous and sustainable global society.

**Collaboration** means moving beyond traditional teams to self-organizing networks that can mobilize global talent. **Openness** involves transparency in operations and decision-making. **Sharing** extends beyond intellectual property to include data, infrastructure, and knowledge commons. **Integrity** has become essential for survival in an age of radical transparency. **Interdependence** recognizes that no organization exists in isolation in our interconnected world.

> "Organizations can succeed and even thrive in this new environment by embracing the five principles of wikinomics: collaboration, openness, sharing, integrity, and interdependence."

**Quick Take**: Success in the 21st century requires organizations to fundamentally rethink their values and operations around openness and collaboration rather than control and secrecy.

### Rebooting Failing Institutions
Traditional institutions across every sector—from finance and healthcare to education and government—are experiencing systemic failures. The book demonstrates how applying mass collaboration principles can transform these failing systems. Examples include: open-source drug discovery at Novartis, peer-to-peer automotive design at Local Motors, collaborative crisis mapping during disasters, and networked scientific research accelerating breakthrough discoveries.

The authors argue that tinkering at the edges isn't sufficient—these institutions require fundamental rebooting based on new collaborative models.

> "It's like every last ounce of oxygen has been squeezed out, leaving a mess of deflated expectations and chronically underutilized resources. This apparent paralysis, in turn, begs some pretty fundamental questions: if the knowledge, leadership, and capability required to solve the really tough problems can't be found in the corporate headquarters and national capitals around the globe, will it be found at all?"

**Quick Take**: The solution to institutional failure isn't incremental reform but fundamental redesign based on networked collaboration and open systems.

### From Crisis to Opportunity: Global Problem Solving
The book demonstrates how collaborative approaches can address seemingly intractable global challenges like climate change, financial regulation, and healthcare transformation. Examples include: open climate data platforms enabling coordinated environmental action, collaborative financial regulation networks preventing systemic risk, and patient communities accelerating medical research and treatment discovery.

The authors show how traditional top-down, nation-state-based approaches are inadequate for today's complex, interconnected global problems.

> "In each sector—whether education, media, health care, energy, finance, or government—there is a mixture of promise and peril ahead. On balance, however, wikinomics offers profound social benefits, including the opportunity to broaden access to science and knowledge, impose greater transparency on financial markets, accelerate the invention and adoption of green technologies, and help make today's leaders in business and government accountable for delivering outcomes that enhance well-being around the world."

**Quick Take**: Global problems require global, collaborative solutions that harness the collective intelligence of distributed networks rather than centralized authority.

### The Prosumer Revolution: Everyone as Innovator
A fundamental shift is occurring from passive consumption to active participation in value creation. The book documents how customers are becoming "prosumers" who co-design products, citizens are becoming co-creators of public services, and patients are becoming partners in healthcare innovation. This transition from passive recipients to active participants represents one of the most profound economic and social transformations of our time.

Examples include Local Motors customers designing their own vehicles, Wikipedia contributors creating the world's largest encyclopedia, and open-source software communities building critical infrastructure.

> "Indeed, as the global economic crisis forced companies in every sector to cut their costs to stay alive, many began to see that hunkering down, while necessary, was insufficient for long-term success. From manufacturing to retail, smart managers have begun to initiate long overdue changes to their structures and strategies."

**Quick Take**: The most successful organizations will be those that effectively engage their customers, citizens, and stakeholders as active partners in value creation rather than treating them as passive recipients.

## Action Items

- **Embrace openness**: Make your organization's data, processes, and decision-making more transparent to stakeholders and the public
- **Build collaborative platforms**: Create systems that enable customers, employees, and partners to contribute ideas and co-create value
- **Develop network leadership**: Shift from command-and-control management to facilitating and coordinating collaborative networks
- **Share strategic assets**: Consider what intellectual property, data, or infrastructure could be shared to create larger value for everyone
- **Foster interdependence**: Build partnerships across traditional boundaries to address complex challenges that no single organization can solve alone

## Questions to Consider

- How can your organization transition from hierarchical control to networked collaboration?
- What valuable assets could you share more broadly to accelerate innovation in your field?
- How can you engage your customers or stakeholders as active partners rather than passive consumers?
- What systemic failures in your industry could be addressed through collaborative approaches?
- How can you balance openness with legitimate needs for privacy, security, and competitive advantage?

## Conclusion

Macrowikinomics is more than a business book—it's a roadmap for transforming how we organize society to meet 21st-century challenges. The authors make a compelling case that the collaborative principles enabled by digital networks represent not just a trend, but a fundamental shift in how humanity creates value and solves problems.

The book's greatest strength lies in its diverse case studies that demonstrate these principles in action across virtually every sector of society. From disaster relief to automotive design, from scientific research to healthcare, the authors show how open, collaborative approaches consistently outperform traditional closed, hierarchical models.

This is essential reading for anyone seeking to understand and participate in the fundamental restructuring of business and society. While the authors are optimistic about the potential of networked collaboration, they're also realistic about the challenges of transitioning from industrial-age institutions to new collaborative models. The book provides both the vision and practical guidance needed to navigate this transformation.

Whether you're a business leader, policymaker, entrepreneur, or concerned citizen, Macrowikinomics offers invaluable insights into how we can harness collective intelligence to build more prosperous, just, and sustainable societies. It's not just about adapting to change—it's about leading the transformation.
