# The Bedtime Story Book
**Original Title**: The Bedtime Story Book
**Authors**: Jen Wainwright (Adapted by), Angel Dominguez (Illustrated by) | **Year**: 2010

## Summary
This heartwarming collection brings together 21 classic children's stories perfect for bedtime reading. The book combines familiar favorites like "Goldilocks and the Three Bears," "Little Red Riding Hood," and "The Three Little Pigs" with lesser-known tales from around the world such as "Anansi and the Sky God," "Crow Brings Daylight," and "The Goose Girl." Each story is carefully adapted for reading aloud, featuring timeless themes of courage, wisdom, kindness, and the consequences of one's actions.

Parents and children will discover stories that teach valuable life lessons while sparking imagination. The collection offers a perfect blend of entertainment and moral education, making bedtime both magical and meaningful.

## Core Stories & Themes

### Classic European Tales
The book features beloved stories from European folklore that have captivated children for generations. "Goldilocks and the Three Bears" teaches about respecting others' property and the dangers of unchecked curiosity. "The Emperor's New Clothes" reveals the power of truth and the folly of vanity, while "The Ugly Duckling" offers hope to those who feel different, showing that true beauty comes from within.

> "Somebody's been sitting in my chair, and they've broken it to bits!"

**Quick Take**: These familiar stories provide comfort through repetition while reinforcing important social values.

### Aesop's Fables & Moral Stories
Timeless fables like "The Boy Who Cried Wolf" and "The Hare and the Tortoise" teach character through simple, memorable plots. These stories use animal characters to demonstrate human virtues and vices, making moral lessons accessible to young minds.

> "Slow and steady wins the race."

**Quick Take**: The collection's moral framework helps children understand right from wrong through engaging narratives.

### Adventure & Ingenuity
Stories like "Puss in Boots" and "The Musicians of Bremen" showcase clever characters overcoming challenges through wit and cooperation. These tales celebrate resourcefulness and the idea that intelligence can triumph over brute strength.

**Quick Take**: Adventure stories inspire problem-solving skills and demonstrate the value of creative thinking.

### Global Folklore
The book includes diverse tales from different cultures: "Anansi and the Sky God" from African folklore, "Crow Brings Daylight" from Native American traditions, and "The Golden Goose" from European collections. These stories expose children to different cultural perspectives while highlighting universal human values.

**Quick Take**: Multi-cultural stories broaden children's worldview while showing that good values transcend borders.

## Bedtime Reading Tips
- Read with expressive voices for different characters
- Pause at dramatic moments to build suspense
- Ask children what they think might happen next
- Discuss the moral lessons after each story
- Establish a consistent bedtime routine with story time

## Conclusion
This collection deserves a permanent place on any child's bookshelf. The carefully selected stories offer the perfect balance of entertainment and education, making bedtime something both children and parents can look forward to. The beautiful illustrations by Angel Dominguez add visual magic to already captivating tales.

The biggest reason to read this book is its ability to create lasting memories and meaningful conversations between generations. These stories have endured for centuries because they speak to universal truths about human nature, growing up, and finding one's place in the world. In our fast-paced digital age, this collection reminds us of the power of simple storytelling to connect hearts and minds.
