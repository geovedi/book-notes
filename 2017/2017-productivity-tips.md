# 2017 Productivity Tips
**Authors**: Patt Tomas | **Year**: 2017
**Category**: Self-Help & Personal Development

## Summary
This concise productivity guide targets entrepreneurs and remote workers who want to maximize their time and output. The book presents eight actionable strategies designed to transform how you approach daily tasks and manage your most valuable resource—time.

Written for anyone working from home or running their own business who feels they could make better use of their time, this guide delivers practical techniques that can be implemented immediately. Readers will learn to identify high-impact activities, eliminate time-wasting habits, and create systems that boost productivity while reducing stress.

## Core Strategies

### The 80/20 Rule: Focus on What Matters
The Pareto Principle reveals that 80% of your results come from just 20% of your efforts. In business, this often means 20% of customers generate 80% of revenue. The key is identifying and doubling down on these high-impact activities while eliminating or reducing the low-value tasks that consume disproportionate time and energy.

> "For many events, roughly 80% of the effects come from 20% of the causes."
**Quick Take**: Analyze your business and life to find your 20%—then focus relentlessly on those activities while scaling back everything else.

### Time Pressure: Use Timers to Focus
Setting timers creates productive urgency that forces concentration and completion. Whether using your phone's timer or online countdown tools, allocating specific time blocks (like 25 minutes for a task) creates natural boundaries that prevent procrastination and perfectionism.

The key is setting challenging but achievable time limits that push you to work efficiently without sacrificing quality. It's easier to complete a basic version first and refine later than to never start due to overwhelming expectations.

**Quick Take**: Use timers for all tasks—even creative ones—to maintain focus and prevent the perfectionism trap that leads to procrastination.

### Structure: Deadlines and Schedules
Without external structure, entrepreneurs can watch entire days slip away. Creating daily schedules with hard deadlines provides the framework needed for consistent productivity. While flexibility is important, having designated work times and specific deadlines prevents aimless drifting.

The author suggests starting times and deadlines but remaining open to extending work when you're in a productive groove. The key is finding what works for your personal work style while maintaining enough structure to ensure progress.

**Quick Take**: Create your own structure with start times and deadlines, but ride productive waves when they hit—don't rigidly stop just because the clock says it's time.

### Rewards: Motivate Through Incentives
The reward system transforms work from a chore into a series of achievable challenges with desirable payoffs. Small rewards (coffee break, social media check, quick chat) provide motivation between tasks, while larger rewards celebrate daily accomplishments.

This strategy breaks the workday into manageable segments and creates positive associations with completing tasks. Rewards should be meaningful to you personally and timed to reinforce productive behavior patterns.

**Quick Take**: Sprinkle small rewards throughout your day and use larger ones as motivation to complete your most important work.

### Time Pockets: Harness Hidden Opportunities
Time pockets are the small gaps between activities—waiting for food, commuting, standing in lines—that most people waste. Having a ready list of 5-minute tasks (email replies, social media drafts, list-making) transforms these moments into productive opportunities.

These pockets can also serve learning opportunities through podcasts or audiobooks. The key is recognizing these moments and having backup tasks ready, turning otherwise wasted time into valuable progress.

**Quick Take**: Keep a phone list of 5-minute tasks and always be ready to transform waiting time into productive time.

### Digital Focus: Fewer Tabs, Better Concentration
Multiple browser tabs and messaging apps create constant distractions that fragment attention and slow down both you and your computer. The solution is maintaining only essential tabs open and completely closing messaging apps during focused work periods.

When you find interesting articles or resources, save them to bookmarks rather than keeping tabs open. This preserves resources for later while maintaining focus on current priorities. Self-control and strategic app usage protect your most productive hours.

**Quick Take**: Bookmark interesting links instead of keeping tabs open, and completely shut down messaging apps during focus periods.

### Task Batching: Group Similar Activities
Batching similar tasks (social media posts, blog content, video creation) reduces mental switching costs and increases efficiency. By grouping like activities together, you stay in the same mindset and leverage momentum across multiple items.

Instead of setting up equipment for one video, shoot five at once. Rather than finding images daily, locate a week's worth in one session. This approach saves setup time and maintains creative flow across similar tasks.

**Quick Take**: Identify similar daily, weekly, or monthly tasks and schedule specific blocks to complete them all at once.

### Time Protection: Defend Your Schedule
Time wasters who don't value their own time won't value yours either. Learning to lead conversations—both in person and digitally—protects your schedule while maintaining relationships. This means being direct but polite about time constraints.

For in-person interactions, acknowledge pleasantries but quickly redirect to the point. For scheduling, offer specific time options rather than endless back-and-forth. People surprisingly respect those who value their time and often view them as more important.

**Quick Take**: Be direct but polite in conversations, offer specific time options for meetings, and don't hesitate to protect your focus time.

## Action Items

- **Identify your 80/20**: Analyze your business activities to find which 20% produces 80% of results
- **Set up timers**: Use time limits for all tasks to maintain focus and prevent perfectionism
- **Create daily structure**: Establish work hours and deadlines to prevent days from slipping away
- **Plan rewards**: Design both small and large rewards to motivate task completion
- **Build time pocket lists**: Keep 5-minute tasks ready for unexpected free moments
- **Minimize digital distractions**: Close unnecessary tabs and messaging apps during focus time
- **Batch similar tasks**: Group like activities together to maintain momentum and efficiency
- **Protect your schedule**: Learn to redirect conversations and control your time boundaries
