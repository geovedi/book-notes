# When Sex Life Becomes Deadlock
**Original Title**: Når sexlivet går i hårdknude
**Author**: Kirsten Ahlburg | **Year**: 2017

## Summary
This essential relationship guide tackles one of the most common yet rarely discussed problems in long-term partnerships: the disappearance of sexual desire. Through a series of relatable case studies and practical solutions, the book explores why sexual desire fades in relationships and offers concrete strategies for rekindling intimacy. The author argues that sexual problems rarely stem from one partner alone but rather from the complex dynamics between couples.

Perfect for couples experiencing desire discrepancies, therapists, and anyone seeking to understand the psychological and practical factors that influence sexual intimacy in long-term relationships. Readers will gain practical tools for communication, intimacy-building exercises, and a deeper understanding of how life stressors impact sexual connection.

## Core Insights

### When Women Lose Sexual Desire
The most common scenario in relationships involves women losing sexual desire while men maintain their interest. This often happens after childbirth when women become consumed with motherhood, household responsibilities, and work. The author explains that desire disappears not because women are "broken" but because they're overwhelmed and emotionally disconnected. Practical overload (doing most household chores plus working full-time) and emotional neglect (lack of attention, love, and intimacy from partners) are primary culprits. Women typically need emotional closeness to feel sexual desire, while men often desire sex to feel close.

> "Sexual desire is not at one's beck and call. It is like food. If you are not hungry, you will not be able to eat: you cannot force a need or desire you do not have."

**Quick Take**: Redistribute household responsibilities and create specific emotional connection rituals to restore desire naturally.

### When Men Lose Sexual Desire
Contrary to common belief, men also experience desire loss, primarily when they feel constantly criticized, inadequate, or "mothered" by their partners. Men who face relentless correction about household chores, work performance, or sexual technique often withdraw emotionally and physically. Performance anxiety and feeling inadequate can lead to complete desire disappearance and even impotence. The author notes that men need respect, appreciation, and a sense of competence to maintain sexual interest.

> "The feeling of falling short of their partner's demands in bed as well as otherwise is one of the most frequent reasons why men's sexual desire disappears."

**Quick Take**: Focus on appreciation instead of correction, and allow men to develop their own sexual style without constant comparison to past partners.

### The Role of Communication
The book reveals that talking about sexual problems can sometimes make them worse. While good communication is essential, detailed discussions about what's wrong sexually can create anxiety and kill the last sparks of desire. Instead, the author recommends focusing on positive aspects and what works rather than analyzing failures. Couples should also avoid mentioning ex-partners' sexual abilities, as this comparison typically devastates current partners.

> "Long conversations about their sex life, where they discuss all the things that went wrong and the consequent problems, may contribute to killing their last spark of sexual desire."

**Quick Take**: Practice appreciative communication and focus on what feels good rather than analyzing problems.

### Intimacy Exercises for Desire Recovery
The book provides structured sensuality exercises designed to rebuild intimacy without pressure. These exercises start with non-sexual touching using hands only, avoiding breasts and genitals entirely. Partners take turns giving and receiving touch for 15 minutes each, focusing on new body areas and sensations. The exercises emphasize creating the right atmosphere with candles, music, and uninterrupted time. Advanced exercises can incorporate full-body contact, kissing, and eye contact while still excluding sexual activity.

> "This first sensuality exercise enables couples to preserve intimacy when sexual desire has disappeared from one or both. They become familiar with new parts of their bodies, parts that were otherwise little noticed."

**Quick Take**: Start with weekly 30-minute sensuality exercises focusing entirely on non-genital touch and exploration.

### The Impact of Stress and Time Pressure
The author identifies stress as a major desire killer, particularly for workaholic men and overwhelmed women. When people are overextended and doing multiple tasks simultaneously, they lose touch with their bodies and senses. The book recommends single-sensory focus exercises to recover from stress: concentrate solely on sounds during a walk, focus exclusively on taste while eating, or spend time watching fish in an aquarium to lower heart rate and blood pressure.

> "The better you get at sensing your surroundings and focusing exclusively on one thing at a time, the more relaxed you will get. And the more relaxed you get, the more you will feel your own body and hence your sexuality."

**Quick Take**: Practice single-focus sensory activities daily to reduce stress and reconnect with your body.

## Best Quotes

- "With a busy everyday life like that of many present-day women it is hard to let go and abandon oneself. And that is necessary to feel one's desire."

- "If you tend to correct and criticise your partner, you may consider whether it is really just. Perhaps the man's way of doing the laundry and cleaning the house is just as correct as that of the woman: who is to decide which way is right or wrong?"

- "Eye contact will make you sense your love and be close to one another. Sometimes you may even get a feeling of physical contact merely by keeping eye contact with your partner."

- "If you have shared a long sex life centred exclusively on the physical aspect, you may find it difficult to change it overnight. So usually the end of it is that you stick to your old routine, failing to make any change."

## Action Items

- Create a weekly schedule that ensures both partners have equal time for rest and personal activities
- Practice specific communication: instead of saying "I need more love," say "I would like us to sit together on the sofa hugging each other for 10 minutes daily"
- Schedule weekly 30-minute sensuality exercises starting with non-genital touch only
- Take regular walks focusing on one sense at a time (sight, sound, smell, touch, or taste)
- Create a dedicated "intimate space" in your home free from distractions and children
- Practice appreciation by identifying three things your partner does right each day
- Consider temporary celibacy agreements to remove performance pressure while working on intimacy

## Questions to Consider

- What practical burdens or emotional needs might be suppressing your or your partner's desire?
- How does stress and multitasking affect your ability to feel present and sensual?
- What specific actions make you feel loved and appreciated beyond sexual activity?
- Are you mothering or criticizing your partner in ways that might be killing desire?
- What expectations about sex frequency and performance are creating pressure in your relationship?
- How can you create more time for connection and romance in your busy schedule?
- What would it look like to focus on intimacy and sensuality rather than performance?

## Conclusion
"When Sex Life Becomes Deadlock" offers an invaluable roadmap for couples navigating desire discrepancies. Rather than treating desire loss as individual pathology, the author presents it as a relational issue requiring mutual understanding and practical adjustments. The book's strength lies in its realistic acknowledgment that desire naturally fluctuates in long-term relationships and that many couples can build satisfying connections that may or may not include regular intercourse.

This guide is worth your time if you're experiencing desire conflicts, as it provides both validation and concrete tools without promising magical fixes. The biggest takeaway is that sexual problems often mask deeper issues of fairness, appreciation, and emotional connection. By addressing these foundational elements and practicing the suggested intimacy exercises, many couples can rebuild desire or discover new forms of satisfying physical connection that work for their unique relationship.

While some may find the case studies slightly dated in their gender dynamics, the core psychological insights remain profoundly relevant for any couple struggling with sexual desire discrepancies in our stress-filled, multitasking modern world.
