# How to Make Money from Public Domain Videos on YouTube
**Authors**: Juha Öörni | **Year**: 2017

## Summary
This practical guide reveals how entrepreneurs can tap into the vast treasure trove of public domain films and videos to generate income through YouTube and other channels. The book breaks down a simple but effective business model that requires minimal upfront investment but offers residual income potential. Rather than chasing popularity, success comes from strategic curation, proper marketing, and creative adaptation of classic content.

Perfect for side hustlers, content creators, and entrepreneurs looking for passive income streams, this book provides a complete roadmap from finding public domain videos to monetizing them effectively. The approach requires persistence and research skills but eliminates the need for content creation from scratch.

## Core Insights

### The Public Domain Goldmine
Public domain contains countless films across every genre imaginable, from classic cartoons like Betty Boop and Popeye to iconic characters like Charlie Chaplin and Sherlock Holmes. These works are free to use, modify, and monetize without copyright restrictions. The key advantage isn't just free content—it's that many of these classics already have built-in fan bases and collector communities eager to consume them.

> "Just because a movie or video clip is a classic or popular does not necessarily make it good business. As you become a little more comfortable and knowledgeable in this type of work, you will find that the money is in how you use and market each work, not in its popularity on youtube."

**Quick Take**: Success comes from strategic curation and marketing, not just uploading popular classics.

### Research & Discovery Framework
Finding valuable public domain content requires systematic research across multiple specialized platforms. The book provides 12+ specialized websites and databases where you can discover, verify, and download public domain films. Key resources include Internet Archive (30,000+ films), OpenFlix (genre-browsable collection), and Public Domain Movie Database.

> "If this process were really easy everyone would do it, and the competition would be so great that you wouldn't make any money. So the fact that some of this may be a little harder is actually a benefit because it weeds out those that are less interested in creating a real business."

**Quick Take**: Use quotation marks in search terms to find exact matches and verify public domain status before investing time.

### YouTube Monetization System
The core YouTube strategy follows a simple 5-step process: create a themed channel, download public domain videos, upload with monetization enabled, optimize with keywords like "watch online" and "free streaming," and potentially create a companion niche site with embedded videos. The system generates residual income as long as videos continue getting views.

> "This is a really simple method of making money off YouTube using public domain videos. It's incredibly simple, although it's by no means a get rich quick scheme. The advantage of this trick is that it's going to be a residual source of income, as long as the channel is getting views."

**Quick Take**: Prepare documentation to prove public domain status if YouTube challenges your content ownership.

### Diversification Strategies
Beyond YouTube, the book outlines multiple additional revenue streams: creating DVD collections for collectors, converting films for modern devices like iPods, producing movie posters, building directory websites with paid downloads, creating dinner theater shows, extracting audio clips for sound effects libraries, and publishing collector's guide ebooks.

> "Turn these classic old films into plays and dinner shows. If you are already in this business it would not be very difficult at all to write up a screen play and re-in act it."

**Quick Take**: Each film can become multiple products targeting different customer segments (collectors, parents, theaters, multimedia creators).

## Action Items
- Create a YouTube channel themed around classical movies or specific genres
- Build a research workflow using the 12+ resources mentioned in the book
- Develop a system for verifying public domain status before downloading content
- Set up keyword optimization strategy for video descriptions and tags
- Create a companion website to embed videos and generate additional ad revenue
- Research collector communities for specific film genres or characters
- Plan multiple revenue streams from each public domain film you acquire

## Conclusion
This book offers a legitimate, accessible pathway to generating passive income through YouTube using public domain content. While not a get-rich-quick scheme, it provides a systematic approach that requires research skills and persistence rather than technical expertise or large investments. The biggest value lies in showing readers how to think entrepreneurially about freely available content and how to build sustainable income streams from creative curation and marketing.

The approach is perfect for content creators who want to focus on business strategy rather than content production, and for entrepreneurs seeking multiple revenue streams from the same source material. With proper research and execution, this could evolve from a side hustle into a substantial media business built entirely on freely available classic content.

Is it worth your time? Absolutely if you're interested in content monetization and willing to put in the research work. The biggest reason to read it is that it opens your eyes to an entire ecosystem of free content that most people overlook, and gives you a practical roadmap for turning that content into multiple income streams.
