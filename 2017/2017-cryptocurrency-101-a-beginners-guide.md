# Cryptocurrency 101: A Beginners Guide
**Authors**: Patt Tomas | **Year**: 2017

## Summary
This beginner's guide introduces readers to the world of cryptocurrency investing, focusing on Bitcoin, Ethereum, and alternative coins (altcoins). The book covers the fundamental concepts of blockchain technology, different cryptocurrencies, investment strategies, and practical steps for getting started in the crypto market. Written during the 2017 crypto boom, it provides a comprehensive overview of how cryptocurrencies work, their market dynamics, and various approaches to profiting from this emerging asset class.

This guide is perfect for complete beginners who want to understand cryptocurrency basics and learn practical investment strategies without getting overwhelmed by technical jargon. Readers will gain enough knowledge to make informed decisions about entering the crypto market and protecting their investments.

## Core Insights

### Understanding Blockchain Technology
Blockchain is a decentralized digital ledger that records all cryptocurrency transactions across a network of computers. Unlike traditional banking systems, there's no central authority controlling it—every participant has a copy of the entire transaction history. This distributed nature makes it extremely secure and transparent.

> "Cryptocurrency is a digital currency in which encryption techniques are used to regulate the generation of units of currency and verify the transfer funds operating independently of a central bank"

**Quick Take**: The beauty of blockchain lies in its decentralization—no single entity can control or manipulate the system, making it resistant to censorship and corruption.

### Bitcoin: The Original Cryptocurrency
Bitcoin started in 2009 worth pennies and reached $4,300 by 2017, following Moore's Law by doubling in price every 8 months. With a hard cap of 21 million bitcoins that will take 50-100 years to mine completely, its scarcity drives value upward. Bitcoin's limited supply gives it an advantage over unlimited coins like Ethereum.

> "Bitcoin was just the beginning though, since then hundreds upon hundreds of cryptocurrencies have been created for dozens of different applications outside of currencies"

**Quick Take**: Bitcoin's value comes from its first-mover advantage, limited supply, and growing adoption—making it the closest thing to "digital gold" in the crypto world.

### Investment Strategies: Two Main Approaches
The book outlines two primary cryptocurrency investment strategies. The "Buy and Hold" approach involves purchasing cryptocurrencies for long-term appreciation, similar to traditional investing. The second strategy is day trading, where investors actively trade altcoins against base currencies like Bitcoin, Ethereum, or USDT, requiring market analysis skills and trend identification.

> "Holding has it risks like chart below of Reddcoin. The price spiked then pulled back all the way to the start as opposed to the stable bitcoin that holds to its spikes"

**Quick Take**: Choose your strategy based on your risk tolerance and time commitment—long-term holding is simpler but requires patience, while day trading offers more opportunities but demands skill and attention.

### Security and Wallet Management
Protecting your cryptocurrency investments requires proper wallet management. Hardware wallets like Trezor store private keys offline, eliminating the risk of online hacks. A private key is essentially your access code to your cryptocurrency—if you lose it, you lose everything. Paper wallets provide another offline storage option by writing down your keys and keeping them physically secure.

> "If you have a substantial amount of bitcoin and you're holding it for the long run for about 10 years you probably don't want to trust it on exchanges like bitrex because they store your private keys on their platform"

**Quick Take**: Not your keys, not your crypto—always store substantial holdings in hardware or paper wallets rather than on exchanges.

### Market Understanding and Altcoins
The cryptocurrency market includes hundreds of alternative coins beyond Bitcoin, each with unique features. Litecoin offers faster transactions, Dash provides instant payments, Monero focuses on privacy, and Ethereum enables smart contracts. Understanding these differences helps investors diversify beyond just Bitcoin.

> "The Ethereum community fractured when a disagreement over how a technically legal theft of funds should be handled. The majority of users wished to change Ethereum's code in order to get the lost funds back"

**Quick Take**: Research altcoins thoroughly—while some offer innovative features, many fail to deliver on their promises or gain meaningful adoption.

## Action Items
- Start with research and education before investing any money
- Only invest what you're willing to lose in this high-risk, high-reward market
- Choose a reputable exchange for initial cryptocurrency purchases
- Set up secure wallet storage for long-term holdings
- Consider tax implications and treat crypto trading as a business
- Decide between buy-and-hold or active trading based on your personality and schedule

## Questions to Consider
- What is your risk tolerance for potentially volatile investments?
- How much time can you dedicate to monitoring your crypto investments?
- Do you prefer the simplicity of long-term holding or the excitement of active trading?
- Are you prepared for the tax implications of cryptocurrency gains?
- What role should cryptocurrency play in your overall investment portfolio?

## Conclusion
This book provides a solid foundation for cryptocurrency beginners, covering essential concepts without overwhelming technical detail. While some price information is dated (written during the 2017 boom), the fundamental principles of blockchain technology, security practices, and investment strategies remain relevant. The author strikes a good balance between enthusiasm and caution, emphasizing both the potential rewards and significant risks involved.

Is this worth your time? For absolute newcomers to cryptocurrency, yes—it offers a comprehensive introduction in an accessible format. However, readers should supplement this 2017 guide with more current resources to understand the rapidly evolving crypto landscape. The biggest takeaway is that while cryptocurrency offers exciting opportunities, success requires education, security consciousness, and realistic expectations about both risks and rewards.
