# Morning Routine
**Authors**: Sylvia Lynne | **Year**: 2017
**Category**: Self-Help & Personal Development

## Summary
Morning Routine" is a practical guide to transforming your mornings from chaotic to purposeful by building sustainable early-rising habits. The book's core premise is that willpower alone isn't enough to maintain early mornings—you need to make them exciting and automatic through habit formation. The author shares her personal journey of watching TV shows in the morning to incentivize getting up early, then gradually replacing entertainment with enriching activities once the habit was established. The book provides a step-by-step framework for designing a personalized morning routine that includes practices like meditation, journaling, exercise, gratitude, and learning, all tailored to fit your lifestyle and interests.

This book is perfect for anyone who's struggled with waking up early, feels their mornings are rushed and reactive, or wants to start their day with intention rather than chaos. You'll learn how to build lasting habits through small, enjoyable steps rather than relying on motivation that inevitably fades, and discover how to create a morning routine that energizes rather than depletes you.

## Core Insights

### Make It Exciting First, Then Elevate It
The foundation of any successful morning routine isn't discipline—it's anticipation. Most people fail at early mornings because they focus on what they *should* do, not what they *want* to do. Start by identifying something that genuinely excites you to get out of bed—whether it's watching a favorite TV series, enjoying specialty coffee, or reading a compelling book. Once you've mastered the habit of waking up early (typically 2-4 weeks), gradually replace or supplement your initial incentive with more purposeful activities. This approach works because it leverages your brain's reward system rather than fighting against it.

> "Think of a time when you were so excited to get up in the morning that you could barely sleep—was that something like Christmas or going on a trip? That's what we want to try to do to our mornings; we want to try to create an environment that's exciting and fun for us."

**Quick Take**: Start with what excites you, not what you think should excite you. Build the early-rising habit first, then gradually introduce more enriching activities once the momentum is established.

### Design with Purpose, Not Perfection
Your morning routine should serve a clear purpose—something you can articulate in 140 characters or less. This primary focus becomes your compass when motivation wanes or when you're tempted to skip activities. The author suggests themes like "self-love and fulfillment to sustain positive habits" or "building clarity of purpose and a good start to the day." This focus isn't just motivational—it's practical. When designing your routine, ask whether each activity truly serves your stated purpose. If not, either eliminate it or reconsider your purpose. Most successful routines fall between 30-90 minutes and include a mix of mindset work (gratitude, meditation), self-knowledge practices (journaling), physical movement, and purposeful action toward goals.

> "What is your morning routine ultimately helping to achieve for you? More self-love, peace, purpose, gratitude?"

**Quick Take**: Define your "why" before deciding your "what." A purpose-driven morning routine is more sustainable than a checklist of popular productivity habits.

### The Auto-Start System
The most critical moment in any morning routine isn't when it begins—it's the transition from bed to action. To overcome morning resistance, create an automatic sequence that requires zero decision-making: place your alarm across the room, immediately get up, turn it off, go to the bathroom, drink water, brush teeth, and get in the shower. This five-step sequence acts as a launch pad—once completed, you're fully awake and ready for purposeful activities. The author emphasizes that this sequence should be identical every single day until it becomes automatic, effectively bypassing your morning brain's preference for the snooze button and creating momentum before your conscious mind can object.

> "You want to get out of bed, turn your alarm off, go to the bathroom, drink a glass of water, brush your teeth, get in the shower. Do those types of things first because it's going to help you wake up."

**Quick Take**: Create a non-negotiable five-step sequence that requires zero morning decision-making. This momentum carries you past the point where you'd normally return to bed.

## Action Items
- **Week 1**: Place your alarm across the room and identify ONE exciting morning incentive (even if it's just watching TV). Complete the 5-step auto-start sequence daily for 7 days.
- **Week 2**: Add a 10-minute purposeful activity after your incentive, such as journaling or light reading. Keep your incentive to maintain the early-rising habit.
- **Week 3**: Define your morning routine purpose in 140 characters or less and post it visibly. Begin phasing in more enriching activities as the early-rising habit strengthens.
- **Week 4**: Create a detailed written routine with specific timing for each activity. Use a timer app to eliminate decision-making.

## Questions to Consider
- What genuinely excites you enough to sacrifice sleep for it? Be honest about what you actually want vs. what you think you should want.
- If you could achieve just one outcome from your mornings, what would it be? How would that change how you spend those first hours?
- What limiting beliefs do you have about sleep and morning energy? Are these beliefs serving or hindering your goals?
- What would your ideal self do first thing in the morning? What's the smallest version of that behavior you could start tomorrow?

## Implementation Guide
1. **Preparation Phase** (Tonight): Write down exactly what you'll do tomorrow morning, including your exciting incentive. Place your alarm across the room.
2. **Wake-Up Protocol**: When alarm sounds, immediately complete the 5-step sequence without exceptions or thinking.
3. **Reward Phase**: Engage in your chosen incentive activity for 15-30 minutes to build positive association with early mornings.
4. **Gradual Enrichment**: Once the early-rising habit is consistent (7-14 days), slowly add one purposeful activity at a time.
5. **Regular Review**: Weekly, assess whether your routine still aligns with your stated purpose and adjust as needed.

## Conclusion
"Morning Routine" offers refreshingly honest advice that acknowledges human nature rather than fighting against it. The book's greatest strength is its emphasis on starting with pleasure rather than discipline—a counterintuitive but effective approach that many productivity books miss. The author's personal stories and vulnerability make the advice feel accessible rather than overwhelming, and the step-by-step framework is genuinely actionable for beginners.

Is this worth your time? Absolutely if you've struggled with traditional morning routine advice that demands perfection from day one. The book is especially valuable for night owls who think they're "not morning people" and will show you that anyone can become an early riser with the right incentives and systems. The biggest reason to read this book is that it removes the shame from not having perfect mornings and instead offers a sustainable path to gradual improvement. By the end, you'll understand that building a morning routine isn't about dramatic overhauls—it's about small, consistent steps that compound into meaningful change.
