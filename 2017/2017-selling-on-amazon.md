# Selling on Amazon: A Blueprint to $2000 Monthly Profit
**Authors**: George Pain | **Year**: 2017

## Summary
This practical guide serves as your comprehensive roadmap to building a successful Amazon selling business that generates at least $2000 per month in profit as a side income. Written for aspiring entrepreneurs and those seeking additional income streams, the book breaks down the complex world of Amazon selling into manageable, actionable steps.

The book recognizes that while many people have heard about Amazon success stories, they often don't know where to begin. It starts from the basics and progressively builds your understanding of finding profitable products, navigating restricted categories, using Amazon's powerful tools, and implementing effective pricing strategies. The guide emphasizes leveraging automation and Amazon's built-in infrastructure to maximize efficiency and profitability.

For anyone looking to escape the constraints of traditional employment or build a flexible online business, this blueprint provides the practical knowledge needed to start selling on Amazon and scale toward the $2000 monthly profit target through strategic planning and smart automation.

## Core Insights

### The Amazon Advantage: Why Start Here?
Amazon offers sellers unprecedented access to a global marketplace with over 300 million customer accounts and $300 billion in annual sales. The platform provides robust logistics infrastructure, secure payment systems, and powerful automation tools that eliminate many traditional business challenges. Unlike physical storefronts, Amazon's variable cost structure means you only pay when you make sales, dramatically reducing financial risk.

> "Amazon is a global online market in consumer goods with over 1.5 million unique visitors a day."

**Quick Take**: Start with Amazon's Individual plan (under 40 items monthly) and scale to Professional when you consistently exceed that threshold - this approach minimizes risk while maximizing access to Prime customers.

### Product Sourcing Strategy: What and Where to Sell
Finding profitable products begins with niche research based on your passion, skills, experience, and available funds. The book outlines four main sourcing methods: direct supplier relationships, private labeling, contract manufacturing, and retail arbitrage. Key sourcing platforms include Alibaba, Global Sources, AliExpress, and Made-In-China for international suppliers.

> "To determine what to sell on Amazon is the first and foremost step every seller undertakes. You can't even open a Seller's account if you don't have a rough idea of what you should sell."

**Quick Take**: Start with products you're passionate about and knowledgeable about, then validate their profitability using Amazon's Sales Rank and competitor analysis before committing to inventory.

### Automation: Your Path to Multiple Income Streams
The fastest way to reach your $2000 monthly goal is through automation, which allows you to manage multiple product lines and income streams simultaneously. The book recommends tools like Jungle Scout for product research, AMZ Tracker for keyword management, and FeedbackGenius for customer communication. Amazon's own Seller App enables mobile management of your entire business.

> "The best way to make $2000 a month profit on side income as fast as possible is to emulate Amazon's way of doing business â€“ automation."

**Quick Take**: Automate repetitive tasks first (inventory management, repricing, customer responses) to free up time for strategic activities like product research and business expansion.

### Sales Rank Intelligence: Making Data-Driven Decisions
Amazon Sales Rank (Best Sellers Rank) provides crucial insights into product performance within categories. Lower numbers indicate better performance, with rank #1 being the category bestseller. The rank updates hourly and considers sales frequency, performance stability, and customer experience factors like reviews and returns.

> "The lower the rank, the higher is the performance of a given product relative to other products within the same category."

**Quick Take**: Use Sales Rank combined with customer reviews to identify profitable opportunities - look for products with ranks under 100,000 in their category and strong positive review patterns.

### Strategic Pricing for Maximum Profit
Pricing requires balancing your costs (production, shipping, Amazon fees) against market dynamics and competitor pricing. Amazon charges referral fees (6-20% depending on category), plus storage and subscription costs. The book outlines several pricing strategies: penetration pricing to gain market share, premium pricing for unique products, and psychological pricing tactics like charm pricing ($9.99 vs $10.00).

> "Pricing your product is the most important decision you have to make in the market. It determines who should buy your product, by how much and in what quantity."

**Quick Take**: Start with cost-plus pricing (all costs + desired profit margin), then adjust based on market conditions and competitor analysis, always maintaining awareness of your break-even point.

## Action Items
- Research Amazon's restricted categories before investing in any product inventory to avoid costly rejections
- Start with Individual Seller plan ($0.99/item) and track your sales volume to determine when to upgrade to Professional plan ($39.99/month)
- Download and master the Amazon Seller App to manage your business remotely and respond quickly to customer needs
- Use Amazon Sales Rank and customer reviews to validate product profitability before making significant inventory investments
- Implement automation tools gradually, starting with the most time-consuming tasks in your selling process
- Develop a pricing strategy that covers all costs while remaining competitive within your chosen niche

- What level of automation can you implement immediately versus as your business grows?

- What's your scaling plan once you achieve the $2000 monthly profit target?

## Conclusion
This book is worth your time if you're serious about building a practical online business with relatively low startup costs. The biggest reason to read it is the systematic approach it provides - from product research to pricing strategies - that can help you avoid common beginner mistakes and reach profitability faster.

The guide excels at breaking down complex ecommerce concepts into actionable steps, making Amazon selling accessible even to those with no prior business experience. While the $2000 monthly profit target might take longer than suggested for some sellers, the strategies and tools provided are solid foundations for building a sustainable online business.

What sets this book apart is its emphasis on automation and leveraging Amazon's existing infrastructure rather than trying to reinvent the wheel. For anyone willing to put in the initial research and setup work, this blueprint provides a realistic path to generating meaningful side income through Amazon's massive marketplace.
