# How To Make Money Blogging: Insider Tips And Tricks That Can Help You Make Money In Less Than 30-Days
**Authors**: Keith Meyers | **Year**: 2017
**Category**: Business & Finance

## Summary
This practical guide demystifies the process of earning income through blogging, offering real-world strategies from someone who experienced both the challenges and successes of freelance writing. Keith Meyers shares his personal journey from being laid off to building an online writing career, providing actionable advice on content creation, monetization methods, and self-publishing eBooks.

The book targets aspiring bloggers and freelance writers who want to quickly establish multiple income streams online. Readers will learn how to identify profitable niches, create compelling content, set up payment systems, and leverage platforms like Amazon Kindle Direct Publishing to maximize their earning potential in a short timeframe.

## Core Insights

### Find Your Niche and Create Fresh Content
The foundation of successful blogging lies in identifying trending topics and providing up-to-date information that solves real problems for readers. Meyers emphasizes the importance of researching current interests using tools like Google Trends, Twitter, and Instagram to discover what people are actively discussing. Your goal is to become the go-to source for specific information that others find valuable and shareable.

> "Are you tired of reading the same old boring information on the Internet? Do you keep asking yourself, 'Why don't Internet writers update their content?' Well, did you know that writing up-to-date 'how to' articles can help you become rich?"

**Quick Win**: Spend 30 minutes daily monitoring trending topics in your chosen niche and create content that addresses current questions or problems people are discussing online.

### Master Attention-Grabbing Headlines
Compelling headlines are crucial for attracting readers in a crowded digital landscape. Meyers provides specific headline formulas that trigger curiosity and drive traffic, including patterns like "5 Warning Signs That [Blank]" and "The Shocking Truth About [Blank]." These proven templates help your content stand out on social media platforms and search results.

> "Before you begin writing an article for your blog, create a headline title that makes readers curious. When someone visits your blog, and reads your articles, it's because they want to know more about what you have to say."

**Quick Take**: Use headline formulas that create urgency, curiosity, or fear of missing out to dramatically increase your article's click-through rates.

### Build Multiple Income Streams
Relying on a single revenue source is risky in the digital economy. Meyers outlines various monetization strategies including affiliate marketing, sponsored content, advertising revenue, and creating digital products. The most lucrative approach he discovered was repurposing blog content into eBooks and selling them on Amazon, which provides access to millions of potential buyers.

> "I write because I want to leave a gleam in my readers' eyes, and help them to feel confident about making real money online. Helping people to succeed in life is a rewarding feeling and frankly, I don't think anyone should have to live in poverty."

**Quick Win**: Start with one monetization method (like affiliate marketing) and gradually add others as your audience grows, with eBook creation as a priority for scaling income.

### Leverage Amazon KDP for Maximum Reach
Amazon's Kindle Direct Publishing platform offers unprecedented access to millions of daily eBook buyers. Meyers reveals that Amazon's US store alone averages over 1 million paid downloads daily, providing a massive marketplace for self-published authors. He recommends pricing eBooks between $2.99-$5.99 for optimal profit margins while keeping 99-cent promotions for customer acquisition.

> "I was amazed after reading that during mid-January 2016, an average 1,064,000 paid downloads were made daily from Amazon's US eBook sales. Wow! ... And I wanted to be a part of this exciting eBook selling industry."

**Quick Take**: Transform your most popular blog posts into a cohesive eBook format, publish on Amazon KDP, and use free promotional periods to build initial momentum and reviews.

## Action Items
- Set up a dedicated email account and PayPal system for your blogging business
- Research trending topics in your niche using Google Trends and social media platforms
- Create compelling headlines using proven formulas that trigger curiosity
- Develop a content strategy focused on solving specific problems for your target audience
- Start building an email list from day one to nurture relationships with readers
- Repurpose your best blog content into an eBook format for Amazon KDP publishing
- Implement affiliate marketing and advertising revenue streams alongside your content
- Use free promotional strategies like press releases and social media marketing

## Questions to Consider

- What multiple income streams can I develop to create financial stability?
- How will I stay motivated during the initial building phase when income is minimal?
- What systems can I put in place to scale my content creation and distribution?
- How can I build a loyal audience that will support my paid products and services?

## Conclusion
This book is worth your time if you're looking for realistic, battle-tested strategies for monetizing online content. Meyers' honest approach about both the challenges and opportunities provides a refreshing alternative to get-rich-quick schemes. The biggest reason to read it is the practical roadmap it provides for transitioning from traditional employment to online entrepreneurship through blogging and digital publishing.

While the "less than 30-days" promise may be optimistic for complete beginners, the strategies outlined are sound and can generate income relatively quickly when implemented consistently. The book's greatest value lies in its emphasis on building sustainable multiple income streams rather than relying on a single source of online revenue.
