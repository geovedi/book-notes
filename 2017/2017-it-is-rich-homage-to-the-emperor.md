# It Is Rich: Homage to the Emperor
**Original Title**: 't Is Rik: Hommage aan de Keizer
**Authors**: Thijs Delrue, Bart Lamers | **Year**: 2017
**Category**: History & Biography

## Summary
This Dutch-language book is a heartfelt tribute to Rik Van Looy, the legendary Belgian cyclist known as "The Emperor of Herentals." Published in 2017, the book captures the enduring legacy of one of cycling's greatest champions through personal anecdotes, historical accounts, and cultural reflections. Van Looy, who dominated professional cycling in the 1950s and 1960s, remains an iconic figure in Belgian sports history and the collective memory of his hometown.

The book explores how Van Looy's influence extends far beyond his impressive palmarés - including being the only cyclist to win all five Monument classics plus Paris-Tours. It delves into his personal character, his relationship with his wife Nini, and the special connection he maintained with his hometown throughout his career and retirement.

This book will appeal to cycling enthusiasts, sports history buffs, and anyone interested in Belgian culture. Readers will gain insight into what made Van Looy not just a champion athlete, but a beloved cultural figure whose presence still resonates in Herentals decades after his retirement.

## Core Insights

### The Emperor's Enduring Legacy
Rik Van Looy's impact extends far beyond his racing statistics. The book reveals how every resident of Herentals carries a piece of "The Emperor" in their collective memory. His presence transformed the city's identity, making it synonymous with cycling greatness. Local children grew up hearing stories about him, knowing where he lived, where his training stable was located, and following his achievements as if they were personal family victories. This deep connection between athlete and hometown created a unique cultural phenomenon that persists today.

> "Every boomer from Herentals carries a piece of Rik Van Looy within them. Whether we saw him race or not, we know he lived in café Ford, on the Molenvest and in the Lantaarnpad, we know where his riding school was."
> _Original: "Elke boomer uit Herentals draagt een stukje Rik Van Looy in zich. Of we hem nog zien koersen hebben of niet, we weten dat hij in café Ford, op de Molenvest en in het Lantaarnpad woonde, we weten waar zijn manege was."_
**Quick Take**: True sports legacy is measured not just in victories, but in the cultural impact and community connection that persists generations later.

### The Star Quality Beyond Sports
Unlike many modern cyclists who lack personality, Van Looy possessed a charisma that transcended sports. The book highlights how his sharp features, impeccable short haircut, and eagle-like gaze allowed him to stand alongside entertainment industry stars of his era. His wife Nini added to this star quality, making them a power couple that captured public imagination beyond cycling circles.

> "The sharp head, the flawless short haircut and the eagle gaze of Rik are not out of place among the shiny chouchous of show business. This says something about the 'star quality' of the Emperor of Herentals."
> _Original: "De scherpe kop, het vlekkeloze korte kapsel en de arendsblik van Rik misstaan niet tussen de glimmende chouchous van de showbizz. Het zegt iets over de 'star quality' van de Keizer van Herentals."_
**Quick Take**: Athletic greatness combined with personal charisma creates a lasting cultural impact that transcends the sport itself.

### Humble Despite Greatness
Despite his legendary status, Van Looy remained remarkably grounded and approachable. The book is filled with anecdotes about chance encounters in local shops where he demonstrated genuine kindness and ordinariness. When the city commissioned a statue in his honor, he insisted it reflect his humble, popular character rather than glorifying his achievements. This authenticity endeared him to fans and created a deeper connection with his community.

> "Rik never sought attention. His athletic build, his feats on the bicycle and his fabulous palmarès spoke for themselves. No show was needed."
> _Original: "Nooir zocht en zoekt Rik de aandacht op. Zijn atletisch postuur, zijn krachttoeren op de fiets en zijn fabelachtig palmares spraken voldoende. Daar hoeft geen show bij."_
**Quick Take**: True greatness doesn't require self-promotion; authentic character speaks louder than any marketing campaign.

### The Living Memory of Sporting Glory
The book captures how memories of Van Looy's achievements are kept alive through oral tradition and personal recollections. Younger generations may not have seen him race, but they know the essential facts of his career and significance to their city. This living memory demonstrates how sporting heroes can become part of a community's DNA, passed down through stories rather than just statistics.

> "It is readily available knowledge, from the tradition. It belongs to the Herentals collective memory, like the memory of the legendary nightlife of the Nieuwstraat."
> _Original: "Het is parate kennis, vanuit de overlevering. Het hoort bij het Herentalse collectieve geheugen, zoals pakweg de herinnering aan het legendarische uitgaansleven van de Nieuwstraat."_
**Quick Take**: Cultural memory thrives through storytelling and personal connections, making sporting achievements part of community identity.

## Conclusion

This charming tribute to Rik Van Looy captures the essence of what makes certain athletes transcend their sport to become cultural icons. The book reminds us that true sporting greatness isn't measured solely in victories and records, but in the lasting impact on communities and the authenticity of character that resonates across generations.

For cycling fans and sports history enthusiasts, this book offers a delightful glimpse into a bygone era of cycling when riders possessed personality and mystique. For general readers, it's a touching exploration of how one man's achievements became woven into the fabric of his hometown's identity.

The most compelling reason to read this book is its celebration of authentic greatness in an age of manufactured celebrity. Van Looy's story demonstrates that lasting legacy comes not from self-promotion or social media following, but from genuine character, community connection, and the kind of excellence that needs no introduction. It's a timely reminder that in sports and in life, true emperorship comes from serving others while staying true to oneself.
