# Day Trading: Strategies to Make Money with Day Trading
**Authors**: Warren Richmond | **Year**: 2017

## Summary
This book is a comprehensive beginner's guide to day trading strategies, designed for individuals who want to earn money from short-term stock market trading. Written in accessible language, it demystifies the technical aspects of day trading while providing practical frameworks for implementing various trading strategies. The book covers everything from basic concepts to specific strategies like momentum trading, breakout trading, and news trading, along with common mistakes to avoid.

This book is perfect for investing enthusiasts looking to diversify into active trading, beginners intimidated by trading jargon, and anyone seeking supplementary income through day trading. Readers will gain actionable strategies, risk management techniques, and the confidence to develop their own trading plan.

## Core Strategies

### Momentum Trading Strategy
Momentum trading capitalizes on stocks showing strong directional movement during trading hours. Traders buy stocks at their lowest point and sell at their peak within the same day, leveraging short-term price volatility for profit.

The strategy focuses on high-volume, high-volatility stocks with significant price ranges. Success depends on identifying stocks with sufficient momentum - typically those breaking through 90-day highs with strong volume confirmation. Technical analysis plays a crucial role, as momentum traders ignore company fundamentals and focus solely on price action and chart patterns.

> "In the world of momentum trading, the stock is all you need to worry about. Every stock chart has a different story to tell."

**Quick Take**: Look for stocks with volume increases and volatility. Entry should be confirmed with price history observation, and always have an exit strategy before entering any position.

### Breakout Trading Strategy
This strategy capitalizes on sudden price movements when stocks break through established support/resistance levels with increased volume and volatility. The optimal trading window is typically one hour after market opening and one hour before closing - avoiding lunch hours when market activity typically slows.

Successful breakout traders avoid cheap stocks under $5, focus on stocks with minimum $2 daily price ranges, and never trade stocks down more than 5% from the previous day's close. The strategy requires quick decision-making and precise timing.

**Quick Take**: Breakout trading thrives on high volatility. Define your entry and exit points clearly before trading, and stick to the optimal trading hours for best results.

### Pullback Trading Strategy
A pullback is a temporary price reversal against the prevailing trend, offering strategic entry opportunities at lower risk levels. This strategy works best after a stock has made a strong breakout and continues trending upward, allowing traders to enter positions at more favorable prices without chasing the stock.

The approach relies on understanding supply and demand dynamics - as prices rise, demand naturally decreases until reaching acceptable levels for buyers. Pullbacks are normal market movements that signal healthy market conditions and present growth opportunities.

> "Pullbacks are quite normal, and they signify that the market is healthy."

**Quick Take**: Wait for confirmation of trend continuation, ensure volume support at the pullback level, and set precise stop-loss limits to minimize downside risk.

### Trend Trading Strategy
Trend trading involves capturing profits by analyzing the momentum of assets moving in consistent directions. Traders take long positions in uptrends and short positions in downtrends, assuming the current direction will continue. The strategy works across all timeframes but requires trend confirmation before entry.

The approach emphasizes going with market flow rather than against it, using price charts rather than news for decision-making. Successful trend traders buy dips in uptrends and sell rallies in downtrends, always maintaining clear exit protocols.

**Quick Take**: Trust price action over news or opinions. Determine the prevailing trend direction first, then align your positions accordingly. Always have predefined exit points to lock in gains or limit losses.

### News Trading Strategy
This strategy capitalizes on market volatility triggered by news events, categorized as either periodic (scheduled announcements like earnings reports) or unexpected (natural disasters, political events). News traders use options with short expiration periods (less than two weeks) to maximize returns from price movements.

Success requires being ahead of news releases, understanding the "buy the rumor, sell the news" principle, and maintaining discipline through pre-planned strategies. The strategy excels in forex markets where currency pairs respond dramatically to economic announcements.

**Quick Take**: Master the timing of economic releases, use options instead of direct stock purchases for leverage, and never let market sentiments override your pre-planned strategy.

## Risk Management Framework

### Common Trading Mistakes to Avoid
- **Overtrading**: Beginners should limit themselves to 1-2 trades per day to maximize learning and minimize losses
- **Ignoring stop-loss**: Always set predetermined exit points to control maximum losses
- **Emotional trading**: Never let fear, greed, or hope drive decisions - stick to your trading plan
- **Insufficient research**: Never skip homework on market conditions, seasonal trends, and pre-release events
- **Overconfidence**: Beginner's luck can lead to disastrous risk-taking - maintain disciplined approach

### Capital Protection Rules
- Never invest your entire capital at once - always maintain backup funds
- Risk no more than 0.5% of your account on single trades, 5% maximum daily
- Start with small positions and gradually increase as experience builds
- Focus on minimizing losses rather than maximizing profits initially

## Action Items

- **Create your trading plan**: Define your motivation, risk tolerance, market conditions focus, working hours, and specific strategies
- **Master one strategy first**: Don't jump between strategies - become proficient in one before exploring others
- **Set up proper tools**: Invest in a reliable trading computer, high-speed internet, and access to real-time market data
- **Practice with paper trading**: Test strategies without real money until consistent profitability is demonstrated
- **Establish daily routines**: Set specific trading hours, pre-market research time, and post-market review sessions

## Questions to Consider

- What is your primary motivation for day trading, and how does it influence your risk tolerance?
- Which trading strategy best matches your personality type and time availability?
- How will you maintain emotional discipline when facing significant losses or gains?
- What is your maximum acceptable loss per day, and how will you enforce this limit?
- Are you prepared to dedicate sufficient time for continuous learning and market research?
