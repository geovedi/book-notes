# Gloaming
**Original Title**: Gloaming
**Authors**: Charlotte E. English | **Year**: 2017
**Category**: Fantasy & Science Fiction

## Summary
In the enchanted valley of Argantel, a mysterious phenomenon called the Gloaming descends daily, bringing twilight and magic to the crumbling estate of Landricourt. When winemaker Oriane Travere discovers a magical mirror in the cellar, she's transported to a parallel version of Landricourt—a vibrant, thriving version from the past inhabited by otherworldly beings known as the Fae. Her disappearance sets in motion a chain of events that will transform the lives of everyone connected to the estate, revealing deep connections between the human world and the magical realm of the Fae.

This enchanting fantasy weaves together themes of transformation, belonging, and the delicate balance between worlds. As the mystery of Oriane's disappearance unfolds through multiple perspectives, readers discover that Landricourt is more than just a winemaking estate—it's a bridge between human and Fae realms, where roses hold starlight, wine contains magic, and time flows differently in different places.

The story appeals to fantasy lovers who appreciate atmospheric world-building, complex magical systems, and character-driven narratives that explore themes of identity, sacrifice, and the power of connection between worlds.

## Core Insights

### The Magic of the Gloaming
The daily Gloaming is more than just atmospheric twilight—it's a magical phenomenon that connects the human world with the Fae realm. This magical twilight affects time, space, and reality itself at Landricourt, creating a permeable boundary between worlds. The silver-pale roses that only grow at Landricourt are manifestations of this magic, containing starlight and serving as ingredients for magical wine that bridges the gap between mortal and immortal realms.

> "The sun dimmed and faded, lingering only as a faint, muted presence upon the far horizon. Shadows crept out of the corners and danced across the dining-room, and what light remained turned as silvery as the stars."

**Quick Take**: The Gloaming represents the liminal space between ordinary reality and magical possibility, where transformation becomes possible and the veil between worlds grows thin.

### Parallel Worlds and Mirrors
The story's central mechanism involves travel between parallel versions of Landricourt through magical mirrors. These portals connect the ruined, mortal version of the estate with its thriving Fae counterpart, allowing passage between worlds for those with the right sensitivity or bloodline. The mirrors serve as both literal and metaphorical gateways, representing how small choices can lead to dramatically different realities.

> "Where was she? This was not her Landricourt. She was come, somehow, to a vision of the past—the past it must be, by some means beyond her comprehension."

**Quick Take**: Our lives exist in parallel with countless possible realities, and sometimes the boundaries between them are more permeable than we imagine.

### Transformation and Identity
Throughout the narrative, characters undergo profound transformations that reflect their true nature. By the story's end, Margot becomes Autumn, Florian transforms into Summer, Sylvaine becomes Storms, and Oriane embodies Snow. These changes aren't merely magical but represent the characters embracing their essential identities and finding their true place in the magical hierarchy.

> "She felt different. She felt, oddly, as though she had no body, though she could see perfectly well that she did. She was too light, her limbs too ethereal; she thought she could float right off the ground if she wanted to."

**Quick Take**: True transformation comes from embracing our authentic nature rather than trying to fit into predetermined roles or expectations.

### The Power of Place and Memory
Landricourt itself is a character in the story, holding memories of its past glory and serving as a vessel for magical energy. The estate's ruined condition in the human world versus its thriving state in the Fae realm demonstrates how places hold multiple realities simultaneously. The traditions of winemaking and rose harvesting connect the present to ancient magic and wisdom.

> "It pleased her, when she walked there alone, to cast her mind back and back, through years of lost time, to the days when Landricourt was new."

**Quick Take**: Physical places can hold multiple layers of reality and memory, serving as bridges between different states of being and consciousness.

### Connection Between Human and Fae Realms
The story reveals deep connections between the human characters and their Fae counterparts, suggesting that the boundary between human and magical beings is more fluid than it appears. Characters like Oriane and Walkelin exist in both realms, and the relationships between humans and Fae reflect fundamental connections that transcend world boundaries.

> "I am still myself. Only a bit more… magical."

**Quick Take**: The distinction between mundane and magical is often a matter of perspective rather than fundamental difference—we all contain both aspects within ourselves.

## Conclusion

*Gloaming* is a beautifully crafted fantasy that operates on multiple levels—as an engaging mystery, a coming-of-age story, and a meditation on the nature of reality and transformation. The novel's strength lies in its atmospheric world-building and the way it seamlessly weaves magical elements into the fabric of everyday life.

This book is worth your time if you appreciate fantasy that honors the subtle magic hidden within ordinary experiences and stories that explore how small openings in reality can lead to profound transformation. The biggest reason to read *Gloaming* is its masterful blend of enchanting atmosphere with deep wisdom about identity, connection, and the ways that different realities can coexist and interact within the same physical space.

The novel reminds us that the boundaries between worlds are often more permeable than we think, and that true magic lies in recognizing and honoring the connections that link all aspects of existence—human and Fae, past and present, ordinary and extraordinary.
