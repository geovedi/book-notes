# Billionaires Under Construction
**Authors**: Sbusiso "DJ Sbu" Leope | **Year**: 2017

## Summary
"Billionaires Under Construction" is a practical entrepreneurship guide that frames success as a continuous journey rather than a destination. DJ Sbu Leope shares his personal story of building multiple businesses, including the energy drink brand MoFaya, while overcoming setbacks like being fired from radio stations and facing public criticism. The book presents entrepreneurship as a mindset available to anyone willing to develop resilience, flexibility, and an unshakeable belief in their vision.

This book is perfect for aspiring entrepreneurs, startup founders, and business students who want real-world insights on building businesses from scratch, especially in African markets. Readers will gain practical strategies for overcoming obstacles, building networks, creating authentic brands, and maintaining motivation through inevitable business challenges.

## Core Insights

### You Can't Stop My Go: The Power of Unstoppable Resilience
Entrepreneurship requires developing the resilience to bounce back from inevitable failures and setbacks. DJ Sbu shares how he was fired from radio stations, faced lawsuits, and endured public criticism but refused to let anything stop his momentum. The key is understanding that challenges are not roadblocks but opportunities to build stronger resilience muscles. When you get knocked down, you come back "seven times stronger" as inspired by the late Mandoza's music.

> "You can't stop my go. We just don't give up. Because entrepreneurs just don't. We are resilient."
> _Original: "You can't stop my go. We just don't give up. Because entrepreneurs just don't. We are resilient."_
**Quick Take**: Your ability to bounce back from setbacks determines your entrepreneurial success more than your initial idea or resources.

### The Flexibility Principle: Bend But Don't Break
Successful entrepreneurs must remain flexible in their approach while staying committed to their ultimate vision. When one path doesn't work, they forge alternative routes to reach the same destination. DJ Sbu demonstrated this when he turned his firing from Metro FM into an opportunity to create the syndicated DJ Sbu Breakfast show, which reached more audiences than his previous platform.

> "If you're not reaching your business goal, don't change that goal. Rather, find a different way of reaching it."
> _Original: "If you're not reaching your business goal, don't change that goal. Rather, find a different way of reaching it."_
**Quick Take**: Have a stubborn vision but flexible strategies—your destination remains fixed while your route can adapt.

### Your Network is Your Net Worth: Strategic Relationship Building
Entrepreneurs cannot succeed in isolation. Building and maintaining a strong network of skilled, passionate people is essential for growth. DJ Sbu emphasizes finding people smarter than yourself and offering value through sweat equity or shared vision when capital is limited. He built his database by genuinely connecting with people in unexpected places, from VIP rooms at clubs to radio interviews.

> "If you hang out at the barber shop long enough, you'll get a haircut. In the same way, if you choose people who are inspiring, passionate and full of greatness to populate your world, you'll soon start thinking in the same way."
> _Original: "If you hang out at the barber shop long enough, you'll get a haircut. In the same way, if you choose people who are inspiring, passionate and full of greatness to populate your world, you'll soon start thinking in the same way."_
**Quick Take**: Surround yourself with people who elevate your thinking and have skills you lack—relationships are your greatest business asset.

### Brand Building is a Marathon, Not a Sprint
Creating an authentic brand takes time, consistency, and patience. DJ Sbu and his partner invested R6 million of their own money into MoFaya and faced numerous challenges, including false media claims about product safety. Yet they remained committed to their vision of creating "the Coca-Cola of Africa." True brand building requires staying power and the ability to weather storms while maintaining consistency in your message and values.

> "Every can of MoFaya represents possibility. It represents black pride, of the fact that we are ready to challenge the status quo."
> _Original: "Every can of MoFaya represents possibility. It represents black pride, of the fact that we are ready to challenge the status quo."_
**Quick Take**: Your brand is built through consistent actions over time, not overnight marketing campaigns or quick wins.

### Think Like a Billionaire, Act Like a Startup
Maintain an abundance mindset while practicing lean startup principles. DJ Sbu introduces the "billionaire generation" concept—the belief that anyone can achieve massive success through the right mindset and work ethic. However, this vision must be paired with practical habits like getting up early, dressing for success, listening more than speaking, and stepping into opportunity despite fear.

> "We are the billionaire generation. We are whom we have been waiting for. Our time is now. It's Africa's time."
> _Original: "We are the billionaire generation. We are whom we have been waiting for. Our time is now. It's Africa's time."_
**Quick Take**: Think big about possibilities while staying grounded in daily disciplines that build momentum.

## Action Items

- **Build your resilience muscle**: View every setback as a learning opportunity and come back stronger each time
- **Start your network database today**: Collect contact information from everyone you meet and nurture genuine relationships
- **Practice flexibility daily**: When faced with obstacles, brainstorm alternative routes to your goals
- **Invest in your brand consistency**: Show up authentically every day, even when results aren't immediate
- **Adopt the Sbu Leope Scale**: Get up early, believe anything is possible, dress for success, listen more than you speak, and overcome fear

## Questions to Consider

- What is your "why" that will keep you going when entrepreneurship gets difficult?
- Are you surrounding yourself with people who elevate your thinking and challenge you to grow?
- How can you reframe current setbacks as opportunities for growth and new directions?
- What daily habits will build the resilience needed for long-term entrepreneurial success?
- Are you thinking big enough about your vision while staying practical about your daily actions?

## Conclusion
"Billionaires Under Construction" is worth your time if you're looking for authentic, tested entrepreneurship wisdom from someone who has actually built successful businesses from nothing. DJ Sbu's greatest strength is his raw honesty about failures and setbacks, making his successes more credible and relatable. The book's power lies in its practical, no-nonsense approach combined with genuine belief that anyone can succeed with the right mindset and work ethic.

The biggest reason to read this book is its message of hope combined with practical application. DJ Sbu doesn't just inspire—he provides actionable strategies and mindset shifts that readers can implement immediately. His story proves that entrepreneurship isn't about avoiding failures but about building the resilience to keep going despite them, making this guide particularly valuable for African entrepreneurs facing unique market challenges.

**Best For**: Aspiring entrepreneurs, startup founders, business students, anyone interested in African business success stories
