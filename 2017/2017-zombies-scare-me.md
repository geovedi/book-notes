# Zombies Scare Me
**Original Title**: Zombies Scare Me 100 (Nederlandse Editie)
**Authors**: I. D. Oro | **Year**: 2017
**Category**: Horror & Thriller

## Summary
"Zombies Scare Me 100" is a compilation book that serves as the foundation for an extensive zombie apocalypse series set in a parallel universe. Rather than following a single protagonist, this collection introduces multiple characters whose individual stories will unfold across books 101-109. Set during the week leading up to Halloween 2015 (October 24-31), the book provides crucial background stories for the main cast before they face the zombie outbreak together.

The narrative alternates between different characters' perspectives, including Diana, a 17-year-old baseball player living with her grandparents; José "Axe," an inmate in Leavenworth Prison; and John "Axe Murderer," a veteran suffering from PTSD. Each character's story explores themes of survival, preparation, and the psychological impact of facing an impending apocalypse.

This book appeals to zombie apocalypse enthusiasts who enjoy character-driven narratives and want to understand the personal histories and motivations of survivors before the outbreak begins. Readers will gain insight into how ordinary people from different backgrounds become zombie apocalypse survivors.

## Core Insights

### The Power of Preparation and Paranoia
Diana's grandfather Mario represents the preparedness mindset that becomes crucial during zombie outbreaks. Despite family resistance, he insists on spending their savings on food supplies and home fortification rather than weapons or vacations.

> "We can't have guns during a zombie apocalypse because we might accidentally kill someone who isn't a zombie."
> _Original: "We mogen geen wapens hebben tijdens een zombie-apocalyps omdat we iemand per ongeluk kunnen doden die geen zombie is."_

**Quick Take**: True survival preparation focuses on sustainable resources (food, water, shelter) rather than weapons, especially when family members have differing views on self-defense.

### The Prison System's Unexpected Advantage
José "Axe" discovers that prison might be one of the safest places during a zombie outbreak due to the secure facilities and isolation from the general population.

> "Run pendejo they're zombies!"
> _Original: "Run pendejo they're zombies!"_

**Quick Take**: Sometimes the systems designed to punish and contain can become unexpected sanctuaries during societal collapse, challenging our assumptions about safety and security.

### PTSD as Survival Training
John "Axe Murderer" finds that his military trauma and combat experience have prepared him for zombie survival in ways that ordinary civilians lack. His conditioned responses to violence become assets rather than liabilities.

> "The only good terrorist is a dead terrorist."
> _Original: "De enige goede terrorist is een dode terrorist."_

**Quick Take**: Past trauma can reframe as survival skills, though the psychological costs remain devastating.

### The Burden of Knowledge
Characters who recognize the zombie threat early face the difficult task of convincing skeptical family and friends while preparing for an apocalypse that others dismiss as fantasy.

> "Everyone will rise from the dead during the apocalypse! It's in the Bible there in the last chapters titled Revelation that everyone can rise from their graves after they have died and live again."
> _Original: "Iedereen zal ook opstaan uit de dood tijdens de apocalyps! Het staat in de bijbel daar in de laatste hoofdstukken met de titel Openbaring dat iedereen uit hun graven kan opstaan nadat ze zijn gestorven en opnieuw leven."_

**Quick Take**: Early knowledge of existential threats creates isolation and the burden of trying to save those who don't want to be saved.

### Parallel Universe Freedom
The book's setting in a parallel universe allows for alternate historical outcomes and rules that differ from our reality, giving the author creative freedom to explore zombie scenarios without being constrained by real-world limitations.

> "This book is set in a parallel universe."
> _Original: "Dit boek staat in een parallel universum."_

**Quick Take**: Creative frameworks can liberate storytelling from real-world constraints, allowing for deeper exploration of what-if scenarios.

## Action Items

- **Stockpile sustainable resources**: Focus on long-term food storage and water purification over weapons when preparing for emergencies
- **Assess your environment strategically**: Consider how existing infrastructure (prisons, schools, churches) might serve as survival locations
- **Build alliances before crisis**: Establish trusted relationships with people who have complementary skills for survival scenarios
- **Mental preparation matters**: Address and manage psychological trauma that could either help or hinder survival responses
- **Question everything**: Challenge conventional wisdom about what constitutes safe spaces and survival strategies

## Questions to Consider

- How would past traumas or specialized training affect your ability to survive a zombie apocalypse?
- Would you rather be in a secure prison or outside with freedom during a zombie outbreak?
- How do you convince skeptical loved ones to prepare for an apocalypse they don't believe in?
- What ordinary skills from your current life might become valuable survival assets?
- How does the parallel universe concept allow for more creative exploration of zombie scenarios?

## Conclusion

"Zombies Scare Me 100" is worth your time if you enjoy character-driven zombie narratives that prioritize psychological depth over constant action. The book's greatest strength is its exploration of how different personalities, backgrounds, and traumas prepare people for apocalyptic scenarios in unexpected ways.

The biggest reason to read this book is its unique approach as a prequel compilation – it's essentially a "getting to know your survivors" piece that enriches the subsequent series. By understanding these characters before the outbreak, readers gain insight into the human element of zombie survival rather than just the mechanics of killing the undead.

While the compilation format can feel disjointed at times, the diverse character perspectives create a rich tapestry of human responses to existential threats. The parallel universe setting provides creative freedom that allows for fresh takes on familiar zombie tropes. This book serves as essential background for readers invested in the larger series but also stands as a thoughtful examination of how ordinary people become extraordinary survivors.
