# Sales Digits: Guaranteed Methods to Turn Prospects into Customers
**Authors**: Eugy Enoch | **Year**: 2017

## Summary
"Sales Digits" is a practical marketing playbook that reveals seven proprietary strategies for converting prospects into lifelong customers. Based on the author's decade of marketing struggles and breakthroughs, this book provides actionable frameworks for both online and offline marketing success. The core premise is that effective marketing requires exposing problems first, then presenting solutions with strategic timing, credibility building, and relationship management.

Sales professionals, marketers, and business owners will benefit from these proven techniques to create immediate cash flow, build customer trust, and develop sustainable marketing systems. The book offers step-by-step methods for crafting compelling messages that resonate with prospects and drive consistent sales.

## Core Strategies: A Practical Playbook

### 1. Reveal/Rhema Marketing Style: Problem-First Approach
**The Foundation**: Expose your prospect's problem before presenting your solution. This method creates immediate attention by shining "light on darkness."

**How to Apply**:
- Start by identifying the core problem your target audience faces
- Use pattern: "Are you tired of [problem]? Let me show you [solution]"
- Generalize problems when possible (e.g., "financial mess" instead of listing specific debt types)
- Create urgency by highlighting consequences of inaction

**Example Template**: "I need your attention: if you have suffered from [problem], then you're about to see for yourself [solution]. Skip this and you skip a shockingly simple [benefit]."

> "Almost no one feels comfortable with that but that is one way that is sure going to get you the attention you need."

**Quick Win**: Practice problem-first messaging in your next sales pitch or email campaign.

### 2. Advanced Reveal Marketing: Fact-Based Enhancement
**The Method**: Layer your problem-solution framework with compelling facts and data to establish credibility and educate customers.

**Implementation Steps**:
- Research relevant statistics about the problem you're solving
- Position yourself as a knowledgeable expert who understands the issue deeply
- Use facts to demonstrate the severity of the problem and effectiveness of your solution

**Example**: "Children need physical activity to develop strong physically and socially, but during this time they're prone to accidents that could threaten their social life for years to come. That's why they depend on you to provide [your product]."

**Quick Win**: Add 2-3 powerful facts to your current marketing copy this week.

### 3. Time-Based Reveal Marketing: The Urgency Engine
**The Strategy**: Use time elements to spur immediate action while building long-term prospect relationships.

**Key Approaches**:
- **Future Projection**: Show consequences of delayed action
- **Deadline Pressure**: Create limited-time opportunities
- **Time-Based Storytelling**: Share your journey over years

**Templates**:
- "Your child may be fine now, but 10-15 years down the line, the story may change unless you act now..."
- "You have [time period] to take advantage of [offer] before the year runs out"
- "How I built wealth after [number] years of trial and error..."

> "Time solves everything. In fact it is one of the greatest teachers in human history."

**Quick Win**: Add a time element to your current marketing campaign within 24 hours.

### 4. Providence Marketing: The Story-Selling Framework
**The Concept**: Focus on the producer's journey, vision, and personal connection to the solution to build emotional trust.

**Implementation Strategy**:
- Share the producer's personal story of struggle and discovery
- Explain what motivated the creation of the product/service
- Detail the cost and pain of developing the solution
- Show how the solution transformed the producer's life first

**Story Arc Template**: "The producer experienced [specific problem] for [duration]. During this time, they discovered/created [solution]. After experiencing [transformation], they decided to help others facing similar [problem]."

**Quick Win**: Create a founder story for your business or product within the next week.

### 5. Surveyor Marketing Approach: Catalog & Build Benefits
**The Method**: Use a two-pronged approach to showcase product offerings and collective benefits.

**Components**:
- **Cataloguing**: List all available products/services briefly
- **Benefits Building**: Explain the collective benefits of your entire catalog

**Implementation**:
- Create concise product catalogs using bullets or asterisks
- Focus on how the entire package benefits customer welfare/health/finances
- Include relevant features, ingredients, or aspects that make products effective
- Use testimonials to support benefit claims

**Example**: "Let me take you through our proven list that will [improve specific area], then show how all these products work together to help you [achieve desired outcome]."

**Quick Win**: Create a one-page catalog of all your offerings with collective benefits.

### 6. Quality Market Approach: Professional Positioning
**The Strategy**: Position yourself as a professional authority and executive in your field to build instant credibility.

**Two Approaches**:
- **PRO Quality Marketing**: Create comprehensive information about your solutions, including ingredients, benefits, and research
- **Quality EX Marketing**: Position yourself as an executive with insider access to producers/owners

**Implementation Tactics**:
- Mention meetings with company founders or executives
- Reference insider communications with producers
- Display company materials or certifications
- Provide detailed product information that demonstrates expertise

**Quick Win**: Update your professional bio to include any executive connections or insider knowledge.

### 7. Recruitment Marketing: Network Expansion
**The Framework**: Structure your marketing to include recruitment opportunities for expanding your sales network.

**Two Classes**:
- **Kind Benefits**: Focus on material rewards (cars, travel, homes)
- **Money Benefits**: Emphasize financial compensation and payment structures

**Implementation Strategy**:
- Present material benefits first, then financial benefits (or vice versa)
- Use charisma and dramatic presentation
- Announce recruitment opportunities after presenting benefits
- Provide immediate next steps for new recruits

**Quick Win**: Create a recruitment presentation based on your current product/service benefits.
