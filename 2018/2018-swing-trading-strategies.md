# 2018 Swing Trading Strategies
**Original Title**: Swing Trading Strategies: Learn How to Profit Fast With These 4 Simple Strategies
**Authors**: Victor Lucas | **Year**: 2018

## Summary
Swing trading occupies the sweet spot between high-stress day trading and long-term trend trading, holding positions from several days to a few weeks. This practical guide reveals that successful swing trading is less about complex algorithms and more about mastering trader psychology, market timing, and four core strategies that help you ride market waves profitably.

This book is perfect for beginners who want to learn trading without the intensity of day trading or the patience required for trend following. You'll learn how to develop the trader's mindset, identify profitable sectors, use technical analysis effectively, and execute four proven strategies that work across stocks, currencies, and even crypto markets.

## Core Strategies

### The Trader's Mindset: Your Psychological Foundation
Before any strategy works, you must develop emotional discipline and mechanical decision-making. The book emphasizes that successful traders view losses as learning opportunities rather than failures. They remain detached from emotional involvement, make decisions quickly based on data rather than feelings, and maintain both mental and physical health to support clear thinking.

> "The mind of the trader must be able to handle stress as well. It is difficult to watch the rise and fall of the market and realize that you missed your window to get in or out at the right time."

**Quick Take**: Your greatest trading advantage isn't technical knowledge—it's emotional control and the ability to learn from every trade, win or lose.

### Sector Rotation Strategy: Ride the Economic Waves
This powerful strategy involves moving your money between industry sectors based on economic cycles. The book breaks down how different sectors perform during various economic phases: technology and industrial stocks shine during recessions, while energy and basic materials lead in early recovery phases. Your goal is to identify which economic cycle we're entering and position yourself in sectors that historically outperform during those periods.

> "The general gist of sector rotation is being able to move your money from one industry to the next in an attempt to glean the most out of the market at any given time."

**Quick Take**: Instead of trying to time individual stocks, time entire sectors by understanding how economic cycles affect different industries.

### The 4-Hour Chart Strategy: Find Your Sweet Spot
This technical approach uses 34 and 55-period moving averages (Fibonacci numbers) to identify trends on 4-hour charts. When prices stay above both moving averages with the 34 above the 55, you're in an uptrend. The strategy involves waiting for pullbacks to the area between moving averages, then entering positions in the direction of the dominant trend. This timeframe gives you enough data for informed decisions while still capturing swing trading opportunities.

> "With the 4-hour chart as your base, you can screen for potential areas where you might find trading signals. Your main goal here is to identify either an uptrend or a downturn and then follow its unique behavior."

**Quick Take**: The 4-hour timeframe provides the perfect balance between trend clarity and swing trading opportunity frequency.

### Trading Fakeouts: Turn Traps into Profits
Experienced traders know that not all breakouts are real. This advanced strategy teaches you to identify false breakouts (fakeouts) where price briefly moves outside support/resistance levels before reversing. Instead of being trapped, you wait for the fakeout to complete, then enter positions in the opposite direction with tight stops. The key is recognizing patterns like false flags, triangles, and channels where naive traders get trapped.

> "Rather than accept a loss as newbies often do, by applying a few basic rules you can turn that potential loss into a possibility for profit."

**Quick Take**: When everyone else is getting trapped by false breakouts, you can profit by trading in the opposite direction once the trap is sprung.

### Momentum Trading: Ride the Acceleration
This strategy focuses on stocks experiencing rapid price acceleration with high volume. Momentum traders look for stocks breaking through resistance levels, heavily shorted stocks (for short squeezes), and stocks reacting strongly to news. The key is intense concentration and discipline—entering quickly when momentum appears and exiting just as fast when it shows signs of slowing.

> "Momentum traders look for acceleration at a stock's price before they enter the market. Once they have identified the momentum, they will take their entry positions with the expectation that the momentum is going to continue to follow that trend."

**Quick Take**: Success in momentum trading comes from speed and discipline—identifying accelerating stocks and riding them until the momentum fades.

## Action Items
- **Master your emotions first**: Practice making decisions based solely on data and technical analysis, never fear or greed
- **Start with sector rotation**: Learn to identify economic cycles and position yourself in sectors that historically outperform
- **Master the 4-hour chart**: Use 34 and 55-period moving averages to identify trends and entry points
- **Learn to spot fakeouts**: Study support and resistance patterns to identify false breakouts before entering
- **Build a watchlist**: Focus on 50 maximum stocks initially, trading them one at a time until you understand their personalities
- **Always use stop-losses**: Never enter a trade without knowing exactly where you'll exit if wrong

## Questions to Consider
- What is your current emotional relationship with money, and how might it affect your trading decisions?
- Which economic cycle do you believe we're currently in, and which sectors should benefit?
- Can you commit to the discipline required for momentum trading, or should you focus on sector rotation?
- How will you handle the psychological pressure when your first few trades inevitably go against you?
- What size trading account can you afford to learn with while keeping risk manageable?

## Conclusion
This book delivers exactly what it promises—four practical, actionable strategies for swing trading that work across different markets. The emphasis on trader psychology as the foundation for success is spot-on, as technical skills alone won't save you from emotional decision-making under pressure.

The biggest strength is how Lucas breaks down complex concepts into digestible strategies that beginners can implement immediately. However, the book would benefit from more concrete examples and backtested results for each strategy.

**Is this worth your time?** Absolutely if you're new to trading and want a solid foundation without the overwhelming complexity of advanced technical analysis. The strategies are straightforward enough to implement immediately while providing room for growth as you gain experience. The real value comes from understanding that successful swing trading is 80% psychology and 20% technique—a lesson that will serve you well throughout your trading career.
