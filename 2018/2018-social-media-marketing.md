# Social Media Marketing 2019
**Authors**: Dave Welch | **Year**: 2018 | **Category**: Business & Finance

## Summary
This comprehensive guide reveals how businesses can leverage social media platforms to maximize their exposure and sales in 2019. The book emphasizes that social media is no longer optional for businesses—it's essential for survival and growth. With platforms evolving rapidly, companies that fail to establish a strong online presence risk being forgotten by customers who increasingly expect brands to be accessible, engaging, and responsive on social media.

Perfect for entrepreneurs, small business owners, and marketing professionals who want practical strategies for Facebook, Instagram, YouTube, and Twitter. Readers will learn how to build organic reach, create effective paid campaigns, and develop authentic relationships with their audience that translate into measurable business results.

## Core Insights

### The Social Media Imperative
Social media has fundamentally transformed how customers discover, evaluate, and purchase from businesses. In 2019, having no social media presence is more damaging than having a poor one—customers simply won't find you or trust you. The first question people ask about businesses, even brick-and-mortar ones, is "are you on social media?" Your online presence directly influences sales, recognition, and customer trust.

> "If your customers cannot reach you on social media, they will likely forget about you."

**Quick Take**: Establish at least basic profiles on all major platforms, even if you focus your main efforts on one or two.

### Platform Selection Strategy
Different platforms serve different demographics and purposes. Facebook (1.44+ billion users) is essential for everyone as a foundational presence, with strongest engagement among 25-34 year olds. Instagram dominates with 18-29 year olds and excels for visually-driven products. YouTube works best for educational content and demonstrations, but requires higher production quality. Twitter serves news-conscious audiences aged 18-55 and offers real-time engagement opportunities.

**Quick Take**: Start where your target audience spends time, then expand. Every business needs Facebook presence, but focus primary efforts on platforms where your customers are most active.

### Organic vs. Paid Marketing Balance
Successful social media marketing requires both organic and paid strategies. Organic reach builds authentic relationships and shows platforms that your content is valuable, while paid advertising amplifies your reach to targeted audiences. The Facebook algorithm favors pages with strong organic engagement, making paid ads more effective and cost-efficient.

**Quick Take**: Build organic engagement first through consistent posting and community interaction, then use paid ads to scale your reach.

### Video Content Dominance
Video content is projected to grow massively in 2019 across all platforms. Live videos create real-time engagement and allow direct Q&A with customers. Standard videos should be shot in 4K minimum (1080p absolute lowest) to meet modern quality standards. Video content provides the most authentic way to showcase products and build personal connections with your audience.

**Quick Take**: Invest in basic video equipment and start with simple content. Even smartphone footage can work if properly lit and edited.

### Influencer Marketing Revolution
Mainstream influencers offer businesses a faster route to target audiences than traditional advertising. Influencer partnerships are becoming more affordable and accessible, even for small businesses. This trend allows smaller companies to compete with larger corporations by leveraging influencers' established trust and rapport with specific demographics.

**Quick Take**: Identify micro-influencers in your niche who align with your brand values and negotiate partnership deals that fit your budget.

## Action Items
- **Profile Optimization**: Complete all sections of your social media profiles with professional images, detailed descriptions, and consistent usernames across platforms
- **Content Schedule**: Post 3 times daily on Facebook, mixing content types (status updates, photos, videos, links)
- **Community Building**: Create Facebook Groups to foster deeper engagement beyond your main page
- **Video Investment**: Purchase basic 4K camera equipment and lighting for professional-quality content
- **Analytics Monitoring**: Regularly review platform insights to understand your audience demographics and engagement patterns
- **Influencer Outreach**: Research and contact 5-10 micro-influencers in your industry each month

## Platform-Specific Strategies
- **Facebook**: Focus on building community through Groups, use live video for Q&A sessions, optimize your About section completely
- **Instagram**: Prioritize high-quality visual content, use Stories for behind-the-scenes content, leverage shopping features for e-commerce
- **YouTube**: Create educational content that solves problems for your audience, optimize titles and descriptions for search, maintain consistent upload schedule
- **Twitter**: Engage in real-time conversations, monitor mentions of your brand, share timely content relevant to your industry
