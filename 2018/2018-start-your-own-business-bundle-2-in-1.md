# Start Your Own Business Bundle 2-in-1
**Authors**: Madison Booker, Evan Jones | **Year**: 2018 | **Category**: Business & Finance

## Summary
This comprehensive 2-in-1 guide transforms creative hobbies into profitable online businesses by combining two powerful approaches: selling handmade crafts on platforms like Etsy and building a complete e-commerce store with Shopify. The book demystifies the entire process from product creation to customer service, showing aspiring entrepreneurs how to escape the 9-to-5 grind and turn their creativity into sustainable income.

Perfect for crafters, artists, and aspiring entrepreneurs who want to build a business around their creative passions. You'll learn practical strategies for product selection, pricing, marketing, and customer service that work whether you're making items by hand or dropshipping from suppliers.

## Core Insights

### Find Your Perfect Product Niche
Success starts with choosing the right products to sell. Focus on crafts you've already mastered or can learn quickly—cross-stitch, embroidery, custom painted items, jewelry, and candles are all popular and relatively easy to start with. Consider whether you'll make items custom-to-order or maintain pre-made inventory, as this decision impacts your fulfillment speed and customer satisfaction. Avoid copyrighted materials unless you want to risk having your listings removed or shop shut down.

> "If you wouldn't give it to a friend, don't send it to a customer."
**Quick Take**: Quality matters more than quantity—master one craft before expanding.

### Choose Your Selling Platform Wisely
Etsy remains the best starting point for craft sellers due to its built-in audience and user-friendly setup. The platform guides you through creating listings, setting prices, and connecting payment methods. While having your own website through Shopify creates a more professional image, you'll miss out on Etsy's organic search traffic. Consider using Etsy's Pattern service to get both benefits—a professional standalone site alongside your Etsy marketplace presence.

> "The best option for selling crafts online is Etsy. It's internationally known, easy to use, and it helps new and seasoned artists alike find their niche."
**Quick Take**: Start with Etsy for immediate traffic, then expand to your own site as you grow.

### Master Shipping Before Your First Sale
Don't wait until you have orders to figure out shipping. Visit your post office with a sample package to get accurate quotes for different destinations. Choose the smallest possible packaging that still protects your items—shipping costs can make or break your profitability. Consider using padded envelopes for smaller items instead of regular envelopes to avoid customs issues. Remember to include packaging materials in your shipping costs or you'll lose money on every order.

> "Shipping can get surprisingly expensive and you don't want to be caught off-guard after you get your first orders."
**Quick Take**: Test your entire shipping process with sample packages before listing anything.

### Price for Profit, Not Just Competition
Your pricing must cover material costs, your time (valued at fair market rates), and artistic skill. Start with your material costs—if $100 of bulk materials creates 5 products, each item must cost at least $20 just to break even. Add $20-30+ per hour for your time, plus extra for intricate or difficult pieces. Research competitors' prices but don't undervalue your work—quality customers will pay for quality products.

> "It's tempting to undervalue your art and your time, especially when you're just starting out, but try to really look at this as a job where your time and skills are just as valuable as they would be at a 9-to-5."
**Quick Take**: Calculate your true costs including time, then price for profit rather than just competing.

### Build Your Brand Beyond the Marketplace
Create social media accounts, a blog, or email list to engage with customers directly. Instagram works perfectly for visual crafts, showing behind-the-scenes content and building community. Use Etsy's tags and subsections strategically—exhaust all available tags with descriptive words buyers might search for. Consider paid promotions on Etsy or Google AdWords once you understand what converts, but focus on building organic relationships first.

> "Business isn't just about sales; it's also about creating relationships with people, and building networks."
**Quick Take**: Use social media to show your process and personality, not just finished products.

### Handle Problems Like a Professional
Set clear shop policies before issues arise, but prioritize customer satisfaction. For lost or damaged items, offer replacement or refund based on what the customer prefers. Many sellers offer replacement even when it costs them out-of-pocket because the goodwill generated leads to better reviews and repeat business. Always respond professionally and promptly to customer concerns—your reputation is your most valuable asset.

> "The customer is always right!"
**Quick Take**: Solve problems generously, even when it costs you short-term money.

### Scale Smartly with Shopify
Once you've mastered selling on marketplaces, Shopify offers professional tools for growth. Start with their Basic Plan and upgrade as needed. Use high-quality product photos (2048x2048px minimum) and write unique descriptions that include what the product is, what makes it special, and why customers need it. Consider dropshipping to expand your product range without holding inventory, but vet suppliers carefully since their mistakes become your reputation problems.

> "Shopify is one of the world's premier online ecommerce systems. It's popular for good reason: it's easy to use, the technical support is excellent, it's fast and reliable, it's affordable, and it's highly customizable."
**Quick Take**: Start with a simple Shopify setup and add features as your business grows.

## Action Items

- Test your craft-making process and calculate true costs including materials and time
- Research Etsy shops selling similar products to understand pricing and competition
- Create sample packages and get real shipping quotes before listing items
- Set up social media accounts to document your making process
- Start with Etsy's Basic Plan and list 5-10 quality products with excellent photos
- Create clear shop policies for common issues like lost/damaged items
- Build an email list from day one to capture interested customers

## Questions to Consider

- Which craft do I enjoy most and can produce at consistent quality?
- Who exactly is my ideal customer and where do they spend time online?
- Can I realistically produce items fast enough to meet demand if orders increase?
- What makes my products unique compared to mass-produced alternatives?
- How much time and money am I willing to invest before expecting profit?
- What would happen if my business doubled overnight—could I handle it?

**Worth your time?** Absolutely if you're serious about building a creative business. The book saves months of trial-and-error by revealing insider secrets from successful sellers.
