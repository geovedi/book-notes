# 2018 Cryptocurrency Trading & Investing
**Authors**: Sean Bennett (Cryptomasher) | **Year**: 2018

## Summary

A practical guide to cryptocurrency trading and investing that covers everything from the essential mindset to specific trading strategies and risk management techniques. The book differentiates between long-term investing (buying and holding) and active trading (frequent buying and selling for profit), providing readers with tools for both approaches.

Beginner to intermediate cryptocurrency enthusiasts will benefit most from this book, gaining foundational knowledge about trading psychology, market analysis, exchange platforms, and practical investment strategies. Readers will learn how to approach the volatile crypto market with discipline, avoid common pitfalls, and implement effective risk management techniques.

## Core Insights

### The Trading Mindset
Success in cryptocurrency trading begins with psychological discipline. The market is driven by two primary emotions: greed and fear. Greed leads to rushing into seemingly promising investments without proper research, while fear causes panic selling during normal market fluctuations. The key is maintaining emotional detachment and sticking to fundamental analysis rather than making decisions based on market hype or panic.

> "Cooler heads prevail. Trading cryptocurrency is without a doubt exciting work, but those who maintain discipline and keep their composure are the most successful."

**Quick Take**: Never trade when emotionally charged - whether overexcited by gains or panicked by losses. Take breaks when caught in negative patterns, and always base decisions on research, not emotions.

### Understanding Trading vs. Investing
Trading involves frequent buying and selling to profit from short-term price movements, while investing means holding assets long-term to benefit from overall market growth. Trading requires active management and quick decision-making, while investing needs patience and fundamental belief in long-term value. Both approaches can be profitable but require different mindsets and strategies.

> "By investing in crypto, you are buying and holding currency to profit from its growth. By trading in crypto, you are buying and selling more frequently to make a greater return than you can by investing."

**Quick Take**: Choose your approach based on your time availability, risk tolerance, and personality. Don't mix strategies - either commit to long-term holding or active trading, not both simultaneously.

### Fundamental Analysis Framework
Before investing in any cryptocurrency, conduct thorough research using four key questions: How unique is the coin compared to competitors? How is the coin supplied and distributed? Are the creators experienced developers? What is the target demographic? Additionally, analyze market capitalization and trading volume to gauge the coin's health and potential.

The best sources for research include white papers, development team communications, and community forums. Be wary of promotional content that may be designed to manipulate prices.

> "Having a good fundamental analysis of a cryptocurrency gives you a distinct advantage in the market and keeps you in the loop."

**Quick Take**: Never invest in a cryptocurrency without reading its white paper and understanding the team behind it. Strong fundamentals are more important than short-term price movements.

### Risk Management Strategies
Dollar cost averaging (investing fixed amounts periodically) reduces the risk of mistiming the market. Portfolio diversification across multiple cryptocurrencies prevents total loss from a single failed investment. Setting stop-loss orders automatically sells positions at predetermined prices to limit losses. Locking in profits by selling portions of winning positions guarantees returns while maintaining upside potential.

> "Risk management is an essential tool for any successful investor or trader in cryptocurrency. Risk management means controlling any possible losses, so you do not lose too much if things go south."

**Quick Take**: Never invest more than you can afford to lose in a single cryptocurrency, and always have an exit strategy before entering any position.

## Action Items

- Create a trading journal to track all decisions, emotions, and outcomes for continuous learning and improvement
- Set up accounts on multiple exchanges to access different cryptocurrencies and compare fees
- Develop a personal investment strategy with clear entry and exit rules before making any trades
- Practice with small amounts first to understand the mechanics of buying, selling, and transferring between exchanges
- Implement dollar cost averaging for long-term investments to reduce timing risk
- Use paper wallets or cold storage for significant holdings to protect against exchange failures or hacks

## Questions to Consider

- Are you trading with money you can truly afford to lose, or are you letting desperation drive your decisions?
- Do you have a clear exit strategy before entering any position, or are you hoping prices will simply go up?
- Are you making decisions based on fundamental research or reacting to market hype and fear?
- Are you diversifying your portfolio across different cryptocurrencies, or putting all your eggs in one basket?
- Do you understand the difference between market corrections and genuine bear markets?

## Conclusion

This book provides solid foundational knowledge for cryptocurrency trading but should be considered a starting point rather than a complete guide. The emphasis on psychological discipline and risk management makes it particularly valuable for beginners who might otherwise fall into common trading pitfalls. However, the cryptocurrency market evolves rapidly, so readers should supplement this knowledge with current market information and continuous learning.

The biggest reason to read this book is its focus on preventing the most common and costly mistakes new traders make. By emphasizing emotional control, thorough research, and proper risk management, it provides a framework that can help beginners survive their first year of trading while building good habits. While specific strategies may need updating as the market evolves, the fundamental principles of disciplined trading remain timeless.

For anyone considering entering the cryptocurrency market, this book offers essential risk-averse strategies that could save thousands of dollars in common beginner mistakes. It's worth your time if you're willing to approach trading as a serious venture rather than a get-rich-quick scheme.
