# What Poor People Do That Rich People Don't
**Authors**: Kevin Solimon | **Year**: 2018

## Summary
This practical guide reveals 15 common habits that keep people trapped in financial poverty and prevent them from building wealth. The book operates on a straightforward premise: your daily habits determine your financial future. While most people focus on earning more money, the real difference between the wealthy and poor lies in how they think, spend, and behave with their resources. Each chapter breaks down a specific poor habit, explains its psychological roots, and provides actionable alternatives to replace it with wealth-building behaviors.

Perfect for anyone stuck in a cycle of financial frustration, this book delivers direct, no-nonsense advice that readers can implement immediately. You'll learn to identify subconscious money-draining patterns and replace them with deliberate wealth-building actions that compound over time.

## Core Insights

### Excessive TV Watching Consumes Your Wealth-Building Time
Poor people spend hours consuming content that makes others rich, while wealthy people use that same time for self-improvement and income generation. The average person wastes precious hours daily watching reality shows, browsing social media, and consuming entertainment that adds zero value to their financial future. Meanwhile, successful people invest that time reading educational material, learning new skills, or working on side businesses.

> "At the end of the day, it is the rich celebrity who is going to earn money for creating the content you're watching. You will be where you were, wasting your precious time consuming irrelevant content."

**Quick Take**: Replace 30 minutes of daily TV watching with reading a finance book or watching an educational documentary. This simple swap can transform your financial trajectory over a year.

### Fast Food Destroys Both Health and Wealth
The true cost of fast food extends far beyond the menu price. A seemingly modest $8 lunch five times weekly adds up to $1,920 annually - money that could be invested or saved. More importantly, fast food creates a cycle of poor health, reduced productivity, and increased medical expenses. The high fat, sodium, and sugar content affects brain function, leading to poor decision-making in all areas of life, including finances.

> "Even if you grab a quick $8 lunch at least five times a week, it will come to an amount of $40 by the end of the week, $160 by the end of the month and a whopping $1920 by the end of the year."

**Quick Take**: Dedicate one weekend monthly to meal prep. Cooking in bulk saves money, improves health, and eliminates the daily temptation of expensive convenience food.

### Assets Build Wealth; Liabilities Destroy It
The fundamental difference between rich and poor spending habits comes down to buying things that make money versus things that cost money. Wealthy people prioritize assets - investments that generate future cash flow like stocks, real estate, or businesses. Poor people accumulate liabilities - expensive items that continuously drain money through maintenance, insurance, and operational costs.

> "An asset is something that can generate cash flow in the future. Unlike a liability, an asset will always help you to make money."

**Quick Take**: Before any purchase over $100, ask yourself: "Will this make me money or cost me money?" If it won't generate income, reconsider the purchase.

### Multiple Income Streams Create Financial Security
Relying on a single paycheck is like building a house with no foundation - one setback can destroy everything. Wealthy people diversify their income through side businesses, investments, affiliate marketing, or passive income streams. This approach provides stability when primary income fluctuates and accelerates wealth building through multiple cash sources.

> "If you want to make maximum wealth, you cannot devote your entire time and energy on a single source of income."

**Quick Take**: Start one small side income stream this month - even an extra $200-500 monthly compounds significantly over time and provides crucial financial security.

### Your Social Circle Determines Your Financial Future
Habits and mindsets are contagious. Surrounding yourself with financially unsuccessful people who complain about money, make excuses, and practice poor financial habits will inevitably influence your own behavior. Wealthy people intentionally build networks of ambitious, financially savvy individuals who challenge them to aim higher and share opportunities.

> "Habits spread easily, we usually pick up our habits from the people in our current social network."

**Quick Take**: Join one professional or financial development group this quarter. The connections and knowledge will accelerate your wealth-building journey exponentially.

## Action Items

- **Track your time**: For one week, document how you spend every hour outside of work. Replace time-wasting activities with wealth-building alternatives
- **Implement a 24-hour rule**: Wait 24 hours before any non-essential purchase over $50 to eliminate impulse buying
- **Start automated investing**: Set up automatic transfers to investment accounts, even if it's just $25 weekly initially
- **Create a "no credit" week**: Use only cash or debit for one week to reset your spending mindset
- **Schedule health checkups**: Preventive medical care saves thousands in future expenses
- **Wake up 30 minutes earlier**: Use this time for exercise, planning, or skill development before daily distractions begin

While some concepts may seem basic, their power lies in implementation and consistency. The 15 habits covered are comprehensive and address the full spectrum of financial self-sabotage.

The book's greatest strength is its direct approach - each chapter identifies a specific problem, explains its financial impact, and provides concrete solutions. There's no complex financial theory or get-rich-quick schemes, just straightforward advice that works if you work it. The writing is accessible and the examples relatable, making it easy to identify your own poor habits within the pages.

**What's the biggest reason to read it?** This book serves as a financial mirror - it will show you exactly how you're sabotaging your own wealth potential and give you the tools to fix it. If you're tired of living paycheck-to-paycheck despite earning decent money, or if you wonder why some people build wealth while others struggle, this book provides the answers. Implement even half of these habits consistently, and your financial future will transform completely.
