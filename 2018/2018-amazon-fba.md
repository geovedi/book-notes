# 2018 Amazon FBA: Proven Step By Step Strategies to Make Money On Amazon FBA
**Authors**: Mark Smith | **Year**: 2018

## Summary

This comprehensive guide breaks down Amazon's FBA (Fulfillment by Amazon) program into actionable strategies for entrepreneurs looking to build profitable online businesses. The book walks readers through the complete process of selling on Amazon, from finding profitable products to scaling operations without getting overwhelmed by logistics. FBA essentially lets you leverage Amazon's massive infrastructure—warehousing, shipping, customer service, and Prime delivery—so you can focus on product sourcing and business growth.

Perfect for aspiring entrepreneurs, side-hustlers, and small business owners who want to tap into Amazon's millions of daily customers without the complexity of traditional e-commerce. Readers will learn how to start a scalable business with minimal upfront investment and maximum operational efficiency.

## Core Insights

### The FBA Advantage: Let Amazon Do the Heavy Lifting

FBA transforms e-commerce by outsourcing all the operational headaches to Amazon. When you enroll, Amazon handles your product storage, packaging, shipping, customer service, and returns. This frees up your time to focus on what actually grows your business: finding profitable products and marketing strategies. Amazon's fulfillment centers can process orders within minutes of purchase, ensuring fast delivery that customers expect.

The real magic is scalability. Whether you're selling 10 or 10,000 products monthly, Amazon's infrastructure handles the volume without you needing warehouse space, packing materials, or shipping staff. This makes it possible to build a substantial business from your laptop.

> "If you are simply trying to sell something on the Internet then there might be an issue of scalability. You might not be able to increase your business on every website."

**Quick Take**: FBA eliminates the biggest barrier to e-commerce growth—operational complexity—letting you compete with established brands on equal footing.

### Product Sourcing: The Profit Engine

Your success hinges on finding the right products at the right prices. The book recommends retail arbitrage as the fastest starting point: scout local stores for clearance items and discounted products, then use the Amazon seller app to scan barcodes and compare current Amazon prices. Look for products with at least 25% discount margins to ensure profitability after Amazon fees.

For sustainable long-term success, focus on products in the $10-50 price range that are lightweight, durable, and have low competition (under 50 reviews on the first page). The sweet spot is products where your manufacturing or sourcing cost is just 25% of the retail price, leaving room for FBA fees and healthy profits.

> "The main aim is find a product whose price can be increased to make a profit and still the price should be lower than what is currently selling on Amazon."

**Quick Take**: Start with retail arbitrage for quick wins, then transition to sourcing unique products that fit the ideal margin and competition profile.

### Prime Power: Your Competitive Edge

FBA makes your products Prime-eligible, which is like getting Amazon's seal of approval. Prime customers typically only view the first few products in search results, and Prime products get priority placement. This visibility boost can increase your sales by 30-50% compared to non-Prime listings.

The Prime badge also builds trust—customers know they'll get fast, free shipping and easy returns through Amazon's trusted customer service. This trust factor significantly increases conversion rates, especially for higher-priced items where customers are more cautious about buying from unknown sellers.

> "It's almost a necessity to be part of the FBA scheme if you want to retain your customers. Most customers tend to pick sellers who are FBA rather than MF even if the price is high."

**Quick Take**: Prime eligibility isn't just about shipping speed—it's a competitive advantage that drives visibility, trust, and conversions.

### Cost Management: The Numbers Game

FBA profitability requires understanding Amazon's fee structure. Amazon charges based on product weight, size, storage duration, and handling requirements. Small, lightweight products are most profitable because they minimize storage and shipping fees. Use Amazon's FBA Revenue Calculator before sourcing any product to ensure your margins work.

The key insight is to think in per-unit costs. While FBA fees might seem high on a single $20 item, when you're selling 100 units monthly, the economies of scale kick in. Plus, FBA eliminates your labor costs for packing and shipping, which is significant as you grow.

> "Selling products in bulk usually reduces their cost. If you sell just one water bottle then obviously the cost of selling it through FBA will be too much but if you sell a bunch of bottles, the cost per bottle would be very low."

**Quick Take**: Focus on small, lightweight products with healthy margins, and always run the numbers through the FBA calculator before investing in inventory.

## Action Items

- Start with retail arbitrage: Download the Amazon Seller app and scan products at local discount stores to practice spotting profitable opportunities
- Calculate your ideal product profile: $10-50 price range, lightweight, durable, low competition, with sourcing costs at 25% or less of retail price
- Set up a Professional Seller account during the free trial period to access advanced selling tools and lower per-item fees
- Invest in essential equipment: shipping scale, box sizer, and thermal printer for efficient product preparation
- Research your competition thoroughly before entering any product category, focusing on review counts and sales rank

## Conclusion

This book is worth your time if you're serious about building an e-commerce business without the logistical nightmares. It provides a clear, actionable roadmap that demystifies Amazon's FBA program and shows exactly how to leverage one of the world's most sophisticated fulfillment networks.

The biggest reason to read this is that it removes the operational complexity that stops most people from starting e-commerce businesses. While the content could benefit from more detailed sourcing strategies and specific examples, it gives you everything needed to start selling within weeks. FBA isn't a get-rich-quick scheme—it's a legitimate business model that requires work, but this book shows you how to work smarter by letting Amazon handle the heavy lifting.
