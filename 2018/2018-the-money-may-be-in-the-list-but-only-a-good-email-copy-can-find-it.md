# The Money May Be In The List. But Only A Good Email Copy Can Find It.
**Authors**: Alex Harris | **Year**: 2018 | **Category**: Business & Finance

## Summary
This practical email marketing guide reveals that while building an email list is important, crafting compelling email copy is what actually drives conversions and revenue. Written by a freelance copywriter who has penned over 2000 email copies, the book serves as a foundational course in email writing mastery.

The book breaks down email copywriting into actionable techniques, emphasizing that effective emails should be short, engaging, and psychologically compelling. It teaches writers how to grab attention with subject lines, maintain reader interest through tactical writing, and drive action with strategic calls-to-action.

This book is perfect for small business owners, marketers, and freelancers who want to transform their email marketing from passive broadcasts into active revenue generators. Readers will learn to write emails that get opened, read, and convert subscribers into customers.

## Core Insights

### The Subject Line is Your First Impression
Your subject line determines whether your email gets opened or deleted. It must be compelling and curiosity-driven while accurately representing the email's content. Compare boring standard subject lines like "Thank you for your order!" with more engaging alternatives like "Your order is no longer with us!" - the second creates urgency and mystery that drives opens.

> "A Subject Line is an introduction that identifies the intent of an email. Where the magic is is that, it's the first piece of writing your email list will see when you send them an email."

**Quick Take**: Treat your subject line like a movie trailer - intriguing enough to make people want to see the full feature.

### Opening Paragraphs Must Hit Hard
Start by addressing readers' pain points immediately. Instead of slow introductions, dive straight into what your readers are struggling with. The most effective technique is to "think about the worst that could happen if your readers don't subscribe to your offer" and write about that scenario first.

> "When the time comes to get new clients to your business, you know the deal. There's usually a set of known and unknowns. Then there's the lack of technical skills, and how to bring your marketing ideas to life."

**Quick Take**: Start strong by validating your readers' challenges - this builds immediate trust and keeps them reading.

### Punch Lines and Cliffhangers Drive Engagement
Keep readers hooked by strategically placing punch lines that deliver impact and cliffhangers that create curiosity. Punch lines define your argument or deliver the key point, while cliffhangers are incomplete thoughts that require reading the next line. Use professional cliffhangers like "What's more?", "And the best of all?", "But that's not all" to maintain flow without sounding gimmicky.

> "Cliffhangers are lines that make you want to read the next line. Like the lines above (But wait, and I just did that, can't see it?). You notice how it pulls you down into reading the line where I explained what cliffhangers are?"

**Quick Take**: Sprinkle engagement triggers throughout your email - think like a storyteller building suspense, not just a writer conveying information.

### Short, Simple Style Wins
Write like you're talking to a 10-year-old. Use short paragraphs, simple words, and conversational tone. Technical jargon confuses readers and kills conversions. Focus on benefits over features, using relatable analogies and visuals to explain complex concepts. The goal is communication, not showing off vocabulary.

> "Write like you are talking to a little kid who just asked you a simple question that needs a simple answer. It's that easy."

**Quick Take**: Simplicity creates clarity, and clarity creates conversions. The more people understand you, the more they'll trust you.

### Calls-to-Action Need Incentives
Never assume readers will buy without clear direction. Your CTA must be specific and compelling, but most importantly, it should offer value. Use incentives like discounts, free strategy calls, webinars, or exclusive content to overcome hesitation. Study competitors to identify what customers want but aren't getting, then offer that as your unique incentive.

> "You should develop a hunter's mindset. Lure the animal in for the kill. Think to yourself, what do your readers want which you can comfortably provide for free."

**Quick Take**: Your CTA isn't just a button - it's an exchange of value. Make the offer irresistible.

## Action Items
- Before writing, outline all points you want to make, then keep only the most impactful ones
- Create subject lines that generate curiosity while staying relevant to your content
- Start emails by addressing readers' biggest pain points immediately
- Break text into short paragraphs (2-3 sentences maximum)
- Sprinkle cliffhangers and punch lines throughout to maintain engagement
- Write in conversational tone, as if speaking to a 10-year-old
- Include specific calls-to-action with compelling incentives
- Add visuals when relevant to enhance engagement
- Ask psychological questions that your product can answer
- Study competitors' emails to identify gaps and opportunities

## Email Template Framework
1. **Subject Line**: Compelling and curiosity-driven
2. **Greeting**: Personalized (Hello, [Name])
3. **Opening Paragraph**: Address pain points immediately
4. **Punch Line**: Deliver key insight or benefit
5. **Body**: Short paragraphs, simple words, cliffhangers
6. **CTA**: Clear action with valuable incentive

## Questions to Consider
- What's the biggest mistake you're making in your current email strategy?
- How can you make your next email more engaging than your last one?
- What incentive would make your ideal customer click immediately?
- Are you writing to impress or to communicate?
- What pain points are you not addressing that your competitors are ignoring?

## Conclusion

Harris's conversational style and real-world examples make complex copywriting techniques accessible to beginners while still offering value to experienced marketers. The emphasis on psychological triggers, engagement techniques, and conversion-focused writing provides a solid foundation for anyone serious about email marketing.

The book's greatest strength is its relentless focus on practical application. Unlike theoretical marketing texts, every concept comes with actionable examples and clear implementation steps. Harris's "write like you're talking to a 10-year-old" philosophy alone could transform most businesses' email performance.

Harris has tested these techniques across 2000+ emails for real clients - this isn't theory, it's proven practice. While some may find the informal style jarring, the results speak for themselves. At under 200 pages, it's a quick read that could immediately impact your bottom line.

For entrepreneurs and marketers who view email as a revenue channel rather than just a communication tool, this book is essential reading.
