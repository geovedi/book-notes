# 3 Steps to Success Negotiations
**Authors**: Anthony Jacks | **Year**: 2018

## Summary
This practical guide demystifies the art of negotiation, presenting it as an essential skill that applies to virtually every aspect of business and personal life. Jacks breaks down complex negotiation theory into three manageable steps: preparation, trading, and finalization. The book emphasizes that negotiation isn't about winning or losing but finding mutually beneficial solutions through strategic preparation and careful communication.

Business professionals, managers, and anyone who needs to make deals will gain a clear framework for approaching negotiations with confidence. Readers will learn how to prepare systematically, trade variables effectively, and secure agreements that protect their interests while maintaining relationships.

## Core Insights

### Step One: Getting to Grips with Core Approaches
Effective negotiation begins with thorough preparation and understanding the fundamental win-win philosophy. Jacks emphasizes that both parties must leave feeling satisfied with the deal, even if it's not exactly what they initially hoped for. Success depends on comprehensive research about the topic, the people involved, and your own position.

> "You don't get what you deserve; you get what you negotiate."

### Step Two: Trading and Making It Work
The heart of negotiation involves trading variables strategically. Jacks identifies three types of variables: loss leaders (what you'll trade reluctantly), ideals (your perfect outcome), and must-haves (deal-breakers). The key is never giving away concessions without trading something in return, always optimizing what you receive while minimizing what you give up.

> "Never give a concession; trade it reluctantly."
**Quick Take**: Start trading early but don't give away significant items too quickly. Always appear to be driving a hard bargain to be taken seriously.

### Step Three: The Fine Print
Every successful negotiation requires proper documentation to prevent misunderstandings and protect both parties. Modern business demands more detailed contracts due to tougher economic conditions. Good contracts should enhance relationships, allow flexibility, and link logically to practical implementation.

> "Any omissions in contracts can cost you dear."
**Quick Take**: Be thorough in documenting details. While legal advice may be necessary for final terms, clear, readable agreements benefit everyone involved.

## Action Framework

### Pre-Negotiation Checklist
- Research the other party's organization and objectives
- Identify all possible variables and categorize them (loss leaders, ideals, must-haves)
- Set clear priorities and determine your immovable positions
- Consider cultural factors in international negotiations
- Prepare your initial stance and opening positions

### During Negotiation Tactics
- Use a mix of closed, open, and probing questions
- Build bridges of rapport to facilitate discussion
- Trade concessions reluctantly but steadily
- Always optimize concessions you receive and minimize those you give
- Keep detailed notes and repeat agreements for clarity

### Contract Finalization Steps
- Document all details comprehensively
- Ensure contracts eliminate misunderstandings
- Maintain flexibility within tight terms
- Consider long-term relationship implications
- Plan for potential contract breakdown scenarios

## Questions to Consider

- How might your current negotiation preparation process be more systematic?
- Where could you build better bridges of rapport in difficult conversations?
- What concessions have you given away too freely in past negotiations?
- How can you appear more confident and authoritative without being aggressive?

## Conclusion
This book is absolutely worth your time if you want a practical, no-nonsense approach to negotiation. Its greatest strength is the clear three-step framework that makes complex concepts immediately actionable. Jacks provides real-world tactics without overwhelming theory, making this ideal for busy professionals who need results quickly.

The biggest reason to read this book is its emphasis on preparation and systematic thinking. Unlike many negotiation guides that focus purely on tactics, Jacks shows that success comes from groundwork, variable analysis, and careful documentation. This isn't about clever tricks; it's about building a reliable negotiation process that consistently produces win-win outcomes while protecting your interests and relationships.
