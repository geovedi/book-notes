# Social Media Marketing in 2018
**Authors**: R.M. Lewis | **Year**: 2018 | **Category**: Business & Finance

## Summary
This practical guide demonstrates how businesses can leverage Facebook, YouTube, Instagram, and Twitter to grow their following, build brand awareness, and more than double their traffic. The book provides platform-specific strategies while emphasizing foundational principles of social media branding that work across all channels.

Business owners and marketers will benefit from actionable techniques for creating consistent brand presence, engaging visual content, and effective customer communication strategies that translate social media followers into paying customers.

## Core Insights

### Brand Consistency Across Platforms
Successful social media marketing requires maintaining consistent brand identity across all platforms. This includes unified messaging, visual elements, and tone of voice that reflects your business values. Whether you have one or multiple social media accounts, they should work together cohesively to present your business in the best possible light.

> "Staying consistent will ensure that you are able to be the same for your customers on all platforms."

**Quick Take**: Assign one person to manage all social media posting to maintain a consistent voice, or create detailed guidelines if multiple people handle posting.

### Visual Content is Essential
People connect more strongly with visual content than text alone. Photos should make up the bulk of your posts across all platforms, supplemented by increasingly popular video content. Visuals help customers visualize your products or services and create more engaging, personalized experiences.

> "Reading is great and learning about a product or a service in that way will give your customers information but being able to visualize it will allow them to truly be drawn in."

**Quick Take**: Every post should include relevant visual content - photos or videos that grab attention without being obnoxious or overwhelming.

### Platform-Specific Strategy Selection
Choose platforms based on your business type and target audience rather than trying to maintain presence everywhere. Facebook offers the broadest reach with mixed content types, YouTube excels for service businesses showcasing their work, Twitter connects with business-minded audiences, and Instagram reaches younger demographics through visual storytelling.

**Quick Take**: Start with one platform that best matches your business type, then expand to others after mastering the first one.

### Engagement Through Authentic Communication
Social media success comes from genuine two-way communication with your audience. Respond to all messages and comments promptly, address complaints publicly and professionally, and show the human side of your business. This builds trust and demonstrates that you value customer relationships.

> "People don't want just some organization or business to be constantly posting, and they never know what type of person is behind it."

**Quick Take**: Enable comments on your business pages and respond within one hour to qualify for Facebook's responsive messaging badge.

### Strategic Growth and Maintenance
Social media marketing requires ongoing effort and strategic optimization. Track your engagement metrics against sales data to measure conversion rates. Consider hiring a social media manager when overwhelmed, and don't be afraid to experiment with different platforms or strategies to find what works best for your specific business.

**Quick Take**: Start with one platform, master it, then expand. Cross-link all accounts and maintain consistent posting across active platforms.

## Platform-Specific Strategies

### Facebook Business Pages
- Complete all profile information including keywords in your description
- Post daily using scheduling tools to maintain consistency
- Use call-to-action buttons for easy customer contact
- Encourage reviews and respond to all messages promptly
- Share community content and connect with non-competing businesses

### YouTube Marketing
- Focus on quality over quantity with professional-looking videos
- Include keywords in video titles for search optimization
- Add clear calls-to-action at the end of each video
- Link to your website in video descriptions and profile
- Consider weekly video series to maintain viewer engagement

### Twitter for Business
- Use hashtags strategically including creating branded ones
- Retweet industry content and engage with other businesses
- Keep conversations professional and avoid controversial topics
- Use shortened URLs to save character space
- Consider paid advertising for expanded reach

### Instagram Marketing
- Mobile-only platform requiring smartphone app
- Focus on high-quality, business-relevant photos
- Use relevant hashtags and tag people in photos
- Include website URL in profile for traffic generation
- Connect with Facebook account for cross-platform benefits

## Action Items
- Choose one primary social media platform based on your business type and target audience
- Create complete profile information with relevant keywords and business details
- Establish a consistent posting schedule (daily for Facebook, weekly for YouTube)
- Set up systems to monitor and respond to all customer interactions within 24 hours
- Cross-link your website and all active social media accounts
- Track engagement metrics against sales data to measure ROI
- Plan content calendar with mix of promotional, educational, and engaging posts

## Questions to Consider
- Which social media platform best matches where my target audience already spends time?
- How can I showcase what makes my business unique through visual content?
- What systems do I need to maintain consistent posting and customer response times?
- How will I measure the success of my social media efforts beyond just likes and followers?
- When should I consider expanding to additional platforms or hiring professional help?

## Conclusion
This book is worth your time if you're looking for practical, platform-specific guidance on establishing your business's social media presence. While some content shows its 2018 publication date (particularly character limits and platform features), the foundational strategies remain relevant.

The biggest reason to read this book is its systematic approach to social media marketing - it doesn't just tell you what to do, but explains how to choose the right platforms, create consistent branding, and maintain the ongoing effort required for success. The emphasis on starting with one platform and mastering it before expanding provides a manageable roadmap for overwhelmed business owners.

Though lacking revolutionary insights, this book serves as an excellent foundational guide for businesses new to social media marketing or those looking to improve their existing strategy with practical, actionable techniques.
