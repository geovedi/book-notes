# 155 Best Business English Vocabulary Terms
**Authors**: Winn Trivette, MA | **Year**: 2018

## Summary

This practical vocabulary guide is designed specifically for Business English professionals and students who want to enhance their word power for career advancement. The book presents 155 essential business vocabulary terms organized into 15 thematic lessons covering various business domains including general business, stock market, international trade, marketing, law, and more. Each lesson provides clear definitions, parts of speech, usage examples, and practical exercises to reinforce learning.

This book is perfect for non-native English speakers working in international business environments, professionals preparing for presentations or negotiations, and anyone wanting to appear more articulate and competent in workplace communications. Readers will gain confidence in using sophisticated business terminology correctly and understand industry-specific jargon that can impact career success.

## Core Insights

### The Power of Professional Vocabulary
The book begins with a compelling premise: vocabulary directly correlates with career success and income potential. Professor Winn emphasizes that colleagues, bosses, and clients often judge intelligence and competence based on vocabulary usage. He introduces the concept of "le mot juste" (the right word) - using precisely the right term at the right moment to demonstrate expertise and credibility.

> "Business English professionals use a sophisticated vocabulary at the office! They are prepared to use le mot juste (the right word) at the right moment."

**Quick Take**: Your vocabulary isn't just communication - it's a career asset that directly impacts how others perceive your professional capabilities.

### Strategic Learning Framework
The book employs a systematic approach to vocabulary acquisition. Each lesson focuses on a specific business domain with 10 keywords, followed by comprehension exercises. This thematic organization helps learners understand not just individual words, but how they function within specific business contexts. The exercises include sentence completion, opposite/same meaning identification, and contextual application.

> "Each lesson represents a field of expertise in Business English. Ten keywords drawn from the field begin each lesson."

**Quick Take**: Learning vocabulary in context by business domain is more effective than random word memorization.

### Comprehensive Business Coverage
The 15 lessons cover essential business areas:
- **General Business**: Core terms like accretion, amortization, collateral, escrow
- **Finance & Markets**: ROI, volatility, hedge funds, prospectus
- **International Trade**: Balance of payments, dumping, tax haven, negotiable
- **Specialized Fields**: Environmental/energy, technology, hospitality, marketing, legal, and more

This comprehensive approach ensures learners acquire vocabulary relevant to their specific industry while building general business fluency.

### Practical Application Focus
Beyond simple definitions, the book provides real-world usage examples for each term. Words like "synergy" (increased effectiveness from combined operations), "due diligence" (reasonable conduct in specific situations), and "burden of proof" (responsibility to prove disputed facts) are presented with authentic business contexts that professionals will actually encounter.

> "The synergy between the mining and transportation company sped up delivery of coal to market."

**Quick Take**: Understanding business vocabulary requires knowing not just definitions, but real-world application contexts.

## Action Items

- **Establish a Vocabulary Routine**: Build word power systematically, word by word and day by day
- **Keep a Vocabulary Notebook**: Record words, definitions, synonyms, antonyms, and usage examples
- **Learn Words in Context**: Copy sentences where you find new words to understand proper usage
- **Create Online Flashcards**: Use tools like Quizlet to review new words weekly
- **Practice Pronunciation**: Use online dictionaries to hear correct pronunciation of new terms
- **Apply Words Immediately**: Use new vocabulary in emails, meetings, and presentations to reinforce learning
