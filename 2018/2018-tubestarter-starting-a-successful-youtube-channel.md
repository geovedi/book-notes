# Tubestarter: Starting A Successful YouTube Channel
**Original Title**: Tubestarter: Starting A Successful YouTube Channel
**Authors**: Devin Street | **Year**: 2018 | **Category**: Business & Finance

## Summary
This practical guide walks aspiring YouTubers through every step of building a successful YouTube channel, from technical setup to monetization strategies. Written by a 17-year-old entrepreneur who grew and sold niche channels for profit, the book covers channel creation, video production, SEO optimization, audience engagement, and multiple revenue streams beyond YouTube ads.

Perfect for beginners who want comprehensive but accessible guidance on turning YouTube from a hobby into a potential business. Readers will learn actionable strategies to grow their audience faster and monetize their content effectively.

## Core Insights

### 1. Setup Your Channel Properly
Create a dedicated Google account separate from personal use, using an email that incorporates your name or brand for professional communication. Choose a memorable channel name that reflects your content niche and create simple, clean branding with a consistent logo and banner that tells viewers what your channel is about.

> "Your Google account will connect you to everything. Gmail, Google Play Store, Drive, Calendars, Google Photos, Contacts, Hangouts, Google Wallets, Google Docs, Google Plus, Google AdSense, and most importantly, YouTube."

**Quick Take**: Don't use your personal Google account - create a dedicated professional presence from day one.

### 2. Strategic Content Creation
Never start with a channel trailer or intro video. Instead, create specific, searchable content within your niche that targets specific keywords rather than broad topics. Stay consistent with your content theme - constantly switching topics will kill your channel growth. Focus on solving specific problems your target audience is searching for.

> "What you will want to do is make a specific video about Minecraft like Killing Endermen in Minecraft, something still getting significant searches but has a lot less competition."

**Quick Take**: Niche down immediately and stick to your content category religiously.

### 3. Thumbnail Mastery
Use your face in thumbnails - statistics show this makes them at least three times more clickable due to psychological connection. Maintain consistent styling across all thumbnails using the same fonts, colors, and visual elements so viewers can instantly recognize your videos in suggested content.

> "Using a face in a thumbnail makes the thumbnail at least three times more clickable. This is because when the human brain sees a face, they have a psychological and emotional connection that makes them more likely to click."

**Quick Take**: Your face is your most powerful thumbnail asset - use it consistently.

### 4. Video SEO Strategy
Implement "triple-keywording" by using your target keyword phrase in your title, description, and tags. Mention your video title within the video itself so it appears in closed captions, creating another SEO signal. Use TubeBuddy to research and optimize your tags for maximum searchability.

> "Don't you think if the video has the same keyword phrase in the title, description, tags, and captions YouTube won't give it a high relevancy score?"

**Quick Take**: YouTube ranks videos by relevance and watch time - nail both with strategic keyword placement.

### 5. Engagement Through Cards
Place cards strategically: immediately at video start (2-4 seconds), at 30 seconds, and throughout when mentioning specific resources. Use the final card like an end screen for viewers who don't click the actual end screens. Always create an escape route to another video for viewers who want to leave.

> "With this card, I recommend using a video suggestion of a video like the topic of the current video."

**Quick Take**: Cards prevent viewer exodus and guide them to more of your content.

### 6. Multiple Income Streams
Diversify beyond YouTube ads through affiliate marketing (Amazon and MagicLinks), brand deals, merchandise, online courses, services, and Patreon funding. Never rely solely on YouTube revenue - build multiple income channels to protect against demonetization and algorithm changes.

> "Anyone who relies on YouTube ads to make their living is living on the thinnest sheet of ice for a career."

**Quick Take**: Build a business, not just a YouTube channel - diversify your income streams from day one.

### 7. Consistency Over Perfection
Quantity beats quality on social media platforms. The creator posting 8 imperfect videos monthly will outperform the one posting one perfect video. Focus on consistent content creation and continuous improvement rather than achieving unattainable perfection.

> "Everything doesn't have to be perfect but you do have to show up. The YouTuber who is putting out 8 imperfect videos a month is going to bet the YouTube who puts out 1 perfect video."

**Quick Take**: Show up consistently and improve gradually - perfect is the enemy of done.

## Action Items
- Create a dedicated Google account for your YouTube channel immediately
- Research and choose a specific niche with searchable but less competitive keywords
- Set up TubeBuddy for tag research and SEO optimization
- Create consistent thumbnail templates that include your face
- Develop a content strategy with 10+ specific video ideas before starting
- Set up accounts for affiliate programs (Amazon, MagicLinks)
- Create a Patreon account for fan funding opportunities
- Plan your first 4 weeks of content with consistent upload schedule

## Questions to Consider
- What specific problems does my target audience need solved?
- How can I create multiple income streams beyond YouTube ads?
- What makes my content different from established creators in my niche?
- How can I build authentic community engagement rather than just accumulating views?
- What unique value can I offer that keeps viewers coming back?

## Conclusion
This book is worth your time if you're serious about building a YouTube channel with business potential. While some platform-specific details may have changed since 2018 (especially monetization requirements), the core strategies for channel growth, audience engagement, and diversification remain highly relevant. The biggest reason to read this is the author's practical experience growing and selling channels for profit - he's not just teaching theory but sharing what actually works in the real world of YouTube entrepreneurship.

The emphasis on treating YouTube as a business rather than a hobby, combined with actionable strategies for multiple revenue streams, makes this particularly valuable for anyone looking to turn content creation into sustainable income. Just be prepared to adapt the technical details to YouTube's current platform requirements.
