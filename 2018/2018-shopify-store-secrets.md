# Shopify Store Secrets
**Authors**: Evan Jones | **Year**: 2018 | **Category**: Business & Finance

## Summary
This essential startup guide provides a comprehensive roadmap for building, launching, and growing a profitable online store using Shopify. The book walks beginners through every critical step, from initial setup and product selection to advanced strategies like drop shipping and promotional campaigns.

Entrepreneurs new to e-commerce will gain practical, actionable insights on how to create a professional-looking store that converts visitors into customers. The guide covers both the technical aspects of setting up a Shopify store and the business strategies needed to succeed in the competitive online marketplace.

## Core Insights

### 1. Store Setup Foundation
Start with a solid foundation by creating your Shopify account with a memorable store name. Focus on adding 5-10 quality products initially, each with compelling descriptions written in your own words (not manufacturer copy). Your product descriptions should follow a three-part formula: what it is, what's special about it, and why the customer needs it. Use high-resolution images (2048x2048px or larger) to maintain professional appearance across all devices.

> "Your description should not be the wording the manufacturer uses. Use your own wording so your page looks unique to Search Engines."
**Quick Take**: Invest time in creating unique, benefit-focused product descriptions rather than using generic manufacturer content.

### 2. Strategic Customization
Design your store appearance with your target audience in mind. Consider factors like age groups (larger text for elderly visitors), cultural preferences (bright colors for family-oriented stores), and subcultures (skate shops vs. classical music stores). Your logo should be professionally designed and properly sized for Shopify's recommended dimensions. Don't be afraid to experiment with different themes until you find one that resonates with your brand and customers.

> "Think about your own online shopping experiences; what online stores do you really like the look and feel of when you visit?"
**Quick Take**: Your store's visual design should match your target customers' preferences and expectations.

### 3. Smart Product Selection
Choose products based on either personal passion or market demand—or ideally, both. Selling products you're passionate about gives you natural knowledge about your customer base, while market-driven selling focuses on meeting consumer trends. The optimal approach combines both: understand who your customers are (age, location, gender, income level) and target them directly with products they actually want to buy.

> "The magic here, or the art of the sell, is finding out everything you can about who your customers are and target them directly."
**Quick Take**: Research your target audience demographics and preferences before selecting products to maximize sales potential.

### 4. Order Management Excellence
Monitor your sales channels regularly to track customer demographics, purchase quantities, and inventory levels. Eliminate non-selling stock quickly—it's dead money tying up your resources. Use Shopify's custom tagging system for orders to enable efficient searching and follow-up. Most importantly, identify and follow up with customers who abandon their carts to recover potentially lost sales.

> "Make sure to use the Shopify system to identify these incomplete checkout customers and follow up with an email to see if you can entice them back to complete the sale."
**Quick Take**: Cart abandonment follow-up emails can significantly recover otherwise lost revenue.

### 5. Shipping Optimization
Master your shipping logistics to ensure customer satisfaction. Understand your carriers' tracking systems, charges for local vs. international shipping, and how product weights and box sizes affect costs. Always include tracking numbers prominently in order confirmation emails to reduce "where is my item?" inquiries. Quality packaging and reliable carriers are essential—most online shoppers expect consistent, high standards of service.

> "It's absolutely essential that customers receive the right product, on time, and in perfect condition; every, single, time."
**Quick Take**: Prominent tracking information in confirmation emails dramatically reduces customer service inquiries.

### 6. Drop Shipping Strategy
Use drop shipping to expand your product range without holding inventory. Partner with reputable suppliers who provide "plain packaging" so customers can't tell the products came from a third party. Some suppliers will even include invoices with your business details. However, be cautious—your reputation depends on your supplier's reliability and professionalism.

> "Drop shipping means you can offer a product range much broader than you could otherwise purchase and keep stock of."
**Quick Take**: Vet drop shipping partners thoroughly since their service quality directly impacts your brand reputation.

### 7. Targeted Advertising Approach
Build advertising into your budget as a core business requirement. Choose platforms where your target audience congregates online—teen girls might be found on Pinterest or YouTube, while professional chefs might be reached through culinary schools. Use A/B testing to compare ad performance across different platforms and formats. Track your return on investment by monitoring traffic sources relative to advertising spend.

> "The best systems of online advertising use click data to establish which ad formats and locations bring the best sales."
**Quick Take**: Focus your advertising budget on platforms where your specific target audience spends their time online.

## Action Items
- Start your 14-day Shopify free trial and add 5-10 products with unique descriptions
- Customize your theme to match your target audience's preferences
- Choose products based on both passion and market research
- Set up order tracking systems to monitor sales and inventory
- Research and select reliable shipping carriers
- Explore drop shipping partnerships with reputable suppliers
- Create an advertising budget focused on your target audience's online habits

## Questions to Consider
- Are you more driven by passion for specific products or by market demand opportunities?
- Who exactly is your target customer and where do they spend their time online?
- How can you distinguish your store from competitors in product selection, price, or service?
- What systems will you implement to track and follow up on abandoned carts?
- Which advertising platforms offer the best potential ROI for your specific products?
- How will you ensure consistent, high-quality shipping experiences for customers?

## Conclusion
This guide is worth your time if you're new to e-commerce and want a straightforward, practical roadmap to launching a Shopify store. The biggest strength is its comprehensive coverage of both technical setup and business strategy, presented in an accessible, step-by-step format. While experienced online sellers might find some content basic, beginners will appreciate how it demystifies the entire process from account creation to marketing campaigns.

The book excels at breaking complex topics into manageable steps and providing real-world examples. However, readers should view this as a starting point rather than a complete business education—the most successful entrepreneurs will continue learning beyond these foundational principles. For anyone willing to follow the steps and implement the strategies, this guide provides everything needed to launch a functional Shopify store and begin the journey toward online retail success.
