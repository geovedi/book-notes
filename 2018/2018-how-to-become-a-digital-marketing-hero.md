# How to Become a Digital Marketing Hero
**Authors**: Rufus Lidman | **Year**: 2018
**Category**: Business & Finance

## Summary
"How to Become a Digital Marketing Hero" is a comprehensive strategic guide that transforms digital marketing from a tactical collection of tools into a cohesive strategic framework. The book addresses the fundamental challenge that only 9% of marketers feel confident their digital campaigns will achieve intended results. Lidman presents digital marketing as having evolved from just 3% of total marketing spend at the millennium to approaching 50% today, with predictions of exceeding 80% within five years. The book provides a seven-step strategic process for developing effective digital strategies, five essential digital marketing means, and insights into digital transformation that affects societies, industries, and individuals.

This book is ideal for marketing professionals, business leaders, and entrepreneurs who want to move beyond tactical execution to develop genuine digital marketing strategies that deliver measurable results. Readers will learn to create strategic frameworks that adapt to rapid digital changes, optimize resource allocation, and build the organizational capabilities needed for digital success.

## Core Insights

### The Digital Tsunami: From Offline to Online
The world is undergoing a massive digital transformation with internet users increasing 1500% since 2000, reaching nearly 4 billion people globally. More mobile devices exist than people, and over 80% of the global population has 3G access with 50% on 4G. This digital shift has fundamentally changed marketing from monologue to dialogue, with user-generated content now comprising two-thirds of all web pages. Daily digital activity includes 270 billion emails, 1 billion YouTube hours watched, 6.5 billion Google searches, and 500 million tweets.

> "It is time to stop using new technology to repair old processes, and instead use them to innovate new ones."

**Quick Take**: Digital marketing isn't just another channel—it's the new paradigm where consumers engage in dialogs rather than receiving monologues. Success requires embracing this fundamental shift from one-way to two-way communication.

### The Five Digital Marketing Means Framework
Lidman identifies five essential digital marketing tools, each with distinct strategic properties:

**1. Social Media Marketing (SMM)**: The engagement powerhouse where 2.8 billion users actively participate. SMM uniquely combines reach and engagement capabilities, with 71% of consumers recommending brands after good social media service experiences. The strategic choice is between using SMM as reach media (small impression on many people) or engagement media (big impact on fewer people).

**2. Display Advertising**: The oldest digital marketing tool, evolving from static banners to video and mobile advertising. Despite challenges like banner blindness (CTR dropped from 2% to 0.04%) and 615 million ad-blocker devices worldwide, display remains valuable when purchased on performance-based CPA models rather than CPM.

**3. Search Engine Marketing**: The king of traffic generation, with Google processing 2 trillion searches annually. SEO generates 14.6% conversion rates compared to 1.7% for traditional media. The strategic imperative is focusing on SEO (free, higher credibility) as much as SEM (paid), and optimizing for CPA rather than CPC.

**4. Site Strategy**: The digital ecosystem heart, evolved from simple brochures to complex conversion platforms. The key is optimizing BAC (Bounce → Activity → Conversion) through horizontal traffic strategy where every page functions as a potential front page, rather than forcing all traffic through a traditional homepage.

**5. Digital CRM**: Email marketing remains the most used digital medium with 270 billion emails sent daily, delivering $40 ROI for every $1 invested. Despite being "unsexy," CRM is increasingly crucial as media noise intensifies, with social CRM and marketing automation representing its evolution toward 1-2-1 personalization.

> "Golden insight #1: If you are on social media, you have to be social. Otherwise there is no point being there."
> "Golden insight #2: Optimize the site, with focus on minimizing bounce, maximizing activity and optimizing conversion (BAC)."

**Quick Take**: Each digital means serves distinct strategic purposes. Success requires understanding their unique strengths and limitations rather than applying them uniformly.

### The Seven-Step Digital Strategy Process
Lidman presents a systematic approach to digital strategy development:

1. **Strategic Platform**: Analyze background materials, market context, and organizational capabilities
2. **Goals Analysis**: Define clear, measurable objectives aligned with business outcomes
3. **Means Analysis**: Evaluate the five digital marketing means against specific goals
4. **Matching**: Optimize alignment between goals and appropriate digital means
5. **Moment of Truth**: Compare economic and HR resource requirements against expected returns
6. **Moment of Strategy**: Identify synergies and redundancies with other organizational strategies
7. **Digital Roadmap**: Create detailed activity plans with goals, subgoals, and timelines

This process transforms digital marketing from reactive tactics to proactive strategy, ensuring resources flow to highest-impact activities rather than simply following digital trends.

**Quick Take**: Digital strategy isn't about adopting every new platform—it's about systematic alignment of business goals with the right digital tools and resource allocation.

### Digital Transformation and Organization
The digital revolution represents not just technological change but fundamental societal transformation affecting industries, companies, and individuals. Companies must evolve from rigid hierarchies to elastic organizations that can adapt rapidly to digital changes. The most successful organizations combine user-friendly CMS systems with AB/MV testing capabilities, creating synergy between content managers and web analysts in hybrid "contemizer" roles.

Digital audits provide the most reliable optimization data, with KPIs identified through a simple 4-step process and 3-dimensional reference sheet. Only 28% of marketers are satisfied with conversion rates, yet only 50% analyze customer journeys—despite those who do seeing average sales increases of 79%.

**Quick Take**: Digital success requires both technical capabilities and organizational evolution, with data-driven decision making replacing gut feelings and traditional hierarchies.

### The Future of Digital Dominance
Three global trends define digital business's future: record-high digital business growth, digital social relations as evolution beyond traditional interaction, and video media as the undisputed content champion. Data serves as the mastermind behind everything, enabling unprecedented personalization and optimization.

The final frontier involves conscious machines and bots that will transform customer service and marketing automation. However, the fundamental human elements of strategy, creativity, and customer understanding remain irreplaceable even as technology advances.

**Quick Take**: Digital marketing's future lies in combining technological capabilities with human strategic insight, using data to enhance rather than replace human judgment.

## Conclusion
"How to Become a Digital Marketing Hero" is essential reading for any marketing professional looking to transform digital marketing from tactical execution to strategic advantage. Lidman successfully demystifies digital marketing by providing clear frameworks, systematic processes, and actionable insights that work across industries and company sizes.

The book's greatest strength lies in its strategic focus rather than tactical recommendations, ensuring relevance despite rapid technological changes. By emphasizing fundamental principles over platform-specific tactics, Lidman creates a timeless guide that helps marketers develop strategic thinking capabilities rather than just technical skills.

What makes this book particularly valuable is its emphasis on measurement, optimization, and ROI-based decision making. In a field where many marketers still struggle to demonstrate effectiveness, Lidman provides the tools and frameworks needed to prove digital marketing's business value and secure organizational support for digital initiatives.

For anyone serious about digital marketing leadership, this book provides the strategic foundation needed to navigate digital transformation confidently and effectively. It's not about becoming a digital tactician—it's about becoming a digital strategist who can build sustainable competitive advantage through thoughtful digital marketing execution.
