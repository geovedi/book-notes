# Forex Trading: A Practical Guide to Scalping Success
**Authors**: Heikin Ashi Trader | **Year**: 2018

## Summary

This comprehensive guide combines two manuscripts that teach forex scalping through Heikin Ashi charts and performance analysis. The first part demonstrates practical scalping techniques using modified candlestick charts that visualize market flow more clearly than traditional charts. The second part presents a case study of a beginner trader's 12-week journey to profitability, showing how statistical analysis and disciplined trading journals transform losses into consistent gains.

The book is designed for traders who want to master short-term forex trading through a systematic approach that combines technical analysis with rigorous performance tracking. It shows how scalping can be a viable business when treated with professional discipline and proper risk management.

## Core Insights

### Heikin Ashi Charts: The Foundation of Modern Scalping

Heikin Ashi charts differ from traditional candlestick charts by using modified calculations that smooth price action and make trends more visible. These charts color-code movements consistently—red for downtrends, green for uptrends—allowing traders to identify market momentum at a glance. The key patterns to watch are dojis (small crosses indicating market balance) and spinning tops (small bodies with long wicks showing high volatility but indecision). These patterns typically signal trend exhaustion and potential reversals, perfect entry points for counter-trend scalpers.

> "The Heikin Ashi charts visualize trends much better than, for example, candlestick charts. They are designed this way in order to identify trends at a glance."

The real power comes from understanding candle size: large candles show strong momentum, while shrinking candles indicate weakening trends. As a countertrend scalper, you specialize in identifying when trends end—large candles with long wicks often mark the peak of market enthusiasm, creating prime opportunities for reversals.

### The Entry Strategy: Riding Market Waves

The core entry signal comes when a doji or spinning top appears after a strong trend movement. This indicates temporary market equilibrium where buyers and sellers are balanced. Enter immediately when the next candle changes color—red after an uptrend or green after a downtrend. This approach works because you're not predicting reversals; you're reacting to clear signals that momentum has shifted.

> "Trading and scalping is a probability game; what's important is that your wins are bigger than your losses."

The beauty of this system is its simplicity: you don't need complex indicators or predictions. The market tells you when to enter through clear visual signals. Your job is to recognize these patterns and act decisively when they appear.

### Exit Management: Letting Colors Guide Your Decisions

The exit rule is equally straightforward: stay in your position until the Heikin Ashi candle color changes again. If you're short and the market keeps producing red candles, stay in the trade and accumulate profits. Don't exit based on hope or arbitrary price targets—let the chart itself tell you when the correction has ended.

> "If you have gone short but realize after a short time that it has only been a break and that the market continues the previous trend, you should immediately close your position."

This color-based exit system removes emotional decision-making and provides clear, objective signals for position management. The first candle color change after your entry typically marks the end of the correction movement.

### Technical Analysis Enhancement: Support, Resistance, and Round Numbers

While the basic setup works independently, combining it with technical analysis can improve precision. Key levels to watch include previous day swing highs and lows, support/resistance zones, and psychological round numbers (like 1.1200 in EUR/USD). These levels act as magnets for price action and often provide excellent entry opportunities when combined with Heikin Ashi signals.

> "Round numbers are important because market participants remember these levels very well. Institutional orders that want to come for a discount in the market are waiting there to be filled."

When price approaches these significant levels, look for the classic doji/spinning top patterns followed by color changes. These confluences of technical levels and Heikin Ashi signals provide high-probability setups with tight stop-losses.

### Risk Management: The Key to Survival

The book emphasizes that losses are simply "costs of business"—inevitable expenses that must be managed systematically. Never move your stop-loss further away, regardless of how convinced you are that the market will turn. Professional traders accept small losses quickly and focus on the next trade rather than hoping losing positions will recover.

> "Losses are the costs of our business—do not forget that! But if you are correct with your assessment and the market corrects, you will make a profit."

Position sizing should start small (mini lots) and only increase after achieving consistent profitability after commissions. Use fixed stops (6-8 pips for experienced scalpers) and respect them absolutely. The goal isn't avoiding losses but keeping them small enough that your wins more than compensate.

### Performance Analysis: Transform from Gambler to Business Owner

The book's second manuscript presents Jenny's 12-week journey, demonstrating how trading journals and statistical analysis transform trading from gambling into a business. Key metrics to track include hit rate (percentage of winning trades), average win vs. average loss (payoff ratio), and expectancy (average profit per trade).

> "With the wealth of data in the back, he can perform with more confidence. He grows with his data. Stable results generate confidence. Out of confidence, in turn, grow stable results."

Jenny's breakthrough came when she focused on improving her payoff ratio rather than her hit rate. She learned to let winners run while keeping losses small, achieving a payoff ratio above 1.5 (wins 50% larger than losses). This statistical approach reveals whether your trading system is truly profitable or just lucky.

### Trend Day Recognition: Don't Fight the Tide

While countertrend scalping works most days (markets trend sideways 70% of the time), recognize trending days and adjust accordingly. Signs include consecutive days of range-bound markets, fake moves in opposite directions, and important economic data releases. On these days, either reduce position size or trade with the dominant trend rather than against it.

> "Nothing is harder than when you try to short a market that has been rising all day. Believe me, I speak from personal experience; I have done this again and again."

The book advises checking higher timeframes (daily/4-hour charts) to understand the underlying market direction. Even as a scalper, you need to know whether you're swimming with or against the current.

## Action Items

- Start practicing Heikin Ashi chart interpretation on demo accounts before risking real money
- Keep a detailed trading journal recording every trade's entry, exit, rationale, and emotional state
- Calculate your key statistics weekly: hit rate, average win/loss, payoff ratio, and expectancy
- Begin with small position sizes (mini lots) and strict stop-losses (8-10 pips for beginners)
- Focus on improving payoff ratio rather than win percentage in early stages
- Specialize in one or two major currency pairs rather than trading multiple pairs
- Negotiate commission-based pricing with brokers once you achieve consistent volume
- Set clear daily profit/loss limits and stick to them religiously
- Practice identifying trend days and adjusting strategy accordingly
- Review weekly statistics with a mentor or trading partner for accountability
