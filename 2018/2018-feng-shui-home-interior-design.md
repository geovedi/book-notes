# Feng Shui: Home Interior Design Household Decoration to Attract Prosperity, Love, Luck & Harmony
**Authors**: Greenleatherr | **Year**: 2018
**Category**: Art & Creativity

## Summary
Feng Shui is the ancient Chinese art and science of arranging your living space to optimize the flow of chi (life force energy) through your environment. This practical guide demonstrates how your surroundings directly influence your thoughts, actions, and overall success in life. The book introduces the bagua map—a powerful tool that divides your home into nine specific areas, each corresponding to different aspects of your life including love, wealth, health, and career.

This guide is perfect for homeowners, renters, and anyone interested in creating harmony in their living space without expensive renovations. Readers will learn how to make simple, strategic changes that can dramatically improve specific areas of their lives through thoughtful placement of furniture, colors, and decorative elements.

## Core Insights

### The Bagua Map: Your Life's Blueprint
The bagua map is the foundation of practical Feng Shui application. This nine-square grid overlays your home's floor plan, with the front door aligning to the northern areas. Each section governs specific life aspects: Southeast (abundance), South (fame), Southwest (love), East (family), Center (health), West (creativity), Northeast (knowledge), North (career), and Northwest (helpful people). By identifying which rooms correspond to each life area, you can strategically enhance those spaces to improve related aspects of your life.

> "All living beings, from plants to people, are vehicles through which universal energy flows. As such, we are all interconnected. Feng Shui is all about your relationship with the environment."

**Quick Take**: Start by identifying which area of your life needs improvement, then locate the corresponding section in your home using the bagua map.

### Elemental Harmony: The Five Elements System
Each life area on the bagua map corresponds to one of the five natural elements—Wood, Fire, Earth, Metal, or Water. Enhancing an area means strengthening its associated element through colors, shapes, materials, and objects. For example, the Southeast prosperity area responds to the Wood element (green colors, plants, wooden furniture), while the South fame area thrives with Fire elements (red colors, candles, lighting).

> "Feng – Chinese word for 'wind'. Shui – Chinese word for 'water'. Note that both natural elements have two things in common: flow and circulation."

**Quick Take**: Use the guide's element associations to select appropriate colors and materials for each area of your home.

### Love and Relationships: The Southwest Palace
The love and relationships area (Southwest) thrives with Earth element enhancements. Use earth tones (light yellow, brown), square-shaped furniture, and items arranged in pairs. For singles seeking love, ensure decorative items come in twos—two nightstands, matching lamps, paired figurines—to signal readiness for partnership. Married couples can display wedding photos here unless this area falls in their bedroom.

> "Stop buying one-piece items and make sure you have two of everything. This is a way of letting the universe know that you are open for a relationship and that you're inviting someone to share your home."

**Quick Take**: Add pairs of objects and earth tones to your Southwest area to attract or enhance romantic relationships.

### Wealth and Prosperity: The Southeast Palace
Activate abundance in your home by enhancing the Southeast area with Wood element features. Incorporate lush green plants, wooden furniture, and water features (avoid in bedrooms). The lucky number for this area is four, so consider groups of four decorative items. Flowing water should always face inward to prevent money from "leaving" your home. Purple, though a fire color, also works well here as it represents nobility.

> "Because water nourishes wood, activating water as a secondary element will also help in inviting prosperity into your life. The southeast is a perfect place to install an indoor fountain."

**Quick Take**: Place plants or a small water fountain in your Southeast area to attract financial opportunities.

### Career Advancement: The Northern Palace
Your career area responds to Water element enhancements. Incorporate black and blue colors, mirrors, glass furnishings, and actual water features. Metal elements support water, so metallic objects work well here. If installing a fountain or aquarium, ensure water flows inward rather than outward. This is ideal for home offices or study areas, especially for those seeking job promotions or career transitions.

> "If you need an extra boost in your career, strengthen the water element in this area with an aquarium or with a fountain."

**Quick Take**: Add a water feature or mirror to your North area to accelerate career growth.

## Action Items
- Map your home using the bagua overlay to identify which areas correspond to which life aspects
- Declutter and clean the areas most needing improvement in your life
- Add appropriate colors and elements to specific bagua areas based on your goals
- Arrange furniture to face doors when possible for command position
- Incorporate pairs of objects in the Southwest area if seeking love
- Add plants or water features to the Southeast area for prosperity
- Display achievements and awards in the South area for recognition
- Keep bathroom doors closed and toilet lids down to prevent energy drain

## Questions to Consider

- Does your furniture placement support your goals or obstruct energy flow?

- How can you use colors and shapes to enhance specific life areas?
- What meaningful objects could you display to reinforce your aspirations?

## Conclusion
This Feng Shui guide offers a practical introduction to creating harmony between your living space and life goals. Its strength lies in the straightforward bagua mapping system and specific elemental associations that make abstract concepts actionable. The book excels at demystifying Feng Shui, presenting it as an environmental optimization tool rather than mystical superstition.

While some recommendations may seem overly specific (like exact numbers of decorative items), the underlying principles of creating intentional, harmonious spaces have universal appeal. The approach of making small, targeted changes rather than expensive overhauls makes these teachings accessible to any budget or living situation.

This book is worth your time if you're seeking a structured approach to home organization that goes beyond mere aesthetics. Whether you believe in energy flow or simply appreciate the psychological benefits of intentional living spaces, these principles can help create more supportive environments for your goals and well-being.
