# Cryptocurrency: Strategies to Make Money with Cryptocurrency
**Authors**: Gavin S. Finney | **Year**: 2018
**Category**: Business & Finance

## Summary
This practical guide serves as a comprehensive playbook for cryptocurrency investors looking to implement effective money-making strategies in the volatile crypto market. The book covers five primary approaches to cryptocurrency earnings: investing, trading, mining, buying and holding, and exploring alternative cryptocurrencies. Written as a follow-up to the author's beginner's guide, this book targets readers who understand cryptocurrency basics but need strategic direction for profitable participation.

The book is ideal for new cryptocurrency investors, traditional investment enthusiasts looking to diversify into digital assets, and readers who want actionable strategies rather than theoretical knowledge. Readers will gain practical frameworks for portfolio diversification, risk management, and strategic planning specifically tailored to the unique characteristics of cryptocurrency markets.

## Core Insights

### Understanding Cryptocurrency Strategy Foundation
Before implementing any cryptocurrency strategy, it's essential to grasp that cryptocurrency is a digital currency operating on decentralized principles using encryption techniques for transaction verification and record-keeping. With over 1,000 cryptocurrencies available since Bitcoin's 2009 introduction, strategic planning becomes crucial for selecting genuine coins and developing appropriate investment approaches. The fundamental principle for beginners: invest only what you can afford to lose, keeping crypto investments between 1-10% of your total portfolio.

> "Be logical, don't get emotional. The uncertainty in the market and the price fluctuations might be too stressful but taking decisions when your 'panic mode' is ON might turn fatal for your financial stability."
**Quick Take**: Emotional decision-making is the biggest enemy of cryptocurrency investors. Strategy requires discipline and rational assessment rather than reactive responses to market volatility.

### Strategic Planning Framework
Effective cryptocurrency investment requires systematic planning through specific steps: writing down goals, determining investment timeframes (short-term vs long-term), setting realistic objectives, comparing theoretical plans with practical constraints (capital, time horizon, risk tolerance), and selecting appropriate strategies. Before committing capital, investors should have clear answers about which cryptocurrency to choose, their understanding of the underlying technology, their preferred investment timeline, capital allocation decisions, chosen exchanges, and security storage methods.

> "When there is a defined strategy, the decision made is mostly right thus helping in smooth implementation process without any bottlenecks."
**Quick Take**: Strategic planning transforms reactive gambling into calculated investing by providing decision-making frameworks before market pressures hit.

### Investment Strategy Implementation
Cryptocurrency investment strategy requires calculated decisions about capital allocation based on the volatile nature of crypto markets. The long-term investment approach is recommended as the primary strategy due to lower risk and reduced transaction costs. Key principles include buying during price dips, avoiding emotional decisions during market fluctuations, understanding the reasons behind price movements (which may include cross-cryptocurrency price influences, rumors, media announcements, or regulatory decisions), and maintaining composure during market volatility. Historical data shows cryptocurrency investments can yield returns up to 900% (as witnessed in 2017), far exceeding traditional stock market performance.

> "Don't fear to acknowledge the dip in price value. When you have decided to invest in cryptocurrency then by default you should be prepared to accept and recognize the dip in price value of the different crypto coins in the market."
**Quick Take**: Price dips are inherent to cryptocurrency markets and often present buying opportunities rather than reasons for panic selling.

### Trading Strategy Approaches
Cryptocurrency trading differs from investing by focusing on shorter timeframes and more frequent transactions. The key to successful trading is maintaining capital preservation through proper position sizing (1-5% of allocated capital), setting clear stop-loss limits (under 25% of position size), and having predefined entry and exit strategies. Altcoin trading can offer higher profit potential than Bitcoin due to smaller market capitalization and larger price swings, though with increased risk. A diversified trading approach might allocate 50% to Bitcoin, 30% to Ethereum, 15% to Dash, and 5% to ZCash, based on individual risk tolerance and market analysis.

> "Perfectly timing the buys and sells is a myth! The truth is however much you try to be perfect in your buying and selling plan there tends to be slippage, and you should always be ready for the same."
**Quick Take**: Trading success comes from consistent strategy execution rather than attempting perfect market timing, which is virtually impossible.

### Mining as Investment Strategy
Cryptocurrency mining involves verifying transactions by solving complex cryptographic puzzles and earning new coins as rewards. While Bitcoin mining now requires specialized hardware (ASIC rigs) and significant investment, alternative mining strategies include GPU mining for altcoins with simpler algorithms, home mining operations with proper equipment and ventilation, and cloud mining services for those avoiding hardware logistics. Successful mining requires choosing appropriate mining rigs, securing coin wallets, selecting mining pools, obtaining suitable mining programs, and implementing proper security measures for storing earned coins.

> "Miners compete with each other to be the first in the race among their counterparts in solving those difficult cryptographic problems."
**Quick Take**: Mining has evolved from a hobbyist activity to a competitive industry requiring significant investment and technical expertise, with cloud mining offering accessible alternatives.

### Buy and Hold Strategy
The buy and hold strategy, adapted from traditional stock market investing, involves purchasing cryptocurrencies and holding them for extended periods regardless of market fluctuations. In cryptocurrency markets, holding periods typically range from months to a year or two (compared to three-plus years in traditional markets) due to the accelerated market pace. This strategy reduces stress from daily trading and temporary downturns, with historical data showing approximately 140% growth in major cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin and Ethereum. Implementation requires choosing promising coins, storing them securely in hardware wallets, and maintaining emotional discipline during price fluctuations.

> "The first and foremost thing an investor should do is to study the currency well and understand the functional aspect of the coin. If you don't know what a blockchain is and you invest in cryptocoins, then you are going to get trapped somewhere in the middle of the process."
**Quick Take**: Successful buy and hold investing requires understanding the fundamental technology and utility of the cryptocurrency, not just following price trends.

### Safe Exchange Practices
Choosing secure cryptocurrency exchanges is critical for investment safety. Important criteria include website security, high liquidity, transparent fee structures, sufficient currency pairs, multiple payment options, positive user reviews, and user-friendly interfaces. Common investing mistakes to avoid include concentrating all investments in one cryptocurrency, becoming obsessed with crypto investments to the exclusion of diversification, emotional panic selling during price dips, and engaging in day trading without sufficient expertise. Security practices include using updated antivirus software, implementing two-factor authentication, using VPNs for trading activities, and maintaining regular offline data backups.

> "Cryptocurrency transactions can never be reversed which makes it all the more important to choose the right cryptocurrency exchanges. If you had sent your crypto coin to the wrong recipient, because you typed an incorrect 'wallet address,' your coin is gone."
**Quick Take**: The irreversible nature of cryptocurrency transactions demands meticulous attention to security protocols and exchange selection.

### Alternative Cryptocurrency Strategies
Beyond Bitcoin and Ethereum, several altcoins offer investment opportunities: Litecoin (faster block generation), ZCash (privacy-focused transactions), Dash (enhanced anonymity), Ripple (low-cost international payments), and Monero (complete transaction privacy). Altcoin strategies include investing during ICO phases for maximum growth potential, buying coins during first market dips, and continuous market monitoring with technical analysis. Each altcoin serves specific niches and requires understanding of its unique value proposition and market dynamics.

> "If you want to comprehend the difference between altcoin investment and Bitcoin investment, you will need to read the graph of the performances. The Bitcoin performance market will be clear, easy to understand and guarantee good returns but when it comes to the performance graph of altcoins, you will see a lot of chaos and confusion."
**Quick Take**: Altcoin investing requires higher risk tolerance and market analysis expertise compared to Bitcoin, but offers potentially higher returns for informed investors.

### Risk Management Strategy
Effective risk management in cryptocurrency investing involves thorough research on coin fundamentals (including reading white papers), avoiding low-volume coins with liquidity issues, maintaining professional emotional discipline, and portfolio diversification across multiple cryptocurrencies. The key is understanding the underlying idea and technology behind each coin rather than simply following price trends. Risk management strategies should be based on personal risk handling capacity, with gradual portfolio rebalancing based on market conditions and financial situations. Successful investors focus on ideas and technologies with long-term potential rather than short-term price movements.

> "A successful investor who is consistently making good and strong returns will have effective risk management strategies in place, just in case he runs into a 'worst case scenario.'"
**Quick Take**: Risk management is the differentiator between successful long-term cryptocurrency investors and those who lose money chasing quick profits.

## Action Items
- Research cryptocurrency fundamentals thoroughly before investing any capital
- Start with small investments (1-10% of total portfolio) to understand market dynamics
- Implement proper security measures including hardware wallets and two-factor authentication
- Develop a clear investment strategy with defined entry and exit points
- Diversify cryptocurrency holdings across multiple established coins
- Avoid emotional decision-making during market volatility
- Keep regular backups of wallet files and security credentials
- Stay informed about regulatory developments and market trends

## Conclusion
This book provides a comprehensive foundation for cryptocurrency investors seeking practical strategies rather than theoretical knowledge. Its value lies in the systematic approach to cryptocurrency investment, emphasizing risk management, strategic planning, and emotional discipline—elements often overlooked in the hype-driven cryptocurrency space. The book is most valuable for investors who understand cryptocurrency basics but need structured approaches for portfolio development and risk management.

The biggest reason to read this book is its focus on preventing common cryptocurrency investing mistakes while providing actionable frameworks for long-term success. While some information may feel dated given the rapid evolution of cryptocurrency markets, the fundamental principles of strategic planning, risk management, and emotional discipline remain timeless and valuable for both new and experienced investors navigating the volatile cryptocurrency landscape.
