# Initial Coin Offering: Make $11,000 in 2018
**Original Title**: Initial Coin Offering: How You Can Make an Additional $11,000 Income this 2018!
**Author**: Edward A. Harrod | **Year**: 2018

## Summary
This practical guide breaks down everything you need to know about Initial Coin Offerings (ICOs) - the cryptocurrency equivalent of stock market IPOs. The book walks readers through understanding ICOs, identifying scams, evaluating potential investments, and participating in token sales to generate substantial returns. Written during the 2018 cryptocurrency boom, it captures the excitement and opportunity of the early crypto crowdfunding era.

Perfect for crypto beginners and entrepreneurs looking to capitalize on blockchain opportunities, this book provides a roadmap to navigate the unregulated world of token sales while minimizing risks and maximizing potential gains.

## Core Insights

### Understanding ICO Fundamentals
An Initial Coin Offering (ICO) is a crowdfunding mechanism where startups sell digital tokens to raise capital, similar to how companies sell shares in an IPO. Tokens come in two types: utility tokens that provide access to platforms, and security tokens that represent ownership stakes. Unlike traditional markets, ICOs operate largely unregulated, offering both massive opportunity and significant risk.

> "An initial coin offering, or ICO, is a type of crowdfunding used in cryptocurrency markets, whereby startup companies provide 'tokens' to investors in an effort to establish their business."

**Quick Take**: ICOs allow anyone to invest in early-stage blockchain projects, potentially earning returns of 100x or more if the project succeeds.

### Why Ethereum Dominates ICOs
Ethereum became the preferred platform for ICOs because Bitcoin was designed as a simple currency system, not as a platform for complex applications. Bitcoin lacks the programming capabilities (it's not Turing-complete) needed for sophisticated token distribution systems. Ethereum offers faster transaction times, smart contract functionality, and standardized ERC-20 token protocols that make it ideal for ICO launches.

> "Ethereum adopts the ERC-20 protocol standard for all tokens in its platform, which means there are guidelines that should be followed in making transfers, as well as being able to make every Ethereum token compatible with one another."

**Quick Take**: Most ICOs run on Ethereum because it provides the technical infrastructure needed for token sales, not because it's inherently better than other platforms.

### Red Flags of ICO Scams
The unregulated nature of ICOs attracted numerous scams, with major warning signs including: empty GitHub repositories, excessive pre-mined tokens (where developers keep >50% of supply), fake team profiles, guaranteed profit promises, and missing or plagiarized white papers. The SEC shut down several major scams like PlexCoin, which raised $15 million through fraudulent claims.

> "A project being open-sourced means that the entire code base is uploaded to GitHub, an online code repository, for everyone to see and examine."

**Quick Take**: Legitimate ICOs have transparent development activity, real teams with verifiable experience, and technical documentation rather than just marketing hype.

### Investment Strategy: HODL vs Quick Flips
The book recommends two approaches: holding tokens long-term (HODL - "hold on for dear life") or taking quick profits after initial exchange listings. While early Ethereum investors saw 152,000% returns by holding, most tokens don't achieve such success. The key is emotional control and resisting FUD (fear, uncertainty, and doubt) spread by market manipulators.

> "HODL eventually came to mean for some as 'hold on for dear life,' and while there is something to laugh about its origins, the term has stuck around cryptocurrency as an investment strategy for some time now."

**Quick Take**: Successful ICO investing requires patience and discipline - the biggest gains come from projects you hold through volatility, not from panic selling during dips.

### The $11,000 Strategy
The book's headline promise focuses on diversifying investments across multiple ICOs, securing bonus tokens from early participation, and thorough research. The author uses the example of EOS, which went from $0.88 to $15-20, turning a $550 investment into $11,000 within 10 months. Success requires identifying projects before they gain mainstream attention and avoiding hype-driven investments.

> "EOS started with their ICO for 88 cents each, and now it is priced on exchanges at the $15-$20 range. So if you bought $550 worth, you could've made $1100 back in just a span of 10 months!"

**Quick Take**: The path to $11,000 involves investing in the right projects early, not following the crowd into already-popular tokens.

## Action Items
1. **Set up cryptocurrency wallets** - Create both online (Coinbase for trading) and offline (MyEtherWallet/Mist for storage) solutions
2. **Research ICO listings daily** - Monitor sites like ICORating, CoinSchedule, and ListICO for new opportunities
3. **Evaluate white papers thoroughly** - Look for technical depth, clear use cases, and contingency plans rather than marketing fluff
4. **Check GitHub repositories** - Verify that projects have active development and aren't just empty repositories
5. **Join crypto communities** - Participate in Facebook groups, Twitter feeds, and forums to stay informed about new ICOs
6. **Diversify investments** - Spread capital across multiple ICOs to minimize the impact of any single failure
7. **Secure bonus tokens** - Register early for whitelisted ICOs to receive additional tokens for early participation

## Questions to Consider
- Are you investing with disposable income you can afford to lose?
- Does the project solve a real problem or is it just blockchain hype?
- Is the team verifiably experienced in relevant fields?
- What makes this token valuable beyond speculation?
- How does the project differ from established competitors?
- What happens if the team fails to deliver on their roadmap?

## Conclusion
**Is this worth your time?** For historical perspective on the 2018 crypto boom and understanding ICO mechanics, yes. For current investment advice, the market has evolved significantly since publication.

The book captures a unique moment in financial history when ordinary investors could participate directly in blockchain startups through token sales. While some of the specific platforms and strategies may be outdated, the fundamental principles of research, due diligence, and risk management remain valuable.

The biggest reason to read this is understanding how financial innovation creates opportunities - and risks - outside traditional markets. The author's practical approach to evaluating investments serves as a good template for analyzing new financial technologies, even as the specific ICO market has matured and professionalized.

**Final take**: Read this as a historical guide to the crypto gold rush of 2018, not as current investment advice, but appreciate the timeless lessons about spotting legitimate opportunities in emerging markets.
