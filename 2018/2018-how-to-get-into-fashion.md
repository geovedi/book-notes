# How to Get Into Fashion
**Authors**: Eunice Olumide | **Year**: 2018
**Category**: Art & Creativity

## Summary
This comprehensive guide demystifies the fashion industry and provides a clear roadmap for aspiring models and fashion professionals. Written by Eunice Olumide, a supermodel who worked with Alexander McQueen, Mulberry, and other top brands, the book offers insider knowledge on breaking into fashion while emphasizing the importance of self-belief and authenticity.

The book serves as both a practical guide and motivational mentor, helping readers identify their unique look, find representation, build portfolios, and navigate the industry's complexities. Beyond modeling, it explores various fashion careers including buying, styling, photography, and public relations.

Perfect for aspiring fashion professionals who want realistic industry insights and actionable steps to launch their careers, this book provides the essential knowledge needed to avoid common pitfalls and maximize opportunities.

## Core Insights

### 1. Master Your Mindset First
Success in fashion begins with cultivating self-belief and resilience. The industry's challenges require strong mental foundations and the ability to handle rejection gracefully.

> "If you don't believe in yourself and think you deserve to be successful, it's highly unlikely that anyone else will believe in you either."

**Quick Take**: Your mindset determines your industry altitude. Develop patience, perseverance, and surround yourself with positive influences who support your dreams.

### 2. Identify Your Professional Look
Understanding where you fit in the fashion industry saves time and prevents wasted effort. The book outlines distinct model categories from editorial high fashion to commercial work.

> "It is vital you identify your own 'look' early on as this will help you save invaluable time and money."

**Quick Take**: Research different modeling categories and honestly assess which aligns with your natural appearance, height, and body type rather than forcing yourself into an unsuitable niche.

### 3. Agent Acquisition Strategy
Securing representation requires professional preparation and persistence. Agents seek natural beauty, confidence without arrogance, and clear potential for commercial success.

**Quick Win**: Attend agency open calls with minimal makeup, hair tied back, and simple Polaroid photos showing headshots, mid-length, full-body, and profile shots.

### 4. Portfolio Development Excellence
Your portfolio is your professional calling card. Quality outweighs quantity—select images that showcase your range while maintaining exclusivity and value.

**Quick Win**: Study fashion magazines and agency websites to understand industry standards. Practice poses regularly and seek test shoots with reputable photographers who understand your brand.

### 5. Industry Career Expansion
Fashion extends far beyond modeling. The book details numerous career paths including fashion buying, styling, photography, public relations, and creative direction.

**Quick Win**: If modeling isn't your calling, explore roles like fashion buyer (attending shows and selecting collections), stylist (creating visual narratives), or photographer (capturing fashion stories).

- "Remember: you are the master of your life and you alone control where you go, who you see, how you are seen, how you live and how you dress."

- "Modelling, like any other industry, is a job. So learn not to take rejection personally. Agents will choose whom they think is most suitable for them."

- "You're like an actor but you're not making a movie. So since you can't use your voice, you must learn to communicate through your facial expressions and – most importantly – with your eyes."

- "No reputable fashion agency will ask you for money to join them. Agents make their money from taking commission on jobs that they book you for."

## Action Items

- **Self-Assessment**: Determine your natural look and research which modeling category best suits your appearance and goals
- **Professional Preparation**: Create simple Polaroid photos showing headshot, waist-up, full-length, and profile views
- **Agency Research**: Compile a list of reputable agencies and attend their open calls during designated times
- **Portfolio Development**: Practice poses by studying fashion magazines and consider selective test shoots with professional photographers
- **Industry Education**: Learn the business side of fashion including commission structures, payment systems, and career progression
- **Alternative Careers**: Explore non-modeling fashion roles if you discover they better align with your skills and interests
- **Network Building**: Maintain positive relationships with photographers, stylists, and other industry professionals

## Questions to Consider

- How will you maintain your authenticity while adapting to industry demands?
- What unique qualities do you bring that could set you apart in the fashion world?
- How will you handle rejection and maintain self-belief during challenging periods?

## Conclusion

**Is this book worth your time?** Absolutely. For anyone serious about entering the fashion industry, this guide provides invaluable insider knowledge that could save years of trial and error. Olumide's honest approach reveals both the glamour and challenges, preparing readers for reality rather than fantasy.

**The biggest reason to read it**: The book combines practical industry knowledge with essential mindset coaching, recognizing that success in fashion requires both technical skills and mental fortitude. Her experience as one of Scotland's first black supermodels adds valuable perspective on diversity and perseverance.

While focused primarily on modeling, the book's insights on self-belief, professional presentation, and industry navigation apply to any fashion career. It's particularly valuable for its realistic approach—Olumide doesn't sugarcoat the difficulties but provides concrete strategies to overcome them.

For aspiring fashion professionals, this isn't just a career guide—it's a mentorship in book form, offering the wisdom that typically comes only through years of industry experience.
