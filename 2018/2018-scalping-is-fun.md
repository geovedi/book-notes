# Scalping is Fun!
**Authors**: Heikin Ashi Trader | **Year**: 2018 | **Category**: Business & Finance

## Summary
This book introduces scalping as a fast-paced trading strategy that capitalizes on tiny price movements in the market. The author presents scalping not just as a technical approach but as an art form that requires flow state—where traders operate in harmony with market movements, much like dancers or elite athletes. The method uses Heikin Ashi charts to identify micro-trends and capitalize on brief imbalances between supply and demand.

Perfect for active traders who want to maximize daily opportunities and those frustrated by traditional technical analysis approaches. Readers will learn a systematic way to extract consistent profits from market micro-movements while maintaining strict risk management protocols.

## Core Insights

### The Scalper's Mindset: Trading as Flow State
Successful scalping operates in a "flow state" where traders move in harmony with market movements. This differs from analytical approaches because scalpers feel the market's rhythm rather than just interpret patterns. The joy of scalping comes from this alignment—if it doesn't feel like dancing with the market, scalping may not be your style.

> "The joy that one can feel in while using this method arises when the trader is in line with the market. Psychologists speak in the context of 'flow'—It is the feeling of happiness that, for example, top athletes perceive during their performances."

**Quick Take**: Scalping should feel natural and enjoyable, not forced or stressful. If you're not experiencing flow, you're probably not ready for this approach.

### Market Mechanics: Supply and Demand Imbalances
Every market movement represents an imbalance between supply and demand. Scalpers exploit these micro-imbalances that occur in seconds and minutes, rather than waiting for larger corrections. When prices reach previous demand or offer levels, they often "turn" as large institutional orders get triggered.

> "Each market is a site where supply and demand meet. Prices vary because there is always an imbalance between demand and supply. As supply or demand comes out of balance, the price will return to a new equilibrium."

**Quick Take**: Focus on identifying where large orders are waiting rather than predicting where they should be.

### The Heikin Ashi Advantage
Heikin Ashi charts provide superior trend visualization compared to traditional candlesticks. They smooth out market noise and clearly show trend strength through candle size and wicks. This makes it easier to identify capital flows and precise turning points in micro-trends.

**Key signals to watch:**
- Candle size indicates trend strength
- Small candles suggest weakening trends
- Long wicks signal trend exhaustion
- Failure to make higher highs/lower lows indicates consolidation

**Quick Take**: Heikin Ashi charts reveal the true market story by filtering out noise and highlighting institutional capital flows.

### The Scalping Setup: Color Changes
The core strategy is beautifully simple: enter when candle colors change (indicating trend reversal) and exit when they change again. This typically involves trading counter-trend movements in 1-minute charts, holding positions for seconds to minutes with tight stops (5-10 pips).

> "We get into the market as soon we detect a change in a clear trend, which is visible through the change of the color of the candles. We get out of the trade as soon as the color of the candle changes again."

**Quick Take**: Let the Heikin Ashi candles dictate your entries and exits—don't anticipate or hesitate when colors change.

### Risk Management: The Mathematical Truth
Risk management isn't optional—it's mathematical necessity. The capital recovery table shows that losing 15% is critical, and losing 50% makes recovery virtually impossible. Successful scalpers focus on keeping losses small through position sizing and strict stops.

> "There is a random distribution of winners and losers. The more you trade and gain experience in the markets, the sooner you will realize that you have no influence on the outcome of the trades."

**Quick Take**: Control what you can—stop losses and position size. Let statistics handle the rest.

## Action Items
- **Test your mindset**: Practice identifying market flow state before risking real capital
- **Master Heikin Ashi charts**: Spend time learning to read candle colors, sizes, and wicks
- **Start with 1-minute charts**: Use the recommended timeframe for beginners
- **Implement strict stops**: Always use 5-10 pip stops regardless of your confidence level
- **Track opportunity costs**: Monitor missed trades to understand the true cost of waiting
- **Limit trading sessions**: Trade maximum 2-3 hours daily to maintain focus and prevent burnout
- **Adjust position sizes**: Increase when winning, decrease dramatically during drawdowns

## Questions to Consider
- Do you naturally enjoy fast-paced decision making or do you prefer more analytical approaches?
- Are you willing to accept that individual trade outcomes are largely random?
- Can you maintain emotional discipline when holding positions for just seconds?
- Do you have the mental stamina to focus intensely for 2-3 hour trading sessions?
- Are you prepared to cut losses quickly without hesitation or second-guessing?

## Conclusion
This is absolutely worth your time if you're considering active trading but feel frustrated by traditional technical analysis approaches. The biggest reason to read it is that it re-frames scalping as an art form rather than a mechanical system, emphasizing the psychological flow state that separates successful traders from struggling ones.

The book excels at demystifying scalping while being honest about the challenges. It's not a get-rich-quick scheme but a professional approach to exploiting market micro-structures. The Heikin Ashi strategy alone could transform your trading, but the real value comes from understanding the scalper mindset and risk management framework.

Whether you're new to trading or experienced with other timeframes, this guide provides a complete foundation for profitable scalping. Just be prepared—it requires the dedication of learning a musical instrument or sport, not just reading a manual.
