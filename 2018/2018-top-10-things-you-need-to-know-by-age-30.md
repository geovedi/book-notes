# Top 10 Things You Need to Know by Age 30
**Authors**: Marc Stachado | **Year**: 2018 | **Category**: Self-Help & Personal Development

## Summary
This practical guide offers essential life advice for navigating the transition from carefree 20s to more practical 30s. The book presents ten fundamental areas young adults should master before entering their third decade, covering financial wisdom, personal growth, health priorities, and mental resilience. Each chapter provides actionable insights designed to build a foundation for a more fulfilling and secure future.

Young adults and those approaching their 30s will gain practical strategies for financial security, personal development, and life management. The book serves as a roadmap for making conscious decisions that compound over time, helping readers avoid common regrets and build lasting habits.

## Core Insights

### 1. Invest Early, Invest Often
Starting investments in your 20s leverages the power of compound interest, transforming modest savings into substantial wealth over time. With $10k invested at 5% interest in your 20s, you could have $70k by age 60. Young investors also have the freedom to take calculated risks and recover from potential losses, something that becomes increasingly difficult with age and family responsibilities.

> "Time is money. And it is even truer when it comes to investment."

**Quick Take**: Even small, regular investments in your 20s create massive advantages through compounding. Start now, regardless of how little you can invest.

### 2. Travel and Explore the World
Your 20s offer the perfect combination of health, minimal responsibilities, and adaptability for world travel. Beyond creating memories, traveling builds self-confidence, exposes you to new cultures, and helps clarify what you truly want from life. Modern remote work opportunities make it increasingly possible to earn while exploring different destinations.

**Quick Take**: Travel isn't just vacation—it's education in perspective, resilience, and self-discovery that shapes your life decisions.

### 3. Be Empathetic
Developing empathy in your 20s prepares you for more meaningful relationships and personal growth in your 30s. By recognizing that everyone faces unseen struggles, you become more considerate and effective in your interactions. Simple acts of kindness and volunteering not only help others but provide perspective on your own blessings and challenges.

> "Empathy is seeing with the eyes of another, listening with the ears of another, and feeling with the heart of another."

**Quick Take**: Empathy isn't just a virtue—it's a practical skill that improves all relationships and decision-making.

### 4. Beware of Credits and Loans
Credit card debt in your 20s becomes a catastrophic burden in your 30s when expenses multiply. The compounding interest that benefits investments works against you with debt. Save for purchases instead of financing them, and if you must use credit, never miss payments. Bad credit damages not just your finances but future relationships and peace of mind.

**Quick Take**: Debt is negative investing—every dollar paid in interest is a dollar that could have been compounding for your future.

### 5. Try Something Adventurous
Confronting fears through adventure builds resilience that transfers to all life challenges. Whether bungee jumping, mountain climbing, or any activity that pushes your comfort zone, these experiences become lifetime memories that remind you of your capacity for growth. Studies show adventurous people handle unpredictable situations better throughout life.

**Quick Take**: Courage is like a muscle—the more you use it in your 20s, the stronger it becomes for life's inevitable challenges.

### 6. Build Your Reading Bucket-List
Continuous learning through reading accelerates personal and professional growth. Create specific reading goals tied to skills you want to master or knowledge areas that interest you. Online reviews and reading communities help select the most valuable books. Reading consistently in your 20s builds a knowledge foundation that compounds throughout your career and life.

**Quick Take**: Reading is the most efficient way to acquire wisdom that took others decades to learn.

### 7. Always Have a Side Hustle
A side hustle provides financial security and personal fulfillment beyond your primary job. Starting in your 20s allows you to build something meaningful while having the safety net of steady income. Many successful entrepreneurs transitioned their side hustles into full-time businesses. Side projects also accelerate skill development and boost self-confidence.

> "Don't put all your eggs in one basket."

**Quick Take**: Side hustles aren't just about extra income—they're about building options and discovering passions.

### 8. Treasure Your Health
Health habits formed in your 20s typically last a lifetime. Your body is at peak potential, making it easier to establish routines for exercise, nutrition, and sleep. Avoid relying on frozen convenience foods, find enjoyable physical activities, and establish consistent sleep patterns. These investments compound just like financial ones, paying dividends in energy and vitality for decades.

**Quick Take**: Your 60-year-old self is either thanking or cursing the health decisions you make in your 20s.

### 9. Your Parents Don't Have All the Answers
As you mature, you must become the primary authority in your own life. Your parents' wisdom comes from different times and circumstances. Their love and support remain constant, but their answers may not fit your unique challenges. This transition requires self-reliance and continuous learning from diverse sources.

**Quick Take**: The goal of good parenting is to make yourself obsolete—your 30s are when you prove their success.

### 10. Don't Sweat The Small Stuff
Mastering the art of letting go preserves mental energy for what truly matters. Your 20s are the perfect time to develop stress management skills and learn to distinguish between temporary inconveniences and significant problems. This skill prevents unnecessary drama and heartbreak while building resilience for life's bigger challenges.

**Quick Take**: Peace of mind is your most valuable asset—protect it fiercely by ignoring minor irritations.

## Action Items
- Start investing immediately, even with small amounts
- Create a travel budget and destination list
- Practice daily empathy through small acts of kindness
- Eliminate credit card debt and save for major purchases
- Schedule one adventurous activity that scares you
- Build a reading list with specific goals and timeline
- Develop a side hustle based on your skills or interests
- Establish consistent exercise, nutrition, and sleep habits
- Take full responsibility for your life decisions
- Practice letting go of minor annoyances and focus on priorities

## Questions to Consider
- What small investment could you start today that will compound over time?
- Which fear could you confront that would build the most confidence?
- What daily health habit would have the biggest long-term impact?
- Are you relying on others' answers instead of developing your own wisdom?
- What "small stuff" is currently stealing your peace of mind?

## Conclusion
This book delivers straightforward, actionable advice for building a foundation in your 20s that supports a more fulfilling 30s and beyond. The strength lies in its practical approach—focusing on habits and decisions that compound over time rather than complex theories.

The ten areas covered address the most common regrets people have about their 20s: not saving enough, not traveling, neglecting health, and failing to develop self-reliance. By addressing these proactively, readers can avoid future regrets and build momentum for continued growth.

**Is this worth your time?** Absolutely if you're in your 20s or early 30s. The biggest reason to read it is the urgency—it's easier to build good habits in your 20s than to break bad habits in your 30s. The advice isn't revolutionary, but its value lies in the timing and presentation as a cohesive life strategy. Implement even half of these principles, and you'll significantly improve your future trajectory.
