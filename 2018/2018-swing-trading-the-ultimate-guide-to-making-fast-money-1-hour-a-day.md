# 2018 Swing Trading: The Ultimate Guide to Making Fast Money 1 Hour a Day
**Authors**: Victor Lucas | **Year**: 2018

## Summary
This practical guide introduces swing trading as an intermediate investment strategy that balances the intensity of day trading with the patience of buy-and-hold investing. The book presents swing trading as a method for capturing short-to-medium term price movements (typically lasting a few days to several weeks) while requiring only about an hour of daily management time.

The book targets beginners who feel intimidated by trading terminology and capital requirements, breaking down complex concepts into accessible frameworks. It aims to help readers escape the "rat race" by making their money work for them through strategic market participation.

This guide is perfect for working professionals seeking additional income streams without committing to full-time trading, or anyone looking to understand how to generate consistent returns through systematic market participation.

## Core Insights

### Understanding Swing Trading vs. Other Methods
Swing trading occupies the middle ground between day trading and buy-and-hold investing. Unlike day trading where positions are opened and closed within the same day, or buy-and-hold where positions might be maintained for months or years, swing trading captures intermediate price "swings" that typically last from a few days to several weeks.

This method allows traders to ride market momentum in either direction—profiting from both upward and downward price movements—while avoiding the stress of constant monitoring required in day trading. The strategy relies on identifying and capitalizing on market oscillations rather than attempting to time exact market tops and bottoms.

> "Swing trading is, therefore, the most flexible method of trading, which tries to balance the defects of other, more extreme methods."

**Quick Take**: Swing trading offers the best of both worlds—more profit potential than long-term investing with less stress than day trading.

### Capital Requirements and Risk Management
The book emphasizes that trading capital should be "available capital"—money you can afford to lose without affecting your basic financial stability. For beginners, the author recommends starting with as little as €100 as "testing capital" to gain experience, while noting that generating meaningful supplemental income requires €3,000-5,000 of available capital.

The critical risk management rule is to never risk more than 2-5% of total capital on any single trade. This approach ensures that even a series of losses won't wipe out your trading account, allowing you to stay in the game long enough to develop consistent profitable strategies.

> "The money we devote to trading if lost, must not, in any way, affect the status of our financial stability greatly."

**Quick Take**: Start small with money you can afford to lose, and never risk more than 5% of your total capital on any single trade.

### The Three Categories of Trading Risk
The book identifies three primary risk categories in swing trading:

1. **Scam Risk**: Avoid by choosing regulated brokers with proper licenses (CONSOB, CYSEC) and reliable payment processes
2. **Capital Risk**: Mitigate through position sizing (2-5% rule) and strategic diversification across multiple trades
3. **Psychological Risk**: Manage by maintaining emotional discipline, avoiding impulsive decisions after gains or losses

Professional traders distinguish themselves not by avoiding losses, but by managing risks systematically and maintaining emotional equilibrium regardless of market outcomes.

> "Even the best traders suffer losses from time to time, the important thing is that they are limited, contained and managed in the best possible ways."

**Quick Take**: Success in trading comes from managing risk, not avoiding it entirely. Emotional discipline is as important as technical analysis.

### Technical Analysis and Multi-Timeframe Strategy
Swing traders use technical analysis to identify optimal entry and exit points, looking for patterns that signal when corrective movements might end and the prevailing trend will resume. A key technique is "multi-timeframe analysis," where traders examine multiple time intervals (daily, hourly, 15-minute charts) to make informed decisions.

For example, if the daily chart shows an upward trend but shorter timeframes indicate a potential short-term decline, a trader might still profit from short positions despite the long-term bullish outlook. This flexibility allows swing traders to profit from various market conditions rather than being locked into a single directional bias.

**Quick Take**: Use multiple timeframes to understand both long-term trends and short-term opportunities—don't rely on a single perspective.

### Getting Started: Practical Steps
The book recommends starting with demo accounts to practice strategies without risking real money. Once comfortable, begin with small positions using derivative instruments like binary options or Contracts for Difference (CFDs) rather than direct stock purchases, as these offer more flexibility and lower transaction costs.

Focus on major, well-known companies listed on primary stock exchanges rather than obscure smaller companies. The author suggests specific platforms like Plus500, 24option, and IQ Option for beginners due to their user-friendly interfaces and reliable services.

**Quick Take**: Practice with demo accounts first, start with major stocks, and consider using CFDs or options for greater flexibility with lower costs.

The emphasis on starting small with demo accounts, implementing strict risk management, and maintaining emotional discipline provides a realistic foundation for those looking to generate supplemental income through trading.

The guide is worth your time if you're seeking a balanced approach to market participation that offers more profit potential than traditional investing without requiring the full-time commitment of day trading. The biggest reason to read this book is its accessibility—Victor Lucas breaks down complex trading concepts into understandable frameworks that beginners can implement immediately, making the prospect of generating additional income through market participation feel achievable rather than overwhelming.
