# 2018 Online Business Secrets: 2 Books for 1
**Authors**: Marc Hayes | **Year**: 2018

## Summary
This comprehensive guide combines two books that teach entrepreneurs how to build profitable online businesses using Alibaba and Shopify. The first book reveals how to source products from Alibaba and implement dropshipping strategies, while the second provides a complete roadmap for setting up and marketing a Shopify store. The book is designed for beginners who want to start an e-commerce business quickly with minimal upfront investment.

Readers will learn practical strategies for finding suppliers, avoiding scams, choosing products to sell, setting up an online store, and implementing effective marketing techniques. This guide is ideal for aspiring entrepreneurs looking to escape the 9-5 grind and create their own online business with low startup costs.

## Core Insights

### How to Win with Alibaba: Finding Reliable Suppliers
Alibaba is the world's largest retailer where you can source products from suppliers worldwide. The platform offers tools like AliSourcePro to help you find suppliers, but success requires careful vetting and due diligence. Look for verification badges and Gold Supplier status, but never trust profiles alone—research companies beyond Alibaba, request samples, and consider hiring translators for language barriers.

> "Don't let a simple accident revolving around language destroy your business before it even has a chance."

**Quick Take**: Always verify supplier legitimacy through independent research, on-site inspections, and clear communication. Never rely solely on Alibaba profile information.

### Understanding Key Alibaba Terms and Payment Safety
Critical terms include FOB (Free On Board) pricing where sellers pay shipping to the marine port, and MOQ (Minimum Order Quantity) which sets minimum purchase amounts. For payments, avoid bank transfers due to fraud risks—use PayPal or Escrow services which offer buyer protection. Remember that suppliers may reduce quality if you negotiate prices too aggressively.

> "Bargaining is the name of the game but be aware if you're requesting a twenty-five percent reduction in their quote then you might be surprised to see the final product be in worse quality."

**Quick Take**: Balance competitive pricing with quality expectations, and always use secure payment methods that protect your investment.

### The Dropshipping Revolution: Low-Risk E-commerce
Dropshipping eliminates the need for inventory storage—you only order products when customers buy them. This model offers incredible flexibility, scalability, and minimal startup costs. You can sell a vast product range without warehouse expenses, work from anywhere with internet access, and easily test different products without financial risk.

> "With drop shipping you could have a backpack in any color and sell kids' toys, vacuums, and trash bins—whatever you'd like to sell, you can."

**Quick Take**: Start with dropshipping to test markets and products, then consider inventory storage once you've validated what sells.

### Dropshipping Challenges: Quality Control and Competition
The main drawbacks include lack of quality control (you don't see products before shipping), high competition in popular product categories, and hidden costs that eat into profit margins. Everyone is dropshipping the same popular products, making differentiation crucial. Customer service responsibility remains yours even when third parties handle fulfillment.

> "Shipping out some poorly made shoes that didn't pass your eye can hurt your business and damage it with poor reviews."

**Quick Take**: Focus on niche products and build strong supplier relationships to maintain quality control despite the distance.

### Choosing Your Sales Platform: Multiple Options
While Alibaba offers huge traffic, diversify across platforms like Amazon (with its review system and analytics tools), eBay (for auctions and instant sales), or create your own Shopify store for direct customer relationships. Consider platform-specific advantages: Amazon's tools for product analysis, eBay's auction format, or Shopify's customization and brand control.

> "You'll never be Wal-Mart by trying to compete with Wal-Mart. You can't fight Costco at their own game."

**Quick Take**: Start with established platforms for traffic, then build your own storefront for brand control and customer relationships.

### Shopify Setup: Choosing the Right Plan
Shopify offers four plans: Lite ($9/month for point-of-sale only), Basic ($29/month for new stores), Standard ($79/month with gift cards and abandoned cart recovery), and Advanced ($299/month for large businesses). Start with Basic to minimize costs, upgrade when you need additional features like professional analytics or multiple staff accounts.

> "The basic plan incorporates everything you could need to open an online shop, including unlimited product listings."

**Quick Take**: Begin with Shopify Basic ($29/month) and upgrade only when your business volume justifies additional features.

### Legal Essentials: Protecting Your Business
Register your business as needed, understand sales tax requirements (typically charge only for customers in your state), plan for quarterly income tax payments, and avoid selling counterfeit products. Comply with shipping regulations and employment laws if hiring contractors or employees.

> "Taking the time to understand how to legally run a business ensures that you're not left scratching your head when the cops or the IRS comes knocking."

**Quick Take**: Consult with professionals about legal and tax requirements specific to your location and business structure.

### Market Research: Finding Your Profitable Niche
Success comes from targeting specific niches rather than broad categories. Use Google AdWords Keyword Planner to assess search volume and competition, run surveys to understand customer needs, and focus on solving problems for your target demographic. High search volume with low competition indicates promising opportunities.

> "The ideal situation is a high volume of searches for several of your related keywords and key phrases, and then a low amount of competition."

**Quick Take**: Research keywords thoroughly, survey potential customers, and choose niches where you can serve specific customer needs better than competitors.

### Product Sourcing Strategies: Multiple Options
Source products from local yard sales and thrift stores (for unique items), create your own products (if you have skills), sell digital products (for passive income), or work with wholesalers and manufacturers. Attend trade shows to network with suppliers, and always test product quality before committing to large orders.

> "Taking the time to make sure a product will be worthwhile will save you a lot of headache in the long run."

**Quick Take**: Start small, test product quality personally, and build relationships with reliable suppliers as your business grows.

### Marketing Mastery: Niche-Focused Strategies
Stick to your niche rather than trying to sell everything to everyone. Use specific keywords rather than broad terms, create valuable blog content, build social media presence, and develop an email list with lead magnets like discount codes. Test multiple ad variations to optimize performance and focus on solving customer problems rather than just selling products.

> "Sticking to your 'niche,' your demographic, your laser-targeted focus is what really makes it easy to keep marketing."

**Quick Take**: Create valuable content for your niche, build an email list from day one, and continuously test and optimize your marketing campaigns.

## Conclusion

This book provides a solid foundation for starting an e-commerce business with minimal risk and investment. The strength lies in its practical, step-by-step approach to both product sourcing (Alibaba) and store setup (Shopify). Hayes demystifies complex topics like supplier negotiations, payment security, and marketing strategies, making them accessible to beginners.

The biggest value comes from the emphasis on starting small through dropshipping, focusing on specific niches rather than trying to compete with major retailers, and using data-driven market research. The book is worth your time if you're looking for a realistic roadmap to launch an online business without risking significant capital.

While the content provides excellent starting points, success ultimately depends on your willingness to implement these strategies consistently, handle customer service challenges, and adapt to market feedback. This guide gives you the tools—your execution will determine the results.
