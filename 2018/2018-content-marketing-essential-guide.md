# Content Marketing: Essential Guide to Learn Step-by-Step the Best Content Marketing Strategies
**Authors**: Joe Wilson Schaefer | **Year**: 2018

## Summary
Content marketing is the strategic approach of creating and distributing valuable, relevant, and consistent content to attract and retain a clearly defined audience — ultimately driving profitable customer action. Unlike traditional marketing, content marketing focuses on providing genuine value that people actively seek out rather than try to avoid.

This comprehensive guide demystifies content marketing for beginners, offering practical strategies for businesses looking to leverage content as a powerful marketing tool. The book covers everything from foundational concepts and mindset development to audience research, content creation, promotion strategies, and common pitfalls to avoid.

Small business owners, marketing professionals, entrepreneurs, and anyone looking to establish thought leadership in their industry will benefit from this step-by-step approach to building successful content marketing campaigns that drive sustainable growth.

## Core Insights

### The Foundation of Content Marketing
Content marketing revolves around creating valuable information that your target audience actively seeks out rather than trying to avoid. The key differentiator is the word "valuable" — content marketing succeeds when people search for your content instead of running away from it like traditional advertising.

> "The basic concept behind content marketing is simple: distribute creative content consistently that is relevant and valuable to your clearly defined, current, and intended audience. The objective of content marketing is to develop profitable customer behavior."

**Quick Take**: Focus on solving your audience's problems and answering their questions before they even ask. When you provide genuine value, sales become a natural byproduct rather than a pushy transaction.

### Understanding the Content Marketing Funnel
Content marketing targets customers in the early stages of the buying cycle — specifically the identification and research phases. By educating potential customers before they're ready to buy, you position yourself as a trusted authority when it's time to make a purchase decision.

The buying cycle consists of four stages: Identification (customer recognizes a need), Research (customer looks for solutions), Decision (customer compares options), and Purchase (customer commits). Traditional marketing focuses on stages 2-3, while content marketing shines in stages 1-2.

> "Content marketing done well takes the pressure off the final transaction and allows you to get down to business."

**Quick Take**: Create content that addresses problems your audience doesn't even know they have yet. By the time they're ready to buy, they'll already see you as the natural solution.

### The Two Critical Components: Content + Marketing
Many businesses focus only on creating content while neglecting the marketing aspect. Successful content marketing requires both: (1) compelling content that tells your story and (2) effective promotion to get that content in front of your audience.

Content refers to your message and story, while marketing encompasses the promotion and distribution across various channels. One without the other leads to failure — great content without promotion goes unseen, while promotion without valuable content alienates your audience.

> "There are two critical components of content marketing; content and marketing. This may seem obvious, but so many companies focus on the first part alone and forget all about the marketing side of the equation."

**Quick Take**: Split your time equally between content creation and content promotion. The best content in the world won't work if nobody sees it.

### What Makes Content Great
Great content must be findable, shareable, usable, readable, memorable, quotable, and actionable. Each element serves a specific purpose in engaging your audience and driving business results.

Findable content uses proper SEO optimization and website organization. Shareable content has compelling headlines and easy sharing mechanisms. Usable content works across different devices and platforms. Readable content uses clear formatting and engaging writing style. Memorable content answers the "so what" question with unique perspectives.

> "Great content is simple: it is something that engages the majority of your target audience in a valuable way and improves your visibility and profits."

**Quick Take**: Before publishing any content, test it against these seven criteria. If it fails on multiple fronts, it's not ready for your audience.

### The Strategic Content Marketing Process
Successful content marketing follows a cyclical process: Strategize, Develop Content, Promote, Measure, and Realign as needed. This continuous loop ensures constant improvement and adaptation to audience feedback.

The strategy phase focuses on research — understanding your audience, competition, and unique positioning. Content development should maintain a sustainable pace for at least eight weeks to determine effectiveness. Promotion requires identifying concepts that resonate and creating content people naturally want to share. Measurement helps refine future strategies.

> "Like the patience required to develop SEO, you need to be patient with your content marketing. The process takes time."

**Quick Take**: Commit to at least eight weeks of consistent content marketing before making major adjustments. Success requires patience and persistence.

### Common Mistakes to Avoid
Three primary mistakes plague new content marketers: creating content based on personal interests rather than audience needs, repeating others' perspectives instead of offering unique insights, and failing to develop a consistent voice.

Additional pitfalls include not fully understanding your target audience, failing to establish credibility through proper branding and personal connection, neglecting editing and proofreading, lacking visual aids, and ignoring details like headlines and comment engagement.

> "If you are just regurgitating content from other places you are not offering anything valuable to your audience and they are not going to seek you out as an industry leader."

**Quick Take**: Before creating any content, ask: "Is this uniquely valuable to my target audience?" If the answer is no, start over.

## Action Items

- **Research your audience thoroughly** before creating any content
- **Develop an editorial calendar** to maintain consistent publishing schedules
- **Create content that solves specific problems** for your target audience
- **Implement proper SEO optimization** to ensure content discoverability
- **Include visual elements** in every piece of content you create
- **Promote each piece of content** across multiple channels
- **Engage with your audience** through comments and feedback
- **Measure performance metrics** and adjust strategies accordingly
- **Develop a consistent brand voice** across all content
- **Focus on providing genuine value** rather than selling directly

## Questions to Consider

- What problems does your target audience face that they might not even be able to articulate yet?
- How can you provide value before asking for anything in return?
- What unique perspective can you offer that differentiates you from competitors?
- Which content formats best serve your audience's preferences and your strengths?
- How will you measure success beyond direct sales and conversions?
- What systems can you implement to ensure consistent content creation and promotion?

## Conclusion
This book provides a solid foundation for understanding content marketing fundamentals, making it particularly valuable for beginners and small business owners. Schaefer breaks down complex concepts into actionable steps, emphasizing the importance of patience, consistency, and audience-focused thinking.

The book's greatest strength lies in its practical approach and emphasis on avoiding common pitfalls. The focus on the seven characteristics of great content provides a useful framework for evaluating content quality. However, more experienced marketers might find some sections overly basic, and the strategies could benefit from more specific, modern examples and case studies.

**Is this worth your time?** Absolutely if you're new to content marketing or struggling to see results from your current efforts. The biggest reason to read this book is its emphasis on providing genuine value to your audience rather than treating content as just another sales channel. This mindset shift alone makes it worthwhile for any business looking to build sustainable marketing strategies.

The book serves as an excellent starting point, but supplement it with current industry resources and platforms as digital marketing evolves rapidly. Focus on the principles of value creation and audience service, and you'll build a foundation that transcends specific tactics or platforms.
