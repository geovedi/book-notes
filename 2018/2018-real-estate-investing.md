# Real Estate Investing: 7 Ways Anyone Can Use To Make Money In Real Estate
**Authors**: Mark Atwood | **Year**: 2018 | **Category**: Business & Finance

## Summary
This practical guide demystifies real estate investing by presenting seven distinct strategies that anyone can use to generate wealth through property. The book emphasizes that successful real estate investing requires education, proper planning, and treating it as a business rather than a get-rich-quick scheme. Atwood breaks down complex investment concepts into actionable steps, making property investment accessible to beginners while providing valuable insights for experienced investors.

Perfect for aspiring investors, busy professionals looking for passive income streams, or anyone seeking to diversify their investment portfolio beyond traditional stocks and bonds. Readers will gain the knowledge needed to start their real estate journey with confidence and avoid common pitfalls that derail many new investors.

## Core Investment Strategies

### 1. Long-Term Rental Properties
The foundation of real estate wealth comes from buying and holding rental properties that generate monthly cash flow while building equity over time. Focus on three-bedroom, two-bathroom homes in familiar neighborhoods near where you live.

**Key Requirements**:
- Ensure positive cash flow of at least $200/month from day one
- Buy in areas you know with good schools and amenities
- Never bet on future appreciation—only buy properties that work immediately
- Recognize that wealth builds slowly over time through mortgage payoff and appreciation

> "Landlording may not make you rich today but, over time, it is probably the number one wealth creator in this country."

**Quick Take**: Start small with local properties you can manage yourself, reinvest cash flow into additional properties, and let your tenants pay down your mortgages while you benefit from long-term appreciation.

### 2. Fix and Flip Strategy
The fastest path to real estate riches involves buying distressed properties below market value, renovating them, and selling for quick profits. This strategy requires finding motivated sellers and securing appropriate funding.

**Success Factors**:
- Target properties needing cosmetic repairs, not major structural issues
- Build relationships with private lenders who offer flexible financing
- Focus on desirable neighborhoods where you can resell quickly
- Accurately estimate repair costs and purchase price to ensure profitable margins

> "Fix and flip is a real estate investing strategy that allows you to make a profit by finding cheap, distressed properties, fixing them up and then selling them for a higher price."

**Quick Take**: Use private investment loans rather than traditional bank financing, buy well below market value, and focus on properties that can be renovated and resold within 3-6 months.

### 3. Property Wholesaling
A low-risk entry point into real estate that requires no capital—only the ability to find deeply discounted properties and connect them with cash buyers. As a wholesaler, you act as a middleman, earning assignment fees without ever owning the property.

**Four Essential Steps**:
1. Locate motivated sellers facing financial difficulties or urgent relocations
2. Negotiate properties at significant discounts (typically 30-50% below market)
3. Get properties under contract with favorable terms
4. Assign contracts to rehabbers or investors for quick profit

> "Anyone of legal working age can do wholesale since there isn't need to acquire a certain amount of capital. All that is needed is to search for interested and motivated sellers."

**Quick Take**: Focus on finding motivated sellers first, build a buyer's list of cash investors, and concentrate on creating win-win solutions for everyone involved.

### 4. Vacation Rental Investments
Combining personal use with investment potential, vacation rentals provide both a paid vacation site for your family and income from other travelers. Success depends on location selection and proper property management.

**Critical Success Factors**:
- Choose popular tourist destinations with year-round appeal
- Factor in seasonal vacancy rates when calculating returns
- Hire reputable property management companies for remote investments
- Ensure properties offer amenities vacationers expect

> "You will be able to rent out the home to others, and also you will have a place to stay if you and your family would like to go on vacation."

**Quick Take**: Start with smaller properties in established vacation areas, budget for seasonal income fluctuations, and use professional management to handle guest turnover and maintenance.

### 5. Short-Term Rental Strategy
Similar to vacation rentals but can apply to any property, offering flexibility and potentially higher income than long-term traditional leases. Success requires working with experienced agencies and setting clear rental policies.

**Implementation Requirements**:
- Partner with reputable rental agencies to reduce risk and increase occupancy
- Establish clear rules regarding guests, pets, and property use
- Verify insurance coverage for short-term rental activities
- Furnish properties completely to appeal to various guest types

> "Short term rentals in this situation can provide you with the flexibility you need and eliminate the risk of having a long-term tenant in the property."

**Quick Take**: Use professional agencies to handle bookings and guest management, set clear rental policies, and ensure your property is fully furnished and equipped for short-term stays.

### 6. Non-Performing Notes
Advanced strategy involving buying mortgages in default from banks at substantial discounts, allowing investors to either modify loans for cash flow or foreclose and acquire properties below market value.

**Key Advantages**:
- Purchase properties for fractions of their actual worth
- Win-win solution helping banks while acquiring assets cheaply
- Multiple exit strategies including loan modification, foreclosure, or property resale
- Less competition than traditional real estate purchases

> "Banks and lenders need a solution to alleviate the damaging impact of these non-performing assets. Buying non-performing notes is a win-win situation."

**Quick Take**: Build relationships with bank managers and brokers, focus on notes with desirable collateral, and be prepared for complex negotiation processes.

### 7. Real Estate Investment Trusts (REITs)
Indirect real estate investment through publicly traded companies that own and operate income-producing properties. Perfect for passive investors who want real estate exposure without property management responsibilities.

**REIT Categories**:
- **Equity REITs**: Own properties and generate rental income
- **Mortgage REITs**: Provide financing and earn interest income
- **Hybrid REITs**: Combine both ownership and lending activities
- **Sector-Specific**: Focus on retail, healthcare, office, or residential properties

> "Investing in REITs is similar to investing in any other venture or business. You invest by buying stocks or shares of a particular company and then receive a percentage of the profit earned by that company."

**Quick Take**: Start with diversified equity REITs for long-term growth, consider sector-specific REITs for targeted exposure, and reinvest dividends for compound growth.

## Action Items

- **Educate Yourself First**: Listen to real estate audiobooks and podcasts during your daily commute before investing any money
- **Start Local**: Begin with properties in familiar neighborhoods where you understand market dynamics and can manage directly
- **Build Your Network**: Connect with experienced investors, lenders, and real estate professionals in your target market
- **Create Cash Reserves**: Maintain emergency funds for unexpected repairs, vacancies, or market downturns
- **Choose One Strategy**: Master one investment approach before diversifying into multiple strategies
- **Set Clear Goals**: Define whether you want cash flow or lump sums, and align your strategy accordingly
- **Market Consistently**: Allocate budget for finding both deals and buyers—marketing is the engine of your business

## Questions to Consider

- Do you want to be an active landlord managing properties, or prefer passive investments?
- Are you seeking monthly cash flow or large lump sums from property flips?
- How much time can you realistically dedicate to real estate investing each week?
- Do you have the temperament to work directly with motivated sellers in difficult situations?
- Are you prepared for the risks and responsibilities of property ownership?
- What's your risk tolerance for different investment strategies?
- How will you finance your first investment deals?

## Pre-Investment Checklist

Ask yourself these critical questions before buying any property:

1. **Business Mindset**: Are you treating this as a business or hobby?
2. **Seller Interaction**: Are you comfortable negotiating directly with motivated sellers?
3. **Buyer Management**: Will you handle buyer calls and showings yourself?
4. **Funding Strategy**: Where will you get money for both purchases and marketing?
5. **Income Preference**: Do you want steady cash flow or large cash payments?
6. **Geographic Focus**: Which markets do you know well enough to invest in?
7. **Education Plan**: How will you continue learning and improving your skills?
