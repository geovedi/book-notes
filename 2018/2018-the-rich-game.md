# The Rich Game
**Authors**: Marten Livingston | **Year**: 2018

## Summary
"The Rich Game" reveals the fundamental mindset shifts and practical strategies that separate the wealthy from everyone else. The book argues that becoming rich isn't about luck or exceptional intelligence—it's about adopting the right way of thinking about money and taking consistent, disciplined actions. Livingston breaks down wealth creation into understandable principles, starting with the crucial foundation of mindset, then moving through goal-setting, investing, and building multiple income streams.

This guide is perfect for anyone feeling trapped in the cycle of living paycheck to paycheck, those who want to build wealth but don't know where to start, or anyone who suspects that the wealthy know something about money that they don't. Readers will gain a clear framework for transforming their relationship with money and building sustainable wealth over time.

## Core Insights

### 1. The Mindset Foundation
Your habitual thinking patterns determine your financial destiny more than any external factors. The research presented shows dramatic differences between how rich and poor people think: 90% of poor people believe fate determines their financial status, while only 10% of wealthy people do. Similarly, 87% of poor people think you need special intellectual gifts to become wealthy, compared to just 10% of the rich.

The brain operates on autopilot 40% of the time, running stored habits from the basal ganglia. This means if you want to become wealthy, you must consciously build wealth-attracting thought patterns until they become automatic habits. The wealthy create their lives rather than letting life happen to them, focus on winning rather than avoiding loss, and associate with other successful people.

> "The main lesson here is, to become rich, you have to think like that rich person you think about."
**Quick Take**: Start by examining your automatic thoughts about money. Replace scarcity thinking with abundance thinking, and practice wealth-building thoughts until they become second nature.

### 2. Pay Yourself First Principle
The most powerful wealth-building habit is paying yourself first—setting aside money for savings and investments before paying any other bills. Most people pay themselves last, saving only whatever is left over after expenses, which is typically nothing. This approach guarantees financial struggle regardless of income level.

The wealthy treat their savings and investments like non-negotiable bills that must be paid. They set up automatic transfers on payday, moving money to retirement accounts, emergency funds, and investment accounts before they have a chance to spend it. This behavioral hack removes willpower from the equation and makes wealth building automatic.

> "Paying yourself first is setting aside your savings before doing anything else. It is not to be confused with spending money on yourself."
**Quick Take**: Set up automatic transfers from your checking account to investment and savings accounts that happen on payday, before you can touch the money.

### 3. Assets vs. Liabilities Mindset
A fundamental wealth principle is understanding the difference between assets and liabilities. Assets put money in your pocket each month, while liabilities take money out. Most people mistakenly think their house and car are assets, but the wealthy perspective is different: if something costs you money monthly, it's a liability.

True assets include rental properties, dividend-paying stocks, bonds, and businesses that generate passive income. Your personal residence, cars, furniture, and electronics are all liabilities that continuously drain your wealth. The wealthy accumulate income-producing assets first, then use the cash flow from those assets to afford luxuries.

> "The rich have lots of money, but the wealthy don't worry about money."
**Quick Take**: Before any purchase, ask yourself: "Will this put money in my pocket or take money out?" Focus your spending on acquiring income-producing assets.

### 4. Passive Income Over Active Income
Active income requires your direct involvement and stops when you stop working. Passive income continues flowing whether you're working or not. The wealthy focus on building passive income streams because this creates true financial freedom—they don't have to trade time for money.

Passive income sources include rental properties, dividend stocks, business investments, and intellectual property. Beyond the financial benefits, passive income offers greater flexibility, tax advantages, creative fulfillment, and the ability to work from anywhere. The goal is to eventually have enough passive income to cover all living expenses.

> "Passive income, just as the name states, is an income that one gets without being directly involved with the work at hand."
**Quick Take**: Start building small passive income streams alongside your job, and reinvest the earnings to accelerate your path to financial independence.

### 5. Multiple Income Streams Strategy
Relying on a single source of income is risky in today's economy. The wealthy typically have three types of income: earned income (from work), portfolio income (from investments), and passive income (from assets). This diversification provides stability and accelerated wealth building.

Creating multiple income streams doesn't mean working multiple jobs—it means building systems that generate money in different ways. This might include starting a side business, investing in real estate, or creating digital products. Each additional income stream reduces your dependence on any single source and speeds up wealth accumulation.

> "The wealthy know that you need to have more than one income method to remain at your current status and to keep growing."
**Quick Take**: Start with one additional income stream beyond your job, then gradually add more as your systems become more efficient.

## Action Items

- **Audit your mindset**: Track your automatic thoughts about money for one week and identify limiting beliefs
- **Set up automatic savings**: Arrange for 10-20% of your income to be automatically transferred to investment accounts on payday
- **Create a financial plan**: Write down specific, measurable financial goals with timelines
- **Eliminate bad debt**: Create a systematic plan to pay off high-interest debt starting with the smallest balances
- **Start learning**: Begin educating yourself about one type of passive income investment each month
- **Build emergency fund**: Save 3-6 months of expenses in a separate account before aggressive investing
- **Audit your possessions**: Identify which of your current assets are actually liabilities draining your wealth

## Questions to Consider

- What automatic thoughts about money might be holding you back from wealth?
- Are you currently paying yourself first or last?
- Which of your current possessions are assets vs. liabilities?
- What passive income opportunities align with your skills and interests?
- How could you start building your first additional income stream this month?
- What lifestyle changes would allow you to invest more in income-producing assets?

## Conclusion
"The Rich Game" is definitely worth your time if you're serious about building wealth but feel overwhelmed by where to start. Its greatest strength is breaking down complex financial concepts into simple, actionable principles that anyone can implement. The book excels at explaining not just what to do, but why wealthy people think differently about money.

The biggest reason to read this book is that it addresses the root cause of financial struggle—mindset—before diving into practical strategies. While some concepts may seem basic, the depth of explanation and real-world examples make them stick. This isn't a get-rich-quick scheme but a realistic roadmap to financial freedom that emphasizes patient, consistent wealth building through proven principles.

If you're ready to transform your relationship with money and start building real wealth, this book provides both the psychological foundation and practical roadmap to get you there.
